There are only **two base classes**: **Fighter** and **Magician**.

- **Fighter** represents mastery of physical combat, positioning, endurance, and action economy.
- **Magician** represents mastery of magical forces, affinities, and mana manipulation.

As characters level, they gain points, proficiencies, and access to **Disciplines** (for Fighters) or **Schools** (for Magicians), allowing specialization into distinct play styles.

Subclasses, pacts, and other systems build on top of this foundation but do not replace it.
### Definitions
**Hit Dice:** When you gain a level, roll this die and add the result to your maximum Hit Points.
**Vitality Dice:** When you gain a level, roll this die, add the result plus your Constitution modifier to your maximum Vitality.
**Mana Dice:** When you gain a level, roll this die, add the result plus your Wisdom modifier to your maximum Mana.
**Stat Point (Stp):** Spend to increase your ability scores: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Wisdom, Intelligence, or Charisma. Normal escalating costs apply for scores above 20.
**Skill Point (Skp):** Spend to increase skill ranks such as Athletics, Stealth, Sleight of Hand, and similar non-combat proficiencies.
**Affinity Point (Affp):** Spend to increase non-generic Affinity scores such as Fire, Earth, Water, Air, Physical, Craft, Creation, or Utility.l 
**Action Point (Ap):** Represents full actions taken during your turn. You cannot have fewer than 1 Ap.
**Bonus Action Point (BAp):** Represents quick or auxiliary actions during your turn. You cannot have fewer than 1 BAp. You may convert 2 BAp into 1 Ap during your turn.
**Reaction Point (Rp):** Represents reactions taken outside your turn. You cannot have fewer than 1 Rp.
**Proficiency Bonus:** If you are proficient with a weapon or skill, you add your Proficiency Bonus plus the relevant ability modifier to applicable rolls. Weapon attacks add the relevant Strength or Dexterity modifier. Proficiency Bonus starts at +1 and Increases by +1 every 3 levels
## Action Types (cost of 1 Ap)
- **Spell Attack**: Make a spell attack against a valid target.
- **Weapon Attack**: Make an attack with a weapon you are wielding against a creature within range.
- **Improvised Weapon Attack**: Attack using an object not designed as a weapon. Base damage: 1d6 bludgeoning. You are not proficient with improvised weapons.
- **Unarmed Attack**: Strike using fists, feet, or body. Base damage: 1d4 bludgeoning. You are not proficient with unarmed attacks unless stated otherwise.
- **Shove**: Make an Athletics check contested by the target’s Athletics. On failure: target is pushed 5 ft. On failure by 10 or more: target is pushed 5 ft and knocked prone
- **Grapple**: Make a contested Athletics check against a creature within 5 ft. On success: Target is restrained and considered prone, Target must spend **1 Ap** on its turn to attempt escape (contested Athletics)
- **Hide**: If you have at least **¼ cover**, make a Stealth check against enemy passive perception. On success, you are hidden and your first attack gains advantage. Enemies may attempt a Spot/Search check on their turn, If remaining hidden is impossible, Hide automatically fails (GM discretion)
- **Dodge**: Must be your final action of the turn. Enemies have disadvantage on attack rolls against you. If you spend **3 or more Ap** Dodging, enemies have double disadvantage
- **Disengage:** You do not provoke opportunity attacks for this turn from your movement.
## Bonus Action Types (Cost of 1 BAp)
- **Equip Item**: Equip or swap a held item (weapon, ring, focus, etc.). 
	- Armor equip time:
		- Light Armor: 2 rounds
		- Medium Armor: 4 rounds
		- Heavy Armor: 8 rounds
- **Use Item**: Activate an item’s effect. Some items may additionally require Ap.
- **Use Consumable**: Drink a potion or consume a one-use item.
## Reaction Types (Cost of 1 Rp)
- **Opportunity Attack:** When a hostile creature leaves your melee range, you may make a melee weapon attack against it.
## Fighting Disciplines
Fighting Disciplines represent **how** a Fighter fights. As Fighters level, they gain **Fighting Discipline Levels (FDL)**.  Each FDL must be assigned to **one Discipline**, unlocking passive bonuses and active techniques within that Discipline. A Fighter may invest in multiple Disciplines, allowing hybrid or highly specialized builds.
##### Discipline Categories
- **Guardian** – damage mitigation, blocking, protection
- **Weapon Mastery** – deep specialization in all weapon groups
- **Heavy Arms** – great weapons, crushing force, area pressure
- **Light Arms** – finesse weapons, precision, mobility
- **Ranged Combat** – bows, crossbows, thrown weapons
- **Unarmed Combat** – fists, grapples, body control
- **Skirmisher** – speed, positioning, hit-and-run tactics
- **Brute** – raw strength, momentum, overwhelming force
- **Duelist** – precision, counters, single-target dominance
- **Infiltrator** – stealth, ambush, sabotage
- **Engineer / Alchemist** – devices, traps, crafted weapons
- **Mounted Combatant** – riding, charges, mobility synergy

Specific mechanics are defined within each Discipline.
## Magician Schools
Magician Schools represent how magic is shaped and applied. Each School governs casting style, resource interaction, and spell structure.  A Magician may study multiple Schools, though mastery requires focus. Similar to FDL magicians gain MSL. 
##### School Categories (non-exhaustive)
- **Elemental** – Fire, Earth, Water, Air
- **Energy** – raw energy control
- **Vital** – life, vitality, regeneration, decay
- **Mental** – mind, perception, control
- **Spatial** – movement, teleportation, distance
- **Illusion** – deception, false perception
- **Binding** – summoning, contracts, containment
- **Runic** – inscribed and persistent magic
- **Ritual** – slow, powerful, prepared casting
- **Nature** - magic from nature
- **Bloodline** - magic from your bloodline

Specific mechanics are defined within each School.
## Pacts
A Pact is a binding agreement made to gain power beyond normal progression.
- Pacts grant persistent benefits, abilities, or scaling bonuses
- Every Pact carries an explicit cost, limitation, or obligation
- Pacts coexist with class progression rather than replacing it
Details are defined per Pact.
