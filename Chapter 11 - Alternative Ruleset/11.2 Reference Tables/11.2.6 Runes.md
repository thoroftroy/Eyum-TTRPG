All runes can be purchased as single use items from stores. They can then be used for enchanting and spells.
# Runic Inscription (Enchanting)
Runes are ancient symbols etched into items to tap into the fundamental forces of the world.
- Xeran Runes: Used for Permanent Enchantments. Once applied, the item is permanently changed.
- Vindroth Runes: Used for Spellcasting. Will be explained lower down.
### Runic Logic & Constraints
- A single Xeran rune can be etched onto an item with no penalty. However, if you wish to place two or more effect runes on a single object, they must be bound within a Runic Circle.
- Enchanted items cannot be modified. You must decide the full runic array before the first inscription.
- A single runic circle can support a maximum of 10 Effect Runes.
- The Carbon Ratio: In any circle, the volatile energies must be balanced by Carbon Runes. The formula is:
	- *Number of Carbon Runes = Number of Effect Runes - 1*

## The Inscription Ritual
Inscribing a runic array is a delicate process of channeling mana through physical matter. To successfully enchant an item, a crafter must perform a sequence of Inscription Checks, one for every rune in the array (including Carbon runes).

1. The Inscription Check
	- For each rune, roll a d20 and add your Relevant Affinity Modifier.
	- The Base DC is 12 and it goes up depending on the DC Mod (see above table).
	- Example: Inscribing a Fire Rune has a total DC of 16.

2. The Ritual Sequence
	- If you are creating a Runic Circle, you must roll for every rune in the circle in one continuous session. 
	- If you pass the roll, the rune is successfully etched. Move to the next rune in the array.
	- Critical Success (Natural 20): The rune is etched perfectly; you gain Advantage on the next roll in the sequence.

3. Failed Inscriptions & Unstable Mana
	- If a single roll in the sequence fails or you are interrupted, the ritual fails and the item becomes Saturated with unstable mana.
	- The item cannot be enchanted further, and the current runes provide no bonuses.
	- To clear the Saturated state, you must spend 1 Hour and 100 Mana to fix the item. Once fixed, the item is restored to its original, non-magical state and can be enchanted again from scratch.
	- All runes that were used are destroyed in the process
# Vindroth Runes
Vindroth runes are written as sentence structures (often called _paragraphs_), allowing rune magic to function like a programmed trigger system, when a condition is met, an effect occurs. Vindroth runes are not arranged in circles. Instead, they are inscribed as ordered “words” that form a readable sequence (ex: If … Than … Else …).
### Inscription Rules
- Each Vindroth rune occupies 1 square inch of space.
- All runes in a paragraph must be inscribed into one continuous piece of material.
- If the material has a complete break that separates words, the paragraph loses the separated words. This may weaken, change, or fully disable the effect.
### Vindroth Structure Limits
A Vindroth paragraph is limited by your Level.
- You may include 1 “If … Than” block per your Level/2.
- Every 2 Levels also increases how many logic-connectors you may include (Else / And / Or / Not).
### Using Xeran as Effect Payloads
Vindroth can call Xeran runes as effect payloads (usually to define damage type / element / output).
Example structure:
- If object (human) entered size (5) shape (sphere) Than Power (Xeran Fire)
To increase output, you may repeat effect runes:
- … Than Power (Xeran Fire) And Power (Xeran Fire)
With each And Power adding another damage as well as consuming 1 Structure Limit

## Example Castings

### Simple Fire Trap (Entry Trigger)
**Structure**
```
If object (any) entered size (5) shape (sphere)
Than Power (Xeran Fire)
```
**What it Does**  
Deals 1d4 Fire damage to any creature that enters a 5 ft sphere around the inscription.

**Structure Use**
- If/Than blocks: 1
- Logic connectors: 0

**Minimum Level**
- **1**

**Notes**  
Baseline Vindroth usage. This is the canonical rune trap example.

### Smart Defense Engine
**Structure**
```
If object (any) entered size (20) shape (sphere)
And lacks (ally)
Than Mark (hostile)

If object (any) has (hostile)
And cast
Than Dispel

If object (any) has (hostile)
And attacked
Than Power (Xeran Force)
And Power (Xeran Force)
And throw (Str)
And throw (15)

Else Heal
```
**What it Does**
- Marks non-allies entering the area
- Dispels hostile spellcasting
- Punishes hostile attacks with 2d4 Force damage (Strength save)
- Heals allies when no hostile condition is met

**Structure Use**
- If/Than blocks: 3
- And connectors: 6
- Else: 1

**Minimum Vindroth School Level**
- 20
