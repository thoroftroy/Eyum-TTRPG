## Grivlit
Grivlits are small humanoids with a light, narrow skeleton and long, thin limbs. The torso is short and tight, with a shallow ribcage and a slight inward taper at the waist that makes the chest look pinched. The shoulders are narrow and slope slightly, while the hips are slim and set high, so the legs start long and straight. The knees and elbows sit a little low on the limbs, giving the arms and legs an overgrown look. Hands are small with long fingers and narrow palms, built more for quick grabbing than raw grip. Feet are long and thin, with high arches and toes that spread wide for balance on rough ground.

Skin is muted green to yellow-green, dull and dry, with a thin, stretched texture over bones. The surface often looks dusty, and it roughens at the knuckles, heels, knees, and elbows. The head is large compared to the body, with a sloped forehead, a small jaw, and a narrow chin. Ears are very long and sharply pointed, flaring outward from the sides of the skull and sometimes reaching below the jawline. The scalp is hairless and smooth. Eyes are large and forward-facing, with dark pupils and dim irises (brown, amber, gray), and heavy upper lids that give a watchful look. Teeth are small, sharp, and uneven, with crowded spacing and frequent chips.
## Tull
Tull are also small humanoids, but their frame is sturdier and more balanced. The torso is longer than a grivlit’s, with a deeper ribcage and a thicker midsection that looks built for carrying weight. The shoulders are broader, the collarbones sit flatter, and the neck is thicker. Arms and legs are still long, but the limbs have more muscle mass and thicker joints, especially at the shoulders, elbows, knees, and ankles. The stance is more stable: hips are wider, feet plant more flatly, and the spine holds a stronger upright curve instead of a constant forward lean. Hands are larger than a grivlit’s, with thicker fingers and a stronger thumb that suggests better grip strength.

Skin tones range from ash-gray to stone-gray or gray-brown. The skin is tougher and more hide-like, with natural calluses forming early on the palms, soles, and outer forearms. The face is broader, with a heavier brow ridge, wider nose bridge, and thicker lips. Ears remain long and pointed, but they are thicker at the base and angle slightly backward rather than flaring wide. The scalp is bald, and facial hair is absent. Eyes are smaller relative to the face than a grivlit’s, often deep-set under the brow, with muted irises and a steady, blunt gaze. Teeth are sharp and irregular, with enlarged lower canines that look like the start of tusks but do not project past the lips.
## Grull
Grulls are medium humanoids with a clear jump in mass and density. The torso becomes wide and deep, with a thick ribcage and a heavy, forward-weighted chest. Shoulders broaden sharply, and the upper back becomes thick with muscle, giving a strong block silhouette. Arms remain long but now look reinforced: the forearms are thick, the elbows are wide, and the wrists are strong. Legs are thick through the thighs and calves, and the knees appear oversized, built to handle load. The spine sits straighter than a tull’s, but the neck and shoulders still carry a slightly forward ready posture. Hands are large, with wide palms and thick fingers that end in hard, dark nails.

Skin is darker gray and more textured, often with rough patches along the spine, shoulders, and outer arms. The skin folds at joints are heavy and crease deeply, especially at the neck and elbows. The head grows heavier and more squared, with a stronger jaw and thick jaw muscles visible under the skin. Small tusks begin: the lower canines extend past the gum line and may slightly press against the lower lip, creating a subtle bulge even when the mouth is closed. Ears shorten a bit relative to head size and sit closer to the skull, still pointed but less delicate. Eyes are smaller and deeper-set, often with thick lids and a hard stare. Teeth are strong and uneven, with frequent wear and chips, and the bite looks built for tearing rather than chewing cleanly.
## Gorul
Gorul are medium humanoids with heavy muscle and thick bone, built like compact power. The chest is broad and deep, the shoulders are wide, and the arms are thick from shoulder to wrist, with clear muscle shape even at rest. The torso looks dense and packed, with a strong abdomen and thick lower back. Legs are slightly shorter relative to the torso than earlier forms, lowering the center of mass and making the stance very stable. The hips are wide, the knees are reinforced, and the ankles look thick, as if braced. Hands and feet are large and blunt, with thick toes and fingers that look like they were built for crushing grip more than fine work.

Skin is dark gray and tough, often scarred, with raised scar tissue that stays visible instead of fading flat. The neck is short and thick, blending into the shoulders, and the head sits low with a heavy brow. Tusks are now clearly visible: the lower canines push past the lips and curve slightly upward, thick at the base and blunt at the tips from wear. The face is wide, with a flattened nose, heavy cheek structure, and thick lips that stretch tight over the jaw. Ears are short and thick, pointed but close to the head. Eyes are deep-set and often narrowed by the brow ridge, giving a permanent stern look. The teeth are large and strong, with irregular spacing and a bite that looks made for tearing meat and cracking hard food.
## Boaf
Boaf are large humanoids with a dramatic increase in size and structural strength. The skeleton thickens everywhere: shoulders widen, joints enlarge, and the spine looks reinforced through the back and hips. The torso becomes barrel-shaped, with a wide chest and thick abdomen supported by dense core muscle. Arms hang low and heavy, and the forearms are especially thick, giving the impression of constant strength even without flexing. Legs are powerful and straight, built for supporting mass over long periods; the thighs and calves are thick, and the knees appear large and armored by bone. The posture is upright but heavy, with slow, stable movement and minimal wasted motion.

Skin is coarse and hide-like, usually gray to charcoal with uneven patches and darker areas around the shoulders, back, and elbows. The surface often looks weathered, with thick calluses and deep creases at the neck, knees, and knuckles. Tusks are medium length and thick, clearly projecting past the mouth and curving outward or slightly upward. The skull is large and blocky, with a thick brow ridge and a wide, heavy jaw. Ears are small and sit close to the head, pointed but blunt at the tips. Eyes are smaller relative to the face and sit under heavy brow bone, giving a constant shadowed look. Teeth are large and uneven, with worn edges, and the mouth appears built for tearing and grinding tough food.
## Brogath

Brogath are large humanoids at the upper extreme of mass and density, shaped like living siege engines. The skeleton is massively thick, with oversized joints and a very deep ribcage. The chest is extremely broad and barrel-like, and the shoulders are so wide they appear to hang outward. The spine is reinforced and often holds a slight forward curve under the weight of the upper body. Arms are long and extremely thick, with forearms and biceps that look layered rather than smooth. Hands are huge, with broad palms and thick fingers that taper only slightly, ending in hard nails that can look almost claw-like. Legs are thick and heavy, built for bracing and sudden forward drive rather than quick turning.

Skin is dark gray and deeply textured, with heavy ridges and rough raised lines along the spine, shoulders, and outer arms. The skin folds are thick and form deep creases at the neck and elbows. Tusks are large and prominent, thick at the base and extending far past the lips; they often curve outward and upward and may show chips or worn flat tips. The face is wide and heavy, with deep-set eyes, a thick nose bridge, and a massive jaw. Ears are small compared to the head, pointed but thick. Eyes sit in deep sockets under heavy bone, often giving a glare even at rest. Teeth are large and strong, irregular in shape, and the bite looks capable of crushing bone or hard armor edges.
## Troll
Trolls are huge humanoids with extreme, uneven proportions and a visibly pulled forward body plan. The spine curves strongly forward, and the neck juts out from the shoulders, creating a hunched silhouette even when they try to stand tall. Arms are very long and heavy, often reaching close to the knees, with massive hands that look oversized even for their body. The shoulders roll forward, and the upper back is thick and ridged. Legs are thick but slightly shorter relative to height, which makes the torso and arms feel even longer. The pelvis is wide and the knees are large, built for raw support and powerful stepping rather than smooth balance. Fingers are thick and heavy, with nails that are blunt, cracked, and dark.

Skin is thick, leathery, and uneven, often mottled gray, green-gray, or brown. The surface is scarred, stretched, and heavily callused, with patches that look almost like hardened bark or old hide. Facial structure is coarse and often asymmetrical, with heavy brow ridges, deep eye sockets, and a wide mouth. Tusks may be present but are frequently irregular, broken, or worn down unevenly. Hair is sparse or absent, and the scalp may show thick folds and scars. Eyes are small and deep-set, often shadowed, with a harsh, predatory stare. Teeth are large and uneven, with blunt wear patterns, and the jaw looks built for tearing rather than clean biting.
## Cyclopse
Cyclopses are huge humanoids similar in mass to trolls but built with more upright balance and cleaner symmetry. The skeleton is massive and dense, but the proportions are more even: shoulders square, spine straighter, and hips aligned to support full height without a constant hunch. The chest is broad and deep, and the torso looks like a thick pillar rather than a forward-bent frame. Arms are long and strong but not wildly exaggerated, ending in large hands with thick fingers and hard nails. Legs are powerful and straight, with reinforced knees and thick ankles that look built for steady, heavy steps. The posture is stable and vertical, giving them a tower presence rather than a lurching one.

The skull is dominated by a single central eye socket set in the middle of the forehead. This socket is massive, ringed by thick bone that protrudes slightly like a natural shield. The eye itself is large, round, and deeply set, with heavy lids and a thick brow rim above it. The rest of the face is broad and flat, with a heavy nose ridge and a wide jaw. Tusks are common, thick and strong, usually more even than a troll’s. Skin is thick and stone-toned (gray, slate, ash), rough but less mottled than troll skin, with fewer random patches. Hair is sparse or absent. Teeth are large and worn, and the mouth looks built for crushing and tearing in equal measure.
## Naram-Sin
Naram-Sin are medium humanoids with a refined, dense build that looks engineered rather than grown wild. The skeleton is heavy and strong without being oversized, and the body proportions are balanced: broad shoulders, thick chest, and strong hips, but without the extreme barrel shape of larger forms. The spine is upright and stable, with a mobile neck that allows controlled head movement. Arms and legs are thick with muscle, but the limb lengths are proportionate, making their stance look deliberate and measured. Hands are large enough to be powerful, but the fingers are more defined and more capable of fine grip than most other tull-forms. The gait is steady and controlled, with minimal sway or wasted motion.

Skin is dark gray to muted stone tones and tends to be smoother and more even in texture than brogath or troll skin. The surface is still tough, with natural callusing, but fewer deep ridges. Tusks are present but smaller and cleaner in shape: lower canines that project modestly and curve slightly upward, usually symmetrical and less chipped. Facial features are more structured and even, with a strong jawline, a thick brow that is less overhanging, and eyes that sit forward with a focused, alert gaze. Ears are short and compact, still pointed but thick and close to the head. Hair is absent. Teeth are strong and more evenly set, with less chaotic crowding, giving the face a more finished look compared to the rougher, more distorted builds of larger forms.
# Racial Abilities
The Tull race and its subraces are unique in that they undergo physical evolution over time. All Tull characters must begin as either a **Tull** or a **Grivlit**, and as they grow and gain levels, they may evolve into progressively higher forms along the Tull lineage, up to **Brogath**. Upon reaching this stage, further evolution requires a final specialization, at which point the character must permanently choose one of three apex forms: **Troll**, **Cyclopse**, or **Naram-Sin**. When you level up and gain Stat Bonuses from your new race, they are additive.

- **Tier 1 Tull Claws.** Your unarmed attacks count as a melee attack and deal 1d6 (Slashing) + 1d4 (Bludgeoning).
- **Tier 2 Evolution.** Evolve into a Grull.
- **Tier 3 Pack Tactics.** Gain advantage on all attack rolls while two or more allied creatures are within 10 ft of you. 
- **Tier 4 Evolution.** Evolve into a Gorul. Gain +1 BAp
- **Tier 5 Stronger Claws.** Your unarmed attacks now deal 1d10 (Slashing) + 1d8 (Bludgeoning). 
- **Tier 6 Evolution.** Evolve into a Boaf.  Gain +1 Ap.
- **Tier 7 Faster Claws.** Each time you make an unarmed attack you may make two unarmed attacks at the cost of an additional bonus action.
- **Tier 8 Evolution.** Evolve into a Brogath. Gain +1 Rp.
- **Tier 9 Strongest Claws.** Your unarmed attacks now deal 1d20 (Slashing) + 1d12(Bludgeoning).
- **Tier 10 Evolution.** Evolve into either a Cyclopse, Troll, or Naram-Sin.
	- **As a Cyclopse:** Advantage on all Strength based rolls (including attack rolls) and your unarmed attacks deal double damage.
	- **As a Troll:** You regenerate 1d8 Hit Points and 1d12 Vitality at the start of each turn.
	- **As a Naram-Sin:** You gain +1 to all skill checks, saving throws, and attack rolls and you can reroll 1s on damage dice (must use new roll).
