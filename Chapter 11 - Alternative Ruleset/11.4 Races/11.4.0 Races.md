#### Human
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism supported by an internal rigid framework that bends at the neck, shoulders, elbows, hips, knees, and ankles. The lower limbs are longer and stronger than the upper limbs and are built for sustained walking and running. The upper limbs branch from the torso and end in flexible five-digit grasping structures capable of both strength and fine manipulation. The torso is widest at the shoulders, narrows toward the waist, and widens again at the hips, allowing stable upright balance.
- **Skin:** Opaque and elastic, capable of stretching and slowly repairing damage. Skin coloration ranges from pale pink-beige through tan, brown, and deep brown. The surface is generally smooth but thickens in areas of repeated pressure such as hands and feet.
- **Hair:** Flexible filament growth concentrated on the scalp, with lesser growth on limbs and torso. Hair grows continuously and ranges from straight to tightly curled in texture.
- **Eyes:** Two forward-facing visual organs with visible whites surrounding a colored central ring. Iris colors include brown, blue, green, gray, and hazel.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are blunt-edged and uniform. No claws, fangs, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and must surface to breathe when swimming.
#### Aurian
- Human but tan skin, red eyes, white hair, and long arms. 
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism supported by a rigid internal framework with the same joint layout as humans but built from denser bone. The limbs are evenly proportioned, with arms and legs of nearly equal length, producing a balanced stance. The hands end in long, narrow five-digit grasping structures suited for precise control. Facial bones are sharply defined, with angular jawlines and prominent cheek structures.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, colored tan, bronze, or ash-beige. The surface resists discoloration from sunlight. Under strain or exertion, faint red or orange light becomes visible beneath the skin.
- **Hair:** Thick filament growth concentrated on the scalp. Hair grows straight or in loose waves. Colors are limited to white, pale silver, and ash-gray.
- **Eyes:** Solid red, ember-orange, or dark rust. No visible whites are present.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are flat and blunt. No claws, fangs, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims without special adaptation.
#### Halofade
- Skinny blonde human who is very yellow
- **Body Structure:** An upright frame with elongated limbs, a narrow ribcage, and a slender neck. The shoulders are narrow and slope gently downward. Fingers and toes are long and thin, with many fine joints allowing delicate movement. The internal structure reinforces joints and load-bearing bones despite the fragile appearance.
- **Skin:** Very pale cream, pearl-white, or faint gold. The surface reflects ambient light strongly, creating a soft luminous sheen. Skin feels smooth and cool to the touch.
- **Hair:** Extremely fine filament growth on the scalp. Individual strands are lightweight and prone to drifting with movement. Colors include platinum blonde, white-gold, and translucent silver.
- **Eyes:** Pale blue, soft gold, faint violet, or white. Pupils remain clearly visible even in low light.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are small and blunt. No claws, fangs, horns, or wings. Cannot fly and floats easily when swimming.
#### Giant
- Big human
- **Body Structure:** A massive upright frame supported by extremely thick bones and oversized joints designed to bear immense weight. The ribcage is deep and wide, housing proportionally large internal organs. Limbs are thick and powerful, with hands and feet disproportionately large relative to the torso. The neck is short and heavily muscled to support the large head.
- **Skin:** Thick and rough-textured, ranging from stone-gray to dark tan. The surface develops calluses rapidly. Skin resists abrasion and cutting.
- **Hair:** Coarse, heavy hair growing thickly on the scalp. Colors include black, dark brown, and rust-red.
- **Eyes:** Brown, dark green, or nearly black.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are broad and flat. No wings or venom. Cannot fly and sinks quickly in deep water.
#### Iceborn
- Human with blue gray skin
- **Body Structure:** An upright, bipedal frame with a compact torso and broad shoulders. Musculature is dense and packed close to the bone, giving a solid appearance. Limbs are slightly shorter relative to torso length, increasing stability. Internal fat layers favor insulation rather than bulk.
- **Skin:** Pale blue, gray, or blue-white. Skin is cold to the touch even in warm environments. The surface firms in extreme cold.
- **Hair:** Thick filament growth on the scalp. Colors include white, silver, pale blue, and frost-gray.
- **Eyes:** Ice-blue, pale gray, or clear white.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are blunt. No claws, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims best in cold water.
#### Fireborn
- Human with red or pink skin
- **Body Structure:** An upright frame with a strong central torso and thick legs. The spine is flexible and built for sustained motion. Musculature is dense and layered around the abdomen and thighs. Hands are broad with reinforced palms.
- **Skin:** Copper, ember-red, orange, or brick-toned. Skin radiates noticeable warmth. The surface feels dry even in humid conditions.
- **Hair:** Coarse filament growth on the scalp. Colors include black, deep red, flame-orange, and gold.
- **Eyes:** Amber, gold, red-brown, or black.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are slightly sharper than average but not true fangs. No wings or venom. Cannot fly and performs poorly in cold water.
#### Kravari
- Super buff humans with massive arms (like the guy with the biggest hands)
- **Body Structure:** A heavily built upright frame with extremely dense muscle layered over thick bone. The chest is deep, shoulders wide, and neck thick. Limbs are heavy and optimized for force rather than speed. Hands are large with thick fingers.
- **Skin:** Dark gray to near-black. Skin scars easily and thickens over time. The surface becomes tougher with age.
- **Hair:** Coarse, thick hair growing densely on the scalp. Colors are limited to black and dark brown.
- **Eyes:** Dark brown, black, or dull red.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are thick and strong. No claws, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims through brute force.
#### Sandman
- Slightly slimy bald humans who have slightly transparent skin
- **Body Structure:** An upright, bipedal frame with slightly narrower shoulders and hips than most humanoids. Muscle tone is even and smooth with minimal excess bulk. Movements are fluid and controlled with little wasted motion. The neck and spine are flexible, allowing subtle posture shifts.
- **Skin:** Semi-translucent pearl, pale sand-pink, or soft gray. The surface allows faint internal shadows to show through. Skin appears glasslike under strong light.
- **Hair:** Fine filament growth lying close to the scalp. Colors include white, pale lavender, muted blue, and soft gray.
- **Eyes:** Milky white, soft violet, or deep black. Some eyes show faint star-like speckling.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are normal. No claws, fangs, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims best in still or shallow water.

#### Elf
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with a light internal framework and long limb proportions that give a tall, narrow silhouette. The lower limbs are built for quick, quiet movement, and the joints bend with a wide, smooth range. The upper limbs end in five-digit grasping structures with long fingers suited for delicate control. The head is slightly narrow with subtly pointed outer ear tips that extend past the usual rounded shape.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, ranging from pale cream to tan to warm brown. The surface shows fine pores but little roughness, and it heals cleanly with minimal scarring. Skin temperature follows ambient conditions but tends to feel slightly cool.
- **Hair:** Filament growth concentrated on the scalp, usually thick and glossy. Colors include black, dark brown, light brown, blonde, copper-red, and white.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and a colored central ring. Iris colors include green, blue, gray, hazel, and deep brown.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are small and blunt-edged with no prominent canines. No claws, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims normally while needing air.
#### Draugir
- Dark elf
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with the same slender proportions as High Elves but with slightly denser muscle along the hips and calves for controlled, silent steps. The lower limbs are long and springy, and the feet strike the ground softly with minimal visible impact. The upper limbs end in five-digit grasping structures with narrow palms and long fingers. The head is narrow with pointed ear tips, and the eyes sit deep, giving a shadowed brow line.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, colored charcoal, deep gray-brown, cool violet-brown, or near-black. The surface reflects little light and appears matte rather than glossy. Skin shows pale scars clearly when they form.
- **Hair:** Filament growth on the scalp that is typically straight or tightly bound in texture. Colors include black, blue-black, silver-white, and ash-gray.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites, but with larger pupils that make the eyes look darker in low light. Iris colors include pale gray, icy blue, violet, and red-brown.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are blunt-edged with slightly sharper canines that do not protrude. No claws, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims normally while needing air.
#### Dryad
- Tree elf
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with long limbs and a flexible spine that allows subtle posture shifts without visible strain. The torso is narrow at the waist with broad shoulders, giving an upright, balanced stance. The hands end in five-digit grasping structures with long fingers and slightly thicker nails. The ears taper to points, and the skin surface shows natural ridging along joints and collarbones like fine raised grain.
- **Skin:** Opaque and firm, ranging from pale bark-beige to warm tan to deep brown, often with faint darker streaking. The surface can feel slightly textured on forearms, shins, and shoulders, as if the outer layer is tougher there. Small scratches close cleanly and leave faint lines rather than rough scars.
- **Hair:** Thick scalp hair that can appear ropey or strand-clustered rather than silky. Colors include dark brown, black, green-black, mossy green, copper, and pale gold.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and a colored central ring. Iris colors include leaf-green, amber, hazel, and dark brown.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are blunt-edged and uniform. No claws, horns, wings, or venom, though nails are often thicker and harder. Cannot fly and swims normally while needing air.
#### Nymph
- Water elf
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with a slim frame and slightly wider hips, built for smooth motion rather than heavy impact. The lower limbs are long and flexible, and the ankles and knees rotate with an easy, fluid bend. The hands end in five-digit grasping structures with faint webbing between the fingers that becomes more visible when spread. The head is narrow with pointed ear tips, and the neck is long, giving a lifted posture.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, ranging from pale pearl to tan to deep brown, often with a faint sheen as if the surface holds moisture. The skin feels cool and slightly slick in humid air. The surface shows fewer rough calluses than most humanoids.
- **Hair:** Scalp hair that tends to lie flat and heavy, as if damp even when dry. Colors include black, dark brown, sea-green, deep blue, and silver.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and a colored central ring. Iris colors include sea-green, blue-gray, dark brown, and pale silver.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are blunt-edged and uniform. No claws, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims strongly, but still must surface to breathe.
#### Siren
- Bitey elf
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with the same slender proportions as other elves but with a slightly broader chest and stronger neck. The lower limbs are long and stable, and the feet and ankles are built for balanced footing rather than sprinting. The upper limbs end in five-digit grasping structures with long fingers and strong grip at the palm. The head is refined and narrow with pointed ear tips, and the jawline is smooth but built to support pronounced canine teeth.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, ranging from pale cream to tan to deep brown, often with a subtle reflective sheen. The surface feels cool and stays smooth even in rough environments. Minor injuries tend to leave faint, thin scars.
- **Hair:** Thick scalp hair that is glossy and dense. Colors include black, dark brown, blonde, copper-red, and silver-white.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and a colored central ring. Iris colors include green, blue, gray, hazel, and deep brown.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth include clear upper and lower canines that are longer and sharper than the rest, forming visible fangs when the mouth opens. No claws, horns, wings, or venom. Cannot fly and swims normally while needing air.
#### Dwarf
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with a compact frame supported by thick internal bone and broad joints. The torso is wide and deep, and the limbs are shorter but heavily muscled, built for strength and endurance. The hands end in five-digit grasping structures with thick fingers and broad palms suited for powerful grip. The head sits on a short, strong neck, and facial structure is heavy at the brow and jaw, giving a blocky profile.
- **Skin:** Opaque and tough, ranging from pale beige to tan to deep brown. The surface thickens quickly into callus at hands, elbows, knees, and feet. Skin tends to scar visibly and holds texture rather than smoothing out.
- **Hair:** Dense scalp hair that grows thick and coarse. Colors include black, brown, red, gray, and white.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and a colored central ring. Iris colors include brown, hazel, green, and gray.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are thick and blunt-edged. No horns, wings, or venom, and nails are thick but not clawlike. Cannot fly and swims adequately, though body density makes floating difficult.
#### Snerin
- Deep Gnome
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with a very compact frame and short limbs, supported by dense bone and tight joints. The torso is barrel-shaped, and the shoulders sit high, giving a hunched-ready posture even when standing straight. The hands end in five-digit grasping structures with short, strong fingers built for firm grip over fine precision. The head is large for the body, with a heavy brow ridge and a short, blunt nose shape.
- **Skin:** Opaque and thick, colored slate-gray, stone-gray, or pale gray-blue. The surface is naturally rougher than dwarven skin and forms calluses quickly. Skin scars easily and holds visible texture from old injuries.
- **Hair:** Scalp hair is sparse or grows in short, coarse patches. Colors include black, dark brown, iron-gray, and dull white.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and usually dark irises. Iris colors include brown, gray, and muddy green.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are blunt and tightly set. No claws, horns, wings, or venom, though nails are thick and squared. Cannot fly and swims poorly due to compact density.
#### Ironwroth 
- Really big dwarf
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with a larger frame than dwarves and longer limbs, approaching tall-humanoid proportions. The torso is broad and heavy, with thick shoulders and a deep chest supported by dense bone. The hands end in five-digit grasping structures with large palms and thick fingers, built for crushing grip strength. The head is wide with a strong jaw and heavy brow, and the neck is thick enough that head movement looks deliberate rather than loose.
- **Skin:** Opaque and tough, ranging from tan to deep brown to dark umber. The surface is rough-textured and shows thickened plates of callus on knuckles, elbows, and shins. Scars are common and remain raised rather than fading flat.
- **Hair:** Coarse scalp hair that grows thick and heavy. Colors include black, dark brown, rust-red, gray, and white.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and dark, intense irises. Iris colors include brown, hazel, dark green, and gray.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are thick and blunt, with slightly larger canines that do not form fangs. No wings, horns, or venom, and nails are thick but not clawlike. Cannot fly and swims with difficulty due to high mass and density.
#### Fairy
- **Body Structure:** A tiny upright, two-legged organism with a lightweight internal framework and very fine joints. The limbs are slender and proportionally long for the body size, giving a delicate silhouette. The hands end in five-digit grasping structures capable of fine manipulation but limited force. A second set of limbs—thin, veined wings—grows from the upper back, large relative to body size and used for lift.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, ranging from pale peach to tan to warm brown, often with a faint reflective shimmer. The surface is thin and bruises easily but heals cleanly. Skin temperature follows ambient conditions and cools quickly.
- **Hair:** Fine scalp hair that grows quickly and is often fluffy or wispy. Colors include black, brown, blonde, red, white, and pastel tones.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes that appear large for the face, with visible whites and bright irises. Iris colors include green, blue, gray, hazel, and violet.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are small and blunt. Wings enable true flight and hovering, but long-distance travel is tiring. Cannot breathe water and swims poorly due to wing drag.
#### Pixy
- **Body Structure:** A tiny upright, two-legged organism with the same lightweight frame as Wingfae but with slightly stronger shoulders and jaw. The limbs are slim and quick-moving, and the hands end in five-digit grasping structures with fine control. A pair of veined wings grows from the upper back, shaped for rapid fluttering and sharp turns in the air. The head is slightly wider at the cheek and jaw, built to support sharper teeth.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, colored purple-gray, slate-gray, charcoal, or cool lavender. The surface often has a faint satin sheen rather than a sparkle. Skin bruises easily and shows pale marks clearly.
- **Hair:** Fine scalp hair that tends to grow thick in messy, light strands. Colors include black, white, silver, deep purple, and gray.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes that appear large and bright, with visible whites. Iris colors include pale gray, violet, icy blue, and bright green.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth include small but distinct fangs that show when smiling or speaking. Wings enable true flight and hovering with quick acceleration. Cannot breathe water and swims poorly.
#### Spritelings
- Fairy with no wings
- **Body Structure:** A small upright, two-legged organism with a light internal framework and compact limbs designed for quick ground movement. The torso is short and the shoulders are narrow, making the body look top-light. The hands end in five-digit grasping structures with fine manipulation but limited strength. The back lacks wings entirely, and the shoulder blades are smooth and flat rather than built for flight muscles.
- **Skin:** Opaque and smooth, ranging from pale cream to tan to soft brown, sometimes with faint freckles or speckling. The surface is thin and marks easily but heals cleanly. Skin temperature follows ambient conditions and cools quickly.
- **Hair:** Fine scalp hair that can be straight, wavy, or fluffy. Colors include black, brown, blonde, red, white, and muted pastel shades.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes that appear large for the face, with visible whites. Iris colors include green, blue, gray, hazel, and amber.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are small and blunt. No claws, horns, wings, or venom, and nails are thin and delicate. Cannot fly and swims only short distances due to low strength and buoyancy.

#### Harpy
- Feathers instead of hair, wings on arms, bird legs under knees and claws
- **Body Structure:** An upright organism with a humanlike torso and a humanlike head, but with the arms replaced by massive wing-limbs. The wing-limbs retain jointed “hand” structures near the tips with hooked digits used for grasping while perched. The legs are humanlike above the knee, then shift into thick bird legs with clawed feet built for gripping and launching. The mouth is wider than most humanoids and the jaw structure supports prominent sharp teeth.
- **Skin:** Skin tones range broadly from pale to tan to brown, but much of the body is patterned with colorful feather growth along shoulders, hips, and thighs. Feathers often form bright bands, gradients, or patchwork coloration that reads decorative even in wild forms. Exposed skin remains smooth and elastic but tends to toughen at the feet and lower legs.
- **Hair:** Scalp hair is common and grows like human hair, often mixed with fine feathering near the temples. Colors include black, brown, blonde, red, and vivid dyed tones that match feather patterns.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites and strong contrast in the iris. Iris colors include green, blue, brown, hazel, gray, and gold.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth include clear fangs capable of tearing, and foot claws are sharp and weight-bearing. Can fly with strong lift and control, but the wing-arms reduce fine two-handed tool use. Can swim only short distances and must surface to breathe.
#### Hushwing 
- Owl head, human torso, arms, legs, cloak like wings around body
- **Body Structure:** An upright, two-legged organism with a humanlike torso and two grasping arms ending in five digits. The head is fully avian, with a rounded skull, a hooked beak, and a neck built for extreme rotation without shifting the shoulders. Large wings grow from the upper back and can fold forward like a cloak, wrapping around the body without interfering with the arms. The legs are humanlike in overall shape but end in taloned feet with thick gripping toes and sharp curved claws.
- **Skin:** Exposed skin is limited to the face around the beak and the lower legs, usually pale, gray, or mottled brown. Most of the body is covered in layered feathers that lie tight when calm and flare slightly when startled. Feather surfaces shed water but hold fine dust easily.
- **Hair:** True scalp hair is rare; head and neck are covered in fine facial feathers that can mimic a hooded “hair” shape. Feather colors range from white and cream to brown, gray, and black with barred or speckled patterns.
- **Eyes:** Large forward-facing eyes with dark pupils and reflective depth that catches light strongly. Iris tones range from gold and amber to deep brown and near-black.
- **Additional Traits:** No fangs or venom; the beak is the primary biting structure, and the claws are capable of tearing. Can fly silently with strong control and can hover briefly when landing. Can swim only short distances and must surface to breathe.
#### Skyclaw
- Hawk head, human torso, arms, legs, powerful wings come out of back
- **Body Structure:** An upright organism with a humanlike torso and two grasping arms ending in five digits. The head is raptor-avian with a strong hooked beak and a sharply defined brow ridge that gives a predatory profile. Powerful wings grow from the upper back and fold tight against the shoulders when not in use. The legs are fully avian from the knee down, ending in heavy talons built for gripping and impact.
- **Skin:** Exposed skin appears along the face and lower legs and ranges from tan to brown to slate-gray, often rougher around the talons. Most of the body is covered in dense flight-feathers across the back and shoulders, with shorter contour feathers across the chest. Feather patterns commonly form hard stripes, mottling, or clean two-tone splits.
- **Hair:** Scalp hair is minimal or absent; head feathers form a sleek crown that lies flat or lifts in a short crest. Colors include brown, black, white, rust, and gold-toned mixes.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing, sharply focused eyes with visible intensity even at rest. Iris colors include amber, gold, dark brown, and pale gray.
- **Additional Traits:** No fangs or venom; the beak can tear, and the talons are built for puncture and grip. Can fly with speed and strong lift, but silent flight is harder than for Nightcloaks. Can swim poorly and must surface to breathe.
#### Blackfeather
- Crow head, humanoid shape to body but covered in black feathers, arms are like wings but with hands at the end (like bat hands but covered in feathers), legs are just thin bird legs
- **Body Structure:** An upright organism with a humanlike torso and two arms ending in five digits, but with wing structures attached along the forearms. The head is avian with a straight, strong beak and a skull shape that reads narrow and clever rather than heavy. The wings extend from the arms like a cape of membrane and feather, allowing short bursts of flight without needing separate back-wings. The legs are avian from the knee down, ending in grasping taloned feet suited for climbing and perching.
- **Skin:** Visible skin is typically limited to the face around the beak and the lower legs, usually gray to black to dark brown. The body is covered in black feathers that can show oil-slick shine in bright light. Feathering is thickest along the shoulders and spine and thinner along the chest to preserve arm movement.
- **Hair:** Scalp hair is absent; the head is covered in tight, short feathers that resemble a smooth cap. Feather sheen can shift between matte black and glossy black depending on light and angle.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes that appear dark at a distance, with small bright highlights. Iris colors include black-brown, deep brown, gray, and occasional pale silver.
- **Additional Traits:** No fangs or venom; the beak is blunt-tipped but strong, and talons are sharp for gripping rather than tearing. Can fly, but not for long distances without rest; they excel at short launches and controlled glides. Can swim only short distances and must surface to breathe.
#### Palehook
- Vulture head and feathery plume around it, human torso, bird legs, human arms, wings come out of back, some feathers cover body but not much
- **Body Structure:** An upright organism with a thicker, heavier torso and long arms ending in five digits. The head is avian with a hooked beak and a mostly bare scalp, framed by a collar of long feathers around the neck. Large wings grow from the upper back and fold into a broad mantle that makes the shoulders look even wider. The legs are avian below the knee, ending in strong talons built more for stability and walking than for high-speed striking.
- **Skin:** Exposed skin is prominent on the head and sometimes the upper neck, ranging from pinkish flesh to gray to dark slate. The body is covered in darker feathers, often black or deep brown, while the neck ruff tends to be pale white or bone-colored. Skin texture on the head is leathery and wrinkled rather than smooth.
- **Hair:** Scalp hair is absent; the head remains bare or covered only in sparse down. Neck feathers form a thick mane-like ring that can flare when threatened.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with a heavy-lidded look that can appear calm or cold. Iris colors include brown, amber, pale gold, and dull gray.
- **Additional Traits:** No fangs or venom; the beak is strong and hooked, and the talons are thick and blunt-tipped compared to raptors. Can fly and glide for long periods, favoring soaring over flapping. Can swim very poorly and must surface to breathe.
#### Roundfeather
- Human head, torso is more bird like but still human shaped, arms are more like wings but with hands like a bat, legs are human to the knee then become bird
- **Body Structure:** An upright organism with a rounded, heavy torso and humanlike arms ending in five digits. The head is fully human. Small wings grow from the upper back but are underpowered relative to body mass and cannot generate sustained lift. The legs are birdlike below the knee, ending in small gripping feet adapted for walking and short hops.
- **Skin:** Exposed skin is limited and usually pale pink, gray, or light tan around the beak and lower legs. The body is covered in thick, soft feathers that create a puffed silhouette even when relaxed. Feather colors are commonly white, gray, and mottled patterns, sometimes with darker neck banding.
- **Hair:** Scalp hair is absent; head feathers form a smooth rounded cap. Some individuals show short neck-feather iridescence that shifts green-purple in strong light.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes that appear round and gentle. Iris colors include orange, amber, brown, and gray.
- **Additional Traits:** No fangs or venom; the beak is short and blunt, and talons are small and not built for combat. Cannot fly, but can glide briefly from height and control falls better than most. Can swim poorly and must surface to breathe.
#### Earfang
- Bat head, human torso, hairy legs, arms are batlike 
- **Body Structure:** An upright organism with a narrow torso and long limbs, built with light bone and flexible joints. #### Therian
- **Body Structure:** A humanoid organism whose overall silhouette remains upright and tool-capable, but whose anatomy is consistently “pulled” toward mammalian animal traits: thicker shoulder girdles, heavier ribcages, reinforced tendons at joints, and a stance that often looks slightly forward-loaded even when standing still. The skull varies widely by lineage but typically includes a lengthened muzzle or broadened nasal structure, prominent cheekbone plates, and jaw hinges built for stronger bite leverage than most humanoids. Hands remain five-digit grasping structures, but finger thickness, nail shape, and wrist mobility shift toward the animal line: many have wider palms, tougher pads, and tendons that lock under load for climbing, dragging, or grappling. Legs are usually either digitigrade (heel lifted, weight on the forefoot) or heavy plantigrade (full foot planted) depending on lineage; either way, the knees and ankles are reinforced for repeated impact.
- **Skin:** Therian skin is rarely “bare” across the full body. Most lineages have fur coverage of some kind, with exposed skin concentrated at the face, hands, elbows, knees, and feet. Exposed skin tends to be thicker than human skin, with fast callus formation and a higher tolerance for abrasion. Pigmentation ranges as broadly as humanoids, but is often visually interrupted by natural coat patterns, countershading, and muzzle/eye markings that read strongly even at a distance.
- **Hair:** Hair presents as fur across most of the body and thicker crest-growth along the scalp, neck, shoulders, and spine. Many lineages have a distinct “mane line” or ridge pattern that changes with mood—lying flat when calm and lifting slightly with agitation, exertion, or adrenaline. Texture ranges from fine and dense to coarse and bristled depending on ancestry.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes are standard, but eyelids tend to be heavier and the brow structure more pronounced, giving Therian faces a strong “set” even when relaxed. Iris colors vary by lineage, but reflective shine in low light is common, and pupils often narrow or widen more aggressively with changing light.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth and jaw strength are a defining baseline: even lineages without obvious fangs usually have thicker molars and tougher enamel. Scent and hearing are generally sharper than humanoid norms, with many Therians unconsciously tracking people by smell and micro-sound without realizing they are doing it. Most cannot fly and must surface to breathe when swimming, though many are strong swimmers in short bursts.
#### Felar
- **Body Structure:** A bipedal, digitigrade frame built around silent control and sudden acceleration. The spine is unusually flexible, allowing the torso to “pour” into crouches, twists, and springing turns without visible strain. Shoulders sit slightly forward and remain highly mobile, with scapulae that slide under the back like plates, letting the arms extend and retract with predatory smoothness. The skull is distinctly feline: shortened muzzle relative to canids, wide cheek structure, and a jaw hinge that supports fast snapping bites. A long tail is common and functions as a living counterweight—its motion is subtle, constantly correcting balance during jumps, climbs, and rapid stops. Hands remain five-digit and tool-capable, but the fingertips are padded and the nails are thicker; many lineages have claw-sheaths that can extend for grip and tearing without remaining constantly exposed.
- **Skin:** Fur coverage is dense and directionally layered, shortest at the face and hands and thickest along the back, shoulders, and tail. Coat coloration ranges from tawny and sand-gold through charcoal and deep black, with frequent patterning: rosettes, spots, faint striping, and high-contrast facial markings around the eyes and muzzle. Paw pads (hands and feet) are thick, slightly textured, and naturally sound-dampening, which is why their footsteps can vanish on stone if they choose.
- **Hair:** “Hair” expresses as longer ruff-fur along the neck, cheeks, and upper spine, sometimes forming a mane-like line in lion-leaning lineages. Ear tufts and cheek whisker-pads are common; whiskers tend to be thick and sensitive, reacting to air movement and tight spaces.
- **Eyes:** Large forward-facing eyes with slit pupils that tighten sharply in bright light and open wide in darkness. Iris colors commonly include gold, amber, green, pale gray, and occasionally near-copper tones. Eye contact can feel intense because the gaze stays steady even when the head moves.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth include pronounced canines and sharp carnassial edges toward the back of the jaw, optimized for tearing rather than grinding. Hearing is extremely directional; the ears pivot independently, often tracking multiple sounds at once. Felar are capable swimmers only in necessity—many can cross water, but their bodies prefer dry movement and they tire quickly if forced into long swims.
#### Wolfkin
- **Body Structure:** A bipedal, pursuit-built digitigrade frame with long legs, dense calves, and a forward-leaning posture that makes them look ready to move even when still. The chest is deep and the shoulders are strong but not bulky; muscle is layered for endurance rather than show. The skull is canid: longer muzzle than Felar, broader nasal cavity, and a jaw built for sustained clamp pressure. The neck is thick with reinforced tendons that stabilize the head during running and sudden directional cuts. A tail is common and tends to be thicker at the base, used less like a balance whip and more like a stabilizing rudder during sprinting and turning.
- **Skin:** Fur is medium-length and weather-resistant, commonly thicker around the neck and shoulders where it forms a natural “collar.” Coat colors range through grays, browns, reds, black, and white, with common markings: facial masks, saddle patterns, leg striping, and lighter bellies. Exposed skin on the hands, muzzle, and feet tends to be tougher and slightly rough, with thicker pads and frequent callusing from travel.
- **Hair:** Scalp and neck fur can form ridges or crests that lift under stress. Some lineages develop heavy cheek ruffs that frame the jaw and throat, giving the face a broader silhouette. Texture trends coarse and resilient rather than silky.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with heavier lids and a strong brow ridge. Iris colors include amber, pale gold, brown, icy gray, and occasionally a muted green-hazel. Pupils are rounder than feline pupils and tend to “lock” visually on targets, giving a tracking stare.
- **Additional Traits:** Scent tracking is the signature trait: the nasal structure supports deep breath sampling, and many Wolfkin can identify individuals, direction of travel, and recent presence from faint traces. Teeth include pronounced canines, and the bite is built for holding and dragging rather than quick tearing. Wolfkin generally swim well, especially in steady strokes, though cold water can sap them fast if their coat is not adapted.
#### Centaur
- **Body Structure:** A merged organism: a humanoid torso and arms rising from the front of a full equine body with four hoofed legs. The equine body is deep-chested and built for load-bearing and speed; the humanoid torso is broad-shouldered with strong back muscles because it must stabilize against the running motion underneath. The transition at the waist is not a simple seam—bone and muscle knit into a rigid core that distributes impact from hooves through the spine without tearing soft tissue. The head is typically equine with a long muzzle, upright ears, and large jaw structure; some lineages retain more humanoid facial planes, but the neck and muzzle remain strongly horse-derived. Movement is defined by shock absorption: hooves strike, the barrel flexes, and the upper torso stays eerily steady, allowing tool use even at a trot.
- **Skin:** The equine body is covered in coat hair with typical horse patterns—solid colors, socks, blazes, dappling, roan mixes, and speckling. The humanoid torso may have short fur, fine hair, or partially bare skin depending on lineage, but it usually shows thicker skin at the shoulders and lower ribs where motion stress is highest. Hooves are hard keratin structures that show wear lines and can chip if not maintained.
- **Hair:** A mane runs along the neck and often continues into thicker hair down the upper back. Tail hair is long and heavy, used for swatting insects and signaling mood. Mane texture ranges from straight to coarse wave and is often kept braided or bound for travel.
- **Eyes:** Large eyes with a wide field of view; even when forward-focused, the gaze has a broader awareness than most humanoids. Iris colors most commonly include dark brown, hazel, amber, and pale gray.
- **Additional Traits:** Centaurs have exceptional sprint capacity and carrying strength. Hooves can strike with brutal impact and can also stabilize on loose terrain. They can ford water and survive short swims, but deep-water swimming is inefficient due to mass distribution and the difficulty of coordinating a four-leg body in strong current.
#### Tuskwroth
- **Body Structure:** A heavy, braced biped with a low center of mass and a posture that reads like it is always “set” against impact. The torso is wide and thick through the ribs and shoulders; forearms are dense and built for shoving, grappling, and carrying. The head is boar-like with a strong neck column and thick jaw musculature. Legs are powerful and slightly bowed, built for short, explosive charges and sudden stops rather than long-distance sprinting. Hands are five-digit but thick-fingered, with stiffened knuckles and reinforced wrists that tolerate blunt impact.
- **Skin:** Hide is thick and rough under a layer of coarse bristle-fur. Bristles are longest along the spine and shoulders and can form a visible ridge that lifts when threatened. Skin coloration tends toward dark browns, grays, black, and muddied reds, often with lighter muzzles and undersides. The snout and nose surface is leathery and slightly wet, built to withstand rooting and abrasion.
- **Hair:** Bristles define the “hair” presentation: a ridge along the head and back, thicker tufts at the shoulders, and rough cheek bristles. Some lineages develop longer shoulder bristles that hang like a crude mantle.
- **Eyes:** Smaller, deep-set eyes protected by heavy brow and cheek structure. Iris colors include dark brown, amber, and muddy green. Their gaze can look narrowed even when calm due to the surrounding facial structure.
- **Additional Traits:** Tusks protrude upward or outward depending on lineage and can be large enough to be visible even with the mouth closed. The bite is crushing and torque-heavy rather than sharp tearing. Smell is strong and ground-oriented; they often “read” an area by scent the way others read a map. Swimming is possible, but most prefer wading, mud, and shallow water where their mass helps rather than hinders.
#### Minotaur
- **Body Structure:** A towering, broad-chested biped with thick legs and a powerful neck designed to carry and drive horn mass. The torso is deep and heavy through the ribs, with shoulders that sit wide and high. The head is bovine with a long face, large jaw hinge, and heavy cheek structure; horns grow from the skull and vary widely—forward-curving, wide-sweeping, upward spirals, or thick blunt arcs. Hands are large with thick fingers, built for crushing grips, lifting, and pinning rather than fine delicate manipulation (though they remain fully tool-capable).
- **Skin:** A mix of thick hide and short fur. The hide is densest at the shoulders, neck, and forearms, where it resists abrasion and forms callus rapidly. Coat coloration includes black, brown, red-brown, cream, and patterned mixes (piebald patches are common). The nose and muzzle are leathery, and the ears are broad, often twitching subtly with sound and mood.
- **Hair:** Coarse hair is common around the crown, nape, and forearms; many develop a thicker neck-ruff that visually bridges the head and torso. Some lineages grow longer, rough hair down the spine that reads like a heavy ridge rather than a mane.
- **Eyes:** Large eyes with heavy lids that can make the resting face look calm or stern. Iris colors are commonly dark brown, amber, and hazel.
- **Additional Traits:** Horns are functional weapons for goring, lifting, and shoving; neck strength supports sustained pushes and brutal head-driven impacts. Teeth are thick and flat for grinding, but the jaw is strong enough to crack hard food. Minotaurs can handle shallow water and slow rivers, but deep-water swimming is inefficient due to mass; they tend to wade and push through rather than float.
#### Murren
- **Body Structure:** A small bipedal frame built for speed and precision movement in tight spaces. The spine is flexible and the shoulders are narrow, allowing quick slips through gaps. The skull is murine with a narrow muzzle, prominent incisors, and large rounded ears that sit high and forward. Legs are lightly digitigrade or “half-digitigrade,” with long feet that absorb impact and allow sudden direction changes. Hands are five-digit and highly dexterous, with thin fingers capable of fine manipulation and quick repetitive work.
- **Skin:** Fine, dense fur covers most of the body, shortest on the face and hands. Coat colors range from gray and brown to black and white, often with countershading—lighter bellies and darker backs. Whisker pads are pronounced, and whiskers themselves are thick and sensitive, often visible even at a distance.
- **Hair:** Fur on the head can form soft “crown fluff” or slight crests, but true long hair is uncommon. Many lineages have thicker cheek fur that frames the muzzle and enhances facial expression.
- **Eyes:** Large eyes that appear bright in low light due to reflective shine. Iris colors include black-brown, amber, pale gray, and occasionally a reddish-brown tone. The gaze tends to flick rapidly, scanning edges and openings.
- **Additional Traits:** Incisors grow continuously and are kept worn down by gnawing; they can bite through surprisingly tough materials given time. Murren balance is exceptional, and they can land quietly from falls that would stagger larger bodies. They can swim short distances, often better than expected, but fatigue quickly if forced into long open water.
#### Probassan
- **Body Structure:** A massive, pillar-built biped with a deep ribcage and heavy shoulders. The skull is elephantine, dominated by a muscular trunk that functions as a third manipulator—capable of gripping, lifting, and delicate handling with surprising finesse. Tusks may be present and vary from short forward prongs to long sweeping arcs. Legs are thick and load-bearing with broad feet designed for stability; the gait is quiet for the size because the body distributes weight across a wide stance. Arms are strong and dense, and the hands are large, built for both grip and blunt force.
- **Skin:** Thick, wrinkled hide with sparse bristle hair. Skin colors range gray through slate and dusty brown, often with lighter creases at joints. The hide forms deep fold lines along the trunk, elbows, knees, and neck, and it resists cutting but can abrade and scar as pale seam-lines if damaged.
- **Hair:** Hair is minimal—coarse bristles at the crown, shoulders, and forearms. Some develop slightly thicker bristle patches along the spine.
- **Eyes:** Smaller, deep-set eyes under heavy brow structure. Iris colors typically include dark brown, hazel, and dull amber. Their expression can appear calm even when they are focused due to the slow blink and heavy lid structure.
- **Additional Traits:** The trunk enables scent tracking and breath control; they can draw and hold air differently than most, making them capable of controlled exertion bursts without immediate panting. Tusks and head mass allow brutal shoves and lifts. They swim well, and many can keep the trunk above the surface like a snorkel while still needing air.
#### Drowseer
- **Body Structure:** A bipedal frame built around hanging strength and endurance rather than speed. Arms are long relative to the torso, with shoulder joints reinforced for sustained load. Hands are five-digit but end in heavy curved claws that are thick, blunt at the base, and sharply hooked at the tip, built for gripping bark, ledges, and beams. The skull is sloth-like: rounded face, shorter muzzle, and a jaw built more for steady chewing than snapping. Posture often looks relaxed or “drooped,” but the body is heavily muscled around the shoulders and back.
- **Skin:** Dense shag fur that tends to layer and mat, especially along the back, shoulders, and forearms. Colors range pale tan through brown and gray, often with natural streaking that reads like mossy staining. Exposed skin at the nose and around the eyes is thicker and slightly leathery.
- **Hair:** Fur is the hair—longer at the arms and shoulders, sometimes forming a draped mantle when the arms hang. Many individuals have a heavier fur “cap” around the crown and nape.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with a soft reflective sheen and slow blink behavior. Iris colors include brown, green-brown, and pale gray.
- **Additional Traits:** Grip endurance is extreme; they can hang or hold weight for long periods without visible trembling. Movement is slow and deliberate, but close-range strength can spike suddenly when they commit. Swimming is poor; the fur and limb layout are not built for efficient strokes, and they avoid deep water when possible.
#### Spinekin
- **Body Structure:** A compact-to-average biped with reinforced shoulders and back where spines anchor beneath the skin. The torso tends to be slightly hunched by default, a protective posture that shields the belly and keeps the spine-field angled toward threats. The skull is small-muzzled with a strong jaw and dense cheek structure; the neck is short and thick. Hands are five-digit and tool-capable, but fingers are thicker and the wrists are braced, favoring pushing and gripping over delicate flourish.
- **Skin:** Short fur overlays most of the body, but a field of hardened spines/quills dominates the back, shoulders, and outer arms. Spine density and length vary: some are brush-like and tight, others long and needle-stiff, flaring outward when raised. Colors range dark brown and black to pale cream and mottled mixes; spine tips often lighten with age or wear. Exposed skin at the hands and face is thick and tends to develop callus quickly.
- **Hair:** Fur remains short and practical, but spine-fields create the primary silhouette—many have a distinct ridge-line from crown to mid-back that can lift in a wave when threatened.
- **Eyes:** Alert, forward-facing eyes that scan quickly. Iris colors include amber, brown, and pale gray, often with a sharp reflective glint in low light.
- **Additional Traits:** Spines can raise under stress, making grapples and back-holds dangerous for opponents. Even when relaxed, the spine-field is abrasive, and clothing often shows wear at shoulder contact points. Swimming is possible but inefficient; spines create drag and can trap debris, so they prefer shallow water crossings.
#### Ursworn
- **Body Structure:** A broad, heavy biped with thick neck, deep chest, and powerful shoulders built for grappling and impact. Legs are often plantigrade, giving a grounded stance and stable weight transfer; their walk reads steady and weighty rather than quick. The skull is bear-like with a strong muzzle, heavy jaw muscles, and thick brow ridges. Hands are large with thick non-retractable claws—capable of tearing and climbing, but also used as blunt hooks in combat and labor.
- **Skin:** Dense fur with a heavy undercoat, thickest at the shoulders, upper back, and neck. Colors include black, brown, cinnamon, pale cream, and gray, often with lighter muzzle and chest patches. Exposed skin at the nose and paw pads is thick and durable, with pads that resist cracking and wear.
- **Hair:** Fur forms natural ruffs and “shoulder capes,” especially in cold-adapted lineages. Some develop longer fur along the forearms that drapes slightly when arms hang.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with a steady gaze and heavy lids. Iris colors commonly include dark brown, amber, and hazel.
- **Additional Traits:** Exceptional resilience and close-range dominance—grip strength, shoulder drive, and raw durability define them. Claws are functional tools as much as weapons, leaving deep scoring marks in wood and stone. They swim well and tolerate cold water longer than most mammal-folk due to mass and coat insulation.
#### Gnoll
- **Body Structure:** A lean, long-limbed biped with digitigrade legs built for sprinting, lateral cuts, and sudden stops. The torso is narrow but muscular, with shoulders that slope forward into a predatory silhouette. The skull is hyena-like: powerful jaw hinge, heavy cheek structure, and a muzzle built for crushing bite leverage rather than narrow snapping bites. Arms are strong and wiry; hands remain five-digit and tool-capable, with thickened nails that can form short claws.
- **Skin:** Short coarse fur with frequent mottling and spotted patterns, especially across the back and shoulders. Colors include tan, dirty gold, gray, brown, and darker patching; many have a darker ridge-line along the spine that can lift when excited or threatened. Exposed skin around the nose and mouth tends to be darker and tougher.
- **Hair:** Fur ridge is the dominant “hair” feature, often running from crown down the neck and shoulders. Some lineages develop thicker jawline fur that frames the muzzle and exaggerates expression.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with intense focus; iris colors include amber, brown, pale gold, and muddy green. The gaze can feel unnervingly fixed because the head stays steady while the body shifts.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are thick and heavy, built for crushing; bite pressure is their defining physical threat. Vocal range is broad and expressive—laugh-like cackles, whoops, and low growls are common and often used as social signaling rather than purely intimidation. Swimming is poor; their build favors land pursuit and dry endurance, and they avoid deep water when possible.

#### Nerid (Mermaid)
- **Body Structure:** Humanoid torso fused at the hips into a powerful fish tail; tail fin width varies by lineage. Arms are fully humanoid and tool-capable.
- **Skin:** Upper body has humanoid skin; lower body is scaled with a slick sheen and visible lateral patterning.
- **Hair:** Thick scalp hair; often coarse or wave-set from constant salt exposure.
- **Eyes:** Bright irises with a glassy reflective quality underwater.
- **Additional Traits:** Fast swimmers; poor long-distance movement on land; must surface to breathe.
#### Mermaid (Reverse Mermaid)
- **Body Structure:** Fish-bodied core with a rigid spine-line and a broad ribcage; two humanoid legs emerge from the rear half, built for wading and short bursts on land.
- **Skin:** Mostly scaled, with tougher skin on the legs and feet; scale coloration often banded or mottled.
- **Hair:** Minimal scalp hair; many have fin-crest “hairlines” instead.
- **Eyes:** Wider-set eyes; strong low-light adaptation.
- **Additional Traits:** Strong in shallow water and surf; awkward in deep water compared to Nerids; must surface to breathe.
#### Cephalid (Octopus-bodied: tentacles replace arms and legs)
- **Body Structure:** Humanoid head and upper torso; below the ribs the body transitions into multiple thick tentacles used for locomotion and manipulation.
- **Skin:** Smooth, damp skin with fine chromatophore-like mottling that shifts subtly with mood and environment.
- **Hair:** None; scalp is smooth or lightly ridged.
- **Eyes:** Large, high-contrast eyes; pupils tend to be narrow slits or rounded ovals depending on lineage.
- **Additional Traits:** Exceptional climbing and gripping; can compress through tight gaps; must surface to breathe.
#### Deepmantle (Human torso/arms; “tentacle skirt” instead of legs)
- **Body Structure:** Fully humanoid torso and arms; lower body is a dense mantle of shorter, stronger tentacles optimized for stability and powerful bursts rather than reach.
- **Skin:** Upper body skin varies; lower body is uniformly slick and heavier-textured than Cephalids.
- **Hair:** Normal scalp hair, often kept tied to avoid catching.
- **Eyes:** Humanlike placement; often has a noticeable reflective “ring” in dim light.
- **Additional Traits:** Stable on uneven rock and ship decks; slower than Cephalids in vertical climbing; must surface to breathe.
#### Backtendril (Mostly human; tentacles from the back)
- **Body Structure:** Humanoid body with normal arms/legs; 4–8 muscular tendrils emerge from the upper back/shoulder blades and act as secondary manipulators.
- **Skin:** Humanoid skin with tougher tendon-anchored scar plates around the tendril roots.
- **Hair:** Normal hair; many lineages grow thicker hair at the nape where tendrils anchor.
- **Eyes:** Humanlike; often intense gaze because the back-tendrils “track” independently.
- **Additional Traits:** High tool capability (hands + tendrils); can grapple while keeping hands free; must surface to breathe.
#### Chelonar (Turtlefolk)
- **Body Structure:** Bipedal reptilian frame with a full shell (carapace) integrated into ribs and spine; thick forearms and broad hands.
- **Skin:** Leathery skin with plate-like scaling on limbs; shell shows growth rings, chips, and polish wear.
- **Hair:** None; some grow keratin ridges or horn-plates instead.
- **Eyes:** Dark, steady eyes with slow blink behavior.    
- **Additional Traits:** Extremely durable; slow acceleration but strong endurance; competent swimmers; must surface to breathe.

#### Demon
- (true demon lineage)
- **Body Structure:** A humanoid form twisted subtly or overtly by infernal origin. Proportions vary widely: elongated limbs, reversed joints, extra vertebrae, or asymmetrical builds are common. Horns, tails, and spine ridges appear naturally.
- **Skin:** Colors range from ash-gray and pitch-black to bruised purple, blood-red, and sickly yellow. Texture varies from smooth to cracked, scaled, or leathery.
- **Hair:** Thick, coarse hair in black, white, or dark red. Often grows unevenly or in unnatural patterns.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with no visible whites. Iris colors glow faintly—red, orange, violet, or sickly green.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth include pronounced fangs. Tails are common. Presence often causes unease. Physical strength varies, but their bodies feel dense and resistant.
#### Ferramite
- **Body Structure:** A tall, broad-shouldered humanoid frame with unmistakably draconic proportions. The torso is deep and armored, the neck thick and reinforced to support a heavy dragon-shaped skull. Arms and legs are powerful, ending in clawed hands and digitigrade, taloned feet. Their posture is upright but predatory, with a forward weight that suggests coiled strength even at rest.
- **Skin:** Fully covered in interlocking metallic scales that replace skin entirely. Scale colors include iron-gray, brass-gold, deep copper, burnished silver, and rare alloy-like hues that shift subtly under light. The surface is cold, hard, and scarred by dents rather than cuts.
- **Hair:** No true hair. Some individuals grow metallic crests, ridges, or swept-back horn-fronds along the skull or neck that resemble forged ornamentation.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing draconic eyes with slit pupils and no visible whites. Iris colors glow faintly—amber, molten gold, white-hot silver, or dull red.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are heavy fangs designed for crushing and tearing. Claws are fully functional. Cannot fly. Breath carries a faint metallic heat or chill depending on lineage.
#### Pyrrakin
- (formerly Salamandarian)
- **Body Structure:** A lean, fast humanoid with long limbs and a low center of gravity. The legs are digitigrade with backward-bent knees and elongated calves built for sprinting. The torso is narrow and flexible, and the head is fully reptilian with a blunt snout and wide jaw hinge.
- **Skin:** Covered in small, overlapping lizard scales that range in vivid colors—emerald, cobalt, crimson, sun-yellow, and patterned mixes. The scales are warm to the touch and slightly rubberized rather than rigid.
- **Hair:** None. The head may show small frills, ridges, or scale-fans along the crown or jawline.
- **Eyes:** Large, forward-facing eyes with round pupils and bright coloration—gold, green, orange, or turquoise.
- **Additional Traits:** Long, muscular tongue capable of rapid extension. No wings. Exceptional runners and climbers. Teeth are sharp but not venomous.
#### Drakari
- (formerly Dragonborn)
- **Body Structure:** A powerful humanoid-dragon hybrid with a broad chest, thick limbs, and a heavy draconic head. Less bulky than Ferramites but still imposing. The spine supports a slight forward hunch, giving them a looming silhouette.
- **Skin:** Fully scaled, but organic rather than metallic. Scale colors include deep reds, blacks, greens, blues, and bone-white. The surface is tough and matte, often scar-lined.
- **Hair:** No hair. Horns, spines, or swept-back cranial ridges are common and vary wildly by lineage.
- **Eyes:** Slitted pupils with intense coloration—amber, red, green, or pale gold.
- **Additional Traits:** Strong claws and fangs. Cannot fly. Breath and presence often carry heat, smoke, or pressure even without active exertion.
#### Cinderkin
- (formerly Tiefling)
- **Body Structure:** Mostly humanoid in proportion, with subtle infernal traits layered on top. Horns curl or sweep from the skull, tails are slender, and the spine often shows faint ridging beneath the skin.
- **Skin:** Smooth skin in warm infernal tones—red, orange, yellow, ember-pink, or ash-gold. Skin radiates mild warmth.
- **Hair:** Normal hair growth, often thick and dark. White, silver, or flame-red hair is common.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites. Iris colors include gold, amber, red-brown, and black.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are slightly sharper than human. No wings. Often mistaken for fully humanoid at a distance.
#### Sylphamare
- **Body Structure:** Large, hunched demonoid bodies with long torsos, sloping shoulders, and heavy arms that hang low even when standing upright. Limbs are thick and powerful, but movement is slow and deliberate. Faces are long and drooping, with oversized ears that hang down past the jaw.
- **Skin:** Thick, dull gray skin with a stone-like texture. The surface looks perpetually tired, with deep creases and sagging folds.
- **Hair:** Long, shaggy black hair that hangs over the face, shoulders, and back in uneven curtains. Rarely groomed.
- **Eyes:** Large, heavy-lidded eyes with deep-set sockets. Iris colors are muted—dull blue, gray, or faded violet.
- **Additional Traits:** No visible claws or fangs. Their bodies feel immensely heavy, as if anchored. Movements are slow, but magical exertion is overwhelming and precise.
#### Varkuun
- (infernal transitional lineage)
- **Body Structure:** A muscular humanoid frame with stronger demonic influence than Cinderkin but less distortion than Maleborn. Horns are larger, tails thicker, and posture more predatory.
- **Skin:** Dark reds, scorched browns, bruised purples, and blackened tones. Skin often shows faint heat cracks or glowing vein-lines under strain.
- **Hair:** Thick, coarse hair in black, dark red, or ashen white.
- **Eyes:** Slitted or near-slitted pupils. Iris colors glow faintly—amber, blood-red, or molten orange.
- **Additional Traits:** Fangs are prominent. Claws may be present. Presence feels oppressive and heated, like standing too close to a forge.

#### Revenant
_(formerly Zombie)_
- **Body Structure:** A humanoid corpse reanimated through persistent animating force rather than living muscle control. Posture is rigid and slightly off-balance, with joints that move too far or not far enough. Limbs are intact but stiff, and motion is slow, deliberate, and heavy, as if every step must overcome resistance.
- **Skin:** Necrotic flesh in varying states of decay. Skin may be gray, green, blackened, or mottled, often split or sagging where muscle has softened. Damage does not heal; instead, it accumulates as tears, exposed bone, and missing tissue.
- **Hair:** Sparse or falling out entirely. Remaining hair is brittle, dry, or clumped with rot.
- **Eyes:** Clouded, sunken, or partially collapsed. Some retain dim points of light deep in the sockets, but expression is minimal.
- **Additional Traits:** Pain response is absent or extremely delayed. Movement is slow but relentless. Physical destruction is required to stop function; loss of limbs reduces capability but does not necessarily end animation.
#### Ossid
_(formerly Skeleton)_
- **Body Structure:** A fully articulated humanoid skeleton animated without flesh. Bones are held together by invisible force rather than ligaments, allowing motion even where joints should not function. The silhouette is unmistakably skeletal, with hollow ribcage and exposed spine.
- **Surface:** Bare bone ranging from clean ivory to stained brown, gray, or black. Cracks, chips, and fractures remain visible and do not regenerate.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Empty sockets, sometimes containing faint pinpoints of light or shadow.
- **Additional Traits:** Extremely lightweight and quiet in motion. Can function without organs, blood, or muscle. Structural damage directly reduces mobility or integrity.
#### Veilbound
_(formerly Ghost)_
- **Body Structure:** A translucent humanoid outline composed of condensed ethereal substance. The body lacks fixed mass and subtly blurs at the edges, especially during movement. Feet rarely make full contact with the ground.
- **Surface:** Semi-transparent, mistlike, or glassy. Details such as clothing, wounds, or facial features appear frozen from the moment of death.
- **Hair:** Appears as a drifting or weightless version of former hair, moving as if underwater.
- **Eyes:** Hollow or softly glowing. Pupils may be absent or indistinct.
- **Additional Traits:** Can pass through solid matter and hover freely. Interaction with the physical world is limited and often indirect. Form destabilizes under intense disruption.
#### Umbrial
_(formerly Shadow)_
- **Body Structure:** A humanoid silhouette formed from dense darkness rather than flesh or bone. Edges blur into surrounding shadow, and the body appears flatter than it should, like depth is missing.
- **Surface:** Pure black with no visible texture. Light bends unnaturally around the form rather than reflecting off it.
- **Hair:** None, though the head shape may imply it.
- **Eyes:** The only visible features. Iris color shifts with emotional state, glowing faintly against the dark body.
- **Additional Traits:** Can merge with shadows, flattening or dispersing into darkness to avoid detection. Physical presence feels cold and empty, as if something is missing from the space it occupies.
#### Sanguivar
_(formerly Vampire)_
- **Body Structure:** A humanoid body preserved at peak physical condition, neither decaying nor aging normally. Musculature is firm, posture precise, and movement unnaturally controlled. The body feels dense and tense even at rest.
- **Skin:** Pale to deathly pale, often cool to the touch. Skin does not scar normally and appears unnaturally smooth or flawless.
- **Hair:** Normal hair growth, often thick and well-maintained. Coloration varies by lineage.    
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites. Iris colors intensify under stress, hunger, or emotion.
- **Additional Traits:** Prominent fangs. No natural decay, but biological functions are sustained through unnatural means. Presence often feels predatory or oppressive even when calm.
#### Relicwraith
- **Body Structure:** A disembodied undead consciousness bound to a specific physical object. When manifesting, the object moves as if animated by unseen limbs, often clumsily or with unnatural precision.
- **Surface:** Defined entirely by the bound object—armor, weapon, tool, furniture, or relic—often accompanied by faint spectral distortion around it.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Sometimes manifests glowing eye-shapes or sigils on the object’s surface.
- **Additional Traits:** Can possess and manipulate only the bound object. Destruction of the object results in immediate termination. Movement style depends entirely on the object’s shape and mass.
#### Lycanthrex
_(formerly Werewolf)_
- **Body Structure:** A humanoid body capable of shifting into a hybrid or fully bestial form. In hybrid state, posture becomes hunched, limbs lengthen, and muscle mass increases dramatically. Joints remain humanoid but reinforced.
- **Skin/Fur:** Dense fur covers most of the body during transformation. Coloration varies widely but often follows natural mammalian patterns.
- **Hair:** Thickens and spreads during transformation, merging into fur.
- **Eyes:** Bright, predatory eyes with strong reflective shine in low light.
- **Additional Traits:** Fangs and claws are present in transformed states. Strength and aggression increase sharply. Retains humanoid intelligence but with heightened instinctual drives.
#### Mycelari
_(fungal undead)_
- **Body Structure:** A humanoid frame grown from dense fungal matter rather than bone and flesh. Limbs are slightly asymmetrical, and joints flex with a soft, fibrous resistance rather than hard articulation.
- **Surface:** Textured layers of mushroom caps, fibrous stalks, and spore-veined tissue. Colors include pale white, tan, gray, brown, and muted bioluminescent hues in some lineages.
- **Hair:** None. Cap structures or frilled growths often replace hair.
- **Eyes:** Dark pits, pale nodes, or glowing fungal clusters depending on strain.
- **Additional Traits:** Body slowly decomposes and regrows through fungal cycles. Flesh does not bleed but releases spores or pulp when damaged. Classified as undead due to decay-based persistence rather than true life processes.

The Tull race and its subraces are unique in that they undergo physical evolution over time. All Tull characters must begin as either a Tull or a Grivlit, and as they grow and gain levels, they may evolve into progressively higher forms along the Tull lineage, up to Brogath. Upon reaching this stage, further evolution requires a final specialization, at which point the character must permanently choose one of three apex forms: Troll, Cyclopse, or Naram-Sin.
#### Tull
_(baseline goblin form)_
- **Body Structure:** A short, compact humanoid frame with a forward-leaning posture and long arms relative to height. The torso is narrow, shoulders slope forward, and the neck is thick for the head size. Limbs are wiry rather than bulky, built for scrambling, climbing, and sudden directional changes rather than sustained force.
- **Skin:** Smooth to faintly textured skin in gray, blue-gray, green-gray, or dull olive tones. The surface is tougher than human skin and calluses quickly at hands, feet, elbows, and knees.
- **Hair:** None. The scalp is bare and slightly ridged, often showing faint vein lines under strain.
- **Eyes:** Large forward-facing eyes with visible whites. Iris colors include yellow, amber, pale green, and muddy brown.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are small but sharp-edged. Ears are long and pointed, angling outward. Cannot fly and swims poorly but can float briefly.
#### Grivlit
_(lesser goblin)_
- **Body Structure:** A smaller, lighter version of the Tull frame with thinner limbs and a more delicate ribcage. Movements are fast and jittery, with a tendency toward crouched or hunched posture even when standing.
- **Skin:** Pale gray, washed green, or bluish tones. Skin bruises easily but heals quickly without thick scarring.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Very large eyes relative to skull size, giving a wide, alert expression. Iris colors skew brighter—yellow-green, pale gold, or light amber.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are narrow and sharp but fragile. Physical strength is low, but balance and agility are exceptional. Often mistaken for juveniles of other Tull lineages.
#### Grull
_(greater goblin)_
- **Body Structure:** A thicker, broader Tull variant with reinforced shoulders, deeper chest, and heavier limb musculature. The posture straightens slightly compared to baseline Tull, and the neck thickens to support a heavier jaw.
- **Skin:** Darker gray, slate, or muted green tones. Skin is tougher and scars visibly.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Smaller relative to head size than Tull, giving a more grounded expression. Iris colors include amber, brown, and dull yellow.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are thicker and more durable. Bite force is noticeably stronger. Movement trades some agility for raw force and stability.
#### Gorul
_(transitional goblin–orc form)_
- **Body Structure:** A tall, heavy-set humanoid frame bridging goblin and orc traits. The torso widens significantly, shoulders broaden, and limb proportions shift toward brute strength. Arms remain long, but muscle density increases sharply.
- **Skin:** Deep gray, green-gray, or dark olive. Skin thickens across shoulders, neck, and forearms, forming natural armor-like density.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with heavier lids. Iris colors include dark amber, brown, and red-brown.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are large and blunt-edged with pronounced canines. Physical presence is imposing, and posture reads aggressive even at rest.
#### Boaf
_(orc baseline)_
- **Body Structure:** A tall, powerfully built humanoid with massive shoulders, thick arms, and a deep ribcage. The neck is short and reinforced, and the head sits forward on the shoulders, creating a charging silhouette.
- **Skin:** Green, dark olive, gray-green, or brown-green. Skin is thick, leathery, and highly resistant to abrasion.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Smaller, deep-set eyes with heavy brow ridges. Iris colors include red-brown, amber, and dark green.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth include large tusk-like canines that may protrude slightly. Bite strength is high. Swimming is possible but inefficient due to density.
#### Brogath
_(greater orc)_
- **Body Structure:** An enormous orc variant with extreme muscle mass, thickened bones, and oversized joints. The chest is barrel-shaped, and the arms hang low even when standing upright.
- **Skin:** Dark green, near-black olive, or ash-brown. Skin shows heavy scarring and thickened plates over time.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Small, intense eyes with heavy lids. Iris colors include dark amber, black-brown, and dull red.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are massive and durable. Strength and endurance vastly exceed baseline Boaf. Movements are slower but crushingly powerful.
#### Troll
_(regenerative giant orc)_
- **Body Structure:** A towering, hunched giant with elongated arms, massive hands, and disproportionate limb length. The spine curves forward, and the neck juts out from the shoulders.
- **Skin:** Thick, rubbery skin in gray, green, or mottled earth tones. The surface knits and re-seals visibly after damage.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** Small, deep-set eyes with dull irises—brown, yellow, or gray.
- **Additional Traits:** Tissue regenerates rapidly, closing wounds and reattaching damaged muscle over time. Teeth are blunt and massive. Movements are heavy but relentless.
#### Cyclopse
_(one-eyed giant orc)_
- **Body Structure:** A giant orc form similar in mass to Trolls but more upright and structurally balanced. The skull is dominated by a single massive central eye socket.
- **Skin:** Thick gray-green or stone-toned skin, often rough-textured and scar-lined.
- **Hair:** None.
- **Eyes:** A single forward-facing eye with a large iris. Iris colors include amber, pale gold, gray, and blood-red.
- **Additional Traits:** Depth perception is compensated by extreme spatial awareness and strength. Bite and grip force are exceptional. Regeneration is slower or absent compared to Trolls.
#### Naram-Sin
_(apex orc lineage)_
- **Body Structure:** A tall but proportionally balanced orc frame, less bulky than Brogath but denser and more refined. Shoulders are broad, posture is upright, and movements are controlled and deliberate.
- **Skin:** Dark olive, ash-brown, or muted green. Skin is tough but smoother and more uniform than other orc lineages.
- **Hair:** Thick scalp hair grows normally, often black, dark brown, or iron-gray. Facial hair is common.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites. Iris colors include amber, brown, dark green, and steel-gray.
- **Additional Traits:** Teeth are large but well-aligned. Physical ability is high, but coordination, perception, and tactical movement far exceed other orc types.

#### Shapeshifter
_(general classification)_
- **Body Structure:** A mutable humanoid organism whose baseline form is vaguely humanlike but lacks fixed anatomical anchors. Skeletal rigidity is reduced compared to most humanoids, allowing bones, joints, and proportions to soften, compress, or elongate during transformation. Internal organs are present but reposition dynamically to match current structure rather than remaining locked in place.
- **Skin:** Smooth, elastic skin capable of extreme stretching and compression without tearing. Pigmentation varies widely and can be altered temporarily during transformation, though abrupt color changes often appear slightly muted or “off” compared to true biological coloration.
- **Hair:** Hair is not fixed; filament structures can be grown, retracted, or reshaped, but fine detail is difficult to maintain for long periods without concentration.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites in baseline forms. During shifts, eye placement can change, but pupils and iris behavior often lag behind the new shape, betraying the transformation on close inspection.
- **Additional Traits:** True mass cannot be created or destroyed—only redistributed. Shapeshifters must conserve total body volume, causing extreme forms to trade thickness for length or vice versa. Cannot fly unless assuming a biologically plausible flying form, and must still obey breathing and movement limits of the assumed structure.
#### Mireglopian
_(amorphous slime-shifter)_
- **Body Structure:** A semi-cohesive organism composed of dense, viscous protoplasm rather than fixed flesh and bone. The body has no permanent skeletal structure; instead, internal density gradients create temporary rigidity where needed. Forms are sculpted rather than grown, flowing smoothly from one shape to another without visible seams.
- **Surface:** Gelatinous, translucent to opaque depending on internal density. Coloration ranges from clear and milky to deep greens, blues, browns, and rare iridescent hues. The surface constantly shifts subtly, even when at rest.
- **Hair:** None. Hair-like structures can be extruded but lack true filament structure and dissolve back into the body when concentration lapses.
- **Eyes:** Formed ad hoc as denser clusters within the body. Eye placement is flexible, and multiple eyes may exist temporarily, but fine ocular detail is usually crude.
- **Additional Traits:** Can pass through narrow gaps, flatten completely, or divide mass briefly before rejoining. Physical impacts disperse mass rather than tearing tissue. Must maintain cohesion; extreme heat, dehydration, or dispersion can cause permanent mass loss. Swimming is effortless; flight is impossible.
#### Mirr
_(illusory mimic)_
- **Body Structure:** A physically fixed humanoid form that remains unchanged beneath all transformations. Bones, muscles, and organs do not shift; instead, the perceived form is altered through layered illusion tightly bound to the body’s outline.
- **Skin:** Pale white to soft gray, uniformly smooth and faintly reflective when unmasked. The true surface never changes texture, even when appearing scaled, furred, or armored.
- **Hair:** Naturally sparse and fine. Illusory hair can appear thick, long, or styled, but lacks true physical interaction—wind, water, and touch may subtly betray it.
- **Eyes:** Forward-facing eyes with visible whites in the true form. Illusory eye coloration and shape can be convincing at a glance but often fail under close observation or physical interaction.
- **Additional Traits:** Cannot physically interact as the assumed form would—illusory claws do not cut, wings do not lift, and altered mass does not apply real force. Mirr must still move, breathe, and bear weight according to their true body. Illusions are visual and occasionally auditory only.
#### Skinwalker
_(true biological mimic)_
- **Body Structure:** A fully mutable organism capable of reorganizing bone, muscle, and organs to exactly match the physical structure of another creature. Transformations are precise and complete, with internal anatomy rearranging to support the assumed form’s movement, posture, and mass distribution.
- **Skin:** Texture, coloration, scars, and markings are replicated exactly, including calluses, blemishes, and asymmetries—provided the Skinwalker has observed them directly.
- **Hair:** True filament growth that matches the copied form in density, texture, and growth direction. Hair behaves naturally under environmental forces.
- **Eyes:** Perfectly replicated in shape, placement, and coloration. Eye behavior matches the mimicked species, including pupil response and reflective qualities.
- **Additional Traits:** Skinwalkers cannot invent forms. They may only assume the exact shape of real creatures or individuals they have personally seen. Forms cannot be blended, exaggerated, or modified. Size and mass must remain within the limits of the copied subject. Extended time in a form does not permanently alter identity, but repeated transformations are physically taxing.
