# The Basics
Welcome to The World of Eyum. Whether you are a veteran of other systems or have never touched a polyhedral die in your life, this guide is designed to get you playing quickly. Eyum is a Tabletop Roleplaying Game (TTRPG).  At its core, a TTRPG is collaborative storytelling. It’s a game you play with friends to create a shared narrative. If you can imagine an action, you can attempt it (although you may not succeed). The idea is simple, it's like playing imagination games with your friends but with a set of rules to follow so things can be more fair. 

To get started simply read through the handbook in order and it will talk you through everything you need to know. If you already have an idea of how to play the game simply use the section headings to jump around. 
#### The Roles at the Table
- **The Players:** Most people at the table are Players. You each control a single Player Character (PC). You decide what they say, how they feel, and what actions they take. Your character’s details are tracked on a Character Sheet.

- The Narrator / Game Master (GM): One person takes on this role. They don’t "play against" the players. Instead, they describe the world, portray the people you meet (Non-Player Characters or NPCs), and act as the referee for the rules.
#### How Play Works
The game follows a simple loop:
1. The GM describes the scene. ("You stand before the heavy iron doors of the tomb.")
2. The Players describe what they do. ("I want to check the lock for traps.")
3. The GM determines the outcome. For most actions the GM will ask you to roll dice to see if you succeed.
#### Core Terms to Know
If you’re new, these terms will appear frequently throughout this handbook:
- **Campaign:** A series of connected sessions that tell a long, evolving story.    
- **One-Shot:** A complete story played in a single sitting.
- **D20:** A 20-sided die. This is the primary tool used to determine success or failure.
- **Difficulty Class (DC):** A number set by the GM representing how hard a task is. To succeed, your roll (plus any bonuses) must meet or beat this number.
- **Session Zero:** A meeting before the game begins where everyone agrees on the tone, rules, and character connections.
- **Race:** The species of your character, things like human, elf, or dwarf. 
#### Reading Dice Notation
We use a specific shorthand to tell you which dice to roll. It follows the format: 

{Number of Dice} d {Number of Sides}

- **1d20:** Roll one 20-sided die.
- **2d6:** Roll two 6-sided dice and add them together.
- **1d8 + 3:** Roll one 8-sided die and add 3 to the result.
