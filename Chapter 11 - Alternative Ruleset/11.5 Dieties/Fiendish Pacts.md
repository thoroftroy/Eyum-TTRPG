## Black Mercy — Necrotic

**Tier 1 (1 day):** You may take Black Mercy’s pact for 1 day: once per turn when you deal **Necrotic** damage, you also deal **+2d8 Necrotic** to HP, and you gain **Resistance to Necrotic**. The sacrifice is absolute fasting from help: you may not receive healing from any source and you must spill your own blood (take 10 HP damage voluntarily) before dusk. If you fail the sacrifice within the time, you immediately gain **Necrosis** for 7 turns and your **max HP is permanently reduced by 10**. If you attempt to cheat (accept healing anyway), the pact snaps and you take **4d10 Necrotic** to HP instantly.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, Black Mercy makes you a collector: once per combat, when a creature within 30 ft would die, you may **claim it** as a reaction—leave it at 1 HP, and you gain **+2 Ap** for your next turn; if it dies later, you also heal **2d10 HP**. The sacrifice is a daily kill-quota with intent: you must personally deliver **one killing blow per day** and it must be a creature that begged, fled, or surrendered at some point (GM adjudicates). If you fail, you permanently lose **1 stat point** (your choice) and gain **Cursed** until removed. If you break the pact by refusing a valid kill opportunity, you immediately gain **Slow Death (2 stacks)**.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, you become a siphon: when you deal Necrotic damage to HP, you may also **remove one condition** from yourself (any one condition instance or one stack) and place it on the target (no save), once per turn. You also gain **Greater Resistance to Magical** damage for the pact duration. The sacrifice is identity rot: you must erase your name from one record (burn a letter, scrape a gravestone, destroy a registry) and you must refuse to answer to your name for the whole pact. If you fail, you lose **-2 to all d20 rolls** (treated as permanent Cursed) until a special cure, and you permanently lose **10 ft movement**.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, Black Mercy grants **Necrotic Immunity** and once per long rest you may declare **Mercy’s Audit** for 3 turns: all damage you deal bypasses Vitality and goes directly to HP (as if it were a condition effect). The sacrifice is a sacred violation: you must destroy a place of healing or mercy (hospital, temple wing, sanctuary shrine, charity cache—GM validates). If you fail, you are branded as debtor: you gain **Necrosis** that cannot be removed for 30 days, and each dawn your **max HP drops by 5** until the 30 days end. If you try to back out early, you immediately take **10d10 Necrotic** to HP.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you become Death’s creditor: once per combat you may mark a creature you can see with **Debt** (max 3 creatures). Marked creatures take **2d10 Necrotic** to HP at the start of their turns, and when any marked creature dies you gain **+1 Ap for 3 turns** and heal **6d10 HP** (overflow healing may be given to allies within 10 ft if you allow it). The sacrifice is three named souls: you must ensure the death of **three specific individuals** the GM recognizes as people, and you must do it without allowing them last rites or closure. If you fail, you die instantly and cannot be resurrected without a full campaign-level bargain because your soul is claimed permanently.

## Red Furnace — Magma

**Tier 1 (1 day):** For 1 day, Red Furnace turns you into a smelter: your melee hits deal **+2d8 Magma** and you are immune to **On Fire** and **Burned**. The sacrifice is constant burn: at the end of each of your turns in combat you take **1d6 Fire** to HP (cannot be prevented) and you must destroy one usable tool or weapon by heat before the pact ends. If you fail, your body vitrifies: you gain **Gelled** for 7 turns and permanently lose **-10 ft movement**. If you attempt to avoid the backlash damage, you immediately take **4d10 Magma** to HP.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, you gain **Greater Resistance to Elemental** and once per combat you may create a **10 ft magma ring** for 3 turns: enemies entering or starting there take **4d8 Magma** to HP and must pass **DC 15 Str** or become **Prone**. The sacrifice is collateral: you must melt something meaningful that belongs to someone else (heirloom, badge, vow token, crafted gift—GM validates) and you must accept the consequences socially. If you fail, you suffer **Hellfire** for 7 turns and your **AC is reduced by 2 permanently** until a major cure. If you break the pact early, you take **8d10 Fire** to HP.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, you gain **Furnace Step**: the first time each turn you move at least 10 ft, enemies you pass within 5 ft take **2d8 Fire** to HP and gain **Burned**, and your attacks ignore resistance to Fire/Magma (not immunity). The sacrifice is abstinence from water and comfort: you may not enter water, be Soaked, or receive cooling magic, and you must go without sleep one night during the pact. If you fail, your blood hardens: you permanently lose **1 Ap** (minimum 1) and gain **Slowed** for 30 days (Remove Curse required). If you try to circumvent the restriction with loopholes, the GM treats it as failure.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, once per long rest you may erupt in a **40 ft radius**: creatures take **10d10 Magma** to HP and must pass **DC 15 Str** or become **Prone** and **Stunned** for 1 turn; terrain becomes difficult for 5 turns. The sacrifice is a life-forge: you must kill a sentient creature and burn the body to ash, then use the ash to temper a weapon or object (ritual act). If you fail, you permanently lose **2 stat points** (your choice) and gain **Cursed** until removed. If you refuse the kill, you take **12d10 Magma** to HP immediately.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you are the Furnace Avatar: you gain **+2 Ap**, **Magma Immunity**, and every melee hit applies **Burned** automatically and deals **+2d10 Fire** to HP (extra instance). Once per long rest you may declare **Total Smelt** for 3 turns: your attacks ignore AC bonuses granted by armor/shields (base AC still applies) and you cannot be grappled or restrained. The sacrifice is a fortress to slag: you must reduce a fortified location to ruin by fire/stone, and you must personally break its central support (gate, pillar, engine, altar—GM validates). If you fail, you become living slag: **Petrified** permanently, and if freed by miracle later you return with **-3 permanent stat points**.

## Knotted Word — Cursed

**Tier 1 (1 day):** For 1 day, Knotted Word gives you a spoken hook: once per turn you may impose **Cursed (-2 to all d20 rolls)** on a target you can see for 1 turn (no save). You also gain **Hexproof** for the pact duration. The sacrifice is self-erasure: you must surrender your true name—no one may speak or write it, and you must not answer to it; additionally, you must break a promise you made this week. If you fail, you become **Hexed** for 7 turns and permanently lose **-1 CHA**. If you try to dodge the promise break, you also gain **Slow Death (1 stack)**.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, you may once per combat speak a **Knotted Verdict**: one creature within 60 ft makes **DC 15 Wis**; on fail they gain **Hexed** for 3 turns and cannot benefit from **Blessed/Hasted** during that time. The sacrifice is truth-theft: you must knowingly convince someone of a falsehood that materially harms them, and you must never speak a direct truth for the pact’s duration. If you fail, you are **Silenced** for 30 days (Remove Curse Upcast x3), and you permanently lose **-1 INT**. If you break the “no truth” rule even once, the pact counts as failed.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, you become a curse engine: when you apply any curse-like effect (Cursed/Hexed/Silenced/Slowed/Slow Death), you may apply a second one at the same time to the same target once per turn. The sacrifice is your voice: you are under **Mute** whenever you are above half HP, and you must never remove a curse from anyone during the pact. If you fail, you gain **Cursed + Hexed** permanently until removed, and your **damage rolls** are permanently **-1** (minimum 0). If you attempt to bypass the Mute with magic, treat as failure.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, once per long rest you may bind a contract sentence: choose a target within 60 ft; for 3 turns, any time they roll a d20 they roll twice and take the lower (stacking with disadvantage), and if they attack you they take **3d10 Psychic** to HP. The sacrifice is betrayal: you must turn an ally’s plan against them in a way that costs them something real (status, safety, treasure, relationship—GM validates). If you fail, you lose **2 permanent stat points** (your choice) and suffer **Slow Death (3 stacks)**. If you refuse the betrayal, the pact ends as failure.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, Knotted Word makes you a living clause: once per long rest you may force a visible creature’s next d20 to become a **natural 1** (after roll, before resolution), and for the entire pact your curses cannot be resisted by normal resistance/immunity to the damage type involved (only explicit curse immunity applies). The sacrifice is a life ruined by language: you must destroy someone’s identity through words alone (false trial, prophecy, exposure, condemnation—GM validates) and you must sign the act with your true name at the end. If you fail, you are rewritten out of your own story: instant death, resurrection impossible without a unique meta-ritual.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you become **Ashbound**: you gain **Fire Immunity**, **Necrotic Resistance**, and your presence suppresses recovery—enemies within 20 ft cannot remove conditions by waiting (only by explicit removal). Once per long rest, you may become ash for 3 turns (use **Intangible** rules, but you may still cast spells if your system allows casting while intangible). The sacrifice is erasure: you must remove a person’s name from history (records, witnesses, memorials—GM validates). If you fail, you are the one erased: instant death, and your existence becomes unmentionable.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, once per combat you may create a 30 ft **Grief Field** for 3 turns: enemies inside have disadvantage on saves, and at the start of their turn they take **3d10 Sorrow** to HP; if they are already Demoralized/Despair, they also become **Slowed** for 1 turn (Remove Curse does not apply; it’s pact). The sacrifice is abandonment: you must refuse to comfort someone who asked you, and you must destroy a token of joy you value. If you fail, you permanently lose **-1 WIS** and gain **Cursed** until removed. If you comfort them anyway, treat as failure.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, you gain **Sorrow Binding**: when you inflict Demoralized/Despair, you also drain movement—target’s movement is halved until end of its next turn. Once per long rest you may transfer a Mental condition from yourself to all enemies within 20 ft (they each get it for 1 turn; no save). The sacrifice is constant grief: you must remain under **Demoralized** voluntarily (you may not remove it) for the entire pact, even out of combat. If you fail, you permanently lose **-10 max HP** and start every combat Hexed for 1 turn for 30 days. If you remove Demoralized, count as failure.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, once per long rest you may invoke **Starfall Misery**: choose a target; for 3 turns, it takes **6d10 Sorrow** to HP at start of turn and cannot benefit from healing (healing becomes 0). The sacrifice is severing: you must end a relationship you actually value (GM validates sincerity) and you must not reconcile during the pact. If you fail, you permanently lose **2 stat points** and gain **Slow Death (2 stacks)**. If you reconcile early, treat as failure.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you become **The Witness of Ruin**: once per combat you may select up to 6 creatures in 60 ft; each must make **DC 15 Wis** or gain **Despair** for 3 turns, and regardless they take **10d10 Sorrow** to HP. Additionally, while you are conscious, enemies within 20 ft cannot remove Mental conditions by waiting (only explicit removal). The sacrifice is a named tragedy: you must cause an irreversible tragedy to a named person (GM validates) and you must not attempt to fix it. If you fail, you die and your soul becomes a permanent “weeping relic” that curses the area.

## Iron Pit — Infernal

**Tier 1 (1 day):** For 1 day, Iron Pit grants **Hellbrand**: once per turn when you deal Infernal damage (Force+Fire), you also apply **Hellfire** automatically (no save), and you gain **Resistance to Infernal**. The sacrifice is blood-forged: you must carve a brand into yourself (narrative) and take **2d10 Infernal** to HP immediately (cannot be prevented), and you cannot receive healing for the rest of the day. If you fail, you gain **Hellfire** for 7 turns and your **max HP is permanently reduced by 10**. If you try to avoid the brand damage, treat as failure.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, once per combat you may open a small pit-surge: a 20 ft line; enemies take **10d10 Infernal** to HP and are pushed 10 ft (DC 15 Str negates push). The sacrifice is violence quota: you must kill one creature each day _in anger_ (GM validates intent), and you must refuse mercy once. If you fail, you permanently lose **-1 CON** and become **Enraged** for 3 turns at the start of every combat for 7 days. If you spare mercy when required, count as failure.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, you gain **Pit Chains**: once per long rest, bind a creature within 60 ft; for 3 turns, its movement is 0 (Grappled-like) and at the start of its turns it takes **4d10 Infernal** to HP and gains Hellfire if it didn’t have it. Your Infernal damage ignores resistance (not immunity). The sacrifice is self-harm tithe: you must voluntarily take **50 HP** damage total during the pact (cannot be healed above the level you had when you took the last tithe until pact ends). If you fail, you permanently lose **1 Ap** and gain **Cursed** until removed. If you heal above the threshold, count as failure.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, once per long rest you may open an **Iron Pit Gate** (30 ft radius) for 3 turns: enemies inside take **6d10 Infernal** to HP at start of turn, and all healing inside is halved. The sacrifice is atrocity: you must destroy a safe haven (shelter, orphan cache, refuge route—GM validates) and you must not assist the survivors. If you fail, you permanently lose **2 stat points** and suffer **Slow Death (3 stacks)**. If you help afterward, GM treats it as failure.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you become **Hell’s Executor**: you gain **Infernal Immunity**, **+2 Ap**, and once per combat you may sentence a creature you damage—until combat ends it cannot be healed and at the start of its turn it takes **8d10 Infernal** to HP (DC 15 Con halves the damage each turn). The sacrifice is an innocent condemned: you must ruin or kill an innocent by your own hand and accept being known for it. If you fail, you are dragged to the Pit: instant death, resurrection only by campaign-level reversal.

## Black Mirror — Obsidian

**Tier 1 (1 day):** For 1 day, Black Mirror grants **Shard Armor**: you gain **+5 AC**, but you take **double damage from Bludgeoning** and you bleed glass—at the start of each combat you take **1d10 Obsidian** to HP (cannot be prevented). Once per turn when you are hit in melee, the attacker takes **2d10 Obsidian** to HP. The sacrifice is reflection taboo: you must not allow anyone to see your reflection and you must break one mirror or reflective surface intentionally. If you fail, you permanently lose **-1 DEX** and gain **Cursed** until removed. If you reveal your reflection intentionally, take **6d10 Obsidian** to HP instantly.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, you gain **Mirror Step**: once per turn you may teleport 10 ft to a space you can see (must be adjacent to a reflective surface), and once per combat you may teleport 30 ft as a reaction when targeted. Your Obsidian damage ignores resistance (not immunity). The sacrifice is identity fracture: you must destroy one portrait/record of yourself and you must not speak in first-person (“I/me/my”) for the pact. If you fail, you permanently lose **-1 CHA** and are **Blurred** for 12 days. If you break the speech rule, count as failure.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, you gain **Shard Counter**: once per turn, when you take damage, reduce it by **4d10** and reflect that reduced amount as Obsidian damage to the attacker (to HP). Once per long rest, you may trap a creature’s shadow—target must make **DC 15 Wis** or become **Intangible** for 1 turn (cannot attack), then returns. The sacrifice is cruelty by reveal: you must expose a secret that ruins someone socially, and you must refuse to retract it. If you fail, you permanently lose **1 Ap** and gain **Hexed** for 12 days. If you retract, treat as failure.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, once per long rest you may create a **Mirror Prison** for 3 turns: one creature within 60 ft makes **DC 15 Wis**; on fail, it cannot move more than 5 ft from its starting square each turn and its attacks have disadvantage, and when it is hit it takes **+2d10 Obsidian** to HP. The sacrifice is replacement: you must frame someone else for your wrongdoing in a way that costs them freedom or life. If you fail, you permanently lose **2 stat points** and suffer **Slow Death (2 stacks)**. If you confess, treat as failure.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you become **The Mirror Tyrant**: you gain immunity to being targeted directly for 1 turn per combat (you “mirror-fade” at will), and once per long rest you may copy one passive trait you witnessed from an enemy for 3 turns (GM defines exact match). Additionally, your AC bonus from this pact becomes **+8**. The sacrifice is identity theft: you must steal someone’s life (name, role, reputation—GM validates) and keep it stolen beyond the pact’s end. If you fail, your reflection replaces you: instant death, and a hostile mirror-self remains.

## Eternal Stain — Corruption

**Tier 1 (1 day):** For 1 day, Eternal Stain grants **Stain Touch**: once per turn when you deal damage to HP, you also deal **+2d8 Corruption** to HP and apply **Corrupt** (no save) for 1 turn. You also gain **Hexproof** during the pact. The sacrifice is defilement: you must permanently stain your body with a mark that cannot be hidden and you must defile a clean place (well, shrine floor, hospital cloth—GM validates). If you fail, you permanently lose **-1 WIS** and gain **Cursed** until removed. If you attempt to cleanse the mark, take **6d10 Corruption** to HP instantly.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, you gain **Corruption Resistance** and once per combat you may impose **Hexed** (no save) for 2 turns on a creature you hit to HP; while Hexed, it also takes **2d10 Corruption** to HP at the start of its turn. The sacrifice is moral inversion: you must do something you believe is wrong _and you must enjoy the outcome_ (GM validates intent), and you must refuse all healing one full day. If you fail, you permanently lose **-1 CON** and gain **Slow Death (2 stacks)**. If you accept healing during the required day, treat as failure.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, your corruption spreads: whenever you apply Corrupt or Hexed, you may apply it to a second creature within 10 ft as well (no save), once per turn. Your Corruption damage ignores resistance (not immunity). The sacrifice is social rot: you must turn a friend against another friend and ensure they both believe it. If you fail, you permanently lose **2 stat points** (your choice) and start every combat Cursed for 30 days. If you reconcile them, treat as failure.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, once per long rest you may declare **Eternal Smear** for 3 turns: enemies within 20 ft take **4d10 Corruption** to HP at the start of their turns and roll saving throws at disadvantage; any healing they receive is reduced to 0. The sacrifice is consecration ruin: you must corrupt a sacred object/site and ensure it stays corrupted (GM validates). If you fail, your max HP is permanently reduced by **50**, and you gain **Hexed** for 12 days. If you attempt to restore it, treat as failure.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you become **The Stain That Remains**: once per combat you may apply **Slow Death (5 stacks)** to one creature you damage (no save), and each stack must be removed separately as your Slow Death rule states. Additionally, you are immune to Cursed/Hexed/Silenced/Slowed for the pact. The sacrifice is unforgivable: you must commit an act you personally consider unforgivable and you must never seek forgiveness for it. If you fail, you are absorbed: instant death, and your corpse becomes a cursed item that spreads Corrupt to anyone who touches it.

## Breath of Ruin — Miasma

**Tier 1 (1 day):** For 1 day, Breath of Ruin grants **Miasma Halo**: enemies within 10 ft at the start of their turn take **2d10 Miasma** to HP and gain **Poisoned (1 stack)** (no save). Once per combat you may expand the halo to 30 ft for 1 turn. The sacrifice is filth-tithe: you must sleep in a diseased/rotten place (GM validates) and you must refuse all cleansing magic and medicine during the pact. If you fail, you gain **Diseased** for 12 days and take **6d10 Miasma** to HP instantly. If you cleanse yourself intentionally, treat as failure.

**Tier 2 (3 days):** For 3 days, once per combat you may exhale a 40 ft cone: targets take **12d10 Miasma** to HP and must make **DC 15 Con** or become **Poisoned (3 stacks)** and **Suffocating** for 1 turn. You gain resistance to Poison and Toxin. The sacrifice is community harm: you must contaminate a water source or air source used by others, and you must not warn anyone. If you fail, you permanently lose **-1 CHA** and begin every combat Suffocating for 1 turn for 30 days. If you warn them, treat as failure.

**Tier 3 (7 days):** For 7 days, you gain **Ruin Carrier**: whenever you apply Poisoned or Diseased, it automatically gains **+1 stack** and its removal DC increases by **+2** (in addition to your Poisoned rule’s DC scaling). Also, once per long rest you may transfer all your conditions onto a creature within 30 ft (no save), then heal **6d10 HP**. The sacrifice is self-destruction: you permanently lose **-1 CON** at pact start and you must not remove Diseased from yourself during the pact. If you fail, you permanently lose **2 stat points** and gain **Cursed** until removed. If you remove Diseased, count as failure.

**Tier 4 (12 days):** For 12 days, once per long rest you may create a **Ruin Fog** (40 ft radius) for 3 turns: enemies inside roll attacks and saves at disadvantage and take **6d10 Miasma** to HP at the start of their turns; allies inside also take **1d10 Miasma** to HP (it is not friendly). The sacrifice is sacrifice-of-innocents: you must allow harm to occur that you could have prevented and you must choose not to stop it (GM validates). If you fail, you permanently lose **1 Ap** and suffer **Slow Death (3 stacks)**. If you intervene, treat as failure.

**Tier 5 (30 days):** For 30 days, you become **Breath of Ruin** fully: at the start of each enemy’s turn within 30 ft, they automatically gain **Poisoned (2 stacks)** and take **2d10 Miasma** to HP (no save), and Poisoned/Diseased they have cannot be removed by waiting—only explicit removal works. Once per long rest, you may “blackout” a region: for 24 hours, an area becomes hazardous (GM scope) and anyone who rests there becomes Diseased. The sacrifice is a plague event: you must deliberately cause a spreading outbreak and you must not attempt to cure or contain it. If you fail, you become permanently contagious to allies within 30 ft and cannot be cured without a legendary quest.
