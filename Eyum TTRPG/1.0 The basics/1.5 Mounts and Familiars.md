## Mounting Rules
Mounting allows characters to ride creatures into battle, across dangerous terrain, or through long-distance travel. A mount must be at least 1 Size larger than its rider unless otherwise stated.

Mounts are separated into three broad categories:

- **Domestic Mounts**: Horses, camels, elk, etc.
- **War Mounts**: Creatures trained for battle.
- **Exotic Mounts**: Magical, monstrous, or rare creatures.

Most mounts will require a saddle, see [[2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Tools]] for more. 
Creatures with Intelligence 6+ may refuse riders they dislike (at GM's discretion, may be tamed with Animal Handling Check).

**Mounting and Dismounting**
- Mounting or dismounting costs:
    - 1 Bonus Action Point
    - OR half your movement (your choice).
- You must be adjacent to the mount.
- The mount must be willing, restrained, or controlled.

If you are knocked prone while mounted, you immediately dismount into an adjacent space.
###### Mounted Movement and Initiative 
- The rider and mount share initiative.
- The mount moves during the rider’s turn.
- When moving into danger (Any situation which could cause harm to the mount counts as danger, this will be chosen at the GM's discretion): 
	- Directing a war/exotic mount requires no action.
	- Directing an domestic mount into danger requires an Animal Handling check (DC 12-20).
	- Failure causes the mount to flee away from the source of danger.
###### Mounted Combat
While mounted:
- You occupy the mount’s space.
- Melee attacks gain Advantage if:
    - using a weapon with +5 ft Reach
    - OR the mount is at least 2 Sizes larger than the target.
- Creatures smaller than your mount have Disadvantage attacking you in melee unless they can reach you.
- Ranged attacks made while the mount moved this turn are made with Disadvantage unless you possess a specific feat (see [[3.4 Feats]]).
- Charging:
	- If a mount moves at least 30 ft directly toward a creature the rider may make one mounted charge attack:
		- On hit target must succeed a Strength Save DC = 8 + rider Proficiency + mount Strength modifier or be knocked [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental-Positional-Conditions|Prone]].
		- Some mounts grant additional charge effects.
###### Falling From a Mount
If the mount dies, falls prone or is forcibly moved the rider must succeed a DC 10 Dexterity Save or fall prone in an adjacent space and take 1d6 Bludgeoning damage.
If falling from a flying mount resolve normal falling damage (see [[1.6 Other Rules#Movement-Terrain-and-Cover]]).
###### Taking Care of Your Mount
Mounts will need to eat, drink, and sleep. How much food, water, and sleep they need will be up to your GM. 
###### Mounts

Untrained or wild specimens cost less but require Animal Handling checks to control (DC 12-20 depending on species).

## Familiars
A familiar is a monster you have tamed and bonded with, turning it into a loyal companion that fights alongside you, assists outside of combat, and grows in power as you do. Any monster with a statblock (see [[7.1 Monsters]]) can potentially become a familiar, provided you have the means to tame it.

Familiars are distinct from mounts, a familiar need not be rideable, though a large enough familiar may serve as both. They are also distinct from summons, which are temporary magical constructs. A familiar is a permanent, living creature that shares your journey.
###### Prerequisites
To tame and command a familiar, you must first unlock the ability to do so through the **Monster Specialist** path (see [[3.3 Leveling Up]]):

| Monster Type        | Required Level                    | Taming Unlock |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------- |
| [[7.3.1.1 Slimes]]  | Monster Specialist: Slime Rancher | Level 3.5     |
| [[7.3.1.2 Golems]]  | Monster Specialist: Golem Tamer   | Level 3.5     |
| [[7.3.1.3 Dragons]] | Monster Specialist: Dragonslayer  | Level 5.6     |

> *Unless otherwise specified by a ability or feat, you may only have one active familiar at a time. You may dismiss a familiar to a safe location (GM's discretion) and recall it later with a short rest.*
###### Taming a Monster
To tame a wild monster, you must meet its taming prerequisites and succeed on a contested check:

1. The monster must be at or below the maximum level you can tame (see Familiar Maximum Level below).
2. You must make an Animal Handling check contested by the monster's Wisdom Save.
   - **DC modifier**: The monster's Affinity modifier is added to the monster's roll.
   - If the monster is already Hostile toward you, the check is made with Disadvantage and suffers a -5 penalty.
   - If the monster is [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental-Positional-Conditions|Restrained]], [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Paralyzed]], or [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental-Positional-Conditions|Unconscious]], the check has Advantage.
1. Taming takes 10 minutes of uninterrupted focus. If you take damage during this time, you must succeed a DC 10 Constitution Save or the attempt fails and the monster becomes hostile.
2. On a success, the monster becomes docile and bonded to you as your familiar. On a failure, you may try again after 24 hours.
###### Familiar Maximum Level
A familiar's maximum level is capped based on its species and your current level. A familiar cannot exceed this cap regardless of experience gained.

| Monster Type      | Max Familiar Level            |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Slimes            | 1/2 your level (rounded down) |
| Golems            | 1/3 your level (rounded down) |
| Dragons           | 1/5 your level (rounded down) |
| Other/Unspecified | 1/2 your level (rounded down) |

> *Example: A level 10 Player (with the Slime Rancher Level 3.5 Unlock) can tame slimes up to level 5. A level 20 Player (with the Dragonslayer level 5.6 Unlock) can tame dragons up to level 4.*

You **CANNOT** tame monsters who are above your Max familiar cap by any means. 
###### Familiar Basics
- A familiar uses its own monster statblock (see the appropriate monster list in [[7.1 Monsters]]]), including its stats, Vitality, Health, Mana, AC, Speed, damage, passives, and actives, exactly as written.
- The familiar shares your initiative and acts on your turn.
- By default, a familiar has 1 Ap, 1 BAp, and 0 Rp per turn (per the standard companion rules in [[1.4 Actions#Turn-Economy]]).
- You may spend your own Reaction Points (Rp) to grant your familiar a reaction once per round.
- A familiar is a creature in its own right, it occupies space, provokes opportunity attacks, can be targeted, and dies when it reaches 0 HP.
- You may communicate basic commands to your familiar as a Free Action on your turn. Complex tactical instructions require the familiar to have Intelligence 6+.
###### Familiar Leveling
A familiar gains experience alongside the party. Each time you level up and your familiar is not yet at it's maximum level, it gains a level.

Each time a familiar gains a level, apply the standard monster level-up bonuses from [[7.1 Monsters#Monster-Levels]]:

| Per Level | Bonus |
|---|---|
| Vitality | +1 Hit Die (roll or average) |
| Health | +1 Hit Die (roll or average) |
| Mana | +1 Hit Die (roll or average) |
| Generic Damage Mod | +1 |
| Affinity | +1 to its primary Affinity |
| AC | +1 |
| Stats (STR, CON, DEX) | +1 to each |
| Stats (WIS, INT, CHA) | +1 to each every 3 levels (3, 6, 9, etc.) |

> This is identical to the monster leveling rules in [[7.1 Monsters#Monster-Levels]]. The familiar's statblock at its current level can be built by applying these bonuses to its level 1 base.

Some Monster Specialist path upgrades (e.g., Golem Tamer's overcharge) may provide alternative methods of leveling a familiar, those override these standard rules where they apply but can never exceed the monsters maximum level.
###### Familiar Bond
Over time, your familiar grows more attuned to you. After the familiar has served you for at least 3 levels gained while bonded, it forms a Bond with you:

- While your familiar is within 30 ft of you, you and the familiar gain +1 to all Saving Throws.
- Your familiar gains its own Reaction Point (Rp) and no longer needs you to sacrifice yours. 
- The familiar gains +2 to its highest stat.

This bond is broken if the familiar dies or if you dismiss it permanently. A new familiar must earn its own bond from scratch.
###### Familiar Equipment
A familiar may wear equipment appropriate to its body type, chosen from the Mounts/Familiar Equipment table in [[2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Tools]]. Armor functions identically for familiars as they do for mounts.

A familiar may also carry items, pouches, or packs at the GM's discretion, based on its size and strength.
###### Multiple Familiars
Unless an ability explicitly states otherwise, you may only have one active familiar at a time. You may maintain a bond with only one familiar at a time, switching to a new familiar breaks the previous bond.

Some Monster Specialist abilities (e.g., Golem Tamer's multiple commands) may allow you to control multiple creatures, but only one of them counts as your bonded familiar for the purposes of the Familiar Bond feature and the standard companion action rules.
###### Losing a Familiar
If your familiar reaches 0 HP, it is dying and follows the same death and dying rules as a player character (see [[1.3 Player Stats#Health-and-Vitality]]). If it dies, the bond is broken.
- A dead familiar can be revived with magic (e.g., *Revivify*, *Raise Dead*) just as any creature can up to a point. 
- If a familiar is permanently lost, you may tame a new one. 

If you release a familiar willingly, it returns to the wild with whatever equipment it was wearing (unless you retrieve it before release). It retains its memories and will not be hostile toward you unless provoked but loses it's bond, if you re-tame the same creature to be a familiar it only takes one level to restore its bond.
###### Familiars and Mounts
A familiar that is at least one Size larger than you (see its statblock entry for Size) may serve as a mount, following the mounted combat rules at the top of this section.  If a familiar is acting as a mount, it uses its own Ap, BAp, and Rp for movement and actions during the mount phase of your turn, separate from your own action economy (as per standard mounted combat rules).
