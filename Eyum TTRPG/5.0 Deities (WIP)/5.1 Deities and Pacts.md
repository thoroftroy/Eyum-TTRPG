All deities are vast celestial beings, colossal living presences that appear to mortals as planets, stars, moons, or other great bodies in the void. They are not symbols of these bodies; they _are_ them. Each is ancient, conscious, and bound to the nature of the sphere it inhabits. In the earliest age, Darwin, known to mortals as Earth, conceived a radical act: the creation of life. From simple, unseen organisms he cultivated an ever-branching tapestry of evolution. Over eons, these organisms grew into complex beings, and from them arose thought, language, and worship. Darwin discovered something the others had not: life generates power. The more minds that speak a deity’s name, the stronger that deity becomes. Influence, belief, memory, these are nourishment. The other celestial beings grew jealous. None had succeeded in creating true life of their own. They began to compete for influence over Darwin’s creations, shaping cultures, inspiring fear, granting gifts, and spreading their names across the living world. From this rivalry, three distinct divine lineages emerged: The Ancients are the eldest celestial beings. Their power does not depend entirely on mortal belief, for their age and mass alone grant them immense strength. Still, they seek influence among humanity, though their methods are often abstract, alien, or incomprehensible. The second generation divided into two factions: The Gods pursue devotion. They grow stronger through worship, reverence, and sacred covenant. The Fiends pursue destruction. They feed upon death, despair, corruption, and the collapse of life. Distance, size, and celestial position affect how directly each being can influence the mortal world. Some loom close and exert great pressure; others drift far beyond and whisper faintly. Yet all share one truth: they envy the living world Darwin created, and all hunger for the power that life provides.
### Deities and Pacts
Deities fall into three groups: Gods, Fiends, and Ancients. Gods are generally viewed as good, Fiends as evil, and Ancients as neutral or alien.
#### Pact Path Gate
You may form pacts once you gain access to the Pact Path. At Pact Path Level 1, you may form pacts with any deity type (Gods, Fiends, or Ancients), but you are limited to Tier 1 pacts. Higher-tier pacts require progress in the appropriate sub-path: Beacon for Gods, Occultist for Ancients, and Reaper for Fiends. Pacts must be taken in order for each deity. You cannot take a deity’s Tier 2 pact unless you have previously taken and completed that same deity’s Tier 1 pact at least once. This rule continues upward: to take a higher tier, you must have taken and completed every prior tier for that deity.

To form a pact, you must obtain a Pact Stone of the appropriate type: Godly Pact Stone, Fiendish Pact Stone, or Ancient Pact Stone. Pact Stones are found and traded throughout the world, but their sources vary. A Pact Stone is usually attuned to a specific deity and can only be used to form pacts with that deity. Pact stones are not single use, they can be used as many times as you wish. The pact stone types are:
- **Godly Pact Stones** are most commonly found in temples devoted to the relevant god. 
- **Fiendish Pact Stones** function similarly to Godly Pact Stones, but are typically acquired through cults or black-market channels, and often require joining or dealing with those groups to obtain them.
- **Ancient Pact Stones** are commonly recovered from ancient altars, burial grounds, ruins, or occasionally appear in shops. Ancient stones are almost always unidentified when found.

If a Pact Stone’s type is unknown, it must be identified with an Arcana check (DC 15). If a pact stone is not yet attuned to a deity it can be attuned to any deity by someone who has taken at least a Tier 3 Pact from that deity with an Arcana check (DC 10). Similarly, a Pact Stone can be unattuned from a deity at the cost of 50 Mana and an Arcana check (DC 17). If you fail that check the mana is spent but nothing else happens. 

Pact stones can usually be purchased for around 1-10 Gold depending on the source or the type. 
#### Pact structure
Every pact has four parts:
- **The Gain**
	- You receive a concrete benefit (skill, passive, stat boost, information, etc.). At least one tier benefit usually relates to the deity’s damage type, conditions, or resistance to them.
- **The Sacrifice**
	- You must give something specific. Higher tiers give more power and demand harsher sacrifices.
- **The Time Frame**
	- Pacts last a limited time. You must complete the sacrifice within that time.
	- Default time frames (use unless a pact says otherwise)
		- **Tier 1:** 1 days
		- **Tier 2:** 3 days
		- **Tier 3:** 7 days
		- **Tier 4:** 12 days
		- **Tier 5:** 30 days
- **The Punishment**
	- If you fail, you suffer a specified consequence. Higher tiers are brutally punishing. Some pacts require you to complete something by the end of the pact period, others may require something daily. Failure to complete the pact is determined on a case by case basis. Failing a pact's condition (if it must be completed by the end of the day) will result in the pact ending immediately and you suffering the penalty. 
	- Any condition that is given as a punishment for failing a pact (or any damage) bypasses all immunities and resistances. 
	- If you have failed a pact with a specific deity 3 times you are no longer capable of attempting to form pacts with that deity again. 
#### Alignments
Some deities have alignments of their own, they will not make deals with you unless your alignment matches theirs. For example they may have the Lawful alignment, it doesn't matter if you are lawful good or lawful evil as long as you are lawful you may form a pact with them. If no alignment is specified anyone may form a pact with them.

# Ranking of Deities 
### 1. The King
**Darwin/Earth** Darwin is slow, deliberate, and difficult to provoke. He values systems that survive long periods more than individual comfort, and he rewards outcomes that increase adaptation, resilience, and long-term survival. He favors people who accept pressure, build sustainable communities, and keep improving methods across generations. In humanoid form he appears as a broad-shouldered, heavy-built Human man in his late 40s to early 50s with tan-brown skin, thick black hair with gray at the temples, and a strong jaw with a short, neat beard. His eyes are deep green with small gold flecks, and his hands show old calluses from physical work. He wears layered green and brown travel clothing, heavy boots, a weathered cloak, a wide belt with pouches for tools and seed vials, and plain iron-and-wood jewelry with no gemstones. Darwin treats life as an ongoing managed project: he introduces environmental pressures, relocates species and bloodlines through events and migrations, and encourages civilizations to solve problems without direct rescue. He sponsors explorers, founders, healers, and agricultural expansion because new environments create new selection pressures and new societies create new cultural competition. He is married to Aegirion as his stabilizing counterbalance, he maintains working contact with most deities to prevent any single influence from collapsing the system, and he opposes any power that attempts total control, total sterilization, or total annihilation of living ecosystems.
## 2. The Great Sovereigns
**Ignivar/Mercury** Ignivar is fast, decisive, and aggressive, and he values immediate action more than planning discussions. He rewards courage, momentum, and leadership under danger, and he punishes hesitation and indecision because they create weakness in groups. He pushes rulers toward conquest, rapid reform, and direct confrontation, and he respects results that are visible and immediate. In humanoid form he appears as a tall, muscular Fireborn man with copper-red skin and faint glowing crack patterns along the collarbones and forearms. He has thick black horns swept back close to the skull, ember-orange eyes, and dense black hair that constantly releases thin smoke. He wears a sleeveless ash-black coat reinforced with brass plates, heavy gauntlets, heat-scarred boots, and a belt of flame-shaped metal talismans and weapon loops, with no loose fabric. Ignivar’s role is to accelerate conflict so societies are forced to adapt quickly or collapse. He pushes warlords, generals, and revolutionaries into positions where their choices reshape regions in short timeframes. He argues constantly with Aegirion over patience versus speed, he clashes openly with Iron Pit over control of violence and chains, and he respects Indravajra’s dominance even while competing with him for fear-based obedience. He is father-linked to Rimeflame, Hemalux, Red Furnace, and Black Mirror and treats them as extensions of his influence that should stay dangerous and active.

**Aegirion/Europa** Aegirion is calm, relentless, and stubborn, and he values stability, continuity, and long-term control that does not require shouting. He rewards discipline, loyalty, and institutions that endure, and he punishes reckless change by applying sustained pressure until opposition breaks. He prefers negotiations that end with only one practical outcome and views open chaos as inefficient. In humanoid form he appears as a tall Nerid man with sea-blue skin, webbed hands, and long dark teal hair tied low behind the neck. His eyes are black with a thin silver ring around the iris, and his face stays controlled even under threat. He wears layered deep-blue robes with pearl stitching, a mantle that continuously drips clean water without soaking cloth, and a collar of smooth stone beads, with minimal metal. Aegirion’s role is to keep Darwin’s living world from destroying itself through constant instability: he strengthens trade routes, treaties, coastal infrastructure, and long-term cultural memory. He is married to Darwin and acts as the primary counterbalance to Darwin’s harsher pressures by keeping societies intact long enough to adapt instead of simply failing. He distrusts Pelagion and Drowned Prophet because they turn water into catastrophe, he tolerates Eldathis as a stabilizing moral authority when useful, and he opposes Iron Pit through slow sabotage and coalition-building rather than direct war.

**Da Vinci/Ariel** Da Vinci is intelligent, controlled, and demanding, and he values functional hierarchy, optimized systems, and measurable improvement. He rewards competence, planning, and technical mastery, and he punishes waste, inefficiency, and talent that is not developed. He prefers structured institutions, standardized training, and solutions that scale.  
In humanoid form he appears as a tall, slim Elf man with pale skin faintly tinted sky-blue and long white-blond hair braided with small silver rings. His eyes are metallic gray, his posture is precise, and his hands are usually gloved. He wears fitted coats with high collars, layered gloves, belts of tools, scroll tubes, instrument cases, and small metal measuring devices clipped across the chest, with clean seams and no loose fabric. Da Vinci’s role is to build civilizations that survive divine interference through engineering, governance, and disciplined magical education. He cultivates architects, inventors, strategists, and administrators, and he pushes standardization so societies become harder to destabilize. He debates Einstein as an intellectual rival, frustrates Lorenz by resisting chaos through structure, criticizes Ignivar’s impulsiveness as inefficient, and challenges Darwin directly because he believes Earth wastes lives through unmanaged variability.

**Eldathis/Pluto** Eldathis is rigid, formal, and judgmental, and he values purity standards, clear guilt lines, and visible consequence. He rewards obedience, moral discipline, and public adherence to law, and he punishes ambiguity and compromise because they enable corruption. He prefers societies where behavior is regulated and punished consistently. In humanoid form he appears as a tall Aurian man with bright pale-gold skin that reflects light softly even in darkness. He has short white hair, glowing white eyes with no visible pupils, and a posture that never slouches. He wears structured white robes trimmed in gold, a rigid shoulder mantle, a thin gold circlet shaped like upright bars, and clean leather sandals or boots that never show dust. Eldathis’s role is to formalize worship into law: courts, priesthoods, purity codes, and systems that define sin and absolution. He is romantically bound to Black Mercy in a relationship defined by constant conflict of purpose and constant personal attachment, and their closeness causes social disasters when their cults overlap. He views Iron Pit as a fallen instrument that should be contained, he despises Eternal Stain’s subtle corruption because it hides guilt, and he positions himself as moral authority over other deities in ways that make him politically useful and personally resented.

**Black Mercy/Charon** Black Mercy is patient, observant, and intimate, and she values endings, debt, and inevitability of mortality. She rewards acceptance, preparation, and honesty about loss, and she punishes denial by taking what people cling to and turning grief into binding obligation. She prefers slow influence through mourning and private bargains over public threats. In humanoid form she appears as a Revenant woman with pale gray skin, black lips, and deep purple shadows under the eyes that look permanent. Her long black hair always looks wet, and her eyes are fully black with a thin white ring around the iris. She wears funeral-black silk, bone jewelry, a trailing veil, soft boots, and layered sleeves that hide the hands until she chooses to show them. Black Mercy’s role is to convert death into power through funerary cults, grief rites, and pact systems that trade survival for future claim. She loves Eldathis and refuses to submit to him, and their bond is both political and personal with constant ideological conflict. She maintains private contact with Iron Pit because Hell and death cooperate efficiently, and she keeps a quiet working understanding with Darwin because life and death are part of the same closed system even when their factions treat them as enemies.

**Iron Pit/Venus** Iron Pit is authoritarian, organized, and ruthless, and she values hierarchy, obedience, and controlled suffering as a tool for shaping behavior. She rewards submission, productivity, and fear-based loyalty, and she punishes defiance publicly so that punishment spreads as deterrence. She prefers strict rules that convert individuals into assets. In humanoid form she appears as a towering Demon woman over eight feet tall with iron-gray skin, large horns, and muscular arms and shoulders. Her eyes glow red-orange like embers, her hair is long black braids threaded with chains, and her voice stays controlled and low. She wears black iron plates bolted together, a jagged crown forged from broken shackles, chain-draped ornaments, spiked bracers, and heavy boots designed for noise and intimidation. Iron Pit’s role is rulership of the Infernal Realm as a functioning empire with legions, ranks, punishment law, and a resource system based on suffering and coercion. She recruits through pacts that operate as ownership contracts, elevates fiends based on utility and cruelty discipline, and exports infernal structure into mortal cults. She clashes with Ignivar because he wants violence without chains, bargains with Eternal Stain and Knotted Word to make corruption scalable and legalistic, and resents Darwin because Earth produces resistance movements faster than Hell can fully suppress.
## 3. Cosmic Law
**Newton/Saturn** Newton is strict, quiet, and absolute about consequence, and he values neutrality, measurement, and rule consistency. He rewards discipline, accurate prediction, and acceptance of cost, and he punishes attempts to escape responsibility through excuses or loopholes. He does not respond to moral argument as authority; he responds to defined terms and consistent results. In humanoid form he appears as an older Kravari man with dense build, stone-pale skin, and faint ring-shaped scar bands around the forearms and throat. His hair is iron-gray and cut short, his eyes are near-black and reflective, and his expression stays flat. He wears heavy dark clothing with reinforced seams, weighted boots, a long coat with inner pockets for rulers and weights, and plain metal bands on the fingers with engraved measurement marks. Newton’s role is enforcement of boundaries on pact power and on divine influence: he ensures deals have defined limits and that violations have reliable outcomes. He respects Darwin for producing a new power source through life, and he treats Edison as dangerous but still containable when rules are enforced. He distrusts Lorenz because instability reduces predictability, and he maintains professional relationships with Knotted Word and Boyle only when they follow constraints without attempting to redefine them.

**Indravajra/Jupiter** Indravajra is proud, volatile, and dominance-focused, and he values fear compliance, public submission, and open displays of superiority. He rewards bravery that serves him and punishes disrespect immediately and visibly. He prefers leadership through intimidation and insists on being treated as the strongest presence in any region he influences. In humanoid form he appears as a towering Kravari man with dark storm-tinted skin and bright eyes that flash pale light in short bursts. His hair is thick and black and lifts slightly as if affected by constant static, and his shoulders are extremely broad. He wears metal-and-leather armor with wide shoulder plates, a heavy cloak lined with storm-gray fur, thick bracers with conductive metal rings, and heavy boots that strike loudly. Indravajra’s role is to build storm-based empires and war cults that obey through fear rather than devotion ethics. He competes with Keraunos over dominance of thunder worship, needles Aeolus as a rival that refuses control, and is willing to speak with Newton because Newton’s rules can be used to formalize obedience. He respects Darwin’s results but treats Darwin’s patience as weakness that should be exploited.

**Edison/Sun** Edison is driven, harsh, and obsessed with progress, and he values productivity, advancement, and visible achievement. He rewards builders, innovators, and administrators who increase output, and he punishes stagnation by forcing people into effort until weakness is exposed. He treats comfort as a risk factor that produces decline. In humanoid form he appears as a radiant Lumari man with bronze-gold skin, sharp amber eyes, and hair that looks metallic and heavy with a warm sheen. His face stays composed and alert, and he never appears tired. He wears clean white and gold clothing tailored tightly, a structured mantle with precise stitching, polished boots, and minimal jewelry that is always perfectly maintained. Edison’s role is to push civilizations into industrial and magical advancement regardless of social strain. He builds schools, workshops, and centralized projects that increase capability, and he encourages leadership styles that prioritize output over rest. He respects Darwin because life generates endless fuel through new problems, he considers Mnemosyne useful but dangerous because memory slows change, and he treats Wither Bloom as a direct threat because rot reverses progress.

**Hawking/Perseus** Hawking is quiet, detached, and highly strategic, and she values distance, informational control, and reducing mortals’ certainty about reality. She rewards caution, secrecy, and attention management, and she punishes curiosity that reaches too far by removing support systems and leaving people isolated. She prefers indirect influence through fear of the unknown. In humanoid form she appears as a Revenant woman with ash-gray skin, a narrow face, and dark eyes with a faint violet rim. Her hair is straight, black, and worn long, and her movements are controlled and minimal. She wears layered matte-black wraps, light armor under-cloth, a high collar that hides the throat, gloves that cover the hands fully, and a single dark stone pendant on a thin cord. Hawking’s role is to expand void-focused cults and to redirect mortal attention away from useful truths and toward paralysis and misinformation. She avoids direct conflict with Darwin because Earth’s living systems generate too much noise for clean control, but she still undermines exploration and unified science when it threatens her influence. She maintains professional respect for Newton’s constraints, treats Boyle’s containment efforts as incomplete against unknown threats, and remains a central ancestral figure through her children and grandchildren in the family tree.

**Einstein/Sirius** Einstein is analytical, relentless about accuracy, and focused on understanding rather than comfort. He values correct models, honest observation, and intellectual integrity, and he rewards people who accept harsh truths and improve methods. He punishes ideology that demands belief without evidence by exposing its contradictions. In humanoid form he appears as a tall Elf man with pale blue-white skin tone, long white-blond hair worn loose but carefully maintained, and sharp silver-gray eyes. His hands are often ink-stained, and he carries notebooks and star charts openly. He wears dark fitted coats, layered scarves, strapped cases for instruments, clean gloves, and practical boots suited for travel rather than ceremony. Einstein’s role is to cultivate astronomers, mathematicians, and high-level mages who can map and predict divine influence. He builds networks of scholars that compete with priesthood monopolies by making knowledge itself a source of power and devotion. He rivals Da Vinci over invention versus understanding, respects Mnemosyne for preserving continuity, and clashes with Knotted Word because rules without comprehension enable control without truth.

**Lorenz/Hyperion** Lorenz is impulsive, socially skilled, and destabilizing by preference, and she values unpredictability, rapid change, and outcomes driven by small choices. She rewards people who embrace uncertainty and adapt quickly, and she punishes rigid control systems by pushing them into failure. She does not build stable institutions and prefers to fragment them. In humanoid form she appears as a tall Skinwalker woman with athletic build and warm brown skin covered in shifting tattoo-like markings that never settle into one pattern. She has one gold eye and one green eye, and her hair is dark and braided with bright cords and small bone beads. She wears layered colorful clothing designed for movement, multiple belts with pouches, light armor underlayers, and jewelry arranged asymmetrically but consistently. Lorenz’s role is to keep societies from becoming stable enough to resist divine influence through predictable systems. She encourages internal conflict, fads, ideological splits, and leadership collapse so people seek external power for stability. She antagonizes Newton because predictability limits her methods, she provokes Mnemosyne because stable records reduce her control, and she remains a major ancestral source in the family tree with direct lineage ties to multiple Gods and Fiends.

**Boyle/Hygiea** Boyle is controlled, clinical, and strict, and she values containment, quarantine, and survival through environmental control. She rewards discipline, sanitation, and boundary enforcement, and she punishes negligence by letting consequences spread until people learn control. She treats unmanaged exposure as failure. In humanoid form she appears as a Chelonar woman with thick build, pale pearl-toned skin, and faint luminous lines along the arms like pressure marks. Her eyes are metallic gray-green, her hair is dark and tightly bound under a hood, and her face is often partially covered. She wears sterile white-and-gray layered clothing, a fitted breathing mask, heavy gloves, and a belt of vials, clamps, sealing wax, and metal tools. Boyle’s role is to prevent uncontrolled spread of threats that destabilize the living world: plague, magical contamination, and uncontrolled planar leakage. She works with Seraphael when purification is possible, she opposes Wither Bloom and Lung Spore as direct enemies, and she distrusts Darwin because life resists containment and creates new vectors constantly. She is the mother-line ancestor of Mnemosyne and a key parent in the family tree with ongoing influence through that lineage.

**Mnemosyne/Moon** Mnemosyne is patient, socially precise, and focused on memory control, and she values recordkeeping, identity continuity, and information leverage. She rewards confession, documentation, and loyalty to history, and she punishes people who attempt to erase or rewrite truth by preserving evidence and distributing it to the right hands. She prefers influence through archives rather than armies. In humanoid form she appears as a tall Aurian woman with pale silver-gold skin and luminous eyes that look white-silver in low light. Her hair is long, straight, and black, kept perfectly brushed, and her expression stays calm and observant. She wears layered gray and silver silk with fine metallic thread, a high waist sash holding scroll cases, a thin veil, and quiet jewelry made of moonstone and polished bone. Mnemosyne’s role is to maintain the mortal record as a power source: names spoken, deeds remembered, and lineages preserved. She supports historians, scribes, courts, and inquisitions that store testimony and bind reputation. She respects Darwin because life produces endless history, she hates Black Mirror because deletion attacks her domain directly, and she competes with Eternal Stain because corruption often functions by rewriting memory and social narrative.

**Eternal Stain/Bellerophon** Eternal Stain is calculating, disciplined, and methodical, and he values corruption that scales through systems rather than impulse. He rewards compromise, rationalized wrongdoing, and institutional rot that produces long-term control. He punishes morality by making it expensive until people abandon it for convenience. In humanoid form he appears as a tall Human man with flawless posture, smooth pale skin with faint dark discoloration under the nails, and dark eyes that do not show warmth. His hair is black and cut neatly, and his face is carefully maintained with no visible scars. He wears expensive formal clothing in dark colors, gloves, polished shoes, and a small set of signet rings that mark office, contract, and authority. Eternal Stain’s role is to corrupt governments, priesthoods, and trade networks so that devotion and obedience flow into him through routine. He cooperates with Iron Pit when corruption needs enforcement, works with Black Mirror when corruption needs secrecy, and competes with Knotted Word over who controls binding systems more effectively. He clashes with Mnemosyne because records make corruption harder to deny, and he avoids Darwin’s direct attention by operating through mortal institutions rather than open divine confrontation.
## 4. Major Forces of Nature
**Keraunos/Neptune** Keraunos is authoritarian, controlling, and fear-driven, and he values obedience under pressure, naval dominance, and public submission. He rewards loyalty that holds during disaster and punishes disobedience with destructive demonstrations. He prefers rule through threat rather than persuasion. In humanoid form he appears as a massive Deepmantle man with dark navy skin and faint bioluminescent lines along the shoulders and arms.
