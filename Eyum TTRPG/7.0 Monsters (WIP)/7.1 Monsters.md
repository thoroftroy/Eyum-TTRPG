Monsters are just creatures which the player will interact with in the World of Eyum. Most are aggressive but not all. Monsters can take all actions that players can take, however they are not as intelligent as a player so they shouldn't take intelligent/strategic actions unless it makes sense for that specific monster. 

Each monster fulfills a specific combat role, which reflects how it fights and what function it serves in an encounter. A monster’s role influences how it positions itself, what targets it prioritizes, and how it applies pressure during combat. These roles include:
- **Berserker** - Constant frenzy of attacks
- **Tanker** - Tries to take hits to allow other monsters to deal damage
- **Guerrilla** - Attacks hard then disengages and runs away and hides
- **Ranger** - Attack from a distance
- **Stealth** - Sneak up on the player and deal lots of damage then sneak away
- **Mixed** - Is capable of attacking from melee and ranged
- **Support** - Makes other monsters stronger or summons other monsters
- **Swarmer** - Attack in large groups and simply overwhelm players with numbers

Monsters are also defined by an affinity, which represents the primary type of energy or damage they embody. Lower-difficulty monsters tend to use more common and easily understood affinities such as Fire, Earth, Water, Air, Radiant, and Necrotic, while stronger monsters may exhibit rarer or more complex affinities. Psychic-affinity creatures are uncommon and appear less frequently. A monster’s main affinity is applied to its attacks as normal and typically grants it Resistance to that same damage type. Weaknesses usually align naturally with the chosen affinity.

Monsters are grouped into categories based on what they are, where they live, and how they function. A GM can use these categories to quickly find the right creature for an encounter.
### Elemental Monsters
Elemental monsters are creatures whose very being is tied to the elemental affinities of Eyum. Every elemental monster category has a variant for every single affinity, meaning you can find a Fire Slime, a Water Slime, a Radiant Slime, an Eldritch Slime, and so on. They are the most numerous monster type by raw variant count and form the backbone of the world's monster ecology. Elementals tend to be found in environments matching their affinity and are driven more by elemental instinct than complex thought.

- [[7.3.1.1 Slimes]]: Semi-liquid core-and-mass organisms that reproduce by merging and splitting
- [[7.3.1.2 Golems]]: Rigid constructs with a core housed in the head or torso, regenerate from surrounding material
- [[7.3.1.3 Dragons]]: Large winged reptiles infused with elemental energy, flight capable, the most powerful of the elementals
### Animal Monsters
Animal monsters are creatures that are or closely resemble beasts. This category covers both animals and mythical beasts. Most can be harvested for hide, meat, bones, and other natural materials.

- [[7.3.2.0 Common Animals]]: Common real world animals
- [[7.3.2.1 Chimera]]: Hybrid animals hatched from eggs, with unique abilities that unlock as they age
- [[7.3.2.2 Phoenix]]: A flaming bird that evolves through death, growing stronger each time it burns and reforms
- [[7.3.2.3 Unicorn]]: A horse with a single affinity-infused horn, bonds to one rider for life
- [[7.3.2.4 Yeti]]: A giant snow monkey of the high mountains
- [[7.3.2.5 Cerberus]]: A multi-headed dog with infernal affinity, more heads means more power
- [[7.3.2.6 Roc]]: A massive bird large enough to carry off livestock
- [[7.3.2.7 Catoblepas]]: A squat swamp boar whose toxic breath kills vegetation and petrifies flesh
- [[7.3.2.8 Thunderbird]]: An enormous eagle whose wingbeats create storms and whose feathers arc with lightning
- [[7.3.2.9 Warg]]: A giant wolf larger than a horse, intelligent pack hunters that can be broken but never tamed
- [[7.3.2.10 Bulette]]: An armored land shark that swims through earth and rock, erupting from below without warning
- [[7.3.2.11 Grootslang]]: A massive elephant-serpent hybrid with curved tusks, hoards gems in deep cave lairs
### Undead Monsters
Undead monsters are creatures animated after death. Some still have a physical body, others exist as pure spirit. They do not breathe, eat, sleep, or age. Most are tied to the place or circumstances of their death and cannot reproduce naturally, every undead that exists was once something else. They are universally vulnerable to Radiant damage.

- [[7.3.3.1 Banshee]]: The spirit of a person who died in betrayal or despair, bound to haunt the location of their death, kills with a scream that grows stronger with age
- [[7.3.3.2 Gashadokuro]]: A colossal skeleton formed from mass graves, mindlessly aggressive, grows larger and stronger forever
- [[7.3.3.3 Facehugger]]: A parasitic creature that latches onto the face of the dead and puppets the corpse
- [[7.3.3.4 Ghoul]]: A flesh-eating corpse with paralytic claws, never sated, stalks graveyards and battlefields
- [[7.3.3.5 Death Worm]]: A massive segmented worm that burrows through mass graves, swallows prey whole and digests with necrotic acid
### Aquatic Monsters
Aquatic monsters live primarily in water, oceans, lakes, rivers, swamps, and the lightless deep. Some can survive on land for short periods but all are at home in the water. They range from mundane giant predators to ancient creatures that have lived for centuries in the depths.

- [[7.3.4.1 Aboleth]]: A flat-bodied tentacled creature with three eyes, reproduces by budding, evolves into deadlier forms with age
- [[7.3.4.2 Kelpie]]: A water horse that can be ridden, lures victims onto its back and drags them under
- [[7.3.4.3 Kraken]]: A massive squid of legendary strength, large enough to drag ships beneath the waves
- [[7.3.4.4 Megalodon]]: A prehistoric shark of impossible size, pure predator with no magic, just teeth and hunger
- [[7.3.4.5 Aspidochelone]]: A sea turtle so vast it is mistaken for an island, entire ecosystems live on its shell
- [[7.3.4.6 Lusca]]: A half-shark half-octopus that lurks in deep ocean blue holes and drags prey into the abyss
- [[7.3.4.7 Serpent Whale]]: A whale-like beast with a long coiling body that constricts ships, its song causes madness
- [[7.3.4.8 Deepcrawler]]: An armored crustacean the size of a carriage that scavenges the ocean floor
### Monstrosities
Monstrosities are creatures that do not fit cleanly into any other category. They are bizarre, unnatural, and often unique in their design. Some are chimeric in appearance but are not true Chimeras. Others are so alien that their biology defies explanation. If a creature makes you ask "what the heck is that," it probably belongs here.

- [[7.3.5.1 Chupacabra]]: A lizard-like creature with a snapping turtle neck and blood-drinking fangs, walks on two legs with a wolf-like body plan
- [[7.3.5.2 Shoggoth]]: A shapeless mass of eyes and mouths, consumes everything in its path, one of the strongest creatures in existence
- [[7.3.5.3 Camazotz]]: A man-sized bat creature, a flying predator somewhere between beast and nightmare
- [[7.3.5.4 Beholder]]: A floating orb of flesh with a central eye, massive toothy mouth, and multiple eyestalks sprouting from its crown
- [[7.3.5.5 Mimic]]: A shape-shifting ambush predator that disguises itself as furniture, chests, or doors
### Sprites
Sprites are tiny magical creatures, most no larger than a housecat and many much smaller. They are rarely dangerous alone but some gather in swarms that can overwhelm the unprepared. Most are harmless or merely mischievous. A few are actively helpful to those who treat them well. They are found in nearly every environment and are often considered good luck by common folk.

- [[7.3.6.1 Dust Bunny]]: A small fuzzy creature, friendly and mostly harmless, strength in numbers
- [[7.3.6.2 Will-o'-Wisp]]: A floating ball of pale light that leads travelers into bogs and sinkholes, feeds on panic
- [[7.3.6.3 Ember Sprite]]: A tiny flame creature born from wildfire embers, drifts on hot air and gathers in firestorm swarms
- [[7.3.6.4 Glimmer Moth]]: A cat-sized moth with iridescent hypnotic wings, drawn to magic, hallucinogenic wing dust
- [[7.3.6.5 Pebble Toad]]: A living stone toad that curls into a sphere when startled and rolls downhill at high speed
- [[7.3.6.6 Whispercap]]: A rooted mushroom creature that releases sleep-inducing spores when disturbed
- [[7.3.6.7 Frostling]]: A tiny creature of living snow and ice, forms in the first frost, melts in warmth
### Monster Modifiers
Monster Modifiers are not a category of monster but templates that can be applied to any monster from any category. They change a creature's stats, passives, actives, and sometimes its very nature. For example, applying the Zombie modifier to a Warg turns it into an undead Zombie Warg. Applying Ghost to a Kraken creates a spectral Ghost Kraken. Modifiers cannot be stacked, a creature can only have one modifier at a time.

See [[7.3.5 Monster Modifiers]] for the full list of templates and their rules.
### Difficulty Rating (DR)
A monster’s **Difficulty Rating (DR)** is an estimate of how many level 5 players are required to defeat a single instance of that monster.
- **DR 1** indicates a monster that a single level 5 player can reasonably defeat alone.
- **DR 2** indicates a monster that would require approximately two level 5 players.
- **DR 1/5** represents a much weaker creature, closer in strength to what a level 1 player could handle.
- Creatures weaker than this can be treated as **DR 0**.

DR is not exact. It is intended as a quick reference for encounter balancing rather than a strict calculation.
### Monster Levels
Monsters can have levels, which increase their overall power. Some monsters do not have a level due to being too young or other factors. For monsters below level 1 or with no level you take their species stat block and half all of the stats and sizes and so on rounding down, that is their statblock. 

Each level provides the following:
- +1 Hit Die to Vitality
- +1 Hit Die to Health
- +1 Hit Die to Mana
- +1 Generic Damage Mod
- +1 to the monster’s main Affinity
- +1 AC (every 3 levels)
- +1 to all Stats (Str, Con, Dex (every 3 levels (3,6,9,etc) gain +1 to Wis, Int, and Char as well))

> Each time you get a Hit Die you can roll for it or just add the average number to the total. In the handbook the averages are always added together and shown as (#) next to the die. 
> The generic damage mod is applied to all damage the creature deals. Typically the Affinity of the creature is also added to the attacks so each level up adds around 2 damage to each attack. Note that the generic damage added is always the damage type of the affinity of the creature. 
## Example Monster
In the handbook every monster is written at level 1. You must scale them up manually to the level you need using the leveling rules above. Below is a look at a real stat block showing three Slimes side by side: Fire, Magma, and Atomic. Each one is written at level 1, same as they appear in [[7.3.1.1 Slimes]].

> All Slimes share the core-and-mass structure. Their body is a shifting semi-liquid mass surrounding a central core. Reducing a slime to 0 HP does not kill it, the mass spills away and the core must be destroyed separately. While the mass is gone, the slime is unconscious and slowly regenerates. See [[7.3.1.1 Slimes]] for the full rules on slime biology, reproduction, and regeneration.

#### Reading the Stat Block

**DR (Difficulty Rating):** The Fire Slime is DR 1/5, meaning it is trivial, weaker than a single level 5 player. Five of them would be about right for one level 1 player. The Magma Slime at DR 1 is a fair fight for one level 5 player. The Atomic Slime at DR 3 would require about three level 5 players. DR is a rough guideline, not a formula. Party composition and tactics make a far bigger difference than the number suggests.

**AC (Armor Class):** Fire Slime has 12, easy to hit. Magma Slime has 17, mid-tier. Atomic Slime has 21, very hard to land a solid hit on. AC comes from the slime's natural composition. Magma slimes are dense rock and molten material. Atomic slimes are vibrating unstable energy.

**Speed:** All three have 20 ft of movement. Slimes are slow. They squish and roll, they do not run.

**Type (Combat Role):** The combat role tells the GM how to run the slime in combat. Fire Slime is a Berserker, aggressive, constantly attacking, never retreating. Magma is a Tanker, absorbs hits, protects other monsters, slow but hard to bring down. Atomic is also a Berserker but with explosive abilities and an unstable nature.

**Affinity:** This is the slime's elemental alignment. It is applied to all damage it deals and determines its resistance and weakness. Save DCs use the Save DC Bonus table in [[2.1 Affinities#Affinity Bonus Tables]] ,  a monster's ability DC is its listed base (typically 10 or 15) plus its [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]. Fire Slime has Fire (+0). At level 1 its fire affinity is too weak to add bonus damage, but it still determines its resistance and weakness. Magma Slime has Magma (+3), at level 1 it already adds +3 to all damage and save DCs. Atomic Slime has Atomic (+6) plus a Gen Dmg Mod of +3, so at level 1 it adds a total of +9 damage to every attack.

**Resistance and Weakness:** A slime is always Resistant to its own affinity. The Fire Slime resists Fire damage. Weakness is determined by the opposing element. Fire is weak to Water. Magma, being a composite of Fire and Earth, is weak to Ice/Cold, Water, and Air. Atomic is weak only to Infernal. When a slime takes damage matching its Affinity, its Affinity Body passive heals it. When it takes damage matching its Weakness, all damage bypasses Resistance and deals full.

**Vitality, Health, Mana:** These use Hit Dice determined by the slime's power tier, not by d10/d8/d12 convention. Fire Slime has 10d6 Vitality (average 35). Magma has 26d6 (91). Atomic has 55d6 (192). The dice type is always d6 for slimes. Other monster types use different dice. [[1.3 Player Stats#Health-and-Vitality|Vitality]] is the pool that absorbs most damage. Health is the smaller pool beneath, when Vitality hits 0, damage goes to Health. When [[1.3 Player Stats#Health-and-Vitality|Health]] hits 0, the slime's mass spills and the core is exposed.

**Stats:** STR and CON are the most important stats for monsters. CON above 10 adds bonus Vitality equal to the modifier. Fire Slime has 14 CON, that is +2 Vitality per Hit Die, already factored into the average. Mental stats (WIS/INT/CHA) are typically low for monsters. Most slimes have 3 at level 1.

**Passives:** Every slime has three universal passives: Slime Regeneration (core survives body destruction), Affinity Body (heals when hit by own element), and Scratch Resistance (reduces slashing/piercing damage). Higher-tier slimes gain additional passives. Magma Slime adds Thorns, Hardened Body, and Damage Threshold. Atomic Slime's Unstable Core passive means it takes a small amount of true damage at the start of each turn if it has no Mana, it is breaking apart.

**Actives:** Slam is a basic melee strike all slimes can use. Spit is a short-range projectile. Higher slimes gain more options. Atomic Slime has Kamikaze, it can detonate itself for massive area damage, taking self-damage in the process. Combined with Affinity Body, if the Atomic Slime's self-damage matches its Affinity it will heal some of it back, letting it chain explosions as long as it has Mana.

### Level 5 Walkthrough (Fire Slime)
Going from level 1 to level 5 means 4 level-ups:

- **Vitality:** 10d6 + 4d6 = 14d6 (avg 49, was 35)
- **Health:** 3d6 + 4d6 = 7d6 (avg 24, was 10)
- **Mana:** 6d6 + 4d6 = 10d6 (avg 35, was 21)
- **Gen Dmg Mod:** +0 → +4
- **Affinity:** Fire (+0) → Fire (+4). Total damage bonus per attack: Gen Dmg Mod + Attack Damage Bonus = +5 ([[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] for +4 Fire Affinity is +1 from [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus]]).
- **AC:** 12 + 1 (at level 3) = 13. The next AC bump comes at level 6.
- **Stats:** STR 14→18, CON 14→18, DEX 8→12. WIS/INT/CHA gain +1 at level 3: 3→4 each.
- **Weight:** (300 + 400) × 0.8 = 560 lbs. Size: (3 + 4) × 1.0 = 7 ft.

The Magma and Atomic slimes follow the exact same per-level math just from higher base stats. Passives and actives do not change with level unless the monster's page says otherwise.
