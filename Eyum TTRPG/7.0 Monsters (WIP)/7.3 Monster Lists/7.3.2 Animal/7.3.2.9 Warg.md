A Warg is a large predatory mammal with a body plan that combines the mass of a wolf with the long serpentine build of a ferret. The torso is elongated and flexible, carried low to the ground on four thick legs that end in broad paws with heavy non-retractable claws. The neck is long and muscular, and the tail is thick and nearly as long as the body, used for balance during high-speed turns. The head is broad and flat with a short smushed muzzle like a pug or a mole, the nose recessed into the face and the lower jaw jutting forward with an underbite that leaves the bottom teeth exposed even when the mouth is closed. The eyes are small and set deep under a heavy brow, pale yellow or amber with slit pupils. The ears are small and rounded, set far back on the skull. The fur is coarse and dense, ranging from dark grey to near black across most variants, with lighter grey or white on the chest and underside. A Warg at full size stands 6 to 7 ft at the shoulder and weighs between 1,200 and 2,000 lbs depending on variant.

Wargs are exceptionally unintelligent. Their behavior is driven entirely by instinct and aggression with no capacity for planning, strategy, or self-preservation. They will continue attacking after they are full, killing everything within reach until nothing moves. They do not retreat. The concept of fleeing does not appear to exist in their psychology. A Warg that is outnumbered, on fire, or badly injured will keep attacking until it dies. This is not courage. It is a near-total absence of survival instinct that makes them effective in combat but useless in any situation requiring restraint or judgment.

Wargs can be captured and trained as mounts. The process is not taming in the traditional sense. It is domination through sustained physical force. A Warg will test its rider constantly: snapping at them, refusing commands, and periodically attempting to throw them and attack. Each time, the rider must physically overpower the animal, pin it, beat it into submission, then immediately offer food. This establishes the only relationship a Warg understands: the rider is stronger, therefore the Warg follows. The moment the rider shows weakness, injury, or hesitation, the Warg turns on them. Successful Warg handlers are not skilled trainers. They are people who have never lost the fight.

Fenrir Wargs are the exception. Bred over generations for temperament rather than raw power, a Fenrir bonds to its owner with genuine loyalty. It is still aggressive and fearless, but its hostility is directed outward at threats rather than inward at its handler. A Fenrir will attempt to protect its owner without being commanded. It is the only Warg variant suitable for anyone other than a dedicated professional handler.

Wargs are pack animals in the wild but the pack structure is not cooperative. It is a hierarchy maintained through constant violence. The strongest Warg leads not because the others respect it but because it has beaten every challenger into submission. Challenges happen constantly: over food, over mates, over the best sleeping spot, over nothing at all. A Warg pack in the wild is a rolling brawl punctuated by brief periods of hunting and eating. Dominance is settled through brutal fights that leave both participants scarred and bleeding, and the loser simply waits a few days and tries again. This dynamic transfers directly to captive Wargs and is the reason they are so difficult to control. They do not understand cooperation. They understand force.

Wargs breed once per year. A female carries a litter of 3 to 6 pups for four months and gives birth in a den dug into frozen earth or packed snow. The pups are born blind and helpless but grow rapidly, reaching the size of a full-grown wolf by six months and gaining the strength to carry a rider by one year. A Warg reaches level 1 after its first year and gains 1 level per year thereafter. They live approximately 20 years in the wild, though captive Wargs used as war mounts rarely survive past 10 due to accumulated injuries and the inevitable day their rider loses the dominance fight.

When a Warg is killed there is little worth taking. The hide is coarse, rough, and covered in scar tissue from a lifetime of fighting. It makes terrible leather: stiff, unpleasant to the touch, and prone to cracking. The meat is dark and stringy with a pungent gamey odor and a slight numbing effect on the tongue from trace compounds in the muscle tissue. Eating it requires a CON save (DC 8) or the consumer gains the [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Poisoned]] condition for 30 minutes. It is edible in the strictest sense but nobody seeks it out. Wargs have no valuable unique materials. Their entire worth is as living war mounts. Dead Wargs are nearly worthless.
###### Variant Descriptions

**Fenrir Warg (DR 1):** The smallest variant, standing approximately 5 ft at the shoulder and weighing around 800 lbs at level 1. The coat is a lighter grey with more white on the chest and underside than other variants. The build is slightly leaner and the head proportionally smaller. Fenrir Wargs have been selectively bred over centuries for temperament, resulting in a creature that is still fearless and aggressive but directs that aggression at threats rather than its handler. A Fenrir bonds to its owner with genuine loyalty and will attempt to protect them from harm, interposing itself between its master and danger without being commanded. It is the only Warg variant suitable for anyone who is not a dedicated professional handler. Fenrir Wargs are common in the northern reaches as guard animals and personal mounts for hunters and trappers who need something that can survive the cold and fight off the things that live in it. They are rideable but not as fast or as strong as the larger variants.

**Garmr Warg (DR 3):** The standard Warg and the variant most people mean when they use the term. Stands 6 ft at the shoulder and weighs approximately 1,200 lbs at level 1. The coat is dark grey to black with minimal lighter markings. The build is heavier and more muscular than the Fenrir, with a broader chest and a thicker neck. Garmr Wargs are the baseline for Warg aggression. They are ruthless, fearless, and brutal in combat. They will fight anything. A Garmr war pack is the standard cavalry unit for northern warlords and mercenary companies who value shock and terror over precision. They can be broken and ridden by experienced handlers but require constant dominance maintenance. A Garmr will challenge a rider who shows any sign of weakness and the challenge is always to the death.

**Hati Warg (DR 5):** Stands 6.5 ft at the shoulder and weighs approximately 1,600 lbs. The coat is solid black. The eyes are pale amber. The jaws are proportionally larger than other variants. Hati Wargs are more aggressive than Garmrs. In combat they enter a frenzy state, attacking everything within reach including allies if no enemies remain. Hati handlers are rare. A Hati Warg is difficult to control and will kill indiscriminately once engaged.

**Skoll Warg (DR 7):** The largest variant. Stands 7 ft at the shoulder and weighs approximately 2,000 lbs. The coat is solid black. The build is heavier than other variants across all dimensions. Skoll Wargs are extremely difficult to control. The success rate for breaking one is below ten percent, with failure resulting in death. A broken Skoll Warg is the most aggressive land mount available, faster and more durable than any comparable creature. It will not retreat or stop fighting until either it or everything else on the battlefield is dead.
###### Unique Abilities
All Wargs share the following unique abilities regardless of variant.

- **Bloodlust (Passive):** The Warg gains advantage on all attack rolls against creatures that are below half Vitality. If the Warg reduces a creature to 0 HP, it may immediately make a free Bite attack against another creature within reach. This can trigger once per turn.
- **Fearless (Passive):** The Warg cannot be [[2.4 Conditions#Mental-Conditions|Frightened]] by any effect. It automatically succeeds on all saves against intimidation, fear, and morale-based abilities. When the Warg would be forced to retreat or flee by any magical or nonmagical effect, it instead takes 2d6 psychic damage and ignores the compulsion.
- **Dominance Challenge (Passive):** At the start of combat, or whenever the Warg takes more than half its remaining Vitality in a single round, the Warg's rider or handler must make a STR save (DC 10 + the Warg's STR mod). On a failure, the Warg breaks control for 1d4 turns and attacks the nearest creature indiscriminately, prioritizing the rider if they are within reach. This does not apply to a Fenrir Warg bonded to its handler. A rider who has successfully dominated their Warg in the past 24 hours (by winning a contested STR check outside of combat) has advantage on this save.
- **Pack Hunter (Passive):** When the Warg is within 30 ft of at least one ally, it gains +1 to attack rolls and +1 AC. This bonus does not stack beyond +2.

Variant-specific abilities are listed below.

- **Loyal Guardian (Passive):** Fenrir Warg only. The Fenrir is immune to its own Dominance Challenge. When its bonded handler drops below half Vitality, the Fenrir gains advantage on all attack rolls and +10 ft speed until the handler is healed above half or the combat ends. The Fenrir will attempt to interpose itself between the handler and threats without being commanded.
- **Frenzied Assault (Active, 12 Mana):** Hati Warg only. The Hati enters a killing frenzy for 3 turns. During the frenzy, it makes one additional Bite attack per turn (no action cost) and cannot be [[2.4 Conditions#Mental-Conditions|Charmed]], put to sleep, or incapacitated. The Hati must attack the nearest creature each turn regardless of whether it is friend or foe. If no creatures are within reach it must use its movement to reach the nearest creature before making its attacks.
- **Juggernaut (Passive):** Skoll Warg only. The Skoll's Bite attack ignores Lesser Resistance and treats physical armor (AC from armor and shields) as halved. When the Skoll charges at least 20 ft in a straight line before making a melee attack, the attack deals an additional 2d8 damage. The Skoll's Dominance Challenge DC is 15 instead of 10 + STR mod.

**Standard Passives:** Pursuer, Scratch Resistance (thick scarred hide), Blood Fueled (Hati and Skoll only)
**Standard Actives:** Bite, Charge, Multi Attack
