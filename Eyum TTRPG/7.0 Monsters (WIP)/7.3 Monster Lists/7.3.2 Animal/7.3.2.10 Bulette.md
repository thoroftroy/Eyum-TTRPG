A Bulette is a large armored quadruped that moves through earth and solid rock by burrowing. The body is thick and torpedo-shaped, broadest at the shoulders and tapering toward the tail, with a wedge-shaped head and a mouth that opens wide enough to swallow a person whole. The entire dorsal surface is covered in overlapping plates of dense scale that form a natural armor comparable to forged steel. The plates are dark brown to grey on the standard variant and shift in color and texture depending on the Bulette's affinity. The underside is softer and lighter in color, protected only when the creature is burrowing or curled defensively. The legs are short and heavily muscled, ending in four-toed feet with massive digging claws that can tear through stone. The tail is thick and used as a rudder during burrowing. A Bulette has no external ears and its eyes are small and recessed behind protective ridges. It senses prey through vibrations in the ground. At level 1 a Bulette is approximately 12 ft long, 6 ft wide, and weighs around 3,000 lbs.

Bulettes are ambush predators. They spend the majority of their time underground, moving through earth and rock in a state of near-total silence despite their size. The digging claws loosen material ahead of the body and the armored plates push it aside, creating a tunnel that collapses behind the creature as it moves. The only surface indication of a Bulette's presence is a low seismic tremor that can be felt for several hundred feet in every direction. When it detects prey through ground vibrations, it accelerates directly upward and erupts from below with enough force to send creatures and debris flying. The initial strike is the Bulette's entire strategy. It surfaces, grabs whatever it can, and drags it underground to be eaten in the dark. If the ambush fails it will fight on the surface but it is noticeably less effective there, relying on its armor to absorb hits while it tries to grab something and retreat.

Bulettes are solitary and highly territorial. They do not tolerate other Bulettes in their hunting range and will fight intruders to the death. The exception is the brief mating period once every few years, after which the female lays a clutch of 4 to 8 eggs in a deep underground chamber and abandons them. The eggs are leathery and roughly the size of a human head. They incubate for six months in the ambient heat of the earth. Hatchlings are fully formed and immediately begin burrowing, feeding on roots, small animals, and each other. Only one or two from each clutch survive to the surface. A Bulette reaches level 1 after its first year and gains 1 level per year thereafter. They can live for up to 40 years.

When killed, a Bulette is a valuable harvest. The meat is pale and lean with a texture and flavor similar to rabbit but less gamy. It is considered good eating in regions where Bulettes are common. The claws are dense and heavily mineralized, usable as digging tools or shaped into tool heads without requiring metal. The scales are the primary prize. Each plate is thick, layered, and naturally resistant to damage matching the Bulette's affinity. When crafted into armor the scales retain this property and additionally grant improved stealth by dampening sound, a residual effect of the Bulette's silent burrowing nature. The scales are also receptive to enchantment.
###### Variant Descriptions

**Bulette (DR 5):** The standard variant and the most common. The scales are thick and heavily layered, dark brown to grey with a rough texture similar to coarse stone. Individual plates overlap like roof shingles and are thickest along the spine, tapering toward the edges. A Bulette's scales account for roughly 15% of its total body weight. Found in temperate hills, rocky plains, mountain foothills, and anywhere the bedrock is solid enough to support their burrowing. Earth affinity. They prefer hard packed soil and sedimentary rock, avoiding loose gravel or sand where their tunnels would not hold. They are ambush hunters, lying motionless underground and waiting for vibrations that indicate prey above. The scales of this variant are the heaviest and most commonly available, making them the standard for Bulette scale armor.

**Dust Bulette (DR 6):** A variant adapted to arid environments. The scales are pale tan to beige, smoother and lighter than the standard variant, with a fine gritty texture of compacted sand fused into the plate surface. The coloration matches desert stone and sand, providing natural camouflage when the creature surfaces. The scales are less dense than the standard variant, providing slightly less protection but weighing significantly less. Found in deep deserts, badlands, and dry scrubland. Dust affinity. The Dust Bulette is the fastest burrower, moving through loose sand at speeds exceeding its surface movement. It hunts by sensing vibrations through the sand and can strike from below before prey even realizes the ground is shifting. The heat-shedding properties of the scales allow it to remain active during the hottest parts of the day when other predators are dormant. The scales make armor that is lighter and quieter than other Bulette variants.

**Mud Bulette (DR 6):** A swamp-adapted variant. The scales are dark brown to black and permanently coated in a thick slick layer of mud that the creature secretes from glands between the plates. The scales themselves are slightly softer and more flexible than other variants, but the mud coating provides additional protection by absorbing impacts and making the creature nearly impossible to grip. Found in swamps, marshes, river deltas, and coastal wetlands. Mud affinity. The Mud Bulette is the heaviest of the standard-sized variants due to the constant saturation of its body. It burrows through waterlogged soil and silt rather than solid rock, and its tunnels fill with water and collapse instantly behind it, leaving no evidence of its passage. The mud coating muffles its seismic signature, making it harder to detect through ground vibrations than other variants. The scales require cleaning and treatment before they can be used for armor, but the resulting material retains useful sound-dampening properties.

**Magma Bulette (DR 7):** A volcanic variant found near active geothermal regions. The scales are black with a glossy finish, shot through with a network of faint orange cracks that pulse with internal heat. The plates radiate consistent warmth and can ignite flammable material on contact. The scales are the heaviest and most protective of any variant. Found near active volcanoes, lava tubes, geothermal vents, and in the deep earth where temperatures exceed what most creatures can survive. Magma affinity. The Magma Bulette burrows through cooling lava and volcanic rock, its body heat softening stone ahead of it. It surfaces through solid basalt and obsidian as easily as other variants move through soil. The heat it generates makes it easy to track on the surface but nearly impossible to fight without fire-resistant equipment. The scales are valued for their protective qualities and the natural heat they provide, making armor that keeps the wearer warm in any climate in addition to its defensive properties.

**Tremor Bulette (DR 8):** The rarest variant. The scales are dark grey with an iridescent purple sheen visible when light strikes them at an angle. The surface of each plate vibrates almost imperceptibly, a residual effect of the creature's internal seismic organ. This organ, located in the creature's skull, generates concentrated seismic pulses that travel through the ground ahead of the Bulette. Prey on the surface experiences vertigo, confusion, and a sense of crushing pressure before the Bulette erupts beneath them. Found in regions with natural seismic activity, deep fault lines, and tectonic plate boundaries. Tremor affinity. The Tremor Bulette is the most methodical hunter of the variants. It does not rely on the initial strike alone. It surfaces, stuns prey with a second pulse, and then kills at its leisure. The scales are the rarest and most expensive, providing both physical protection and a residual vibration that can unsettle enemies. The purple iridescence is purely cosmetic but highly valued by collectors.
###### Unique Abilities
All Bulettes share the following unique abilities regardless of variant.

- **Burrow (Passive):** The Bulette has a burrow speed equal to its land speed and can move through earth, soil, sand, and solid rock without penalty. It leaves a tunnel behind it that collapses at the start of its next turn. While burrowing, the Bulette cannot be targeted by attacks or effects that require line of sight. The Bulette can sense the location of any creature in contact with the ground within 200 ft through seismic vibrations, ignoring darkness, invisibility, and visual obstructions.
- **Erupting Strike (Active, 12 Mana):** The Bulette bursts upward from below a creature within its burrow speed that is standing on earth or stone. The target must make a DEX save (DC 10 + STR mod). On a failure, it takes 2d10 + STR mod bludgeoning damage, is knocked [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental-Positional-Conditions|Prone]], and the Bulette surfaces in an adjacent space and may make a free Bite attack. On a success, the target takes half damage, is pushed 10 ft to the side, and the Bulette surfaces in the vacated space. All other creatures within 10 ft of the eruption point must make a DEX save or take 1d10 bludgeoning damage and be knocked Prone. This ability can only be used while the Bulette is burrowing.
- **Armored Hide (Passive):** The Bulette has Resistance to physical damage (bludgeoning, piercing, slashing) from nonmagical sources. Its AC cannot be reduced below its base value by any effect. When the Bulette takes the Defend action, it curls into a defensive posture and gains an additional +4 AC until the start of its next turn.

Variant-specific abilities are listed below.

- **Sandstream (Active, 6 Mana):** Dust Bulette only. While burrowing through sand, the Dust Bulette can create a vortex of abrasive sand in a 20 ft radius on the surface above its position. Creatures in the area must make a CON save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 1d8 Dust damage and be [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Blinded]] for 1 turn. The area becomes difficult terrain for non-flying creatures for 2 rounds.
- **Mire Pit (Active, 12 Mana):** Mud Bulette only. The Mud Bulette churns the ground in a 20 ft radius centered on a point within 60 ft, turning it into deep sucking mud. Creatures in the area must make a STR save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or become [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental-Positional-Conditions|Restrained]]. They may repeat the save at the start of each turn. The area remains mud for the duration of combat.
- **Magma Vent (Active, 12 Mana):** Magma Bulette only. The Magma Bulette creates a fissure that vents magma in a 30 ft line. Creatures in the line must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 2d8 Magma damage. The line remains as a hazard for 3 rounds; any creature that enters or starts its turn in the line takes 1d8 Magma damage.
- **Seismic Pulse (Active, 16 Mana):** Tremor Bulette only. The Tremor Bulette releases a concentrated seismic wave in a 40 ft radius while burrowing. All creatures in the area must make a CON save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]). On a failure, they take 2d6 Tremor damage, are [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Stunned]] for 1 turn, and have their AC reduced by 2 for 2 turns. On a success, they take half damage and are not Stunned. This ability is used before the Tremor Bulette surfaces to soften prey.

**Standard Passives:** Hardened Body, Damage Threshold, Stable Core
**Standard Actives:** Bite, Slam, Charge
