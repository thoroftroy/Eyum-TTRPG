A Thunderbird is an enormous avian predator with a wingspan that can exceed 200 ft in adults. The body is built like an eagle scaled to extreme proportions, broad chested and heavily muscled for sustained flight. The feathers are bright yellow with a slight metallic sheen on juveniles, darkening progressively with age from the wingtips inward toward the body. A Baby Thunderbird is vivid yellow across its entire body. A Teenage Thunderbird has grey tips on the primary flight feathers that extend roughly a third of the way up each wing. An adult Thunderbird is steel grey across most of the wings and tail with yellow remaining only on the head, chest, and upper back. An Elder Thunderbird is charcoal grey across nearly its entire body with only faint yellow patches on the crown and throat. At all stages visible arcs of electricity crawl continuously across the wing edges and tail tips. The beak is a curved black hook, the talons are black and smooth, and the eyes are pale electric blue with no visible pupil.

The Thunderbird generates a powerful electromagnetic field through specialized organs embedded in its flight muscles and along the leading edge of its wings. This field ionizes the surrounding atmosphere and causes continuous electrical discharge across the bird's surface. The field extends outward for approximately 1 mile and directly influences local weather patterns, producing heavy precipitation, high winds, and frequent lightning strikes. The intensity scales with the bird's age and stage. The field is not consciously controlled at lower stages. Adult and Elder Thunderbirds can suppress or intensify the effect at will up to once per day.

Thunderbirds are solitary and extremely rare, with an estimated global population under two dozen individuals at any time. They nest on isolated mountain peaks above the altitude at which most creatures can breathe, favoring regions with persistent natural storm activity. An adult claims a territory spanning thousands of square miles and patrols it by riding high-altitude wind currents. Thunderbirds communicate over continental distances using patterned lightning strikes, though the system is not understood. They descend from altitude only to feed or to drive intruders from their territory.

Thunderbirds are intelligent, comparable to elder dragons, but their psychology is poorly understood. They do not appear to perceive individual humanoids as significant and do not interact with them unless provoked. When threatened, a Thunderbird attacks by diving from above cloud cover at speeds exceeding 300 miles per hour, striking with its talons and immediately returning to altitude.

Thunderbirds reproduce rarely. A mated pair nests on a flat-topped mountain peak and the female lays a single egg approximately the size of a carriage. The egg crackles with static discharge throughout the incubation period. Both parents incubate for one year. The chick hatches at level 1, approximately 2 ft long with a 5 ft wingspan and weighing around 10 lbs, covered in bright yellow down that crackles when touched. Both parents feed the chick for the first 5 years, during which time the nest area experiences continuous severe weather. At approximately 5 years of age the chick undergoes its first molt, grows flight feathers, and is driven from the nest. This marks evolution into the Teenage stage.

A Thunderbird gains 1 level per year and evolves through four distinct stages determined by age and accumulated power. A hatchling is a Baby Thunderbird (Lightning affinity) until level 5, at which point it evolves into a Teenage Thunderbird (Lightning affinity). At level 15 the Teenage evolves into an adult Thunderbird (Thunder affinity). At level 35 the adult evolves into an Elder Thunderbird (Storm affinity). There is no known maximum lifespan.

When a Thunderbird dies, its electromagnetic field collapses inward and the body is consumed by electrical discharge. The only materials that survive are the feathers and a quantity of meat shielded from the discharge by the bird's own body. Thunderbird Feathers retain the electrical properties of the living bird and can be used to augment spellcasting for Lightning, Thunder, or Storm affinities depending on the source bird's stage. When incorporated into light armor or clothing, the feathers grant resistance to Water and Lightning damage and increase the material's durability. Thunderbird Meat is a luxury food with a clean, slightly metallic flavor and a numbing tingle, served raw and considered a delicacy.
###### Unique Abilities
All Thunderbirds share the following unique abilities.

- **Storm Herald (Passive):** The Thunderbird generates a storm within 1 mile of its position. The intensity scales with stage: a Baby produces light rain and occasional thunder, a Teenage produces heavy rain, wind, and frequent lightning, an Adult produces a full thunderstorm with gale-force winds and hail, and an Elder produces a cataclysmic storm with tornadoes and continuous lightning. The Adult and Elder can suppress or intensify this effect at will as a free action once per day. Baby and Teenage stages cannot control the effect.
- **Eye of the Storm (Passive):** The Thunderbird is immune to Lightning, Thunder, and Storm damage. It cannot be Blinded, Deafened, or knocked Prone. It ignores all environmental penalties from weather, including magical weather, and can see through fog, rain, and clouds without impairment.
- **Static Field (Passive):** Any creature that hits the Thunderbird with a melee attack takes 1d6 Lightning damage (2d6 Storm for an Elder). Any creature that starts its turn within 30 ft of an Adult or Elder Thunderbird must make a CON save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 1d4 Lightning damage (2d4 Storm for an Elder).
- **Spellcaster (Passive):** The Thunderbird can cast spells from the Lightning, Thunder, or Storm affinity lists depending on its current stage, using its Mana pool as the casting resource. The Thunderbird adds its Affinity and Gen Dmg Mod to all spell damage. Save DCs use 10 + Affinity. The bird may cast using AP or BAp as normal for a player character.

**Baby Thunderbird Spells (Lightning):** Static Shock, Paralytic Bolt
**Teenage Thunderbird Spells (Lightning):** Chain Lightning, Lightning Surge, Lightning Collapse
**Adult Thunderbird Spells (Thunder):** Thunderclap, Howling Gale, Resonant Shot, Breaking Note, Rolling Choir, Ruin Chime
**Elder Thunderbird Spells (Storm):** Tempest Strike, Storm Call, Thunderstorm, Tempest, Hurricane Aura, Maelstrom

- **Death Discharge (Passive):** When the Thunderbird reaches 0 HP, its electromagnetic field collapses. All creatures within 100 ft must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 10d6 Lightning damage (10d6 Storm for an Elder). On success, they take half. The body is consumed in the discharge. Only feathers and meat remain.

**Standard Passives:** Affinity Body, Energy Overflow, Fast Action
**Standard Actives:** Talon (deals damage matching the bird's current affinity), Wing Buffet
