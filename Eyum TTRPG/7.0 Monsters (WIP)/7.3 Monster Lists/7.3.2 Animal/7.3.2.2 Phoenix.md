A phoenix is a large bird covered in feathers that shift between solid orange and gold plumage and open flame depending on the bird's mood. At rest the feathers lie flat and look like normal bright feathers with a heat shimmer rising off them. When the phoenix is angry, frightened, or fighting, the same feathers ignite into jets of fire that do not burn the bird but will set fire to anything else they touch. The flames are hottest at the feather tips and cool toward the base where they attach to the skin. 

The body is built like a heavy bird of prey. Two legs end in four talons each, one pointing backward for gripping prey and branches. The talons are always hot enough to scorch wood and leave glowing marks on stone. The beak is hooked and sharp, colored dark grey with an orange glow along the cutting edge. The eyes are bright yellow with no visible pupil, glowing softly in dim conditions. The wings are broad and long, built for soaring more than quick turns. Each feather has a stiff central spine that keeps its shape even when fully on fire. The tail feathers are extra long and trail behind the bird like burning streamers. The whole body gives off a constant dry heat that can be felt from several feet away.

Phoenixes are social birds that live in large flocks called courts. A single court can contain anywhere from twenty to over a hundred birds of all ages and types. They nest together in colonies built into the sides of active volcanoes, along geothermal vent fields, in dense tropical jungles near hot springs, and on sunbaked cliff faces in hot coastal regions. The specific environment determines what type of phoenix the adult birds become. A court builds its nests on ledges and in hollows, layering them with packed ash, dried tropical leaves, and shed feathers from the whole flock. The nests are reused generation after generation, growing into large communal structures over time. Eggs are laid in a central brooding chamber where the temperature stays between 100 and 150 degrees Fahrenheit from the body heat of the birds tending them. Incubation takes 4 months. A hatchling grows about 1 ft in length and gains 5 lbs per month during its first year, reaching level 1 after roughly 12 months. The entire court works together to raise the young. Chicks that hatch together form a single creche and every adult in the court takes turns bringing them food and watching for predators. There is no concept of individual parentage. All adults treat all chicks as the court's collective responsibility. This social structure is what allows phoenixes to survive in harsh environments where a single bird raising young alone would fail.

Phoenixes are notably intelligent for animals, roughly on par with a clever dog or a young child. They are curious about everything and have a strong mischievous streak. A phoenix will steal bright objects, hide them in its nest, and then watch with obvious amusement as the owner searches for them. Despite this they are deeply loyal. A phoenix that bonds with a person will follow them everywhere, perch on their shoulder, and refuse to leave their side even when shooed away. They are reckless in a way that suggests they know they can come back from death. A phoenix will dive into dangerous situations without hesitation, fight creatures far larger than itself, and throw itself into harm's way to protect its bonded companion. This combination of loyalty, bravery, mischief, and affection makes them highly sought after as pets and companions. The only reason they are not more common is that they are difficult to acquire and expensive to feed. A well bonded phoenix will never willingly leave its person and will fly across the continent to find them if separated.

The bond between a phoenix and its person forms at hatching. A phoenix that hatches from an egg that was incubated and raised by a single individual will imprint on that person and treat them as part of its court for life. Phoenixes raised in a natural court can still be befriended but the process is much harder and requires earning the trust of the entire court, not just one bird. However once a court accepts an outsider, all the birds in it will treat that person as one of their own.

A phoenix gains 1 level per year of life. Each level increases the bird's size, power, and abilities as shown in the growth table. Beyond normal leveling, a phoenix can evolve into a more advanced form when it dies. Each time a phoenix would be reduced to 0 HP, it must make a CON save against the DC for its current type. On a success, the body burns away in a flash and the phoenix reforms at the start of its next turn at full Vitality and Health in its next evolutionary stage. On a failure, the phoenix dies permanently and its body crumbles into ash. A phoenix must have reached a minimum level in its current form before it can attempt to evolve. If a Phoenix dies before reaching that minimum they simply stay their same form on revival. The save DC gets higher for more advanced forms, making each evolution harder to achieve than the last. A court with even one or two Elders is considered ancient and powerful.

The evolution path and minimum level requirements are as follows. A hatchling starts as an Ash Phoenix. An Ash Phoenix cannot evolve until level 3. Once it reaches level 3, it may attempt to evolve into a Blaze Phoenix on its next death by making the CON save. A Blaze Phoenix must reach level 10 before it can evolve into a true Phoenix. Phoenixes cannot breed until they reach at least the stage of Phoenix. Blaze Phoenixes and Ash Phoenixes are too young to breed. A Phoenix must also reach level 10 before it can evolve into a branch form determined by the court's environment. Phoenixes in volcanic regions become Magma Phoenixes. Those in corrupted or hellish environments become Infernal Phoenixes. Those in tropical high altitude or sunbaked regions become Solar Phoenixes. A branch form must reach level 30 before it can evolve into its Elder variant. An Elder Phoenix is the final stage for that branch. It cannot evolve further, but it can continue leveling indefinitely with no maximum level. When an Elder Phoenix reaches level 50 or higher and feels its fire beginning to dim, it may choose to accept its end instead of rolling a death save. Its body shimmers into golden light and disappears. This ascension leaves behind a single Phoenix Tear.

When a phoenix dies at any stage, whether it succeeds or fails its death save, it drops 1d4 Phoenix Feathers at the location where it fell. These feathers glow faintly and stay warm indefinitely. They are used by crafters to augment fire magic items, boost fire affinity in enchanting, and create heat resistant gear. The feathers are the only thing a phoenix leaves behind when it dies. The body is consumed entirely by the flames of its own death, leaving no meat, bones, claws, or hide. A Phoenix Tear is only produced when an Elder Phoenix ascends voluntarily, never from a normal death.

A Phoenix Egg that has been separated from its court can still be hatched artificially, though the process is difficult. The egg must be kept between 100 and 150 degrees Fahrenheit for the full 4 month incubation period. More importantly it must be fed a steady supply of fire mana every day. The person incubating the egg must channel at least 5 mana per day into the egg using a Fire, Ash, Magma, Solar, or Infernal affinity (Must have greater than 10 in the respective affinity, channeling the mana costs an action). If a day is missed the incubation timer goes back by one week. After 4 months of consistent feeding the egg will hatch into an Ash Phoenix that imprints on the first creature it sees and becomes a loyal familiar to that person. A Phoenix will eat 4 rations a day while in its growth stages then 3 a day for the rest of their lives. If not fed properly for a day they gain a level of exhaustion per day, death from exhaustion will result in the phoenix automatically failing its revival save.

**Evolution CON save DCs by type**: Ash Phoenix DC 10, Blaze Phoenix DC 12, Phoenix DC 15, Magma/Infernal/Solar Phoenix DC 28, Elder Phoenix DC 20.
###### Unique Abilities
The following abilities are unique to phoenixes. Branch types and Elders unlock additional abilities as shown in their stat block.

- **Cycle of Flame (Passive):** When this phoenix would be reduced to 0 HP, it makes a CON save against the DC for its type. On a success, the body burns away in a flash. All creatures within 10 + (Fire Affinity / 5) ft must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d6 + Attack Damage Bonus fire damage. The phoenix then reforms at the start of its next turn at full Vitality and Health in its next evolutionary stage (or the same stage if already an Elder or does not meet its evolution requirement). On a failure, the phoenix dies permanently. Whether the save succeeds or fails, the phoenix drops 1d4 Phoenix Feathers where it fell. 
- **Phoenix Fire (Passive):** The phoenix's fire damage ignores Lesser Resistance. Creatures with full Resistance treat it as Lesser Resistance instead. Creatures with Immunity treat it as Resistance instead.
- **Solar Flare (Active, 12 Mana):** 20 ft radius burst centered on the phoenix. All creatures in the area must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 2d8 + Attack Damage Bonus fire damage and be Blinded for 1 turn. Creatures that succeed take half damage and are not Blinded.
- **Wings of the Sun (Active, 7 Mana):** The phoenix beats its wings, sending out a wave of fire in a 15 ft cone. Creatures in the cone take 1d8 + Attack Damage Bonus fire damage and must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or be pushed 10 ft away and knocked Prone.
- **Final Ascension (Passive):** When an Elder Phoenix reaches level 50 or higher and would normally need to roll a death save, it may choose to accept its end instead. Its body shimmers into golden light and disappears, leaving behind a single Phoenix Tear. This is a peaceful death and does not drop Phoenix Feathers.
- **Searing Talons (Passive):** The phoenix's melee attacks deal an additional 1d4 fire damage.
