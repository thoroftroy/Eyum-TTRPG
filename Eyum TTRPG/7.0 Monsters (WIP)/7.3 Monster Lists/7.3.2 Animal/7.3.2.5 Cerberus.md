A Cerberus is a multi-headed dog with a heavy muscular build, a short dense coat, and an infernal heritage that runs hot under its skin. The body is that of a large working dog, broad chested with thick legs and a tail that hangs low with a slight upward curl at the tip. The defining feature is the multiple heads. Each head is fully formed and functional with its own jaw, eyes, ears, and brain. The heads share a single throat past the neck joint, which allows them to eat with any mouth but bark or breathe through only one at a time. On a young or low-tier Cerberus the heads are identical copies of each other, the same eye color, the same ear shape, the same facial markings. As a Cerberus ages and gains power the heads begin to differentiate. One might develop a wider muzzle. Another's ears might stand more upright. On the oldest and most powerful specimens each head has its own distinct personality and expression, though all act in perfect coordination toward the same goal.

The coat is short and dense like a boxer or a hunting hound, smooth to the touch and naturally warm at all times. The color can be almost anything: solid black, deep red, dusty brindle, slate grey, bone white, or patterned with patches and stripes. No two Cerberus have the exact same markings. In bright light a faint heat shimmer rises off the coat, especially along the spine and around the heads. The eyes glow softly in dim conditions, usually amber or orange but occasionally pale blue on lighter-coated individuals. The mouths are filled with thick teeth built for crushing bone, and the breath always carries a faint sulfurous warmth like the air above a geothermal vent. A Cerberus does not pant to cool down. Its body regulates temperature internally through its infernal metabolism. It is always warm to the touch but never overheated.

Cerberus are among the most sought-after familiars in Eyum for one reason: once bonded, they are unfailingly loyal for life. A Cerberus that has bonded to a person will follow them anywhere, guard them while they sleep, and throw itself into mortal danger without a second thought. The bond is not magical in the traditional sense. There is no spell cast and no ritual performed. It forms through shared experience over time: training, feeding, fighting alongside, and simply being present. A Cerberus raised from a pup by a single person will bond to that person by the time it reaches level 1. An adult Cerberus can be bonded as well but the process is harder and requires earning the animal's trust through sustained care and respect. A bonded Cerberus can be handled by other people, petted, fed, even ridden if it is large enough, but its true loyalty belongs to one person alone. It will not take commands from anyone else if those commands conflict with its master's wishes. A Cerberus whose master dies will often refuse food and waste away.

A Cerberus is not aggressive by nature but it is vigilant. It watches everything. Each head scans independently, covering different angles at all times. The effect is unnerving, one head locked on you while another watches the door and the third stares at your hands. They are hard to surprise and almost impossible to sneak up on. They are protective of their bonded person to an almost paranoid degree and will place themselves between their master and any perceived threat, growling low and steady until the threat backs off or is dealt with. They do not start fights but they finish them. A Cerberus in combat is methodical and relentless, one head bites and holds, another tears, a third watches for counterattacks. They fight like a pack of dogs compressed into a single body.

Cerberus breed like normal dogs. A female carries a litter of 2 to 4 pups for two months and gives birth in a secluded den. The pups are born with a single head. A second head begins to bud within the first month and is fully formed by month 3. The variant is genetically determined and apparent by the time the pup reaches 6 months old: an Orthrus pup's second head finishes growing and stops there. A Cerberus pup's third head begins emerging. A Barghest or Sharvara pup will be noticeably larger than its littermates even at this age and will continue growing heads as it levels. Pups reach level 1 after 1 year and gain 1 level per year thereafter. They reach sexual maturity at level 3 and can breed from that point on.

When a Cerberus is killed its body can be harvested for meat and hide. The meat is dark, lean, and has an odd warm aftertaste that some describe as faintly metallic or sulfurous. It is edible and nutritious but nobody's first choice. The hide is the valuable part. Cerberus hide is thick and durable with the same infernal warmth found in the living animal. When tanned and crafted into light armor it imparts partial resistance to fire and infernal energy, making it highly valued by monster hunters, spelunkers working near volcanic regions, and anyone who expects to face fire-affinity threats.
###### Variant Descriptions

**Orthrus (DR 1):** The smallest and most common variant. Two fully formed heads sit on a body about the size of a medium dog, somewhere between a border collie and a retriever in build. Stands about 2.5 ft at the shoulder at level 1 and weighs around 80 lbs. The coat is usually black or dark grey with lighter markings on the chest and paws. Orthrus are the most common Cerberus kept as familiars by ordinary people. They are small enough to live indoors, eat about as much as two normal dogs, and are gentle with children once bonded. They are not combat animals by nature. An Orthrus will fight to protect its master but it is not built for sustained battle. Its value is as a companion and watchdog. Two heads means twice the alertness and twice the affection, as Orthrus owners will tell you: one head is always watching the door and the other is always trying to lick your face. They live about 25 years.

**Cerberus (DR 3):** The namesake variant and the most balanced in terms of size, power, and temperament. Three heads on a body comparable to a large mastiff or wolfhound, standing 3.5 to 4 ft at the shoulder at level 1 and weighing around 250 lbs. The coat is most commonly deep red, black, or a brindle mix, and the eyes are almost always amber. A Cerberus is large enough to be a serious threat in a fight but not so large that it cannot accompany its master through doorways and corridors. This is the variant most adventurers seek out. It is strong enough to pull a person from danger, fast enough to keep pace with a mounted party, and tough enough to survive the kinds of fights adventurers get into. It can wear light barding and carry saddlebags but is not large enough to ride. A bonded Cerberus will sleep at the foot of its master's bed, walk at their heel through town, and sit silently outside the tavern until they come out. They live about 35 years.

**Barghest (DR 6):** The rideable variant. Four heads on a body the size of a draft horse, standing 6 to 7 ft at the shoulder at level 1 and weighing around 1,800 lbs. The coat is almost always solid black or a dark charcoal grey, with eyes that burn bright orange. A Barghest is massive. Its paws are the size of dinner plates. Its jaws are strong enough to crush a steel breastplate. Its stride at full speed eats up ground faster than any ordinary horse. Unlike the smaller variants a Barghest is not content to sit at home. It needs a job. It wants to run, to fight, to travel. A Barghest that is left idle becomes restless and destructive. A Barghest that is given a purpose becomes the finest war mount money cannot buy, fearless in combat, intelligent enough to act independently, and utterly devoted to its rider. The fourth head typically rests against the rider's knee when mounted, watching for threats from behind. A Barghest can carry a rider in full armor plus gear without slowing. They are rare and expensive, and owning one is a status symbol among mercenary companies and monster-hunting guilds. They live about 45 years.

**Sharvara (DR 10):** The apex of the Cerberus bloodline. Five heads on a body that exceeds the largest Barghest, standing 8 to 9 ft at the shoulder at level 1 and weighing over 3,000 lbs. The coat is always a deep scorched black with faint orange cracks running through the skin like cooling lava, visible only in dim light. Each head is distinctly different in expression and personality: one is aggressive and forward-facing, another is watchful and calculating, a third is almost playful, a fourth seems permanently furious, and the fifth hangs back observing everything with unnerving calm. A Sharvara does not bark. It rumbles. The sound is felt in the chest before it is heard. They are not pets, not familiars, and not mounts in the traditional sense. A Sharvara chooses its bond, not the other way around, and it will only bond to someone it considers worthy. What "worthy" means is unclear. Some Sharvara bond to legendary warriors. Others bond to a quiet farmer who never lifted a sword in his life. No one has managed to predict it. A bonded Sharvara will fight like a living siege engine, each head tearing in a different direction while the massive body bulldozes through anything in its path. They are so rare that most people believe they are a myth. They live at least 60 years though no one has ever documented one dying of old age.
###### Unique Abilities
All Cerberus share the following unique abilities regardless of variant. The number of heads determines how many bite attacks and how many independent Spot checks the Cerberus can make.

- **Heads of Cerberus (Passive):** A Cerberus with N heads can make N Bite attacks per action, each targeting the same or different creatures. Each head also grants the Cerberus an additional Spot check per round to detect hidden creatures, and the Cerberus cannot be flanked by any number of creatures fewer than its head count (for example a 3-headed Cerberus requires at least 4 flanking creatures to gain advantage against it).
- **Infernal Metabolism (Passive):** The Cerberus is immune to environmental heat effects and takes no damage from nonmagical fire such as campfires, burning buildings, or lava proximity. Its body temperature is constantly elevated, granting it Resistance to Ice/Cold damage.
- **Unshakeable Bond (Passive):** A Cerberus bonded to a master can sense that person's general direction and emotional state (calm, afraid, hurt, unconscious) from up to 1 mile away. If the master drops to 0 HP, the Cerberus immediately knows and gains advantage on all attack rolls and saving throws for the next 3 turns. This bond cannot be broken by mind control, charm, or fear effects and no mental effect or spell can force a Cerberus to go against what their master wishes.
- **Sulfur Breath (Active, 10 Mana):** The Cerberus exhales a blast of superheated sulfurous gas in a 15 ft cone from one head. Creatures in the cone must make a CON save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 1d8 + Affinity fire damage and be Blinded for 1 turn. Creatures that succeed take half damage and are not Blinded. Each additional head beyond the first increases the cone width by 5 ft; a Sharvara with all five heads breathing at once creates a 35 ft cone. Only one head may use Sulfur Breath per turn; additional heads must use Bite attacks.

Variant-specific abilities are listed below.

- **Watchdog (Passive):** Orthrus only. The Orthrus cannot be surprised. If an encounter would normally begin with the party surprised, the Orthrus is not surprised and may use its reaction to bark a warning, granting one ally within 30 ft advantage on their initiative roll.
- **Coordinated Assault (Active, 3 Mana):** Cerberus variant only. The Cerberus designates a single target and all three heads focus fire. Make three Bite attacks against the same creature. If at least two hit, the third attack automatically hits. If all three hit, the target must make a STR save (DC 10 + STR mod) or be knocked Prone.
- **War Mount (Passive):** Barghest only. A Barghest can carry a rider of size Large or smaller without any movement penalty. The Barghest's Sulfur Breath costs 1 less Mana (minimum 1) when used while carrying its bonded rider. The rider may use their bonus action to direct one of the Barghest's attacks, giving that attack advantage.
- **Fivefold Fury (Passive):** Sharvara only. Whenever the Sharvara succeeds on a Bite attack roll their next 4 bite attacks against the same creature this turn automatically hit. 

**Standard Passives:** Thorns, Pursuer, Fast Action (level 5+)
**Standard Actives:** Bite, Charge
