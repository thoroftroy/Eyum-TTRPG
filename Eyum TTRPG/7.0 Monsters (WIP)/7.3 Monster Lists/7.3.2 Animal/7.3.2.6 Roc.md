A Roc is a bird of impossible proportions, a creature whose wingspan can blot out the sun and whose shadow passing overhead sends entire herds stampeding. The body plan is that of a bird of prey scaled up to absurd dimensions, a heavy barrel chest packed with flight muscle, a short thick neck, a hooked beak deep enough to swallow a sheep whole, and legs as thick as tree trunks ending in talons that can punch through plate armor. The feathers are stiff and dense, more like flexible scales than the soft plumage of smaller birds, each one anchored deep into a hide that is closer to leather than skin. The wings are broad and long, built for soaring across continents rather than quick maneuvering. A Roc in flight does not flap often. It rides thermals and wind currents, gliding for hours without a single wingbeat, and when it does beat its wings the downdraft is strong enough to flatten tents and knock standing humans to the ground.

The head is proportionally large with a heavy brow ridge that shades eyes the size of dinner plates. Those eyes are the Roc's greatest weapon before its talons ever come into play. A Roc can spot a rabbit twitching its nose from two miles up and can track a galloping horse across open terrain with the same casual ease a hawk watches a field mouse. The beak is the primary difference between variants, some are hooked and razor-edged for tearing flesh, others are broad and club-like for crushing bone, and one variant has a beak so massive and shaped so strangely it looks like a mistake of nature until you watch it use the thing. The legs are built for gripping and crushing. The toes are long and powerful, three facing forward with one reversed, each tipped with a curved talon that can be up to three feet long on the largest variants. When a Roc closes its foot around something, whatever is inside does not get out.

Rocs are solitary hunters by nature but they are also deeply prideful creatures, far more intelligent than their animal appearance suggests. A Roc does not simply react to the world. It judges it. Every creature it encounters is evaluated and categorized: threat, prey, equal, or beneath notice. This intelligence makes them nearly impossible to control through force or domination. A Roc will die before it submits to a master it does not respect. However, a Roc that has been genuinely earned, through patience, courage, and a refusal to back down, becomes an ally of terrifying capability. They do not love. They do not bond the way a dog bonds. They make a decision that you are worth their time, and as long as you continue to prove them right, they will fight beside you, carry you across continents, and guard your back with the same ferocity they apply to everything else in their lives. The moment you show weakness or cowardice the arrangement is over, and the Roc will leave without a backward glance. You do not own a Roc. The Roc simply allows you to exist in its presence.

All Rocs are solitary outside of mating. A male and female will meet at a traditional nesting site, usually a high cliff ledge or a flat-topped mountain, and the courtship is a violent and spectacular thing. The birds circle each other at altitude, perform synchronized dives, lock talons in midair and spiral toward the ground before releasing at the last possible second. If both survive the display and neither is obviously injured or incompetent, they mate over the course of a few days and then separate. The female lays a single egg the size of a small barrel and incubates it for six months. During this time the male brings food, dropping carcasses onto the nest from altitude with terrifying precision. Once the chick hatches both parents feed it for the first year, after which the chick is large enough to hunt on its own and is driven out of the territory. A Roc reaches level 1 after its first year and gains 1 level per year thereafter. They can live for over 80 years and never stop growing, though the rate slows with age.

A Roc does not hunt out of malice. It hunts because it is always hungry. The metabolic demands of a body that size are staggering, and a Roc spends the majority of its waking life either hunting or digesting. It will eat anything it can carry, and it can carry almost anything. Sheep, cattle, horses, carts, small boats near the surface of the water, and on at least one documented occasion a fully loaded wagon with the horses still attached. It does not toy with its food. A Roc strikes once, kills or incapacitates instantly, and carries the carcass back to its roost to eat in peace. It is not aggressive toward humanoids by default but it is also not afraid of them. A Roc that has learned humans carry livestock will start treating farms like a buffet, and once that happens the only solution is to kill the bird or abandon the land.
###### Variant Descriptions

**High Roc (DR 5):** The classic Roc of legend and the variant most people picture when they hear the name. Its body is that of an eagle scaled to nightmare proportions, with dark brown plumage across the back and wings that lightens to a golden cream on the chest and underside. The wings are the broadest of any variant, spanning 80 to 100 ft at level 1 and adding nearly 10 ft per level. The head is pure white with a golden cast in direct sunlight, and the eyes are a piercing amber that seems to glow from within. The beak is hooked and sharp, black at the tip fading to yellow at the base, with a cutting edge that can shear through steel cable. High Rocs are found in the tallest mountain ranges where the air is thin and the thermals are strong. They build their nests on peaks that have never felt a human foot and only descend to hunt. They are the strongest fliers of all Rocs, capable of staying aloft for days at a time and crossing oceans without stopping. They are also the most prideful. A High Roc considers the entire world beneath it, and it is not wrong. They are impossible to tame through force or coercion. The only recorded instances of High Rocs cooperating with humanoids involve individuals who returned a stolen egg, defended a nest from a greater threat, or otherwise proved themselves in a way the bird could not ignore. Even then, the relationship is one of mutual respect between equals, not master and mount. A High Roc's dive from altitude is one of the most terrifying sights in nature, a silent plummet that crosses a mile of vertical distance in seconds, ending in a strike that hits with enough force to crack stone. They will hunt anything, including small whales if the Roc's territory includes a coastline.

**Marsh Roc (DR 4):** The most intelligent and the most unsettling of all Rocs. Built like a shoe-bill stork stretched to monster dimensions, the Marsh Roc has a leaner, more upright body than other variants with exceptionally long legs built for wading through deep water and thick mud. The plumage is a dusty blue-grey that darkens along the wings and tail, with a patch of bare pink-grey skin around the eyes that gives the bird a perpetually intense, unblinking stare. The defining feature is the beak, enormous, broad, and shaped like a wooden clog, pale yellow-grey with a dark hooked tip. The beak is capable of a behavior unique to this variant: the Marsh Roc will clatter its upper and lower mandibles together in a rapid, percussive burst that sounds eerily like a rotary crank or a distant machinegun. It uses this sound to communicate over long distances across the marsh, to warn off rivals, and, most notably, as a greeting. A Marsh Roc bows. It is not a trained behavior and it is not a trick. Wild Marsh Rocs will lower their heads, spread their wings slightly, and clatter their beaks at creatures they have decided are worthy of acknowledgment. No one fully understands what criteria a Marsh Roc uses to make this judgment. Captured Marsh Rocs who were treated cruelly never bowed at anyone. Marsh Rocs who were rescued or treated with steady, patient respect sometimes began bowing after months or years. The bow is not submission. It is the opposite. It is the Marsh Roc saying "I see you, and I have decided you are worth my time." A Marsh Roc that bows at you will allow you to approach, to touch its beak, to stand in its presence without threat. It will not allow anyone else to do the same. Attempts to touch a Marsh Roc that has not bowed at you are met with violence. They will remember faces for years. Fishermen in marsh country leave offerings of fish at the water's edge for the local Marsh Roc, hoping that one day the bird will bow. Marsh Rocs are ambush hunters. They stand motionless in shallow water for hours, their blue-grey plumage blending perfectly with mist and reeds, and then strike with a speed that seems impossible for something their size. The beak does not tear. It crushes. A Marsh Roc that bites down on something pulverizes it into a slurry of bone and meat regardless of whether the thing is a fish, a crocodile, or a man in full plate. They are less aggressive than High Rocs and will tolerate humanoid settlements near their territory as long as the fishing is good and nobody tries anything stupid. Local fishing villages often treat the Marsh Roc with a mixture of fear and reverence. A Marsh Roc that bows at the village elder is considered the greatest possible blessing. A Marsh Roc that stops bowing is an omen of disaster.

**Crag Roc (DR 3):** The smallest and fastest variant, built like a peregrine falcon or a goshawk. The body is compact and muscular with pointed wings and a long narrow tail that gives it maneuverability the larger Rocs cannot match. The plumage is a rust red across the back with white barring on the chest and underwings, and the head is dark grey with a distinctive black stripe through each eye like a falcon's hood. The wings span 40 to 50 ft at level 1. What it loses in size it makes up for in speed. A Crag Roc in a stoop can exceed 300 miles per hour, hitting prey with such force that the impact alone is fatal before the talons even close. They are found in rocky canyon systems, sea cliffs, and eroded badlands where the terrain is broken and vertical. Unlike other Rocs they are social during the non-breeding season and will roost in loose colonies of up to a dozen birds tucked into cliff faces. They hunt in pairs or trios, taking turns diving at prey to keep it off balance until one bird lands the killing strike. Crag Rocs are the variant most commonly tamed by humans, though "tamed" is generous. Their pride is just as ferocious as any other Roc, but their smaller size and more social nature make them slightly more open to arrangement. A Crag Roc raised from a chick will tolerate a handler and may cooperate in a hunt, but it tests constantly. Every interaction is a negotiation. The handler must prove themselves again and again: by keeping up on foot during training flights, by never flinching when the bird dives directly at their face, by providing better kills than the bird could get on its own. A Crag Roc that decides its handler is no longer worthy will simply leave. It will not attack. It will not make a scene. It will fly off one morning and never come back, and the handler will spend the rest of their life wondering what they did wrong. Falconers who work with Crag Rocs are a rare and mildly insane breed. They are the only people in Eyum who can look at a creature with a 50 ft wingspan and a dive speed that breaks the sound barrier and think "I can work with this."

**Scrub Roc (DR 2):** A flightless variant that looks less like a bird of prey and more like a prehistoric terror bird given feathers and a cheerful disposition. The body is heavy and muscular with legs so thick they could be mistaken for a bear's, ending in broad three-toed feet with blunt crushing claws rather than gripping talons. The wings are small and vestigial, useless for flight but still powerful enough to deliver a stunning blow to anything that gets too close. The plumage is a mottled brown and tan like dried grassland, with a bare blue-grey neck and head topped with a short bristly crest that stands erect when the bird is agitated or excited. The beak is short, thick, and hooked like a giant chicken's, good for pecking and tearing. Scrub Rocs are found in open grasslands, savannas, and scrub plains. The Scrub Roc is the friendliest Roc variant and the only one that seems to genuinely enjoy humanoid company. Where other Rocs tolerate humans at best, a Scrub Roc that has been treated well will seek out attention, follow its handler around like an oversized chicken, and make a low warbling coo when content. They are still proud. They will not be ridden by just anyone and they will not take commands from strangers. But for the person they have chosen, a Scrub Roc is rideable, trainable, and fiercely protective. Scrub Roc riders are common among the plains nomads who live in the bird's natural range. The birds can run at over 60 miles per hour in short bursts and maintain a steady 40 mile per hour lope for hours. A Scrub Roc with a rider on its back is one of the fastest things on land that is not using magic. They are omnivorous and will eat almost anything, including grasses, roots, insects, and small game, which makes them easy to feed on long journeys. Their proud nature means they will not tolerate mistreatment. A Scrub Roc that is struck, starved, or neglected will turn on its handler with the same cheerful violence it applies to everything else, and unlike the other Rocs, it will not leave quietly. It will make sure you remember the mistake.
###### Unique Abilities
All Rocs share the following unique abilities regardless of variant.

- **Talon Snatch (Active, 7 Mana):** The Roc makes a talon strike against a creature of its size or smaller. On a hit, the target takes 1d10 + STR mod piercing damage and is [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental / Positional Conditions|Grappled]]. While grappled, the Roc can fly at full speed and the target takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage at the start of each of the Roc's turns from the crushing grip. The target remains grappled until it succeeds on a STR save (DC 10 + STR mod) to break free or until the Roc uses a free action to release it. A creature released from altitude falls normally. The Roc can only grapple one creature at a time this way.
- **Eyes of the Sky (Passive):** The Roc has effectively unlimited visual range in open terrain and can see fine details from miles away. It has advantage on all Spot checks relying on sight and cannot be surprised while flying or perched at elevation. The Roc can track a moving creature from over a mile away as long as it maintains line of sight.
- **Proud Hunter (Passive):** The Roc cannot be [[2.4 Conditions#Mental-Conditions|Frightened]] and automatically succeeds on all saves against intimidation or fear effects. Additionally, if the Roc is reduced below half Vitality by a single attack, its next attack against the creature that dealt that damage has advantage and deals an additional 2d6 damage.
- **Wingstorm (Active, 12 Mana):** The Roc beats its wings downward, creating a violent blast of wind in a 30 ft cone. All creatures in the cone must make a STR save (DC 10 + STR mod) or be pushed 15 ft away and knocked [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental / Positional Conditions|Prone]]. Small and smaller creatures that fail by 5 or more are pushed an additional 15 ft. Unsecured objects weighing less than 50 lbs are scattered. Flying creatures in the cone have disadvantage on the save. This ability can be used while grounded or hovering. Scrub Rocs use a weaker version with a 15 ft cone and the save DC is reduced by 2.

Variant-specific abilities are listed below.

- **Death Dive (Active, 16 Mana):** High Roc only. The High Roc climbs to altitude and plummets in a straight line at a creature on the ground. The target must make a DEX save (DC 10 + DEX mod). On a failure, the Roc's talon strike deals an additional 4d8 piercing damage and the target is automatically [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental / Positional Conditions|Grappled]] by Talon Snatch without requiring a separate attack roll. On a success, the target takes half the additional damage and is not grappled. The Roc must start its turn at least 30 ft above the target to use this ability.
- **Sovereign of the Peaks (Passive):** High Roc only. The High Roc cannot be moved against its will by any effect that would push, pull, knock [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental / Positional Conditions|Prone]], or forcibly reposition it while it is flying. Additionally, creatures of level 10 or lower must make a WIS save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) to voluntarily attack the High Roc. On a failure, they must choose a different target or forfeit their attack. This effect ends for a creature once the High Roc attacks them.
- **Crushing Beak (Active, 7 Mana):** Marsh Roc only. Instead of its normal talon attack, the Marsh Roc makes a devastating beak strike against a creature within reach. The target must make a CON save (DC 10 + STR mod). On a failure, it takes 2d10 + STR mod bludgeoning damage and is [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Stunned]] for 1 turn. On a success, it takes half damage and is not [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Stunned]]. Creatures with an exoskeleton, carapace, or heavy armor have disadvantage on this save.
- **Bill Clatter (Active, 7 Mana):** Marsh Roc only. The Marsh Roc clatters its beak in a rapid percussive burst that carries for miles across open terrain. All creatures of the Marsh Roc's choice within 60 ft that can hear it must make a WIS save (DC 10 + WIS mod). On a failure, they become [[2.4 Conditions#Mental-Conditions|Frightened]] for 2 turns and cannot approach the Marsh Roc during that time. Creatures that succeed are immune to this effect from that Marsh Roc for 24 hours. A Marsh Roc that uses Bill Clatter as a greeting (non-combat) instead grants allies within 60 ft advantage on their next WIS or CHA save. Using Bill Clatter as a greeting does not cost Mana.
- **Harrier Stoop (Active, 7 Mana):** Crag Roc only. The Crag Roc dives at a creature from above, striking with blinding speed. Make a talon attack with advantage. On a hit, the attack deals an additional 1d12 piercing damage and the target must make a CON save (DC 10 + DEX mod) or be [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Stunned]] for 1 turn from the sheer force of the impact. The Crag Roc can use this ability without provoking opportunity attacks from the target.
- **Mated Pair (Passive):** Crag Roc only. When fighting within 100 ft of an allied Crag Roc, both gain +1 to all attack rolls and +1 AC. This bonus increases to +2 if either bird is below half Vitality. Does not stack with more than one allied Crag Roc.
- **Trampling Charge (Active, 12 Mana):** Scrub Roc only. The Scrub Roc lowers its head and charges up to twice its ground speed in a straight line. All creatures in the line must make a DEX save (DC 10 + STR mod) or take 2d8 + STR mod bludgeoning damage and be knocked [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental / Positional Conditions|Prone]]. Creatures that fail the save by 5 or more take double damage. The Scrub Roc may make a free beak attack against the last creature it passed through at the end of the charge.
- **Plains Runner (Passive):** Scrub Roc only. The Scrub Roc can carry a rider of size Medium or smaller without any movement penalty. While carrying a rider it has bonded with, the Scrub Roc's speed increases by 10 ft and both the Scrub Roc and rider gain +2 to DEX saves against area effects. The Scrub Roc will only accept a rider it has chosen. Attempting to mount an unwilling Scrub Roc results in the offender taking 2d6 bludgeoning damage and being thrown 10 ft.

**Standard Passives:** Pursuer, Scratch Resistance, Hardened Body (Scrub Roc only)
**Standard Actives:** Talon, Beak, Wing Buffet
