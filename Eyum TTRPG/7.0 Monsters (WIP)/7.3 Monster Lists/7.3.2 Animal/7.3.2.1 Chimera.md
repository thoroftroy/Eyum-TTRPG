Chimeras are hybrid animals hatched from eggs, their bodies pressed from two or more different creatures into a single stable form. The term covers a vast range of species. A griffin and a hydra have about as much in common as a horse and a snake, both are chimeras, but they hunt differently, live in different territories, and are built from completely different parts. Each species breeds true, and a chimera egg always hatches into the same hybrid form as its parents.

All chimeras hatch from eggs. Incubation time, egg size, and clutch number vary by species, but every chimera begins as a hatchling no larger than a housecat. They grow steadily through adolescence, reaching full size and power as they age. Level is determined by age. A hatchling is level 1. Each year of life grants one level, up to the species maximum listed in the table below. As a chimera gains levels its body grows, its natural weapons sharpen, and it unlocks abilities that make it far more dangerous than a simple increase in size would suggest.

A chimera gains the following each level:
- +1 Hit Die to Vitality
- +1 Hit Die to Health
- +1 Hit Die to Mana
- +1 Generic Damage Mod
- +1 to the monster’s main Affinity
- +1 AC (every 3 levels)
- +1 to all Stats (Str, Con, Dex (every 3 levels (3,6,9,etc) gain +1 to Wis, Int, and Char as well))

Size increases are listed per species in the Growth Table.

Each chimera species unlocks unique abilities at specific level milestones. These are listed in the creature's description. A level 1 hatchling has only its natural attacks and any L1 abilities noted. An adult at max level has all abilities unlocked.
#### Unique Chimera Abilities
 > The abilities below are unique to chimeras. Each species has a list of unlocked abilities by level.

- **Swoop (Active, 2 Mana):** The chimera flies up to its speed in a straight line and makes a Claw attack at the end. If the attack hits, it deals an additional 1d6 damage. This movement does not provoke opportunity attacks.
- **Eagle Eye (Passive):** The chimera has Advantage on ranged attacks and Spot checks. Its vision range is effectively unlimited in open terrain.
- **Aquatic Charge (Active, 2 Mana):** The chimera moves up to its speed through water. Creatures in its path must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d8 bludgeoning damage and be pushed to the side.
- **Riddle Aura (Passive):** Creatures within 15 ft of the chimera must make a WIS save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) at the start of their turn or be forced to use their Action to solve a riddle rather than attacking. This can only affect each creature once per combat.
- **Venom Sting (Active, 2 Mana):** Make a melee attack with the tail. On hit, deal 1d8 piercing damage and the target must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or gain the Poisoned condition for 3 turns.
- **Volley (Active, 3 Mana):** The chimera launches a barrage of tail spines. Make a separate ranged attack against each creature in a 15 ft radius. On hit, deal 1d6 piercing damage and apply the Poisoned condition.
- **Warding Aura (Passive):** Allied creatures within 15 ft gain +1 AC and Advantage on saves against curses and fear effects.
- **Devour (Active, 3 Mana):** Make a melee attack against a creature below half Vitality. On hit, deal 2d8 piercing damage and the chimera regains 1d8 Vitality. If this kills the target, the chimera regains 2d8 Vitality instead.
- **Triple Breath (Active, 4 Mana):** The chimera exhales a 20 ft cone of fire, poison gas, and lightning simultaneously. Creatures in the cone must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus). On fail, they take 2d6 Fire damage, 2d6 Poison damage, and 2d6 Lightning damage. On success, they take half of each.
- **Petrifying Gaze (Active, 3 Mana):** Choose a creature within 30 ft. The target must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus). On fail, it begins to Petrify: its speed is halved and it has Disadvantage on all rolls for 2 turns. If it fails the save twice consecutively, it becomes Petrified for 1 turn.
- **Sonic Screech (Active, 2 Mana):** 20 ft cone. Creatures must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d8 Thunder damage and become Deafened for 1 turn.
- **Noble Charge (Active, 2 Mana):** The chimera moves up to its speed in a straight line. Creatures in its path must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d10 bludgeoning damage and be knocked Prone.
- **Venomous Spit (Active, 1 Mana):** Make a ranged attack against a creature within 20 ft. On hit, deal 1d6 Poison damage and the target gains the Poisoned condition.
- **Multi-Head Strike (Active, 4 Mana):** The chimera makes a Slam attack against each creature within its melee reach. Each attack uses the same attack roll with a -2 penalty per additional target beyond the first.
- **Storm Wings (Passive):** Creatures within 10 ft of the chimera when it flies or takes off take 1d4 Thunder damage and must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or be pushed 5 ft away.
- **Soul Rend (Active, 3 Mana):** Make a melee attack. On hit, deal 1d8 Necrotic damage and the target cannot regain Vitality until the end of its next turn.
- **Three-Headed Guard (Passive):** The chimera has Advantage on Initiative rolls and cannot be Flanked. It can make one additional Reaction per round.
- **Golden Hide (Passive):** The chimera has Resistance to Physical damage. Critical hits against it are treated as normal hits.
- **Feather Storm (Active, 2 Mana):** The chimera launches a cloud of loose feathers in a 15 ft radius. The area is heavily obscured for 1 turn. Creatures inside must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or become Blinded for 1 turn.
- **Grapple and Carry (Active, 2 Mana):** Make a Claw attack. On hit, the target is Grappled and lifted. The chimera may move up to its speed, carrying the grappled creature. The grappled creature takes 1d6 slashing damage at the start of each of its turns from the grip.

## Chimera Descriptions

**Griffin**
A griffin has the body and hind limbs of a lion and the head, wings, and forelimbs of an eagle. The transition between the two animals sits at the shoulders, where feline torso gives way to avian breast muscles covered in tawny feathers that darken to brown at the wing tips. The front legs end in sharp curved eagle talons, the hind legs in heavy feline paws with retractable claws. The head is fully avian with a hooked beak and large forward-facing golden eyes that can spot movement from miles away. The wings are broad and proportioned for sustained soaring. A griffin at adulthood is roughly 8 ft long from beak to tail with a wingspan of 20 ft. Males develop manes of thick feathers around the neck and chest. Its voice is an unsettling mix of a lion's roar and an eagle's shriek.

**Unique Abilities:** Eagle Eye (L1), Swoop (L1), Marked Prey (L3), Golden Hoard (L5)

- **Marked Prey (Active, 2 Mana, L3):** The griffin picks one target it can see. For the rest of the encounter, its attacks against that target deal an extra 1d6 damage and the griffin knows the target's location at all times.
- **Golden Hoard (Passive, L5):** When defending a location the griffin considers its territory or hoard, it gains +2 AC and Advantage on all saves. This bonus persists as long as the griffin remains within 200 ft of that location.

**Hippocampus**
A hippocampus has the upper body, front legs, and head of a horse, with the lower body tapering into a long powerful fish tail instead of hind legs. Fine overlapping scales cover the entire body, transitioning from equine hair at the front to smooth fish scales at the rear. The front legs end in slightly webbed hooves suited for both shore and surf. The tail is broad and finned with translucent blue-green membranes. The head is horse-like but with large dark eyes that have transparent inner eyelids for underwater vision, and gill slits behind the jaw. The mane is made of thick water-resistant strands that float and drift even in still air. An adult hippocampus is roughly 10 ft long from nose to tail fin, the tail making up roughly half that length.

**Unique Abilities:** Aquatic Charge (L1), Whirlpool (L3), Tidal Wave (L5)

- **Whirlpool (Active, 3 Mana, L3):** The hippocampus creates a whirlpool in a 15 ft radius within 30 ft. Creatures inside must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or be pulled 10 ft toward the center and take 1d8 bludgeoning damage. The area counts as difficult terrain for 3 turns.
- **Tidal Wave (Active, 4 Mana, L5):** The hippocampus summons a wave of water in a 30 ft cone. Creatures in the area must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 2d8 bludgeoning damage and be knocked Prone and pushed 15 ft. On success they take half damage and are not knocked Prone.

**Sphinx**
A sphinx has the body of a lion, the wings of an eagle, and the head of a human. The body is large and heavily muscled, covered in tawny fur, with a leonine tail that flicks with constant deliberate motion. The wings are feathered in gold and brown, folding along the lion's back. The head is human in shape but larger than life, with calm knowing features and amber eyes with vertical slit pupils like a cat. The sphinx does not speak aloud. Its voice resonates directly in the minds of those within 30 ft, a clear melodic tone free of accent or inflection. An adult sphinx is roughly 10 ft long and stands 6 ft at the shoulder, with a wingspan of 18 ft.

**Unique Abilities:** Riddle Aura (L1), Mind Trap (L3), Temporal Riddle (L5)

- **Riddle Aura (Passive, L1):** Creatures within 15 ft of the sphinx must make a WIS save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) at the start of their turn or be forced to use their Action to solve a riddle rather than attacking. This can only affect each creature once per combat.
- **Mind Trap (Passive, L3):** Any creature that hits the sphinx with a melee attack must make a WIS save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d8 Psychic damage and have its next attack before the end of its next turn made at Disadvantage.
- **Temporal Riddle (Active, 5 Mana, L5):** Choose a creature within 30 ft. That creature must make a WIS save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus). On fail, it is Stunned for 1 turn as its perception of time fractures. On success, its speed is halved for 1 turn instead.

**Hippogriff**
A hippogriff has the body, hind legs, and tail of a horse and the head, wings, and forelimbs of an eagle. It is built lighter and faster than a griffin, with a more equine body shape. Feathers on the head and chest are typically gray or white, grading into brown on the wings. The front legs end in sharp talons and the hind legs are heavy equine limbs with hard hooves. The eyes are large and avian, positioned for depth perception during flight. The beak is slightly curved. An adult hippogriff is roughly 7 ft long and stands 5 ft at the shoulder, with a wingspan of 18 ft. It is more social than the griffin, often found in small family groups.

**Unique Abilities:** Eagle Eye (L1), Swoop (L1), Bonded Flight (L3), Wind Cushion (L5)

- **Bonded Flight (Passive, L3):** When ridden by a creature it trusts, the hippogriff and rider share the same turn. The rider's movement and the hippogriff's movement are combined, and the rider gains Advantage on saves against being dismounted.
- **Wind Cushion (Active, 2 Mana, L5):** The hippogriff beats its wings to create a cushion of air in a 20 ft radius below it. All falling creatures in the area take no fall damage for 1 turn. Non-flying creatures in the area have their speed halved for 1 turn from the downdraft.

**Bunyip**
A bunyip has the head and front body of a dog, with a thick powerful seal-like tail and hindquarters. Coarse waterproof fur covers the body, dark brown or black on top and lighter on the belly. The head is broad and dog-like with large dark eyes positioned for low-light vision and ears that fold flat against the skull when swimming. The mouth is filled with sharp conical teeth. The front legs are muscular and end in webbed paws with strong claws. The rear of the body tapers into a flat powerful tail that provides propulsion in water. An adult bunyip is roughly 9 ft long from nose to tail, equally at home in rivers and coastal waters.

**Unique Abilities:** Aquatic Charge (L1), Lurking Strike (L3), Terrifying Bellow (L5)

- **Lurking Strike (Passive, L3):** When the bunyip attacks a creature that is in water while the bunyip was fully submerged at the start of its turn, the attack has Advantage and deals an additional 1d6 damage.
- **Terrifying Bellow (Active, 3 Mana, L5):** The bunyip lets out a deep resonant bellow. All creatures within 60 ft that can hear must make a WIS save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or become Frightened for 2 turns. Creatures already in water have Disadvantage on this save.

**Manticore**
A manticore has the body and face of a lion, a scorpion's tail, and large leathery bat-like wings. The body is powerfully built, covered in dark red or brown fur with a thick mane around the neck and shoulders. The face is lion-like but with an unsettling intelligence in the eyes, and the mouth contains three rows of teeth. The wings are broad and bat-like, dark brown or black, with visible bone structure supporting the membrane. The tail is long and flexible, ending in a bulbous stinger that drips with green venom. The stinger can also launch a volley of spines. An adult manticore is roughly 10 ft long with a tail of equal length, and a wingspan of 22 ft.

**Unique Abilities:** Venom Sting (L1), Volley (L3), Hunting Scent (L5), Impaling Flight (L6)

- **Hunting Scent (Passive, L5):** The manticore can track any creature that has taken damage within the last 24 hours by scent alone, regardless of distance or terrain. It gains Advantage on attacks against tracked prey.
- **Impaling Flight (Active, 3 Mana, L6):** The manticore flies up to its speed and makes a Claw attack against a target. On hit, the target takes 2d8 piercing damage and is Grappled. At the start of the target's next turn, it takes 1d8 piercing damage from the grip. The manticore can release the target as a free action, dropping it.

**Lamassu**
A lamassu has the body of a bull, the wings of an eagle, and the head of a human. The body is massive and heavy, covered in thick hide and fur, with legs ending in heavy hooves. The wings are large and feathered, typically white or gold, folding against the bull's broad back. The head is human in shape but proportioned for the massive body, with calm watchful eyes and an expression of serene authority. Some lamassu grow horned bone formations from their skull. An adult lamassu is roughly 12 ft long and stands 8 ft at the shoulder. They do not speak but communicate through subtle telepathic presence.

**Unique Abilities:** Warding Aura (L1), Righteous Shield (L3), Celestial Judgment (L5), Sanctuary (L6)

- **Righteous Shield (Active, 3 Mana, L3):** The lamassu grants one ally within 30 ft a protective shield of light. That ally gains +2 AC and Resistance to Necrotic damage for 3 turns.
- **Celestial Judgment (Active, 4 Mana, L5):** Choose a creature within 60 ft that has harmed an innocent creature in the last minute. The target must make a CHA save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 3d8 Radiant damage and be Stunned for 1 turn.
- **Sanctuary (Passive, L6):** The lamassu and allied creatures within 20 ft cannot be targeted by curses or possession effects. Any creature that attempts to possess or curse an ally inside the sanctuary must make a CHA save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 2d8 Psychic damage.

**Ammit**
An ammit has the forequarters of a lion, the hindquarters of a hippopotamus, and the head of a crocodile. The body is a patchwork of three different textures: lion's tawny fur on the front, hippo's thick gray hide on the rear, and crocodile's rough scaly green-brown head. The front legs end in heavy lion paws with retractable claws. The back legs are squat and powerful with wide spread toes. The head is massive and crocodilian with a long snout filled with interlocking teeth and eyes that sit high on the skull. The tail is thick and muscular. An adult ammit is roughly 11 ft long and weighs over a ton.

**Unique Abilities:** Devour (L1), Soul Eater (L3), Heartless (L5), Judgement of the Scale (L8)

- **Soul Eater (Passive, L3):** When the ammit kills a creature, it gains 2d8 temporary Vitality and +1 to its next attack roll. The temporary Vitality lasts for 1 hour.
- **Heartless (Passive, L5):** Creatures killed by the ammit cannot be resurrected or revived by any means short of divine intervention. Their soul is consumed.
- **Judgement of the Scale (Active, 5 Mana, L8):** Choose a creature within 30 ft that has killed another creature in the last minute. The target must make a CHA save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus). On fail, the target takes 4d8 Blight damage and is Stunned for 1 turn as their heart is weighed. A creature that fails this save while below half Vitality dies instantly.

**Chimera**
The chimera that gives the category its name has three heads: a lion's head at the front, a goat's head rising from the middle of its back, and a serpent's head at the tip of its tail. The body is that of a lion with a goat's hindquarters and a serpent for a tail. The lion's head breathes fire, the goat's head exhales poisonous gas, and the serpent's head spits lightning. The body is covered in tawny lion fur that transitions to shaggy goat hair at the rear. The serpent tail is scaled and green or black. An adult chimera is roughly 10 ft long and stands 5 ft at the shoulder. Damage to any of its three heads kills it instantly.

**Unique Abilities:** Triple Breath (L1), Multi-Head Strike (L3), Reactive Heads (L5), Elemental Fury (L7)

- **Reactive Heads (Passive, L5):** The chimera can make one additional Reaction per round. It can use this Reaction to make a Bite attack against a creature that moves within its reach.
- **Elemental Fury (Passive, L7):** When the chimera uses Triple Breath, it may target two separate 20 ft cones instead of one. Each cone can be aimed independently. The chimera must still pay the full Mana cost.

**Cockatrice**
A cockatrice has the head and body of a rooster and the tail of a lizard or serpent. The body is about the size of a large turkey, covered in glossy feathers typically a mix of red, gold, and green. The head has a prominent comb and wattles, but the eyes are yellow with vertical slit pupils, reptilian rather than avian. The beak is sharp and slightly hooked. The tail is long and serpentine, covered in scales, ending in a small cluster of spikes. The legs are bird-like with small claws. An adult cockatrice is roughly 4 ft long (including tail) and stands 2 ft tall. Its most dangerous feature is its petrifying gaze.

**Unique Abilities:** Petrifying Gaze (L1), Venomous Spit (L1), Crow of Fear (L3), Stone Aura (L5)

- **Crow of Fear (Active, 2 Mana, L3):** The cockatrice crows loudly. All creatures within 30 ft must make a WIS save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or become Frightened for 2 turns. Creatures that are already Petrifying have Disadvantage on this save.
- **Stone Aura (Passive, L5):** Any creature that starts its turn within 10 ft of the cockatrice must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or begin to Petrify: its speed is halved and it has Disadvantage on all rolls for 1 turn. A creature that fails three consecutive saves becomes Petrified for 1 hour.

**Pegasus**
A pegasus has the body of a horse and the wings of an eagle. The body is that of a particularly fine horse with a deep chest, strong legs, and a flowing mane and tail that stay clean and well-groomed. The coat is pure white without any blemish. The wings are large and feathered, white or silver with slight iridescence in sunlight. The eyes are large and dark with calm intelligence. The hooves are hard and polished. An adult pegasus is roughly 9 ft long and stands 6 ft at the shoulder, with a wingspan of 22 ft. Pegasi cannot be tamed through force or coercion.

**Unique Abilities:** Eagle Eye (L1), Noble Charge (L1), Celestial Gallop (L3), Purifying Light (L5)

- **Celestial Gallop (Active, 3 Mana, L3):** The pegasus and any rider move up to double the pegasus's speed in a straight line. During this movement, the pegasus is intangible and passes through creatures and obstacles harmlessly. The pegasus must end this movement in an unoccupied space.
- **Purifying Light (Active, 4 Mana, L5):** The pegasus flares with radiant light. All creatures within 20 ft must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus). On fail, they are Blinded for 1 turn. Undead and Fiend creatures take 2d8 Radiant damage on a failed save. The pegasus also removes all curses and diseases from itself and any rider.

**Basilisk**
A basilisk has the head of a rooster, the body of a serpent, and small vestigial wings at the shoulders. The body is long and serpentine, covered in dark green or brown scales, with a ridge of spines running down the back. The head is rooster-like but scaled, with a fleshy red comb and wattles, and eyes that are bright yellow with slit pupils. The mouth is wide and filled with venomous fangs. The small wings are too small for flight but used for display and intimidation. The basilisk moves with a serpentine crawl, surprisingly fast for its size, and can rear up to look creatures in the eye. An adult basilisk is roughly 12 ft long and as thick as a human thigh.

**Unique Abilities:** Venomous Spit (L1), Serpent King (L3), Venom Aura (L5), Petrifying Gaze (L6)

- **Serpent King (Passive, L3):** Snakes and serpentine creatures within 60 ft of the basilisk will not attack it. The basilisk can issue simple commands to snakes within this range, which they follow to the best of their ability.
- **Venom Aura (Passive, L5):** Any creature that starts its turn within 5 ft of the basilisk must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d6 Poison damage and gain the Poisoned condition for 1 turn.
- **Petrifying Gaze (Active, 3 Mana, L6):** Choose a creature within 30 ft. The target must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus). On fail, it begins to Petrify: its speed is halved and it has Disadvantage on all rolls for 2 turns. If it fails the save twice consecutively, it becomes Petrified for 1 turn.

**Hydra**
A hydra has the thick scaled body of a crocodile, four short powerful legs with heavy claws, and multiple long serpentine necks ending in snake heads. Each head is serpentine with dark eyes, a forked tongue, and rows of sharp teeth. The body is covered in overlapping dark green or brown scales, a mosaic of crocodile scutes and serpent scales. The tail is long, thick, and used as a blunt weapon. Each head is independently intelligent and can coordinate attacks, but damage to any single head kills the hydra instantly. A hatchling hydra is born with three heads and roughly 5 ft long. As it ages it grows additional heads and greater size.

**Unique Abilities:** Multi-Head Strike (L1), Regeneration (L3), Reactive Heads (L5), Bile Spray (L7), Head Growth (L8)

- **Regeneration (Passive, L3):** At the start of its turn, the hydra regains 2d8 Vitality. If the hydra took Fire damage since its last turn, this regeneration does not happen until its next turn.
- **Reactive Heads (Passive, L5):** The hydra can make one additional Reaction per round for each head beyond the first (starting at 3 heads at L1, gaining additional heads as it ages).
- **Bile Spray (Active, 4 Mana, L7):** The hydra vomits a spray of digestive bile in a 20 ft cone. Creatures in the cone must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 2d8 Poison damage and 2d8 Acid damage. On success, they take half of each.
- **Head Growth (Passive, L8):** The hydra grows a fourth head. It gains an additional Slam attack per round and its Multi-Head Strike targets an additional creature. The hydra's size increases to its maximum dimensions.

**Anzu**
An anzu has the body of a lion, the wings of an eagle, and the head of a serpent. The body is leonine, covered in tawny fur that darkens at the paws and tail. The wings are large and powerful, covered in dark brown feathers with a metallic sheen. The head is serpentine, scaled, and broad, with a forked tongue and eyes that glow with internal lightning. The mouth contains long hollow fangs that channel electrical discharge. The fur grades into scales seamlessly along the neck. The tail is lion-like but tipped with a cluster of spines that crackle with static. An adult anzu is roughly 10 ft long with a wingspan of 24 ft.

**Unique Abilities:** Storm Wings (L1), Thunderclap (L3), Summon Storm (L5), Lightning Hunt (L7)

- **Storm Wings (Passive, L1):** Creatures within 10 ft of the anzu when it flies or takes off take 1d4 Thunder damage and must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or be pushed 5 ft away.
- **Thunderclap (Active, 3 Mana, L3):** The anzu creates a thunderclap centered on itself. All creatures within 20 ft must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 2d6 Thunder damage and be Deafened for 2 turns.
- **Summon Storm (Active, 5 Mana, L5):** The anzu summons a storm cloud in a 60 ft radius centered on itself. The area is heavily obscured by rain. Each turn, the anzu may call a lightning strike on one creature within the storm as a Bonus Action, dealing 2d6 Lightning damage (DEX save DC 10 + Save DC Bonus for half). The storm lasts for 5 turns.
- **Lightning Hunt (Passive, L7):** The anzu's fang attacks deal an additional 1d6 Lightning damage. When the anzu deals Lightning damage to a creature, it may choose to have the lightning arc to a second creature within 10 ft of the first, dealing 1d6 Lightning damage (no save).

**Tarasque**
A tarasque has the body of a massive bull, the shell of a great turtle, the tail of a scorpion, and six legs. The body is covered in thick armored plates, with a domed turtle shell on its back studded with spikes. The head is bull-like with six horns and eyes that burn with inner fire. The scorpion tail is long and segmented, ending in a venomous stinger. Each of the six legs ends in a heavy clawed foot. The shell plates are dark brown and cracked with veins of glowing orange, as if the creature's inner heat bleeds through. An adult tarasque is roughly 20 ft long and stands 10 ft at the shoulder. It is a living siege weapon capable of leveling fortified positions.

**Unique Abilities:** Shell Defense (L1), Retribution Spikes (L3), Tail Sweep (L5), Magma Blood (L7), Cataclysm (L9)

- **Shell Defense (Passive, L1):** As a Bonus Action, the tarasque can retract its head and legs into its shell. While retracted, its AC increases by 4, its speed becomes 0, and it cannot take Actions or Reactions. It can emerge from its shell as a Bonus Action. Emergence deals 2d6 Thunder damage to all creatures within 10 ft.
- **Retribution Spikes (Passive, L3):** Any creature that hits the tarasque with a melee attack takes 1d6 Piercing damage from the spikes on its shell.
- **Tail Sweep (Active, 3 Mana, L5):** The tarasque sweeps its scorpion tail in a 15 ft arc behind it. Creatures in the arc must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 2d8 Piercing damage and be knocked Prone.
- **Magma Blood (Passive, L7):** Any creature that starts its turn grappling the tarasque or being grappled by it takes 2d6 Magma damage. When the tarasque is reduced to half Vitality or below, its blood pools on the ground, creating patches of molten rock that deal 1d6 Magma damage per turn to any creature standing in them.
- **Cataclysm (Active, 6 Mana, L9):** The tarasque slams the ground with all six legs. A 60 ft radius shockwave erupts. All creatures in the area must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 3d8 Bludgeoning damage, be knocked Prone, and be pushed 20 ft away. Structures in the area take triple damage.

**Peryton**
A peryton has the lower body of a great stag and the wings, upper body, and head of a massive eagle. The stag half is covered in brown fur, with four slender but powerful legs ending in sharp hooves. The eagle half rises from the stag's shoulders, covered in dark brown feathers with a white chest. The head is fully avian with a hooked beak and intelligent golden eyes. The wings are broad and powerful. The antlers of the stag are retained and branch outward through the feathers. Perytons cast the shadow of a human rather than their own true form, a quirk of their nature that unnerves all who notice it. An adult peryton is roughly 6 ft long and stands 5 ft at the shoulder, with a wingspan of 16 ft.

**Unique Abilities:** Eagle Eye (L1), Swoop (L1), Man-Shadow (L3), Shadow-Pierce (L5)

- **Man-Shadow (Passive, L3):** The peryton's shadow appears as a human silhouette rather than its own shape. The peryton can use a Bonus Action to swap places with its shadow within 30 ft, teleporting to the shadow's location. The shadow then reforms at the peryton's previous location. This ability can be used once per short rest.
- **Shadow-Pierce (Active, 2 Mana, L5):** The peryton makes a Beak attack that ignores DR. On hit, deal 2d6 Piercing damage and the target's shadow is pinned to the ground. The target cannot move more than 10 ft from its current position for 1 turn, as its shadow is literally nailed in place.
