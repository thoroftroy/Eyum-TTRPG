A Yeti is a massive ape-like creature built for cold climates, standing on two thick legs with a forward-hunched posture and arms long enough to drag its knuckles across the ground even at full height. Its body is covered in a dense double-layered coat: a coarse outer layer of long guard hairs that shed snow and water, and a fine wooly undercoat that traps heat against the skin. The fur color varies by variant, ranging from pure white to deep reddish brown to slate grey, but all share the same dense insulating structure. The face is bare or thinly furred, with a short flat muzzle, deep-set eyes under a heavy brow ridge, and a mouth full of thick blunt teeth built for crushing rather than tearing. The hands and feet are broad and padded, with thick calloused knuckles and short powerful fingers tipped in black nails that are closer to hooves than claws. A Yeti can walk upright or drop to all fours for a burst of speed, knuckle-walking like a gorilla when it needs to close distance quickly.

Yetis are deeply mischievous creatures with no concept of loyalty, property, or fair play. They do not hunt out of malice or hunger alone, they hunt because causing chaos is entertaining. A Yeti will steal a traveler's pack not because it wants the contents but because watching the traveler chase it up a cliff is genuinely fun for the Yeti. They hide tools, untie knots, push boulders down slopes at passing caravans, and mimic distress calls to lure rescuers into dead ends. They throw things constantly, rocks, snowballs, livestock, each other. A Yeti that finds a shiny object will carry it for weeks, showing it off to other Yetis and then forgetting it entirely when something more interesting appears. They do not form attachments and will abandon a companion mid-fight if something more amusing catches their attention. A bonded Yeti is impossible, they cannot comprehend the concept. Even a Yeti raised from infancy will eventually wander off, steal something important, and never come back.

Yetis live in loose troops of 8 to 20 individuals with no clear hierarchy. The troop shares a territory that can span dozens of square miles and moves constantly, never staying in one place for more than a few days. There is no alpha, no leader. Decisions appear to be made by momentum, if one Yeti starts moving in a direction, others follow until someone else starts moving somewhere else, at which point the troop splits and reforms. Fights within the troop are common and usually start over nothing, one Yeti takes a rock another was looking at, and within seconds the entire troop is shrieking and throwing things. These fights are almost never lethal. A Yeti will pummel another into the snow, take whatever started the fight, and then lose interest entirely within minutes.

Yetis breed slowly. A female carries a single infant for 9 months and gives birth in the spring when food is most available. The infant clings to its mother's chest fur for the first year and is weaned at roughly 18 months. It reaches level 1 after its first year and gains 1 level per year from that point onward, same as most creatures in Eyum. They can live up to 50 years in the wild, though most die much younger from fights, avalanches, or picking fights with things much larger than themselves. A female will typically produce 4 to 6 offspring in her lifetime. Yetis do not pair bond. Mating is opportunistic and uncommitted, and males play no role in raising the young. The mother carries her infant everywhere and the troop provides incidental protection simply by being nearby.

When a Yeti is killed its body can be harvested for meat and hide. The meat is stringy, gamey, and deeply unpleasant, eating it requires a CON save (DC 8) to keep it down, and even then it provides no satisfaction. The hide is the valuable part. Yeti hide is thick, flexible, and retains the insulating properties of the living creature's undercoat. When properly treated it makes excellent leather for light armor and cold-weather clothing. The fur layer can be left on for warmth or shaved down for a cleaner leather finish. Higher level Yetis produce thicker, larger hides with better insulation.
###### Variant Descriptions

**Snowback Yeti (DR 4):** The largest and most iconic variant. Stands 9 to 11 ft tall at level 1 with a shoulder width that makes it look nearly square. Its coat is pure white with a faint silver sheen in direct light, blending perfectly into snowfields and glaciers. The guard hairs are long and coarse, up to 8 inches along the back and shoulders, giving the Snowback a shaggy, hunched silhouette. Its face is dark grey with a pinkish cast around the eyes and mouth. The hands are enormous, each palm wide enough to engulf a human head, and the knuckles are covered in thick calloused pads from a lifetime of knuckle-walking across ice and rock. Snowbacks are solitary outside of breeding season, staking out vast territories high in mountain ranges above the treeline where nothing else lives. They are the strongest of the Yetis by a wide margin and know it, a Snowback will not flee from anything. It stands its ground, beats its chest, and dares the intruder to try something. Found only on the highest permanently snow-covered peaks and glacial fields.

**Timber Yeti (DR 3):** A forest-dwelling variant built for climbing rather than bulk. Slightly shorter than a Snowback at 8 to 9 ft tall but with disproportionately long arms that hang past its knees, giving it an orangutan-like silhouette. Its coat is a deep reddish brown that darkens to nearly black along the spine and lightens to rust on the chest and belly. The fur is shorter and denser than the Snowback's, shedding water more effectively for the wetter forest environment. Its hands and feet are more dexterous, with longer fingers and an opposable grip strong enough to snap branches as thick as a human leg. Timber Yetis spend most of their time in the canopy of old-growth northern forests, moving from tree to tree with surprising quiet for something their size. They drop onto prey from above, snatch it, and climb back up before it can react. They are the most mischievous of the variants and will spend hours undoing campsite setups, pulling out tent stakes, scattering firewood, and rearranging supplies into nonsensical piles. They are also the only variant that actively hides from threats rather than confronting them, disappearing into the trees and watching from above with obvious amusement.

**Screecher Yeti (DR 2):** The smallest and most social variant. Stands 6 to 7 ft tall at level 1 with a lean, wiry build and a pronounced muzzle like a baboon. Its coat is a patchy grey-brown, shorter and rougher than other variants, with bare patches on the face, chest, and rump that show dark leathery skin underneath. The face is long and dog-like with a bright pink muzzle and sharp canine teeth visibly longer than other Yeti variants. Screechers live in large troops of 30 to 60 individuals in rocky canyon systems, ravines, and steep cliff faces. They are diurnal and incredibly loud, the troop maintains constant shrieking communication that echoes through the canyons for miles. A Screecher troop hunting is a cascade of sound and motion as dozens of them pour down a cliff face together. They mob larger prey through sheer numbers, biting and tearing and then scattering before the victim can retaliate. A lone Screecher is a nuisance. Thirty of them is a natural disaster. They are the least mischievous and most directly aggressive of the Yetis, driven by food more than entertainment, though they will still steal unattended objects and throw them off cliffs for the fun of watching them fall.

**Frostmane Yeti (DR 3):** A tundra-dwelling variant built for running. Stands 7 to 8 ft tall with a more horizontal posture than other Yetis, often moving on all fours at speed and rising to two legs only to fight or survey territory. Its coat is a pale silver-grey with darker grey stripes running down the back and legs, providing natural camouflage against patchy snow and exposed tundra grass. The most distinctive feature is a thick mane of longer white-grey fur around the neck and shoulders that stands erect when the Frostmane is agitated, making it appear significantly larger. The face is narrow and almost mandrill-like, with a long muzzle and bright blue-grey skin around the eyes and mouth that flushes red with exertion. Frostmanes are pursuit predators, they run prey down across miles of open tundra, loping on all fours at speeds no biped can match. They hunt in small packs of 3 to 6 and coordinate surprisingly well, taking turns harrying the prey while the rest of the pack cuts off escape routes. They are the least mischievous variant, more focused on survival in the harshest environment any Yeti inhabits. The open tundra offers no cover and no room for games. Frostmanes are all business.
###### Unique Abilities
All Yetis share the following unique abilities regardless of variant.

- **Thick Fur (Passive):** The Yeti's dense double-layered coat grants resistance to Ice/Cold damage and advantage on saving throws against environmental cold effects. Additionally the Yeti takes -2 damage from all physical ranged attacks (arrows, bolts, thrown weapons) as the thick fur and hide absorb much of the impact.
- **Hurl (Active, 2 Mana):** The Yeti grabs a nearby object or creature and throws it. If targeting an object of size Small or smaller within reach, the Yeti makes a ranged attack against a target within 30 ft, dealing 1d8 + STR mod bludgeoning damage on hit. If targeting a creature of size Medium or smaller within reach, the target must make a STR save (DC 10 + STR mod) or be Grappled. While grappled this way, the Yeti can use its next action to hurl the creature up to 20 ft. The hurled creature takes 2d6 bludgeoning damage and lands Prone. The grapple ends after the throw whether it hits anything or not.
- **Knuckle Run (Passive):** The Yeti can drop to all fours as part of its movement. While on all fours, its speed increases by 15 ft, but it cannot make melee attacks until it stands upright again. Standing upright is part of movement and does not cost an action.
- **Mischief (Passive):** The Yeti has advantage on checks made to steal, disarm, or otherwise take objects from creatures that are not actively watching it. This includes picking pockets, snatching weapons from belts, and grabbing items from unattended packs. The Yeti cannot use this ability on creatures that are facing it or aware of its presence.

Variant-specific abilities are listed below.

- **Crushing Embrace (Active, 3 Mana):** Snowback Yeti only. The Snowback grabs a creature of size Large or smaller within reach. The target must make a STR save (DC 10 + STR mod) or be Grappled and Restrained. While restrained, at the start of each of the Snowback's turns, the target takes 2d8 + STR mod bludgeoning damage. The target remains grappled until it succeeds on a STR save to break free (DC 10 + STR mod, repeat at the start of each of its turns). The Snowback cannot use its hands for other actions while maintaining this grapple.
- **Canopy Drop (Active, 2 Mana):** Timber Yeti only. The Timber Yeti drops from a height of at least 10 ft onto a creature below. The target must make a DEX save (DC 10 + DEX mod) or take 2d6 + STR mod bludgeoning damage and be knocked Prone. The Timber Yeti takes no falling damage from this drop as long as it lands on the target. If the target succeeds on the save, the Timber Yeti lands in an adjacent space and takes half the normal falling damage.
- **Screeching Cry (Active, 2 Mana):** Screecher Yeti only. The Screecher lets out an earsplitting shriek in a 20 ft cone. Creatures in the cone must make a CON save (DC 10 + CON mod) or take 1d6 thunder damage and be Deafened for 2 turns. Any other Screecher Yetis within 60 ft that hear the cry gain advantage on their next attack roll against the targeted creatures. This does not stack: multiple cries from different Screechers do not grant additional advantage.
- **Tundra Charge (Active, 3 Mana):** Frostmane Yeti only. The Frostmane drops to all fours and charges up to twice its speed in a straight line. Any creature in its path must make a STR save (DC 10 + STR mod) or take 1d10 + STR mod bludgeoning damage and be knocked Prone. If the Frostmane hits at least one creature, it may make a free Slam attack against the last creature it struck at the end of the charge.

**Standard Passives:** Scratch Resistance, Pursuer, Skirmisher
**Standard Actives:** Slam, Multi Attack
