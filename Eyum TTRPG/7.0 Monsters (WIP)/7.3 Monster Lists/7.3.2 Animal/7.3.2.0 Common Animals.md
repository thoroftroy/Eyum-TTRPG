# Common Animals
Common animals are mundane real-world creatures found throughout Eyum on farms, in forests, on plains, in rivers, and in the skies. They are not magical, not elemental, and not undead. They are simply the living beasts that fill the world's ecosystems, domesticated and wild alike. Most are harmless to an armed adventurer. A few are dangerous if provoked or cornered.

Common animals serve three main purposes in the game:
- **Harvesting:** They are the primary source of meat, hide, bone, fat, wool, feathers, eggs, and other organic materials used in crafting and alchemy (see [[2.6 Materials]]).
- **Mounts and Labor:** Horses, donkeys, mules, camels, and oxen pull carts, carry riders, and plow fields (see [[1.5 Mounts and Familiars]]).
- **World Realism:** A forest feels real when there are deer, squirrels, and foxes in it. A farm feels real when there are chickens, pigs, and cows. These creatures make Eyum a living world.

All common animals have Generic Affinity unless noted otherwise. They gain 1 level per year of life. Most live 5 to 40 years depending on species. Their stats reflect the creature at physical maturity (level 1 is a young adult). None of these creatures have Mana pools of any significance, they are not magical beings.

Common animals are not built for combat. Even the dangerous ones (bulls, bears, boars) are acting on instinct, not tactical thought. They will flee when badly injured unless defending young or cornered. They do not use strategy, do not coordinate attacks beyond basic pack hunting, and do not target specific threats. Run them as animals, not as soldiers.
###### Animal Skills
- **Flee Instinct (Passive):** When reduced below 25% Vitality, the animal must use its turn to Dash away from the nearest threat. It will not return to the fight unless cornered or defending young. Domesticated animals that are highly trained (warhorses, guard dogs) may suppress this instinct with a successful Wisdom save (DC 12).
- **Territorial (Passive):** The animal will warn intruders before attacking. It postures, growls, stamps, or charges short distances to intimidate. If the intruder retreats, the animal does not pursue. If the intruder advances, the animal attacks.
- **Grazing/Browsing (Passive):** Herbivores spend most of their waking hours eating. They are not alert to danger while grazing and have disadvantage on Spot checks to notice hidden threats.

- **Spray (Active, 0 Mana):** Skunk only. The skunk releases a foul musk in a 10 ft cone or at a single target within 15 ft. Creatures in the cone must make a CON save (DC 12) or gain the Nauseated condition for 1 hour and have disadvantage on Stealth checks for 24 hours due to the lingering odor. A creature directly hit by the spray has disadvantage on the save. The skunk can spray once per combat (once per hour outside combat). The scent is nearly impossible to wash out with water alone; removing it requires strong soap, alcohol, tomato juice, or similar treatment over 10 minutes. Creatures that rely on scent (wolves, bears, dogs) have disadvantage on this save.
- **Death Roll (Active, 3 Mana):** Crocodile only. The crocodile must have a creature grappled in its jaws (successful Bite attack that the target failed to escape). The crocodile spins its entire body rapidly. The grappled creature takes 2d6+1 bludgeoning damage and must make a STR save (DC 14) or have one of its limbs broken (Injury: Broken Bone). The grapple ends after the roll. This ability can only be used on creatures of size Medium or smaller.
- **Maul (Passive):** Bear only. When a bear hits the same target with both Claw attacks in a single turn (via Multi Attack), the target must make a STR save (DC 12 for black bear, DC 14 for brown bear) or be knocked Prone. The bear's next Bite attack against a Prone target has advantage.
- **Snake Venom (Passive):** Snake only. On a successful Bite attack, the target must make a CON save (DC 10 + snake's level) or gain the Poisoned condition for 1 hour. The snake's venom is not lethal to Medium or larger creatures at level 1, but multiple bites stack the condition duration. At level 5+, the DC increases to 12 + half level and deals 1d4 poison damage per failed save.
- **Stomp (Active, 2 Mana):** Elephant only. The elephant makes a melee attack against a Prone creature or a creature of size Medium or smaller. On hit, the target takes 2d6+3 bludgeoning damage and remains Prone. This attack can be made as a bonus action if the elephant moved at least 10 ft toward the target this turn.

**Standard Passives:** Pack Bonus (wolves, some dogs), Skirmisher (small fast animals), Pursuer (predators)
**Standard Actives:** Slam, Claw, Charge, Spit (skunk only)
