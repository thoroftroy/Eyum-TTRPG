The following templates can be applied to any base monster. Templates can not be stacked with each other. Any stat modifier cannot reduce the stat below 4 and speed cannot be reduced below 5 ft from these modifiers. If a skill is gained which the monster already had nothing happens, if a skill is lost which the monster didn't have nothing happens.

> For condition references see [[2.4 Conditions]]. For damage type references see [[2.2 Damage Types]].

### Zombie
Zombies are reanimated corpses driven by a single impulse: feed. They are mindless, relentless, and feel no pain. Any monster can be zombified, the process strips it of intelligence, will, and most memories, leaving only a rotting shell that attacks the living on sight. Any creature with the Zombie modifier is considered Undead. The zombie modifier cannot be applied to any creature already considered undead. 

| Stat              | Change                    |
| ----------------- | ------------------------- |
| STR and CON       | +4                        |
| DEX               | -4                        |
| INT, WIS, and CHA | -6                        |
| Speed             | Halved (minimum of 10 ft) |
| AC                | -2                        |
| Initiative        | -4                        |
| Mana              | Halved                    |
- **Passive Gained: Creature of Darkness:** If the base creature's Affinity or damage types include Radiant in any form (e.g. Radiant, Hallowed, Solar, Starlight), replace the Radiant portion with Necrotic. If the Affinity was purely Radiant, it becomes Necrotic. Mixed affinities become Necrotic + the non-Radiant components (e.g. Solar (Radiant + Earth) becomes Blight (Necrotic + Earth)). Gains Resistance to Necrotic damage and a weakness to Radiant damage. 
- **Passive Gained:** **Undead Fortitude:** When a damage instance would reduce the zombie to 0 HP, it may make a CON save (DC 5 + damage taken). On a success, it drops to 1 HP instead. If the damage dealt to the zombie which dropped it below 1 HP was radiant damage the zombie automatically fails the save. 
- **Passive Gained:** **Zombie Plod:** The zombie's speed cannot be reduced below its current speed by any effect. It ignores difficult terrain. It cannot take the Dash action.
- **Passive Gained:** **Deathless Vitality:** The zombie is immune to Poison damage, the Poisoned condition, Exhaustion, and the Sickened condition. It does not need to breathe, eat, or sleep. 
- **Passive Gained:** **Mindless:** The zombie is immune to the Frightened, Charmed, and Demoralized conditions. It automatically fails all INT, WIS, and CHA saves against effects that would give it information or read its thoughts (there are none to read). It cannot be reasoned with, intimidated, or persuaded. Any effect which would rely on feeling pain is instantly nullified. 
- **Passive Gained:** **Feeding Hunger:** At the start of each of its turns, the zombie must move toward the nearest living creature and attack it if possible. If multiple living creatures are equidistant, choose randomly. This can be overridden by magical control.
- **Passive Gained:** **Creature without Mana:** All skills, spells, and abilities which require more than 2 + Level mana to cast cannot be used
- **Passive Gained:** **Dead Speak:** The zombie loses all languages except Dead Speak (see [[4.2 Languages]]). It cannot speak or understand any other language. It can understand simple one-word commands in Dead Speak from a necromantic controller, but cannot formulate responses.
## Ghost Template
Ghosts are the incorporeal remnants of a creature that died with unfinished business or under extreme duress. Ghosts retain fragments of memory and intelligence, but their connection to the living world is distorted by death. Any creature with the Ghost modifier is considered Undead. The Ghost modifier cannot be applied to any creature already considered undead. 

| Stat        | Change                      |
| ----------- | --------------------------- |
| STR         | -6                          |
| CON         | -4                          |
| DEX         | +4                          |
| INT         | -2                          |
| WIS and CHA | +4                          |
| Speed       | +20 ft (Flight (Hoverable)) |
| AC          | +2                          |
| Initiative  | +4                          |
- **Passive Gained: Creature of Darkness:** If the base creature's Affinity or damage types include Radiant in any form (e.g. Radiant, Hallowed, Solar, Starlight), replace the Radiant portion with Necrotic. If the Affinity was purely Radiant, it becomes Necrotic. Mixed affinities become Necrotic + the non-Radiant components (e.g. Solar (Radiant + Earth) becomes Blight (Necrotic + Earth)). Gains Resistance to Necrotic, Cold, and all Physical damage and a weakness to Radiant damage. 
- **Passive Gained: Unfinished Business:** The ghost is here for unfinished business, be that revenge or protection of something or just being dissatisfied with its death. If the ghost feels satisfied it will fade away into nothing leaving behind a Soul Fragment. Killing the ghost will not provide a soul fragment. The ghost does not have to be satisfied with its unfinished business to fade, it simply has to be satisfied in general, but it's typically easiest to just help with their business. See [[2.6 Materials]] for more information.  
- **Passive Gained: Ghost Form:** The ghost is immune to the Prone, Grappled, and Restrained conditions. Any attacks that would normally do physical damage from the ghost have their damage type changed to necrotic. 
- **Passive Gained: Ghostly Touch:** The ghost's melee attacks ignore physical armor (AC from armor and shields is not counted). The ghost can interact with physical objects in a very limited way, it can only exert up to 10 lbs of force per STR mod on anything at a minimum of 10 lbs.
- **Passive Gained:** **Incorporeal:** The ghost can move through solid objects (walls, creatures, terrain) as if they were difficult terrain. It cannot interact with physical objects directly (cannot open doors, pick up items, etc.) unless it uses Ghostly Touch.
- **Passive Gained:** **Terrifying Presence:** Creatures that start their turn within 30 ft of the ghost must make a WIS save (DC 10 + ghost's CHA modifier) or be Frightened until the start of their next turn. A creature that succeeds is immune to this ghost's Terrifying Presence for 24 hours.
- **Passive Gained:** **Spiritual Echo:** If the ghost is reduced to 0 HP, it fades into mist and reforms 1d4 hours later at a bound location (its place of death, its remains, or a place significant to its unfinished business). If the damage source which caused its death was Radiant it is destroyed permanently. 
- **Passive Gained:** **Ethereal Sight:** The ghost can see invisible creatures and objects. It is aware of all living creatures within 300 ft.
- **Passive Gained:** **Dead Memory:** The ghost retains fragments of its former life. It understands Dead Speak and one language it knew in life. It may remember faces, places, and its unfinished business, but little else. 
- **Passive Gained:** **Unnatural Cold:** The area within 20 ft of the ghost is unnaturally cold. Non-magical flames are extinguished. Living creatures in the area feel a deep chill (narrative effect; at the GM's discretion, may impose disadvantage on concentration checks).
- **Active Gained:** **Withering Touch (5 Mana):** Make a melee touch attack. On hit, deal 2d6 Necrotic damage and the target's maximum Vitality is reduced by the damage dealt until a long rest.
- **Active Gained:** **Terrifying Visage (7 Mana):** All living creatures in the cone (60 ft) must make a WIS save (DC 10 + CHA mod) or be Frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns.
- **Active Gained:** **Possession (4 Mana per turn):** Choose one living creature within 30 ft. It must make a CHA save (DC 10 + CHA mod). On a failure, the ghost enters its body and controls it for 1 minute. While possessing, the ghost's body becomes invisible and invulnerable. If the host is reduced to 0 HP, the ghost is expelled. The host remembers nothing of the possession. The ghost can use any of the skills or abilities of the creature it is possessing but none of its own skills or abilities. If the ghost runs out of mana the possession ends early.
