An Aboleth is an aquatic creature with a broad flat body, four long primary tentacles ringing its mouth, two thinner manipulator tentacles near the front, and a paddle-shaped tail that drives it through the water. Its skin is smooth and slick, somewhere between a fish and an eel in texture. The most distinctive feature is its three eyes, arranged in a horizontal row across the front of its face. All three eyes are the same size and color, each about as wide as a human palm. They do not blink. They glow faintly in dim water. The mouth is hidden beneath the tentacles and is filled with rows of small sharp teeth. All Aboleths share the same basic body plan regardless of their variant, but each variant's size, color, texture, and specific adaptations differ dramatically based on the environment and affinity of the individual. The variants are not separate species. Any Aboleth can theoretically produce offspring of any variant type if the offspring buds in an environment that triggers that particular affinity development. The variant is determined at the moment of budding, not inherited.

Aboleths reproduce asexually through budding. A mature Aboleth develops a small growth on its underside that gradually separates into a fully formed but miniature Aboleth over the course of several weeks. The bud is genetically identical to the parent but may develop into a different variant if the environmental conditions favor a different affinity. A single Aboleth can bud once every few years. The offspring reaches level 1 after about one year of growth and is considered mature at level 3, at which point it can begin budding itself.

Aboleths are social creatures that form loose groups called schools. A school typically contains 10 to 30 individuals of various types, usually clustering around a specific water source or region. Within the school there is no hierarchy. They simply coexist, drifting together and occasionally touching tentacles in what appears to be communication. They are not aggressive by nature but will defend their school and their territory against intruders. They are about as intelligent as an octopus and can solve simple problems, recognize individual humans, and hold grudges against specific people who have harmed them or their school.

An Aboleth gains 1 level per year. Their bodies grow slowly over time, adding mass and length. The tentacles lengthen and the eyes grow larger. Higher level Aboleths are simply older individuals. There is no upper limit to how long an Aboleth can live or how high its level can go. Two specific variants occupy unique positions in the growth path: an Ice/Cold Aboleth that reaches level 50 or higher may evolve into a Glacial Aboleth. Any Aboleth of any variant that reaches level 100 or higher while in prolonged proximity to strong necrotic energy may evolve into a Corruption Aboleth. These evolutions are permanent and cannot be reversed.

When an Aboleth is killed its body can be harvested for several useful materials. The meat is edible but mildly toxic, producing a pufferfish-like numbing high if eaten without proper preparation. The blood is a thick clear fluid that can be used as a lubricant or oil which does not catch fire. The three eyes are the most valuable part. Each variant's eyes retain a spark of the Aboleth's affinity and can be used to enhance magic of that element, growing more powerful the higher level the Aboleth was when it died. Some variants have additional unique materials.
###### Variant Descriptions

**Water Aboleth (DR 1):** The baseline form. Pale blue-grey skin, smooth and slick, with a faint silvery shimmer along its sides. About 3 ft long at level 1, small enough to be held in two hands. Its three eyes are a calm sky blue. The tentacles are short and delicate, suited more for grasping small prey than for combat. Found in lakes, slow rivers, and shallow coastal waters. It is shy and will flee from anything larger than itself. Its presence in a body of water is considered a good omen by fishermen because it only lives in clean water.

**Mud Aboleth (DR 3):** A stunted, squat variant with rough pebbly brown skin that looks like wet river stones. Slightly smaller than the Water Aboleth at about 2.5 ft long. Its eyes are a dull amber brown. Its body is flatter and wider than other variants, and its underside is covered in small suction pads that let it cling to rocks and drag itself across land. On land it moves by rolling and flopping sideways in a slow ungainly motion, leaving a trail of slime behind it. It can survive out of water for up to 6 hours as long as its skin stays damp. Found in swamps, marshes, muddy riverbanks, and shallow ponds. Often shares habitat with Poison Aboleths. It is placid and hard to provoke, preferring to bury itself in mud and wait for threats to pass.

**Torrent Aboleth (DR 4):** A sleek streamlined variant built for speed. Its skin is a dark navy blue with bright white streaks running from behind the eyes to the tail, giving it the appearance of rushing water. About 4 ft long and noticeably more muscular than the Water variant. Its tail is broader and more powerful, and its body tapers more sharply. Found in fast-moving rivers, rapids, and strong ocean currents. It is energetic and restless, constantly swimming even when there is nowhere to go. It will chase anything that moves near it out of instinct rather than aggression, releasing it once the intruder has left its territory.

**Ice/Cold Aboleth (DR 5):** A pale variant that looks almost carved from ice. Its skin is white-blue and slightly translucent, and a thin layer of frost constantly coats its body even in water. Its three eyes are a pale crystalline blue with no visible pupil. About 5 ft long. The tentacles are shorter and stiffer than other variants, the cold having thickened the tissue. Found in frozen oceans, under sea ice, and in deep cold water trenches. Its body temperature is below freezing and the water around it is always several degrees colder than the surrounding area. At level 50 or higher, an Ice/Cold Aboleth evolves into a Glacial Aboleth.

**Poison Aboleth (DR 5):** The most colorful variant. Its skin is patterned in bright warning colors, alternating bands of vibrant yellow and deep green with occasional spots of orange. About 4 ft long. Its three eyes are a vivid lime green. The skin constantly secretes a thin layer of mild neurotoxin that causes numbness and disorientation on contact. The tentacles have slightly swollen tips that contain a higher concentration of the toxin. Found in rainforest pools, jungle rivers, swamps, and stagnant water. Often lives alongside Mud Aboleths, the two variants forming mixed schools. It is more aggressive than other Aboleths, willing to stand its ground and bite when threatened rather than flee.

**Deluge Aboleth (DR 8):** A deep-water variant that embodies crushing pressure. Its skin is deep blue-black, almost black, with faint bioluminescent dots along its flanks that pulse slowly in patterns. About 6 ft long and built heavier and thicker than any other base variant. Its eyes are a deep navy, nearly black but glowing faintly. The water pressure around it feels wrong, heavier than it should be, as if the ocean itself is pressing down on anything near it. Found only in the deepest parts of the ocean, far below where light reaches. It moves slowly and deliberately, never hurrying. It is the most intelligent of the baseline variants and has been observed using simple tools.

**Glacial Aboleth (DR 18):** The final form of an Ice/Cold Aboleth that has survived past level 50. Massive and ancient, 20 ft or longer. Its body is nearly solid white with glowing blue veins of frozen light pulsing beneath the skin. The eyes are bright white with no pupil, like three small suns of cold light. Every surface of its body is sheathed in a layer of frozen water that never melts, giving it the appearance of being made from ice rather than flesh. The water within 30 ft of a Glacial Aboleth is cold enough to cause frostbite on contact. It radiates a profound stillness, as if even sound and time slow down near it. Found only in the deepest frozen trenches and under ancient polar ice. One of the most powerful creatures in the ocean.

**Corruption Aboleth (DR 25):** Formed when any Aboleth of any variant reaches level 100 or higher while exposed to strong necrotic energy for an extended period. The body becomes bloated and the smooth skin turns a sick purple-black, covered in weeping sores and patches of regenerating tissue. The three eyes fuse partially into a single swirling mass of purple corruption, though three distinct pupils can still be seen moving independently within it. It is larger than a Glacial Aboleth, often exceeding 30 ft. Its tentacles have grown additional smaller tendrils along their length, each one twitching independently. A Corruption Aboleth is always alone. Schools will not tolerate one in their midst and will flee or die rather than stay near it. It is not more aggressive than other variants but its mere presence corrupts the water around it, causing fish to die and rot within hours.

###### Unique Abilities
All Aboleths share the following unique ability regardless of variant.
- **Aboleth Mucus (Passive):** The Aboleth's skin constantly produces a slick coating of mucus. The Aboleth has advantage on checks to escape grapples and cannot be restrained by nonmagical means such as ropes, nets, or chains. Any creature that strikes the Aboleth with a melee attack must make a DEX save (DC 8 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take -4 to their damage roll. 

Variant-specific unique abilities are listed below each variant's description.

- **Mudslide (Active, 5 Mana):** Mud Aboleth only. The Aboleth secretes a wave of slick mud in a 15 ft cone. All creatures in the cone must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or fall Prone. The affected area becomes difficult terrain for 3 turns.
- **Torrent Jet (Active, 5 Mana):** Torrent Aboleth only. The Aboleth propels itself with a burst of water, moving up to twice its speed in a straight line. Any creature in its path must make a STR save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d8 + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] bludgeoning damage and be pushed 10 ft to the side.
- **Freezing Pulse (Active, 5 Mana):** Ice/Cold and Glacial Aboleths only. The Aboleth releases a burst of cold in a 10 ft radius. Creatures in the area must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d8 + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] ice/cold damage and have their speed reduced by 10 ft for 2 turns.
- **Neurotoxic Cloud (Active, 10 Mana):** Poison Aboleth only. The Aboleth releases a cloud of neurotoxin in a 20 ft radius. Creatures in the area must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus) or take 1d6 + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] poison damage and gain the Poisoned condition for 3 turns. Creatures that fail the save by 5 or more also gain the Confused condition for 1 turn.
- **Crushing Pressure (Active, 10 Mana):** Deluge Aboleth only. The Aboleth focuses water pressure on a single creature within 30 ft. The target must make a CON save (DC 10 + Save DC Bonus). On a failure it takes 2d8 + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] deluge damage and is Stunned for 1 turn. On a success it takes half damage and is not Stunned.
- **Glacial Prison (Active, 16 Mana):** Glacial Aboleth only. The Aboleth freezes a 20 ft radius area of water solid, trapping any creatures inside. Creatures in the area must make a STR save (DC 15 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or become Restrained in solid ice. They may repeat the save at the start of each of their turns. The ice melts after 3 turns or if exposed to fire damage of 20 or more.
- **Corrupting Gaze (Active, 12 Mana):** Corruption Aboleth only. The Aboleth fixes all three of its corrupted eyes on a single creature within 30 ft. The target must make a WIS save (DC 15 + Save DC Bonus). On a failure it takes 2d10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] corruption damage and gains the Corrupt condition for 3 turns. On a success it takes half damage.

**Standard Passives:** Affinity Body, Regeneration, Creature of Mana
**Standard Actives:** Slam, Burst Attack, Spit, Pull, Rooting Strike
