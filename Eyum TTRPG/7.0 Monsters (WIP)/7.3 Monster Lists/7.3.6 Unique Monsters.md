# Phoenix
A phoenix is a large bird covered in feathers that shift between solid orange and gold plumage and open flame depending on the bird's mood. At rest the feathers lie flat and look like normal bright feathers with a heat shimmer rising off them. When the phoenix is angry, frightened, or fighting, the same feathers ignite into jets of fire that do not burn the bird but will set fire to anything else they touch. The flames are hottest at the feather tips and cool toward the base where they attach to the skin. 

The body is built like a heavy bird of prey. Two legs end in four talons each, one pointing backward for gripping prey and branches. The talons are always hot enough to scorch wood and leave glowing marks on stone. The beak is hooked and sharp, colored dark grey with an orange glow along the cutting edge. The eyes are bright yellow with no visible pupil, glowing softly in dim conditions. The wings are broad and long, built for soaring more than quick turns. Each feather has a stiff central spine that keeps its shape even when fully on fire. The tail feathers are extra long and trail behind the bird like burning streamers. The whole body gives off a constant dry heat that can be felt from several feet away.

Phoenixes are social birds that live in large flocks called courts. A single court can contain anywhere from twenty to over a hundred birds of all ages and types. They nest together in colonies built into the sides of active volcanoes, along geothermal vent fields, in dense tropical jungles near hot springs, and on sunbaked cliff faces in hot coastal regions. The specific environment determines what type of phoenix the adult birds become. A court builds its nests on ledges and in hollows, layering them with packed ash, dried tropical leaves, and shed feathers from the whole flock. The nests are reused generation after generation, growing into large communal structures over time. Eggs are laid in a central brooding chamber where the temperature stays between 100 and 150 degrees Fahrenheit from the body heat of the birds tending them. Incubation takes 4 months. A hatchling grows about 1 ft in length and gains 5 lbs per month during its first year, reaching level 1 after roughly 12 months. The entire court works together to raise the young. Chicks that hatch together form a single creche and every adult in the court takes turns bringing them food and watching for predators. There is no concept of individual parentage. All adults treat all chicks as the court's collective responsibility. This social structure is what allows phoenixes to survive in harsh environments where a single bird raising young alone would fail.

Phoenixes are notably intelligent for animals, roughly on par with a clever dog or a young child. They are curious about everything and have a strong mischievous streak. A phoenix will steal bright objects, hide them in its nest, and then watch with obvious amusement as the owner searches for them. Despite this they are deeply loyal. A phoenix that bonds with a person will follow them everywhere, perch on their shoulder, and refuse to leave their side even when shooed away. They are reckless in a way that suggests they know they can come back from death. A phoenix will dive into dangerous situations without hesitation, fight creatures far larger than itself, and throw itself into harm's way to protect its bonded companion. This combination of loyalty, bravery, mischief, and affection makes them highly sought after as pets and companions. The only reason they are not more common is that they are difficult to acquire and expensive to feed. A well bonded phoenix will never willingly leave its person and will fly across the continent to find them if separated.

The bond between a phoenix and its person forms at hatching. A phoenix that hatches from an egg that was incubated and raised by a single individual will imprint on that person and treat them as part of its court for life. Phoenixes raised in a natural court can still be befriended but the process is much harder and requires earning the trust of the entire court, not just one bird. However once a court accepts an outsider, all the birds in it will treat that person as one of their own.

A phoenix gains 1 level per year of life. Each level increases the bird's size, power, and abilities as shown in the growth table. Beyond normal leveling, a phoenix can evolve into a more advanced form when it dies. Each time a phoenix would be reduced to 0 HP, it must make a CON save against the DC for its current type. On a success, the body burns away in a flash and the phoenix reforms at the start of its next turn at full Vitality and Health in its next evolutionary stage. On a failure, the phoenix dies permanently and its body crumbles into ash. A phoenix must have reached a minimum level in its current form before it can attempt to evolve. If a Phoenix dies before reaching that minimum they simply stay their same form on revival. The save DC gets higher for more advanced forms, making each evolution harder to achieve than the last. A court with even one or two Elders is considered ancient and powerful.

The evolution path and minimum level requirements are as follows. A hatchling starts as an Ash Phoenix. An Ash Phoenix cannot evolve until level 3. Once it reaches level 3, it may attempt to evolve into a Blaze Phoenix on its next death by making the CON save. A Blaze Phoenix must reach level 10 before it can evolve into a true Phoenix. Phoenixes cannot breed until they reach at least the stage of Phoenix. Blaze Phoenixes and Ash Phoenixes are too young to breed. A Phoenix must also reach level 10 before it can evolve into a branch form determined by the court's environment. Phoenixes in volcanic regions become Magma Phoenixes. Those in corrupted or hellish environments become Infernal Phoenixes. Those in tropical high altitude or sunbaked regions become Solar Phoenixes. A branch form must reach level 30 before it can evolve into its Elder variant. An Elder Phoenix is the final stage for that branch. It cannot evolve further, but it can continue leveling indefinitely with no maximum level. When an Elder Phoenix reaches level 50 or higher and feels its fire beginning to dim, it may choose to accept its end instead of rolling a death save. Its body shimmers into golden light and disappears. This ascension leaves behind a single Phoenix Tear.

When a phoenix dies at any stage, whether it succeeds or fails its death save, it drops 1d4 Phoenix Feathers at the location where it fell. These feathers glow faintly and stay warm indefinitely. They are used by crafters to augment fire magic items, boost fire affinity in enchanting, and create heat resistant gear. The feathers are the only thing a phoenix leaves behind when it dies. The body is consumed entirely by the flames of its own death, leaving no meat, bones, claws, or hide. A Phoenix Tear is only produced when an Elder Phoenix ascends voluntarily, never from a normal death.

A Phoenix Egg that has been separated from its court can still be hatched artificially, though the process is difficult. The egg must be kept between 100 and 150 degrees Fahrenheit for the full 4 month incubation period. More importantly it must be fed a steady supply of fire mana every day. The person incubating the egg must channel at least 5 mana per day into the egg using a Fire, Ash, Magma, Solar, or Infernal affinity (Must have greater than 10 in the respective affinity, channeling the mana costs an action). If a day is missed the incubation timer goes back by one week. After 4 months of consistent feeding the egg will hatch into an Ash Phoenix that imprints on the first creature it sees and becomes a loyal familiar to that person. A Phoenix will eat 4 rations a day while in its growth stages then 3 a day for the rest of their lives. If not fed properly for a day they gain a level of exhaustion per day, death from exhaustion will result in the phoenix automatically failing its revival save.

**Evolution CON save DCs by type**: Ash Phoenix DC 10, Blaze Phoenix DC 12, Phoenix DC 15, Magma/Infernal/Solar Phoenix DC 28, Elder Phoenix DC 20.
###### Unique Abilities
The following abilities are unique to phoenixes. Branch types and Elders unlock additional abilities as shown in their stat block.

- **Cycle of Flame (Passive):** When this phoenix would be reduced to 0 HP, it makes a CON save against the DC for its type. On a success, the body burns away in a flash. All creatures within 10 + (Fire Affinity / 5) ft must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 1d6 + Affinity fire damage. The phoenix then reforms at the start of its next turn at full Vitality and Health in its next evolutionary stage (or the same stage if already an Elder or does not meet its evolution requirement). On a failure, the phoenix dies permanently. Whether the save succeeds or fails, the phoenix drops 1d4 Phoenix Feathers where it fell. 
- **Phoenix Fire (Passive):** The phoenix's fire damage ignores Lesser Resistance. Creatures with full Resistance treat it as Lesser Resistance instead. Creatures with Immunity treat it as Resistance instead.
- **Solar Flare (Active, 3 Mana):** 20 ft radius burst centered on the phoenix. All creatures in the area must make a CON save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 2d8 + Affinity fire damage and be Blinded for 1 turn. Creatures that succeed take half damage and are not Blinded.
- **Wings of the Sun (Active, 2 Mana):** The phoenix beats its wings, sending out a wave of fire in a 15 ft cone. Creatures in the cone take 1d8 + Affinity fire damage and must make a STR save (DC 10 + Affinity) or be pushed 10 ft away and knocked Prone.
- **Final Ascension (Passive):** When an Elder Phoenix reaches level 50 or higher and would normally need to roll a death save, it may choose to accept its end instead. Its body shimmers into golden light and disappears, leaving behind a single Phoenix Tear. This is a peaceful death and does not drop Phoenix Feathers.
- **Searing Talons (Passive):** The phoenix's melee attacks deal an additional 1d4 fire damage.

# Unicorn
A unicorn is a horse with a single straight horn growing from the center of its forehead. Its body is built exactly like a riding horse with a deep chest, strong legs, flowing mane and tail, and a coat of fine short hair. The color of the coat depends on the unicorn's type. Flame Unicorns have coats the color of dried red clay with orange manes. Water Unicorns are a deep blue-green with darker teal manes. Air Unicorns are pale grey with white manes that seem to shift even when there is no wind. Earth Unicorns are brown with a dark dun stripe running down the spine and black manes. Light Unicorns are pure white with no blemishes or discoloration of any kind, their horns are pale gold and seem to glow faintly. Dark Unicorns are a deep violet black with manes the color of smoke, their horns are dark grey.

The horn is the defining feature. It is a single straight spiral that grows from the forehead and can reach up to 2 ft in length on an adult. The horn is made of a dense crystalline material that is harder than steel but lighter than bone. It is infused with the unicorn's elemental affinity from the moment it first emerges, which happens when the foal reaches level 1. Before that the horn is a soft nub of cartilage. The horn is the only truly magical part of the unicorn. Without it the unicorn is just a horse. The horn channels the unicorn's affinity magic and is the source of all its special abilities.

Unicorns are friendly toward creatures that are not evil. They will approach strangers out of curiosity and allow themselves to be petted and fed. They do not start fights and will only attack if cornered or if their foals are threatened. This makes them popular as mounts and companions, though they are expensive and somewhat rare. A unicorn will bond with a single rider for life and refuse to carry anyone else. Unicorns cannot be forced to do anything they do not want to do. They are intelligent enough to understand simple commands and complex requests, roughly on par with a well trained dog.

Unicorns breed like normal horses. A mare carries a single foal for 11 months and gives birth to one foal at a time. Twins are extremely rare. The foal reaches level 1 after about 2 years and is considered an adult at level 5, at which point it can breed and its horn is fully developed. Unicorns gain 1 level per year of life, same as most animals. They typically live up to 60 years, with some reaching 80 in protected environments.

When a unicorn is killed it can be harvested like any large animal. The horn is the only truly magical part and by far the most valuable. Their horns augment all spells by adding damage of their specific type even to spells which normally would not deal that damage type. The hide is a high-quality leather that takes dye well and is prized for decorative armor and clothing. The meat is edible and tastes like lean beef. The rest of the body has no special value beyond what any horse carcass would provide.
###### Unique Abilities
All unicorns share the following abilities based on their type.

- **Horn of [Affinity] (Passive):** The unicorn's horn is infused with its elemental affinity. When the unicorn hits with its Slam attack, it deals an additional 1d4 affinity damage. When the unicorn uses Charge, the target's STR save DC to avoid being knocked Prone increases by the unicorn's Affinity modifier.
- **Unicorn's Charge (Active, 2 Mana):** The unicorn moves up to its speed in a straight line and makes a Slam attack at the end. If the attack hits, it deals an additional 1d6 damage and the target must make a STR save (DC 10 + Affinity) or be knocked Prone.
- **Flame Gallop (Active, 2 Mana):** Flame Unicorn only. The unicorn's hooves ignite with flame for 1 turn. Its speed increases by 10 ft and any creature it moves adjacent to during this movement takes 1d4 fire damage.
- **Cleansing Touch (Active, 2 Mana):** Water Unicorn only. The unicorn touches a creature with its horn. The target is cured of one poison, one disease, or the Bleeding condition. If none of those are present, the target instead heals 1d8 + Affinity Vitality.
- **Wind Step (Active, 1 Mana):** Air Unicorn only. The unicorn's speed increases by 20 ft for 1 turn. It can move through enemy spaces without provoking opportunity attacks during this movement.
- **Tremor Stomp (Active, 2 Mana):** Earth Unicorn only. The unicorn stomps the ground. All creatures within 10 ft must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 1d4 bludgeoning damage and be knocked Prone.
- **Radiant Burst (Active, 3 Mana):** Light Unicorn only. The unicorn's horn flares with bright light. All creatures within 15 ft must make a CON save (DC 10 + Affinity) or be Blinded for 1 turn. Undead and Fiends take 1d6 + Affinity radiant damage on a failed save in addition to the Blindness.
- **Shadow Strike (Active, 2 Mana):** Dark Unicorn only. The unicorn's horn is coated in shadows. Make a Slam attack with Advantage. On hit, deal an additional 1d6 necrotic damage and the target cannot regain Vitality until the end of its next turn.
- **Noble Heart (Passive):** Light and Dark Unicorns only. The unicorn cannot be affected by alignment based spells or effects, such as Hallowed Iron or Infernal Iron damage or spells that specifically target good or evil creatures.

# Banshee
A banshee is the visible spirit of a powerful humanoid that died in a way that left its soul trapped between life and death. The body is gone. What remains is a translucent figure that looks like the person it used to be, but faded to a pale blue-white and lit from within by a dim cold light. The banshee appears to be wearing the clothes it died in, though these are just as spectral as the body. Its feet do not touch the ground. It floats a few inches above whatever surface is below it, and it moves without any sound of footsteps.

The face of a banshee is frozen in the expression it wore at the moment of death. A person who was betrayed may show shock and hurt. One who died angry may have a look of permanent rage. One who died screaming will have its mouth wide open in a silent wail that never closes. The eyes glow with the same cold light as the rest of the body and do not blink. The banshee's hair drifts and moves as if underwater even in still air.

Banshees do not breathe, eat, sleep, or age. They are already dead. Each year a banshee exists counts as one level. A banshee that has been haunting for 10 years is level 10. They do not grow physically because they have no physical body to grow. What increases with level is the density and power of the spirit. Higher level banshees are more solid looking, their light burns brighter, and their scream becomes more destructive. A level 1 banshee is barely visible, little more than a heat shimmer with a face. A level 15 banshee looks almost solid, its features sharp and clear, and its scream can shatter stone.

Banshees cannot breed. They are not alive and cannot reproduce. Every banshee that exists was once a living person who died in a way that left its soul anchored to the world of the living. The most common causes are betrayal by a trusted person, death while protecting someone who then abandoned them, death in a fire or drowning that they could have escaped if help had come, or any death where the person's last emotion was not fear but fury or despair. Not everyone who dies this way becomes a banshee, but those who do are stuck until something releases them. A banshee can be permanently killed by reducing it to 0 HP, at which point its soul finally passes on and it leaves behind a Soul Fragment.

Banshees are not social. They do not form groups or communicate with each other. Each one haunts a specific location tied to its death, and it will not leave that area willingly. A banshee that forms in a burned down house will stay in the ruins of that house. One that drowned in a specific river bend will stay on that bend. They do not hunt or seek out victims. They simply exist in their space, and any living creature that enters that space will be screamed at until it leaves or dies.
###### Unique Abilities
All banshees share the following abilities. Higher level banshees unlock additional scream abilities at specific level thresholds.

- **Phase Skin (Passive):** The first instance of damage the banshee takes each round is halved. 
- **Creature of Mana (Passive):** If the banshee's Mana falls below 1, it takes 1d4 true damage at the start of its turn. 
- **Unstable Core (Passive):** When the banshee is reduced to 0 HP, its spirit collapses and releases a burst of necrotic energy. All creatures within 10 ft must make a DEX save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 1d6 necrotic damage.
- **Unnatural Movement (Passive):** The banshee has a flying speed equal to its listed speed and can hover. It ignores difficult terrain and can move through creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. If it ends its turn inside a solid object, it is shunted to the nearest open space and takes 1d10 force damage.
###### Level Threshold Abilities
A banshee unlocks new scream abilities as it gains levels. All abilities listed below are available to a banshee that has reached the listed level.

- **Level 1: Wail (Active, 3 Mana):** The banshee opens its mouth and releases a piercing scream in a 30 ft cone. Creatures in the cone must make a CON save (DC 10 + Affinity) or take 10 + Affinity shatter damage and be Deafened for 1 turn. Creatures that succeed take half damage and are not Deafened.
- **Level 5: Lament (Active, 6 Mana):** The banshee's scream shifts into a low mournful sound that fills a 30 ft radius centered on the banshee. Creatures in the area must make a WIS save (DC 15 + Affinity) or become Frightened and demoralized for 3 turns and take 20 + Affinity shatter damage. 
- **Level 10: Death Scream (Active, 10 Mana):** The banshee focuses its scream into a single point. Choose a creature within 30 ft. The target must make a CON save (DC 10 + Affinity). On a failure, the target takes 50 + Affinity shatter damage. On a success, the target takes half damage.
- **Level 20: Wail of the Damned (Passive):** The banshee's Wail ability improves. The cone increases to 40 ft, damage increases by 20 and creatures that fail the save also gain the Withered condition for 1 turn.
- **Level 50: Death's Embrace (Active, 30 Mana):** The banshee pours enormous amounts of energy into one scream. Choose a creature within 30 ft. The target must make a CON save (DC 15 + Affinity). On a failure, the target takes 100 + Affinity Shatter damage, ignoring resistances and immunities. A creature killed this way cannot be revived by any means short of divine intervention. On a success, the target takes half damage. After using this ability, the banshee is Stunned for 1 turn.
