A Death Worm is a massive segmented undead worm that burrows through mass graves, ancient battlefields, and any earth saturated with centuries of decay. A Death Worm is not born into its final form; it begins as a small crawling thing barely longer than a man's arm and grows over decades into a colossal underground predator. Every Death Worm is the same species at a different stage of life. The variant names denote age and size, not separate creature types. A Death Worm of any age can live indefinitely and never dies of old age.

The body is a long cylindrical tube of ringed segments, each segment a distinct band of muscle and chitin that contracts and expands independently, allowing the worm to push through soil and stone with a rippling peristaltic motion. The body is smooth and glistening, coated in a thin film of mucus that lubricates its passage through the earth and slowly dissolves organic matter on contact. As a Death Worm ages and grows, its coloration darkens: a newly hatched worm is pale grey-white, a mature worm deepens to dark reds and purples, and an ancient Elder is near-black with faint green veins of blighted energy pulsing along the segmented seams. The head is the widest segment, dominated entirely by a circular mouth that grows proportionally with the worm's overall size. The inside of the mouth is lined with concentric rings of inward-angled teeth that prevent anything entering from backing out. Behind the teeth, the throat opens into a stomach chamber filled with blighted digestive fluid that dissolves flesh, bone, and metal alike. The worm has no eyes. It navigates entirely through vibration sensing along its entire body length and mana signature detection concentrated in the head segment. Vegetation above a Death Worm's tunnel dies within days, the soil turning grey and barren from the blight seeping out of the worm's body.

A Death Worm reproduces by splitting itself exactly in half. This is not a parent-offspring relationship; both halves are perfect equals, each retaining all memories, knowledge, and instincts of the original. Neither copy is the original and neither is the child, the original worm ceases to exist and is replaced by two identical copies. Each copy has exactly half the original's level (rounded down) and half the original's weight and length. Each copy is a fully restored new creature at its new level with full Vitality, Health, and Mana. A Lesser Death Worm at level 1 splits into two level 0.5 worms, each with half the level 1 stats (3d6 Vit, 1d6 HP, 2d6 Mana, half weight and length), fully healed. Level 0.5 worms use the Lesser stat block with all values halved; they gain no benefits from leveling until they reach level 1 through natural growth after 1 day. If a copy's halved weight falls within the weight range of a younger variant, that copy de-evolves to that variant at its half level, fully healed. A Death Worm can split voluntarily as an action at any time. A Death Worm will never voluntarily split if doing so would de-evolve it to a younger variant, but it can be forced to by damage. Splitting requires the worm to be at least twice the minimum weight of its variant; attempting to split below this threshold kills both halves.

The involuntary split is a defensive reaction to physical trauma. Any damage that reduces the worm's Health has a chance to trigger an uncontrolled split: when the worm takes slashing damage to Health it must make a CON save (DC 5 + the damage taken). When the worm takes any other damage type to Health it must make a CON save (DC 15 + the damage taken). On a failure, the worm involuntarily splits using the same rules as the Split ability. The worm's body convulses and tears apart at the injury site, each half sealing the wound with blighted tissue and becoming independent within seconds. A worm forced to de-evolve this way does not choose it, but the resulting younger worms are fully functional.

Death Worms gain 1 level per year. They grow slowly, adding length and girth with each level within their current variant. A Lesser Death Worm that started at 3 ft and 8 lbs will reach about 6 ft and 50 lbs by level 6. If it continues to level past the variant's maximum, its weight eventually crosses the threshold into the next variant's weight range, at which point it undergoes a natural growth surge and evolves to the next stage, resetting to level 1 of the new variant. When a Death Worm of any variant is killed, its body can be harvested for Death Worm Mucus, the blighted lubricant coating its skin. See [[2.6 Materials#Special-Materials]] for harvesting and usage. The body dissolves into foul brown sludge within an hour of death.
###### Variant Weight Ranges
A Death Worm's variant is determined entirely by its current weight. These weight bands determine when a worm evolves (by outgrowing its band through leveling) or de-evolves (by splitting below its band's minimum).

| Variant            | Weight Range   | Size   | Max Level |
| ------------------ | -------------- | ------ | --------- |
| Lesser Death Worm  | 1 - 49 lbs     | Small  | 6         |
| Death Worm         | 50 - 99 lbs    | Medium | 10        |
| Greater Death Worm | 500 - 4999 lbs | Large  | 20        |
| Elder Death Worm   | 5000+ lbs      | Huge   | None      |

A worm that exceeds its variant's weight range through leveling naturally evolves to the next variant at level 1. A worm that drops below its variant's minimum weight through splitting de-evolves to the previous variant at its half level. An Elder Death Worm can continue growing indefinitely past level 20, but most Elders choose to split once their weight reaches roughly twice the variant's minimum (around 10,000 lbs) rather than continue growing.

**Lesser Death Worm (DR 1/2):** A young Death Worm in its first years of life. Pale grey-white, smooth, uniformly segmented. 3 to 5 ft long, about 4 inches in diameter, weighing up to 49 lbs. The mouth is about the size of a human fist, lined with two rings of small needle teeth. A Lesser Death Worm cannot swallow prey whole; it feeds by burrowing into corpses and absorbing liquefied tissue through its permeable skin. It moves through loose soil and grave dirt, leaving tunnels about 6 inches wide that collapse within seconds of its passage. Lesser Death Worms are the most common stage encountered; a single graveyard may contain dozens of them, many the product of larger worms that were forced to split by adventurers. They pose little threat individually but a swarm can strip a corpse to bones in under a minute.

**Death Worm (DR 2):** A mature worm that has grown past the Lesser stage. Dark red-black with a translucent underside showing a faint pulse of blighted green light. 6 to 12 ft long, about 1 ft in diameter, weighing 50 to 499 lbs. The mouth is a circular sucker with three concentric rings of inward-curved teeth, capable of expanding to engulf a human head. An adult Death Worm is an active predator: it burrows toward vibrations, erupts from the ground, latches onto prey with the sucker mouth, and injects blighted digestive fluid directly into wounds. It can survive out of soil for up to an hour if kept moist. Adult Death Worms are the variant most commonly fought by adventurers; they are large enough to be dangerous but still small enough to encounter in groups.

**Greater Death Worm (DR 5):** A massive worm that has lived for decades. Deep purple-grey with short stiff bristles (setae) about 3 inches long covering each segment, hard as iron and sharp enough to draw blood on contact. 15 to 35 ft long, about 3 ft in diameter, weighing 500 to 4999 lbs. The mouth has four concentric rings of teeth, the outer fangs up to 8 inches long, and can stretch to swallow a human-sized creature whole. A swallowed creature is pulled into the stomach chamber and takes Blight damage each round. Greater Death Worms are ambush predators: they sit motionless beneath the ground for days, then erupt directly beneath their prey and clamp the mouth shut around the target. Their tunnels are 3 ft wide and remain open for about 10 minutes before collapsing. A single Greater Death Worm is a serious threat to a party of adventurers.

**Elder Death Worm (DR 12):** An ancient worm that has survived for centuries. Near-black with dark green veins of blighted energy pulsing along each segmented seam. 30 ft and larger, 6 to 10 ft in diameter, weighing 5000 lbs or more. The mouth can expand to 15 ft wide with five concentric rings of teeth; the outer fangs are hooked daggers that snap shut like a bear trap. An Elder can swallow a horse or carriage whole. Its throat is a muscular tube that can be fired forward up to 20 ft to grab prey and retract in under a second. Stomach fluid dissolves iron in under a minute. Elder Death Worms are region-scale threats; a single individual can depopulate a graveyard and the surrounding countryside. Tunnels are 8 ft wide and remain open for up to 24 hours. Entire sections of battlefield have collapsed into Elder tunnel networks. Most Elders choose to split into two Greater Death Worms at around 10,000 lbs rather than continue growing indefinitely, making Elder encounters rare.
###### Unique Abilities
- **Split (Active, 15 Mana):** As an action, the Death Worm tears itself exactly in half. The original worm ceases to exist and is replaced by two identical copies. Each copy has exactly half the original's level (rounded down) and half the original's weight and length. Each copy is a new fully restored creature with full Vitality, Health, and Mana for its new level. If the original was level 1, each copy is level 0.5 with all stats, weight, and length halved from the level 1 stat block. Both copies retain all memories and knowledge of the original; neither is the parent. Each copy's variant is determined by its new weight using the Variant Weight Ranges table. If both copies fall within the original's variant weight range, both remain the same variant at half level. If a copy's weight falls within a younger variant's range, that copy de-evolves to that variant at half level. If a copy's weight falls below the Lesser Death Worm minimum (1 lb), that copy dies immediately. 
- **Traumatic Split (Passive):** Any damage that reduces the Death Worm's Health can trigger an uncontrolled split. Slashing damage: make a CON save (DC 5 + the damage taken). All other damage types: make a CON save (DC 15 + the damage taken). On a failure, the worm involuntarily splits using the same rules as the Split ability. If the damage was dealt by a weapon made of glass, Nerite, Vacuum Mass, or Voidsteel, the worm has disadvantage on this save. When a worm dies it automatically splits but instead of both copies fully regaining all pools (Vitality, Health, and Mana) their pools are halved. If these variants are split through damage without death the new copies will be fully restored. 
- **Evolution (Passive):** When a Death Worm's weight exceeds the maximum of its variant's weight range through leveling, it undergoes a growth surge over 1 minute, during which it is immobile and has disadvantage on all saves. At the end of the minute, the worm elongates and thickens to the base dimensions of the next variant and resets to level 1 of that variant.
- **Necrotic Stomach (Passive):** The Death Worm's digestive tract is filled with blighted fluid. Any creature swallowed takes Blight damage at the start of each of the worm's turns. Damage dice by variant: Lesser 1d6, Death Worm 2d6, Greater 3d6, Elder 5d6. A swallowed creature is [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental-Positional-Conditions|Restrained]] and [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Blinded]]. It can escape by dealing damage equal to one quarter of the worm's maximum Vitality to the stomach wall in a single turn (AC 8 inside). If the worm is reduced to 0 HP, the stomach ruptures and all swallowed creatures are released.
- **Burrow (Passive):** The Death Worm has the listed burrow speed and can move through earth, soil, sand, and loose stone. It leaves a tunnel behind it. Tunnel diameter and collapse time by variant: Lesser 6 inches (1 round), Death Worm 1 ft (1 minute), Greater 3 ft (10 minutes), Elder 8 ft (24 hours).
- **Tremorsense (Passive):** The Death Worm detects the exact location of any creature in contact with the ground within range by sensing vibrations. A creature moving at half speed or slower does not register. Flying, swimming, and levitating creatures are not detected. Ranges: Lesser 60 ft, Death Worm 120 ft, Greater 300 ft, Elder 600 ft.
- **Mana Sense (Passive):** The Death Worm detects creatures with a mana pool which is not empty within range, through solid earth and stone. Undead register twice as strongly at double range. Ranges: Lesser 60 ft, Death Worm 150 ft, Greater 400 ft, Elder 1000 ft.
- **Swallow (Active, 8 Mana):** The Death Worm makes a Bite attack. On a hit, instead of dealing Bite damage, the target is swallowed (subject to Necrotic Stomach). Usable only against creatures at least one size category smaller than the worm.
- **Erupt (Active, 10 Mana):** The Death Worm bursts from the ground beneath a creature within range (half the worm's burrow speed), making a Bite attack with advantage. On hit the target must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or be knocked [[2.4 Conditions#Environmental-Positional-Conditions|Prone]]. The worm ends on the surface in the target's space. Usable only while burrowing.
- **Bite (Active, 6 Mana):** Melee attack. Damage dice: Lesser 1d6, Death Worm 2d6, Greater 3d6, Elder 5d6, all plus [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] Blight damage.

**Standard Passives:** Affinity Body, Regeneration, Hardened Body (Greater and Elder only)
**Standard Actives:** Charge (burrow only, Greater and Elder only)
