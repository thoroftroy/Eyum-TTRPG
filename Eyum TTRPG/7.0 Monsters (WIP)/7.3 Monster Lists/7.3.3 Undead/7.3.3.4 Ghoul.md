A ghoul is a humanoid corpse reanimated by an insatiable hunger for living flesh. Its body is emaciated, the skin pulled tight over visible ribs and sharp joints. The skin is a green-gray and slightly damp. The fingers are elongated, each tipped with a ragged yellow-black claw. The mouth is filled with rows of needle-like teeth, jagged and uneven, jutting at odd angles. The jaw unhinges wider than any living human's can. The eyes are sunken pits with a dull green pinprick of light deep inside. The tongue is black and swollen. The ghoul does not breathe but a wet rattling sound comes from its throat constantly, the sound of trapped air escaping through vocal cords that still twitch. It moves hunched low to the ground but can spring into a full sprint in an instant, covering ground with a loping gait that looks wrong on a human frame.

The ghoul's claws carry a paralytic toxin secreted from glands at the base of each fingernail. The toxin seeps into the keratin and coats the claw's surface. Any cut that draws blood introduces the toxin into the victim's bloodstream. The effect is rapid: within seconds the muscles around the wound stiffen and lock, then the stiffness spreads outward until the limb is frozen. Multiple claw strikes compound the effect, eventually seizing the entire body. The ghoul is immune to its own toxin and to the toxins of other ghouls.

Ghouls are created when a humanoid with more than 100 mana dies from starvation (roll 1d20, raises on 17 or above). The corpse rises within 3 days as a ghoul. They retain no memories of their former life. Every ghoul is driven by a single overwhelming impulse: to eat living flesh. They do not need to eat to survive, they are already dead, but the hunger never stops. A ghoul that has just consumed an entire human will still be just as hungry. They favor graveyards and battlefields because freshly buried bodies are easy to dig up and consume. A ghoul will eat anything that was once alive but strongly prefers flesh that is still warm. They do not reproduce. New ghouls are created only from humanoids who die of starvation. A ghoul gains 1 level per year of existence, growing slightly faster and deadlier with age. The oldest ghouls are withered, leathery things barely resembling humans, but they move with terrifying speed and their toxin can drop a grown man in seconds. When killed a ghoul's body collapses into a heap of brittle bones and sloughing flesh. Nothing of value can be harvested.
###### Unique Abilities
- **Paralytic Toxin (Passive):** When the ghoul deals Health damage to a creature with a Claw or Bite attack, that creature must make a CON save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]). On a failure, the creature gains 1 stack of Paralytic Toxin. For every 3 levels the ghoul has apply one additional stack at once. 
- **Pack Hunter (Passive):** For each allied Undead creature within 30 ft of the ghoul, the ghoul gains +1 to attack rolls (maximum +4) and +1 damage. 
- **Feeding Frenzy (Passive):** When the ghoul reduces a creature to 0 HP, it immediately regains 2d6 Vitality and its speed increases by 15 ft until the end of its next turn. This speed increase stacks with itself if multiple creatures are reduced to 0 HP in the same turn but resets at the end of its next turn.
- **Stronger Leveling (Passive):** Instead of gaining +1 Gen Dmg Mod each level, the ghoul gains +2. Instead of gaining +1 to its main Affinity each level, it gains +3. Every 3 levels the Ghoul gains +5 ft of movement speed. 
- **Bite (Active, 6 Mana):** Melee attack, 1d8 + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] piercing damage. On a hit the ghoul may immediately make a Claw attack against the same target as a free action.
**Standard Passives:** Frenzy, Blood Fueled, Quick Reactions, Second Wind
**Standard Actives:** Claw, Charge
