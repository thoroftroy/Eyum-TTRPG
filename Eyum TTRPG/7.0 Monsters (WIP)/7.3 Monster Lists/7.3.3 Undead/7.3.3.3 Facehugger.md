A Facehugger is a parasitic creature measuring about 8 inches across its main body with eight jointed legs spanning roughly 1.5 ft when fully extended. The body is flat, oval, and covered by a segmented carapace of pale grey-white chitin with darker grey ridges along the seams between segments. The carapace is tough but brittle, cracking under sharp impacts. Eight legs extend from the sides of the body, each ending in a curved gripping claw of the same chitinous material. The legs are thin but strong enough to cling to vertical surfaces and launch the creature up to 15 ft in a single leap. A long segmented tail extends from the rear of the body, roughly twice the bodyspan in length, rhythmically flexing to draw air across moist internal respiratory membranes. When the Facehugger is agitated or dying, the tail thrashes violently.

The underside of the body contains a circular orifice surrounded by a ring of twelve small fleshy tendrils, each about 1 inch long and tipped with a microscopic barbed hook. The orifice is the mouth and attachment point. The tendrils secrete a bonding mucus that hardens within seconds of contacting flesh or bone, permanently anchoring the Facehugger to a host. There are no visible eyes. The Facehugger perceives its environment through heat sensitivity along its carapace ridges, detecting creatures by body heat, and through mana signature detection via organs in its tail, sensing magical or enchanted creatures from up to 60 ft. It is blind to inanimate objects that are neither warm nor magical.

Internally the Facehugger is filled with a thick yellowish-green fluid that serves as both circulatory blood and hydraulic fluid for its legs. This fluid is a potent miasmic substance, carrying the foul necrotic-toxic energy of the Facehugger's Miasma affinity. It is highly corrosive to nearly all materials except glass, Nerite, Vacuum Mass, and Voidsteel, capable of dissolving iron in about one minute and flesh in seconds. The Facehugger's own carapace and internal tissues are coated in a thin mucus layer that neutralizes the fluid on contact, preventing self-damage. When the carapace is breached, the blood smokes faintly with a sharp acrid smell and hisses on contact with air. A dead Facehugger yields 1d4 vials of Facehugger Acid. See [[2.6 Materials#Special-Materials]] for harvesting and usage.

Facehuggers reproduce asexually. A single Facehugger contains both reproductive organs and can produce eggs without a mate. Egg production requires a host. A Facehugger attached to a host for at least 24 continuous hours can lay 1 egg per month. The egg is about 4 inches long, pale grey and leathery, with a faint warmth radiating from it. The egg must be kept above 60 F with moderate humidity or it desiccates and dies within 3 days.

Facehugger eggs do not hatch on a fixed schedule. Each egg has a continuous sensory ability: it can detect suitable dead creatures within 60 ft through heat and mana signature detection. A suitable host is any dead creature of size Tiny or larger with an intact head, dead for no more than 7 days. The egg remains dormant indefinitely as long as no suitable host is within range. The moment a suitable dead host enters the egg's 60 ft detection radius, the egg hatches in 1 round and the Facehugger hatchling immediately springs toward the dead host using its Face Lock ability. If two or more suitable dead hosts are within range, the egg hatches toward the nearest one. If no dead host is available but a living creature enters within 10 ft, the egg may hatch as a last resort, targeting the living creature.

A newly hatched Facehugger is level 1, about 2 inches across. It must successfully attach to a host within 10 rounds of hatching. If it fails to attach within that time, its internal metabolism consumes it and it dies, leaving no usable materials. Facehuggers gain 1 level per year while attached to a host. Upon first attaching to a host, the Facehugger's body stretches and reshapes over the course of 1 hour to cover the host's face, regardless of the host's size. The Facehugger's length and weight after this reshaping are determined by the host's size category as shown in the Host Dimensions table. After this initial reshaping the Facehugger only gains weight per level when attached to a host that is larger than its own current size category; if the host is the same size or smaller, weight does not increase. Level-based length increases (+0.1 ft legspan per level) apply normally regardless of host. A Facehugger forcibly removed from a host reverts to its unhosted Tiny dimensions over the course of 1 minute as it dies.

When a Facehugger attaches to a dead host, its tendrils extend microscopic filaments into the host's brain stem and spinal column. These filaments route nerve signals from the Facehugger's own simple nervous system through the host's motor pathways, allowing the Facehugger to puppet the corpse with full motor control. The host body does not decay while the Facehugger is attached. The Facehugger circulates preservative fluids through the host's vascular system, preventing rot, rigor mortis, and tissue breakdown. If the Facehugger dies, normal decomposition resumes immediately from the exact point where it was paused. If the host body is destroyed (reduced to 0 structural HP), the Facehugger dies immediately. If the Facehugger is forcibly removed from the host by any means that separates it from the skull, the Facehugger dies immediately and its body ruptures, triggering the death burst of Acidic Blood. A Facehugger cannot voluntarily detach from a host it has successfully attached to.
###### Unique Abilities
- **Egg Sense (Passive):** (Only eggs). A Facehugger egg continuously senses heat and mana signatures within 60 ft. While any suitable dead creature (size Tiny or larger, intact head, dead no more than 7 days) is within this radius, the egg hatches in 1 round. If multiple suitable dead hosts are within range, the egg hatches toward the nearest one. If no dead host is within 60 ft but a living creature is within 10 ft, the egg may still hatch as a last resort. An egg that hatches this way targets the living creature. Eggs not within range of any creature remain dormant indefinitely. An egg has AC 5 and 1 HP; if destroyed before hatching, it yields nothing.
- **Facehugger Senses (Passive):** A Facehugger cannot see but can sense any creature or object with heat or mana within 120 ft. It cannot sense objects or creatures without heat or mana. 
- **Face Lock (Active, 5 Mana):** The Facehugger makes a melee attack against a dead, unconscious, or [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Stunned]] creature within 5 ft. This attack automatically hits dead or unconscious targets. On a hit, the Facehugger attaches to the target's face. This does not deal initial damage. If the target is dead, the Facehugger gains the full benefits of Host Parasite and Nervous Override at the start of its next turn. If the target is alive but unconscious or [[2.4 Conditions#Physical-Conditions|Stunned]], the Facehugger attaches and Living Host Strangle begins at the start of the Facehugger's next turn. A conscious creature targeted by Face Lock who is not unconscious or stunned can make a STR save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) as a reaction to resist. On a success, the Facehugger is thrown into an adjacent space and the ability fails. Face Lock cannot be used on a creature already hosting another Facehugger. A Facehugger that has just hatched from an egg can use Face Lock as a free action during the round it hatches.
- **Living Host Strangle (Passive):** When attached to a living creature, the Facehugger cannot control the host and does not gain the benefits of Host Parasite or Nervous Override. Instead, at the start of each of the host's turns, the host takes Miasma damage. On the first turn the host takes 1d8 Miasma damage. On the second consecutive turn this increases to 2d8, on the third to 3d8, and so on with no upper limit. This damage applies directly to Health. The damage counter resets to 1d8 if the Facehugger is removed. The host can attempt to remove the Facehugger by making a STR save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) as an action. On a success, the Facehugger is torn off and thrown into an adjacent space. On a failure, the Facehugger remains attached and the damage continues escalating on subsequent turns. If the host dies while the Facehugger is attached, the Facehugger gains full control at the start of its next turn as per Host Parasite and Nervous Override.
- **Fatal Bond (Passive):** The Facehugger is permanently bonded to its host once its tendrils fully anchor (1 round after a successful Face Lock). If the Facehugger is forcibly removed from the host by any means, it dies immediately. If the host body is destroyed, the Facehugger dies immediately. In either case the Facehugger's death triggers the death burst of Acidic Blood. A Facehugger cannot voluntarily detach from a host.
- **Host Parasite (Passive):** While attached to a dead creature, the Facehugger gains control of the host body. The host body is treated as having the Zombie template applied (see [[7.3.5 Monster Modifiers]]) for the purpose of determining its physical durability, Vitality, and Health. The host body's maximum Vitality and Health are the values the host creature would have as a Zombie of its level at the time of death. The host body's STR, CON, DEX, and AC are also determined by the Zombie template applied to the host's original stats. The Facehugger does not use the Zombie template's INT/WIS/CHA modifications; it retains its own WIS, INT, and CHA scores. The Facehugger uses its own Mana pool and its own Gen Dmg Mod and Miasma Affinity for all damage calculations. All of the host's natural movement speeds and movement types (walk, swim, fly, burrow, climb, etc.) are available and use the modified Zombie speed values. Worn armor and equipment on the corpse remain and function normally using the Zombie template's physical stats. All damage dealt to the hosted Facehugger is applied to the host body's structural HP first. When the host body's structural HP reaches 0, the body is destroyed and the Facehugger dies immediately. The Facehugger's own Vitality and Health from its unhosted stat block are not used while attached and are not affected by damage the host body absorbs. While attached, the Facehugger itself can be targeted directly by an attacker who specifically declares they are targeting the Facehugger rather than the host body. The Facehugger has 40 AC against any attack that targets it directly but uses its own statblocks health and defense. The Facehugger is immune to all area-of-effect damage; AoE damage is applied entirely to the host body instead. Any direct damage saving throw effect to a Facehugger will go to the host instead of the Facehugger itself. Any damage that hits the Facehugger directly bypasses the host body and applies to the Facehugger's own Vitality and Health. 
- **Nervous Override (Passive):** While controlling a dead host via Host Parasite, the Facehugger can use any passives and actives the host possessed in life. All skills, abilities, spells, passives, and actives are accessible with no restrictions. Spells and mana-based abilities pull from the Facehugger's own Mana pool rather than any mana the host had in life. The host has no mana pool of its own while controlled. All damage calculations for host abilities use the Facehugger's own Gen Dmg Mod and Miasma Affinity. 
- **Acidic Blood (Passive):** Whenever the Facehugger takes Health damage from any source, its blood sprays from the wound. All creatures within 5 ft must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 1d6 Miasma damage. This triggers each time Health damage is taken, including multiple instances in the same round. When the Facehugger is reduced to 0 Health and dies (whether from direct damage, host destruction, or forced removal), its body ruptures and sprays blood in a 10 ft radius. All creatures in the area must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 2d6 Miasma damage.
