Dragons are large, winged reptilian creatures composed of dense biological tissue suffused with elemental energy throughout every cell. Unlike slimes or golems, dragons are fully organic organisms with complete skeletal, muscular, circulatory, and nervous systems, all of which are infused with their elemental affinity. Their flesh, blood, and scales all carry traces of their element, but the core difference between a dragon and a mundane creature is the concentration of this energy. A dragon does not channel its element so much as it is physically made of it: fire dragons have blood that burns, ice dragons have lungs that freeze the air inside them, and lightning dragons have nervous systems that fire in constant, visible arcs.

A dragon's body follows a standard tetrapod layout with two forelimbs, two hind limbs, and two wings. The wings are supported by a modified skeletal structure where elongated finger bones stretch a membrane of thick, element-infused hide. The forelimbs and hind limbs are heavily muscled and end in claws that are dense, pointed growths of hardened keratin and crystallized elemental residue. The tail is long and heavy, used for balance during flight and as a blunt weapon on the ground. The head is large relative to the body, housing a developed brain, sensory organs, and the specialized organ clusters required for elemental breath attacks. The jaw structure allows for a wide gape and powerful bite force, with teeth that are broad, conical, and reinforced with the same crystalline deposits found in the claws.

At the center of a dragon's circulatory system sits the *dragon heart*, a large, multi-chambered organ that serves as both a biological pump and an elemental condenser. The heart draws ambient elemental energy from the dragon's surroundings and from its own metabolic processes, concentrating it into the bloodstream. This element-saturated blood circulates through the body, feeding each tissue with the energy needed to maintain the dragon's passive elemental effects and active abilities. The heart is the only truly vital organ—damage to the brain, lungs, or spine will impair the dragon but not kill it. Only the complete destruction of the heart results in death, and dragons have evolved dense layers of muscle, bone, and scale to protect this organ specifically.

Dragons grow throughout their lives, adding mass and elemental capacity as they age. Growth is driven by the heart's ability to condense elemental energy into new biological tissue. A dragon that feeds regularly and fights often will grow faster than one that does not, but even a sedentary dragon will continue accumulating elemental energy over time, slowly increasing in size and power. Each level represents a measurable increase in the dragon's physical dimensions and elemental output. A dragon will gain a level approximately every five years if it is sedentary but if it is eating lots and fighting it can gain a level up to every year. 

- A Level 1 dragon is approximately:
    - **Length:** 20 ft
    - **Wingspan:** 30 ft
    - **Weight:** 2,000 lbs
- Each level:
    - +5 ft length
    - +5 ft wingspan
    - +400 lbs weight

Dragons reproduce through sexual reproduction with distinct male and female individuals. Mating requires specific environmental conditions tied to the element of the dragons involved, fire dragons mate near active volcanoes, ice dragons meet in high-altitude frozen regions, storm dragons mate during severe thunderstorms. The specific trigger conditions are not fully understood, but it is known that dragons will only attempt reproduction when their territory is secure, their elemental energy reserves are full, and the environmental conditions are matched to their element. After mating, the female carries a clutch of fertilized eggs internally for several months. The number of eggs varies by type, typically ranging from 3 to 12. The eggs are laid in a carefully constructed nest infused with the parent's elemental energy, nests are built in locations that naturally provide the required elemental conditions, such as geothermal vents, deep earth fissures, or high-altitude cloud formations. The eggs are leathery and semi-translucent, with the developing embryo visible as a dark shape within a glowing elemental fluid. Incubation takes 8 to 14 months depending on the type. Hatchlings emerge fully formed and immediately capable of elemental breath attacks, though at a fraction of the power of an adult (stats approx 1/4 that of a level 1 dragon, after approx 10 years they will reach level 1). Dragons cannot mate until reaching level 5. 

When a dragon dies, its elemental saturation causes a rapid release of stored energy. The body releases this energy in a visible discharge that lasts roughly an hour, during which the softer tissues dissolve into elemental vapor. The denser structures, scales, bones, and concentrated muscle tissue, remain intact and can be harvested. Approximately 30% of the dragon's pre-death weight is recoverable as solid material, with the remaining 70% dissolving into elemental vapor and being lost.

All dragons understand Dragonic. They cannot speak/converse freely in it, its more of just intent and general understanding. Once a dragon reaches level 5 they can speak dragonic as well. 

The harvested materials break down into three categories:
- **Dragon Scales:** Each dragon type has unique scales with distinct properties. Scales make up approximately 5% of a dragon's body weight. These scales retain the dragon's elemental affinity and are used to craft powerful equipment.
- **Dragon Meat:** The muscle tissue of dragons is infused with elemental energy. Dragon meat is a universal material that can be used in various ways. It makes up approximately 15% of a dragon's body weight.
- **Dragon Bones:** The skeletal structure of dragons is incredibly dense and retains elemental properties. Dragon bones are a universal material used for crafting. Bones make up approximately 10% of a dragon's body weight.

Dragons can serve any role depending on their element and build.

**All dragons possess the following passive abilities:**
- **Elemental Aura (30 ft)**: Dragons emit an aura of their element in a 30 ft radius. Creatures within this aura at the start of their turn take damage equal to the dragon's [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] + half their level. This damage does not trigger resistances or weaknesses. The dragon can choose to suppress this aura at any time.
- **Flight**: Dragons have a flying speed equal to their listed speed. They can hover.
- **Elemental Form**: Dragons are immune to damage from their own element. Additionally, Dragons can move through their own element without taking damage or being hindered. The immunity granted from Elemental Form cannot be removed or reduced in any way, even from other effects which would normally ignore immunity. Instead of having regular Weakness to their respective opposite elements all dragons have lesser weakness. 
- **Dragon Vitality**: A dragon cannot take health damage by any means until its Vitality is totally exhausted. Any effect which would be split or go straight to HP will instead all go into Vitality until there is no more Vitality left. Any effect which would rely on Health specifically (like life steel) will do nothing and instantly fail at long as any vitality remains. 
- **Elemental King:** When the Dragon makes an attack, double its affinity in its main affinity and look up the [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] on [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus]] then move the bracket to the next highest bracket (if at highest add +2). Add that bonus to the attack's damage.

## Dragon Descriptions

**Fire Dragon**
A heavily built dragon covered in thick scales of overlapping orange and dark red plates. The scales are hard and dense, radiating heat that blisters bare skin from several feet away. Smoke curls from the gaps between scales constantly. The head is broad and blocky, with nostrils that glow orange from within and release thin jets of flame with each exhale. The eyes are yellow white with no visible pupil. A wide mouth filled with blackened teeth opens to reveal a throat that glows orange. The wings are large and leathery, colored dark red with orange veins running through the membrane. The legs are thick and muscular, ending in claws that glow red at the tips. The tail is long and heavy, sheathed in the same orange and red plates. The air within thirty feet shimmers with heat distortion, and any exposed paper or dry grass will ignite after prolonged proximity.

**Earth Dragon**
A squat heavy bodied dragon with scales of overlapping granite and sandstone that are hard as solid rock. The scales are thick and rough textured, often covered in patches of moss and small lichen growths. The head is wide and blunt, with no distinct neck, the skull sitting directly on the shoulders. The eyes are small and dark, set deep in heavy bony ridges that protrude from the skull. The mouth is wide and filled with flat grinding teeth. The wings are short and thick, built more for display than flight, with a stony texture. The legs are short but incredibly thick, ending in blunt claws designed for digging rather than slashing. The tail is a solid mass of bone and hard scales that drags behind, carving a shallow trench in loose soil. When the dragon moves, the ground trembles and dust shakes loose from nearby surfaces.

**Water Dragon**
A long serpentine body covered in smooth hard scales in shades of deep blue and teal. The scales are flat and polished with a glossy surface, each one hard and solid to the touch, though always damp with a thin film of water. The head is narrow and elongated, with a mouth that extends well past the eyes. Gills sit behind the jawline, fluttering open and closed. The eyes are large and dark, with a transparent inner eyelid that closes when submerged. The mouth contains rows of needle sharp teeth. The wings are broad and fin shaped, with a membrane that stretches between long flexible bones. The legs are long and webbed, ending in claws that curve inward. The tail is broad and finned, used for propulsion through water. The dragon is equally graceful in air and water, its hard scales offering protection in both elements.

**Air Dragon**
A lean lightweight body covered in pale gray blue scales that are thin but hard, each one overlapping loosely for maximum flexibility. The edges of the scales are translucent, but the centers are solid and firm. The head is small and aerodynamic, with a pointed snout and large eyes positioned forward for binocular vision. The mouth is narrow with small sharp teeth. The wings are unusually large relative to the body, broad and long, with primary feathers made of the same hard scale material extending to the tips. The legs are slender, with small sharp claws. The tail is long and narrow, tapering to a point. The bones are hollow but reinforced, making the dragon far lighter than its size suggests. On the ground it walks with a hunched posture, but in the air it moves with instant responsiveness and grace.

**Radiant Dragon**
A dense heavy bodied dragon covered in overlapping metallic gold scales polished to a mirror finish. The scales are hard and smooth, reflecting the surroundings with perfect clarity. Light radiates from between the scales constantly, creating a soft halo around the dragon's entire body. The head is proportionally large, with a blunt snout and eyes that glow with steady golden light. The mouth is wide, and the inside glows bright enough to cast shadows on the ground. The wings are large and metallic, each scale on the wing surface catching and reflecting light. The legs are thick and pillar shaped, ending in golden claws. The tail is heavy and plated in the same reflective gold. Each scale has significant weight, and when the dragon moves, they produce a faint high pitched ringing sound as they shift against each other.

**Necrotic Dragon**
A lean angular body covered in thin black scales with a matte nonreflective surface. The scales are hard but light, absorbing almost all light that hits them. Fine green veins run through the scales, pulsing with a sickly light from within. The head is narrow with flesh pulled tight over the skull, giving it a skeletal appearance. The eye sockets are hollow, with a dim green light visible at the back of each socket. The teeth are yellow brown and uneven in length, some missing entirely. The wings are thin and bat shaped, the membrane a dark translucent gray with green veins tracing through it. The legs are long and bony, ending in dark glossy claws that curve sharply. The tail is thin and whip like, tipped with a bony spur. Vegetation within fifty feet turns brown and dies over several days, and the air around it carries the smell of decay.

**Psychic Dragon**
A long bodied dragon with smooth semitranslucent hard scales that shift slowly through shades of violet, indigo, and deep purple. The scales are thinner than those of most other dragons but remain completely solid. The head is elongated and smooth, with three large eyes arranged in a triangle on the front of the skull, each one solid purple with no visible pupil or iris. The mouth is small and rarely opens, revealing no visible teeth. The wings are wide and swept back, the membrane semi transparent with shifting violet patterns. The legs are long and slender, ending in three toed claws. The tail is long and tapers to a fine point. The body pulses with faint internal light in rhythm with its heartbeat. Creatures near it experience a low pressure sensation behind the eyes, a feeling of the air pressing inward.

**Lightning Dragon**
A sharp angled body covered in hard scales of oxidized copper and blue white crystal formations. The scales are not smooth, with many projecting outward as jagged points and ridges that create a silhouette full of spikes and edges. Thin arcs of electricity jump constantly between these projections, creating a continuous crackling sound. The head is angular with a pointed snout, and crystal growths around the jaw and brow glow brighter when the dragon is agitated. The eyes are white blue and emit visible light. The mouth contains jagged metallic looking teeth. The wings are angular and bat shaped, with sharp points at each joint. The legs are long and jointed, ending in claws that spark against the ground. The tail is long and segmented with crystal barbs along its length. The air carries the sharp smell of ozone, and metal objects within thirty feet vibrate when the dragon is near.

**Steam Dragon**
A bulky round bodied dragon whose hard pale gray scales are barely visible through the constant thick cloud of white gray steam that surrounds it. The scales beneath the steam are solid and dense, slick with condensed moisture but hard underneath. The head is broad with small eyes and wide nostrils that vent steam in steady pulses. The mouth opens wide, revealing a pale throat, and releases pressurized breath in visible jets. The wings are thick and rounded, steam curling off their surface continuously. The legs are short and stout, each footstep leaving a damp impression that lingers for hours. The tail is thick and blunt, wrapped in the same steam wet scales. Scale surfaces near the mouth and throat are stained with white mineral deposits from years of condensation. The fog extends in all directions, obscuring vision and muffling sound.

**Magma Dragon**
An immensely heavy dragon built of black obsidian and dark stone, covered in thick irregular hard scales of volcanic glass. The scales are split by glowing orange cracks where molten rock flows just beneath the surface, forming a branching network across the entire body that is brightest at the chest and throat. The head is massive and blocky, with a lower jaw that hangs slightly open to vent heat. The eyes are liquid orange, bright enough to leave afterimages. The mouth contains teeth of solid obsidian. The wings are heavy, with glowing vein patterns across the membrane. The legs are thick as pillars, each step melting the ground beneath, leaving glowing footprints that cool to black glass over time. The tail is a massive club of obsidian and magma. Smoke rises from every joint and the air is thick with sulfur.

**Ice/Cold Dragon**
A crystalline body built from layers of clear and milky white ice that form overlapping hard plates. The plates are dense and solid, segmented by deep cracks that never quite reach the flesh beneath. The head is angular with a pointed snout, with a mouth that releases a constant stream of frozen mist. The eyes are pale blue and glow faintly. The teeth are translucent and sharply pointed. The wings are thin and glassy, each wing bone visible through the frozen membrane. The legs are thick and heavily scaled, each footstep crunching frost into the ground. The tail is long and segmented, with icicles hanging from its length. Frost crystallizes on everything within fifty feet, leaves become brittle, puddles freeze solid, and breath condenses into ice particles. The dragon's body creaks and pops as it moves.

**Dust Dragon**
A thin but solidly built dragon whose body is covered in hard scales made of tightly compressed sand and fine grit. The scales are solid and dense, but their surface constantly erodes and reforms, shedding fine particles with every movement. The head is vaguely defined with a blunt snout, darker hollows where the eyes sit, and a mouth that is a thin crack in the dusty surface. The teeth are gritty and sand colored. The wings are thin and dusty, shedding particles as they beat. The legs are long and light, able to stand on surfaces that would not support other dragons. The tail is thin and tapers to a dusty point. A trailing cloud of fine particles follows everywhere it goes, and the air is thick and gritty within a wide radius. The dragon's voice has a dry rasping quality from particles passing through its throat.

**Mud Dragon**
A thick bodied dragon whose hard brown scales are coated in a slow moving layer of dark sludge several inches deep. The scales themselves are solid and dense underneath the mud, never fully visible but providing a hard foundation for the coating that constantly sloughs off and is replaced. The head is barely distinguishable from the body, a rounded mass with two dim points where the eyes are and a sluggish opening for a mouth filled with flat brown teeth. The wings are heavy and mud caked, each beat spraying droplets. The legs are thick and planted wide, ending in blunt claws that squelch with each step. The tail is thick and drags through the mud, leaving a wide furrow. Mud pools beneath wherever the dragon stands, and the smell is wet earth and decay.

**Nova Dragon**
A broad chested dragon covered in white and gold hard scales that pulse with expanding rings of light. The scales are smooth and dense, each pulse starting at the center of the chest and radiating outward across the body in waves.
