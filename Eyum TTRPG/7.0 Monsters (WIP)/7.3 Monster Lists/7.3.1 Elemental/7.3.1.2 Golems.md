Golems are elemental constructs composed of dense, solidified material bound around a singular, animating core. Unlike slimes, whose bodies are fluid and adaptive, golems possess rigid, stable forms that do not naturally deform or redistribute. Their bodies are structured for durability, typically forming large, humanoid silhouettes with reinforced limbs, thick torsos, and compact centers of mass. Every aspect of a golem’s physical structure exists for one purpose: protecting the core. The outer structure of a golem functions simultaneously as armor, weapon, and locomotion system. Limbs are often oversized relative to the torso, allowing for powerful, momentum-driven strikes. Joints are simple and reinforced, prioritizing strength over flexibility. Movement is slow, deliberate, and forceful, each step transfers significant weight, often cracking or compressing the ground beneath it. Golems do not rely on agility; instead, they rely on inevitability.

At the center of every golem is its *core*, embedded deep within the head or torso. This core is the entirety of the golem’s consciousness and existence. It is not simply a weak point, it is the golem itself. The surrounding body is inert without it. The core directs all movement, perception, and behavior, controlling the body as a single unified construct with no separation between thought and action. Golems do not possess organs, nervous systems, or traditional senses. Instead, the core provides full spatial awareness within a fixed radius (default: 120 ft), detecting all matter and energy in that space regardless of visibility or obstruction. This awareness is constant and does not rely on sight, sound, or other sensory input. Because of this, golems cannot be blinded, deafened, or otherwise impaired through conventional sensory disruption.

Golems grow by accumulating elemental material through mana absorption. This growth is physical and directly tied to their level. Level is determined purely by total mass.
- A Level 1 golem is approximately:
    - **Height:** 5 ft
    - **Width:** 2 ft
    - **Weight:** 450 lbs
- Each level:
    - +1 ft height
    - +90 lbs weight
-  Golems passively convert mana into material, the growth this causes is slow but amounts to roughly ~90 lbs of growth per level. 

Golems reproduce through two distinct processes, both driven by the accumulation and conversion of mass and mana rather than any biological function. In standard reproduction, a golem must first reach a sufficient physical and energetic threshold, requiring a minimum total weight of 900 lbs (or at least twice the base weight of a level 1 golem of its type) and the accumulation of 100 mana. Once both conditions are met, reproduction occurs when the Golem deems it time (of which they usually do not). The golem’s structure destabilizes and splits apart, forming two entirely new golems. During this process, all stored mana is completely consumed, acting as the catalyst for the division. The original body cannot be evenly divided, so a portion of excess material is forcibly expelled and scattered onto the ground as loose elemental matter, which can be harvested. The result of this process is the creation of two new golems, each reset to level 1, each possessing no stored mana, and each reduced to their base physical weight and size regardless of the original golem’s prior mass. The amount of material which can be collected from the Golem reproduction can be calculated as follows:
- Take the weight of the Golem (900 lbs) and divide it by 2 (450). A normal golem at level 1 will have 450 lbs of mass, you can subtract the mass of the resulting golem from the weight of the original golem/2. In this case we get 0 and there is no extra material to collect. If a Golem is say 990 lbs then it would be 495 lbs of material per golem resulting in 45 lbs of excess material per golem or 90 lbs of excess material in total which can be collected. 

In contrast, advanced reproduction, referred to as fusion, is a deliberate and far more destructive process used to create higher-order golems with combined elemental properties. This process requires multiple golems, with a minimum of two participants, though there is no upper limit to how many may be involved. Each participating golem must have reached at least five times its base level 1 weight, ensuring a sufficient mass threshold to sustain the transformation (so for a typical 450 lbs golem it must reach 2,250 lbs, and you'll need two of them at least). When fusion begins, all participating golems physically merge into a single unstable construct, their bodies collapsing together while their cores converge and forcibly reconfigure into a new unified core. During this transformation, the vast majority of the combined material is converted directly into mana to fuel the restructuring process. This conversion is highly inefficient, resulting in the loss of most of the total material, which is neither recoverable nor left behind as usable substance. Once the process completes, a single new golem is formed, possessing a combined elemental affinity derived from its components. Despite the immense material and energy involved, the resulting golem is always level 1 and begins with zero mana, representing a complete reset into a new form.

When a golem is destroyed, its body does not decay or dissipate but instead collapses into inert elemental material that retains all inherent properties of its original affinity. This material remains stable over time and does not degrade, making it highly valuable for collection and use. No specialized containment is typically required, as the material is typically solid or semi-solid depending on its type. In most cases, approximately 70% (changes based on golem type) of the golem’s total body weight can be successfully harvested after destruction, with the remaining portion lost due to structural breakdown or conversion during the destruction process. The core of a golem always fades away into nothing after a few seconds of being destroyed.

All golems have the role of Tanker.

**All golems possess the following passive abilities:**
- **Golem Body (Passive)**: A golem’s durability is divided into two distinct components, Its Vitality and Health. The Vitality (Material Integrity) represents the structural condition of the outer body. A Golem will never attempt to dodge out of the way, instead it will always try to block attacks with its body. The Health (Core Integrity) represents the condition of the core itself. Damage is applied to Vitality first. As long as any Vitality remains, the core cannot be directly damaged even with AOE or Critical attacks or even attacks which would normally deal damage directly to health. When Vitality reaches 0, the golem’s body begins to collapse, exposing the core. At this point, all remaining damage is applied directly to Health, the golem loses access to all actions, bonus actions, and reactions and its movement speed is reduced to 5 ft. If Health reaches 0, the core is destroyed and the golem dies permanently.
- **Golem Material Regeneration (Passive)**: At the start of each turn regain 1d8 + CON modifier Vitality at the cost of 2 mana per turn, this will not happen if the Golem is out of mana or took damage from a damage type it is weak to on the previous turn. If a Golem has any health lost it may instead heal 1d4 + Con health at the cost of 5 mana. 
- **Golem Mana Regeneration (Passive)**: At the end of each turn regain 1 mana unless the Golem took damage from a damage type it is weak to on the previous turn. 
- **Golem Overcharge (Passive)**: A golem can store up to 3x its maximum mana, for every 3 mana above their maximum they will take 1 Vitality damage per turn at the end of their turn. 
- **Greater Elemental:** When the Golem makes an attack, look up the [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] on [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus]] for its main affinity then move the bracket to the next highest bracket (if at highest add +2). Add that bonus to the attack's damage.

- **Shatter Cycle (Passive)**: When the golem reaches 0 Vitality, it shatters into pieces. It automatically reforms at full Vitality after 3 turns, but is stunned during this time. - **Glass Rain (Active)**: (6 Mana) The golem shatters into sharp fragments, dealing 1d6 slashing damage to all creatures in a 10 ft radius who fails a Dex save (DC 15). Creatures must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 1d4 additional piercing damage from embedded fragments on their next turn.

- **Quake Stomp (Active)**: (10 Mana) 15 ft radius. Creatures must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 1d6 bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone. Those who succeed take half damage and aren't knocked prone.
- **Sonic Chamber (Passive)**: When the golem uses Beam, targets must make a CON save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or be deafened for 1 turn.
- **Sonic Boom (Active)**: (10 Mana) 20 ft radius. All creatures in range take 1d8 + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] thunder damage. Targets make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) for half damage.
- **Thermal Shock (Passive)**: When the golem hits a target with a melee attack, the target must make a CON save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 1d10 frostfire damage. Creatures wearing metal armor or wielding metal weapons take double this damage.
- **Shatter Blast (Active)**: (10 Mana) 10 ft radius. Targets must make a CON save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or take 1d6 cold damage and 1d6 fire damage. Creatures with Metal weapons and armor in the area take 3d8 + 4 Frostfire damage.
- **Fissure Step (Passive)**: Whenever the golem moves, it leaves cracks in the ground. Creatures that move through spaces the golem occupied must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or fall prone.
- **Ground Crack (Active)**: (10 Mana) The golem slams the ground, creating fissures in 3 15 ft lines (in separate directions). Creatures in the line take 1d6 bludgeoning damage and must make a DEX save (DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]) or fall prone.

## Golem Descriptions

**Fire Golem**
A dense, squat humanoid form carved from thick layers of solidified tar and burned pitch. The body is approximately five feet tall with a broad, barrel-shaped torso that measures about three feet wide at the shoulders. The outer layer is black, glossy, and sticky to the touch, like hardened tar that has been burned and reheated many times. The surface is covered in a network of wide, jagged cracks that run vertically down the chest and horizontally across the belly, and these cracks glow steadily with orange and red light from the flames burning just beneath the surface. Where the cracks intersect, flames occasionally flickers out like small fires catching wind. The material is softest and most pliable around the shoulders, elbows, hips, and knees where movement is needed, and these joints are visibly darker and more tar-like. The arms are thick and cylinder-shaped, ending in blunt, squared-off fists that are flattened from repeated impact. The legs are short and stumpy, dragging slightly as it walks due to the weight, with feet that are wide and flat. The head is a smooth, rounded dome about the size of a pumpkin, with no features at all, just a slightly darker patch on the front where heat radiates most intensely. The core sits deep in the chest cavity, a perfectly round sphere of dense, burning coal about the size of a cannonball, glowing white-hot at the center with orange cracks radiating outward. The heat the golem radiates is intense enough to cause sweating fifteen feet away, and the air above it shimmers constantly. The golem leaves melted tar footprints that cool into black, sticky marks whenever it stands still.

**Earth Golem**
A massive, hulking figure sculpted from tightly packed earth, clay, and stones of various sizes. The body stands approximately five feet tall but appears much larger due to its extreme bulk and density. Every part of the form is completely rigid with no visible joints or flexibility; instead, movement occurs through grinding shifts between different segments of compressed earth that rub against each other like tectonic plates. The surface is extremely rough and grainy, like coarse sandpaper, with embedded pebbles, fist-sized rocks, and occasional larger stones acting as natural armor. The torso is a thick cylinder with faint horizontal bands where different layers of soil compress together. The arms are thick columns about two feet in diameter, ending in massive fists that are just rounded masses of compacted earth. The legs are rooted to the ground as if grown from it, with feet that are wide and flat, pressing deep impressions into soft ground. The head is a squared block, slightly wider than the shoulders, with no facial features whatsoever, just a slightly recessed area on the front where the core sits embedded in solid stone. The core is a smooth, dark gray river stone about the size of a basketball, perfectly round and incredibly dense, set in the center of the chest and fused directly to the surrounding earth. Moss and lichen grow on the shoulders and back, suggesting centuries of perfect stillness.

**Water Golem**
A tall, undulating humanoid shape made entirely of contained liquid with no solid structure whatsoever. The body is approximately five feet tall, constantly shifting and changing shape, with the water never settling into one fixed form. The liquid is deep blue and semi-transparent, dense enough to obscure what is inside but clear enough to see roughly a foot through. The water flows constantly in slow currents within the body, circulating from the feet up through the torso and back down again in an endless cycle. Limbs are formed by pushing water outward into roughly arm-shaped extensions and pulling it back inward to retract them, with no bones or framework to define their shape. The surface has an unnaturally strong tension that holds the form together despite the liquid nature. Small bubbles rise constantly through the body from the bottom and pop at the surface, creating a faint gurgling sound. The core is a small, perfectly clear diamond crystal about the size of a fist, suspended dead-center in the chest and glowing faintly with blue light. The crystal spins slowly in one direction while the water around it flows in the opposite direction, creating a perpetual motion. The golem doesn't walk so much as roll and slosh across surfaces, leaving wet trails behind, and it makes a constant sound like water running over rocks.

**Air Golem**
A swirling humanoid figure made entirely from dense, swirling fog given solid form. The body is approximately five feet tall made from dark gray fog. The fog is dark gray with occasional lighter wisps mixed in, and small lightning bolts crackle randomly throughout the mass. The form is a dense humanoid shape held together by static charge. Arms are dense fog trailing off at the ends. The legs are denser fog at the bottom. The head is a slightly more solid area, a small dark stone about the size of a grapefruit. The core crackles with constant low-energy lightning that illuminates the fog. The golem moves by flowing quickly in one direction and reforming. The air around it feels heavy and charged, and the constant low rumbling can be heard from twenty feet away.

**Radiant Golem**
A towering, gleaming humanoid form sculpted entirely from solid gold. The body stands approximately five feet tall with perfectly proportioned limbs that catch and reflect light from every angle. The gold surface is polished to a mirror shine, with no imperfections or blemishes anywhere, reflecting the surroundings perfectly. The torso is a smooth cylinder with subtle curves where muscles would be, and the gold flows seamlessly from one part to the next with no visible seams or joints. The arms are perfectly round cylinders with hands that have five defined but blunt fingers each, every digit smooth and featureless. The legs are straight and sturdy, with feet that are slightly pointed at the toes. The head is a smooth, featureless oval about the size of a watermelon, with no nose, no mouth, no eyes, just perfectly polished gold. The core is embedded in the center of the chest, a massive flawless diamond about the size of a fist, glowing with steady golden light so intense that looking directly at it causes eye pain. The gold is heavy beyond its apparent size, pulling at the ground beneath it, and the golem moves with deliberate, measured steps that produce a soft chime with each footfall. The air around it carries the faint smell of incense, and plants bend toward it naturally as if following the sun.
