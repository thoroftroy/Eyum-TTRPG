To start building your character you should write a backstory for your character then build a character sheet that fits your story. For the most part you will be starting at Level 1, that means you are a regular person, you have not accomplished many great things and if you have you should have some kind of in world explanation for how you did that while being so low level. Some campaigns may start at higher levels which allows your characters to have more backstory. 

Your character sheet will look something like this as a base, and we will work on filling out the blank parts together (for blank character sheets see [[1.2.6 Character References]]):

## Filling Out Your Character Sheet
#### Step 1: Core Identity & Bio
Before crunching numbers, define the fundamental traits of your character.
- **Name & Title:** Choose your name and any starting titles, for more information about titles see [[1.3.2 Character Information]].
- **Race & Background:** Select a Race (e.g., Human, Elf) and a Background (e.g., Soldier, Scholar). For more information about backgrounds see [[1.3.2 Character Information]] and for more information about races see [[1.4.0 Races]] or look through the race lists and choose your favorite. 
- **Physical Characteristics:** Fill in Sex, Size, Height, Weight, age, and Build. Your height, weight, and age ranges will be different depending on which race you choose. 
- **Alignment:** Determine your character's moral and ethical outlook. For more information on alignments see [[1.3.2 Character Information]]. If you don't have a backstory yet you can worry about this later. 
#### Step 2: Determine Ability Stats
Your character has six core Stats. (Strength, Dexterity, etc) the standard baseline is 8 (+1).
- **Assign Scores:** First check your Race to see what stat bonuses you get (if any) and add them to your stats. You then are given 24 Stat Points (Stp) to spend at level 1 (located in the first table of your character sheet on the bottom right), you may put them into any of the main 6 stats as you choose, you may also remove from other stats but no starting stat can be lower than 4. For each point above 20 it will now cost 2 stat points to go up 1 point and above 30 it goes to 3 and so on. Please note you are not required to spend all of your points at this time, you may spend them at any time.
	- 0-20 costs 1 point
	- 21-30 costs 2 points
	- 31-40 costs 3 points
	- There is no finite cap to how high the numbers can go, just follow the pattern for higher numbers. 
- **Calculate Modifiers:** For every 2 points away from 10, the modifier changes by 1 (Or to calculate it take your stat subtract 10 and divide by 2).
    - _Example: 8 = -1, 10 = +0, 12 = +1, 14 = +2, etc._
#### Step 3: Calculate Combat Vitals
Use your Stats to fill in the top-middle section:
- **Level:** Start at Level 1 (unless otherwise instructed).
- **Armor Class (AC):** 10+Dexterity Modifier+Armor (You will not have armor usually at level 1 so ignore that for now)
- **Initiative:** Dexterity Modifier
- **Speed:** Determined by your Race
- **Inspiration:** Leave this blank; it is typically earned during gameplay.
- **Action Points (Ap):** Starts at 1
- **Bonus Action Points (BAp):** Starts at 1
- **Reaction Points (Rp):** Starts at 1
#### Step 4: Select Affinities
This system features a long list of 40+ Affinities, for now just worry about the first 7. Fire, earth, water, air, radiant, necrotic, psychic.
- **Assign Values:** Based on your Race mark your relationship with these elements.
	- **Negative Affinity:** -3
	- **Neutral Affinity:** 0
	- **Positive Affinity:** 3
- You are then given 5 Affinity Points (Affp) which you may assign to any of the affinities as you see fit (located next to your stat points on the bottom right of the first table in your character sheet). See [[1.2.1 Affinity List]] to see what you can add points to. At the start you are unable to assign points to anything beyond the base 7. Please note you are not required to spend all of your points at this time, you may spend them at any time.
- Remember that you can never put points into your generic affinity modifier. 
#### Step 5: Skill Proficiencies & Misc
The Skills section is grouped by their governing Ability Stat.
- **Calculate Skill Mods:** Write the corresponding Ability Modifier in the Mod column for each skill.
- **Add Bonuses:** You are given 5 Skill Points (Skp) which you may use freely on any skills you would like. **The cost to go up in a skill point raises as the skill raises (see chart below).** Skills have a maximum modifier of 20. Please note you are not required to spend all of your points at this time, you may spend them at any time, life affinity and stat points they are located at the bottom right of the first table on your character sheet.
- **Mark Proficiency:** As you level up you will gain proficiency in certain skills. If you are proficient in a skill it becomes cheaper to level it up and it gains a bonus from your proficiency bonus. Your proficient bonus starts at +1 and goes up by +1 every 3 levels (Located in the middle of the first table on your character sheet). At level 1 you will also gain Proficiency with 1 Weapon type, for more information see [[1.2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Materials]]. Remember, you are always proficient with your first, you may not add any bonuses (proficiency or stat) to any weapon attack with a weapon you are not proficient in.
- **Mark Expertise:** As you level up you may gain expertise in certain skills. You must already be proficient to gain expertise in any skill. Expertise and proficiency's effects do stack. If you are an Expert in a skill you gain advantage on skill checks using that skill and leveling up that skill becomes cheaper. 
- **Mark Saving Throws:** Choose two saving throws to be proficient in at level 1, these cannot change and you will never be able to gain more saving throw proficiencies. Saving throws cannot gain expertise and you cannot spend points to raise their modifiers. 
- **Skill Tree:** At level 1 you may put one point into the skill tree (see [[1.3.3 Leveling Up]]). you will gain the next Skill Tree Level at Level 2. We will come back to this later when we level you up (give you the first level).
- **Languages:** At level 1 you know common and your racial language. If your intelligence is >14 you may learn an additional language for each 2 points in intelligence beyond 14. To see your racial language check your race in the race section. For more information see [[1.4.1 Languages]].
- **Coinage/Inventory:** You will start with 5 Gold (+-some according to your background and your GM), you may keep this money or spend it shopping before the first session. You may also start with a weapon of your choice, it must be iron tier or lower but does not cost you anything. The normal alternative system would be to roll 1d4 for gold, 1d10+1d6 for silver, and 2d10 for copper as starting money. 
###### Skill Point Cost Chart

| Range | Normal | Proficient | Expert |
| ----- | ------ | ---------- | ------ |
| 1-3   | 1      | 1          | 1      |
| 4-6   | 3      | 1          | 1      |
| 7-9   | 6      | 2          | 1      |
| 10-12 | 9      | 4          | 2      |
| 13-15 | 12     | 6          | 3      |
| 16-18 | 15     | 8          | 4      |
| 19-20 | 18     | 10         | 5      |
> All values within a range cost the listed amount per point. For example, increasing a skill to 7, 8, or 9 costs 6 points for each individual point gained. Skill Points are applied after all other bonuses. Stat Modifiers are calculated first, which means higher base stats increase the cost of improving related skills. Other sources of skill increases (such as proficiency or stat changes) raise the skill value normally and do not increase in cost. The value of each Skill Point depends on when it is allocated.
#### Step 6: Health, Vitality, and Mana
At level 1 you will have to roll for your health vitality and mana. These will be the dice you use each level. When you gain a Dice in any level you simply roll it and add it to your total.
- **Vitality Dice:** 1d8
- **Health Dice:** 1d6
- **Mana Dice:** 1d6

Once you roll the dice you may add relevant modifiers to your vitality and mana stats. By default you would add your Constitution Modifier to your Vitality roll and your Wisdom Modifier to your Mana roll. Your health gets nothing added to it. 
#### Step 7: Level 1
Up to this point, you have only assigned your base stats. Now you formally become Level 1.

At Level 1, you gain the standard “per-level” bonuses (See [[1.3.3 Leveling Up]]):

- +2 Skill Points
- +1 Vitality Dice
- +1 Health Dice
- +1 Mana Dice

These are in addition to the values already assigned earlier (including your initial 5 Skill Points and starting dice). This is because your character is treated as having progressed from Level 0 → Level 1.
##### Skill Tree (Level 1)
You also gain 1 Skill Tree Level at Level 1.

- Normally, Skill Tree Levels are gained every 2 levels (2, 4, 6, etc.)
- However, you receive one at Level 1

Choose one of the following paths:

- **Physical Path:** +2 Base Melee Damage and +1 Base Melee Accuracy
- **Magical Path:** You learn a spell, learn the Magic Blast spell, and gain +1 to your generic affinity
- **Utility/Professional Path:** +2 Skill Points
- **Racial Path:** Unlock Tier 1 Racial Ability
- **Pact Path:** Gain access to Tier 1 Pacts

Now you are done building your character and ready to start!
