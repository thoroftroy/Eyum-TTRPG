To start building your character you should write a backstory for your character then build a character sheet that fits your story. For the most part you will be starting at Level 1, that means you are a regular person, you have not accomplished many great things and if you have you should have some kind of in world explanation for how you did that while being so low level. Some campaigns may start at higher levels which allows your characters to have more backstory. 

Your character sheet will look something like this as a base, and we will work on filling out the blank parts together (for blank character sheets see [[2.7 Character Reference]]):

## Filling Out Your Character Sheet
#### Step 1: Core Identity & Bio
Before crunching numbers, define the fundamental traits of your character.
- **Name & Title:** Choose your name and any starting titles, for more information about titles see [[3.2 Character Information]].
- **Race & Background:** Select a Race (e.g., Human, Elf) and a Background (e.g., Soldier, Scholar). For more information about backgrounds see [[3.2 Character Information]] and for more information about races see [[4.1 Races]] or look through the race lists and choose your favorite. Your race will impact a lot of other things later on so make sure to save this page! 
- **Physical Characteristics:** Fill in Sex, Size, Height, Weight, age, and Build. Your height, weight, and age ranges will be different depending on which race you choose. 
- **Karma:** Determine your character's moral and ethical outlook. For more information on Alignment see [[3.2 Character Information]]. If you don't have a backstory yet you can worry about this later and just set it to 0 for now. 
#### Step 2: Determine Ability Stats
Your character has six core Stats. (Strength, Dexterity, etc) the standard baseline is 8 (-1). Each stat will start at 8 plus or minus some from your race. 
- **Assign Scores:** First check your **Race** to see what stat bonuses you get and add them to your stats, remember to do this **before** assigning your Stat Points. You then are given 24 Stat Points (Stp) to spend at level 1 (located in the first table of your character sheet on the bottom right), you may put them into any of the main 6 stats as you choose. For each point above 20 it will now cost 2 stat points to go up 1 point and above 30 it goes to 3 and so on. Please note you are not required to spend all of your points at this time, you may spend them at any time. You **cannot** lower stats manually to gain more points, and remember if a stat reaches 0 you die. 
	- 0-20 costs 1 point
	- 21-30 costs 2 points
	- 31-40 costs 3 points
	- There is no finite cap to how high the numbers can go, just follow the pattern for higher numbers (every 10 increase the cost by 1).
	- If a stat is increased directly (such as +1 INT) and the increase would place you in a range where raising the Stat normally costs multiple Stat Points, track the gain as a fractional value instead. For example, increasing a Stat from 42 to 43 would normally require 4 Stat Points, so a +1 INT effect would instead grant +0.25 INT, making your score 42.25 INT. Fractional Stat values provide no mechanical benefit on their own, but are retained and combined with future fractional gains until they reach a whole number.
	- Your stat mods are your (score - 10) / 2 rounded down, this means that having an even number in a Stat is desirable because that will actually increase the modifier, all rolls using the stat will use the modifier.

| ----- | --- | --- | ----------- | ---- |

> **How stat buying actually works with an example**
>
> Let's say you want to build a strong fighter. You have 24 Stat Points and your race gives +2 Strength.
>
> **Step 1:** Start with 8 in all stats. That's the baseline.
> **Step 2:** Add your racial bonuses, for this example lets assume that gives you +2 to strength which puts it to 10. 
> **Step 3:** Now you get to add your points, you decide to raise strength. 10 to 20 costs 10 points (1 per point). Now lets say you want to go above 20, you can spend another 10 to get to 25 and now you have 5 points left. If you wanted to just spend everything in strength you'd end up with 27 strength and 1 point left over, you can hold on to it for now or dump it into another stat. 

- **Calculate Modifiers:** For every 2 points away from 10, the modifier changes by 1 (Or to calculate it take your stat subtract 10 and divide by 2).
    - _Example: 8 = -1, 10 = +0, 12 = +1, 14 = +2, etc._
#### Step 3: Calculate Combat Vitals
Use your Stats to fill in the top-middle section:
- **Level:** Start at Level 1 (unless otherwise instructed).
- **Armor Class (AC):** 10+Dexterity Modifier+Armor (You will not have armor usually at level 1 so ignore that for now)
- **Initiative:** Dexterity Modifier
- **Speed:** Determined by your Race
- **Inspiration:** Leave this blank; it is typically earned during gameplay.
- **Action Points (Ap):** Starts at 1
- **Bonus Action Points (BAp):** Starts at 1
- **Reaction Points (Rp):** Starts at 1
#### Step 4: Select Affinities
This system features a long list of 40+ Affinities, for now just worry about the first 7. Fire, earth, water, air, radiant, necrotic, psychic.
- **Assign Values:** Based on your Race mark your relationship with these elements.
	- **Negative Affinity:** -3
	- **Neutral Affinity:** 0
	- **Positive Affinity:** 3
- You are then given 5 Affinity Points (Affp) which you may assign to any of the affinities as you see fit, however you may not spend these points in your Generic Affinity. See [[2.1 Affinities]] to see what you can add points to as some affinities (usually complex ones) require reaching prerequisites *(typically just reaching high enough affinity in other affinities)* before they can be increased with Affinity Points. Please note you are not required to spend all of your points at this time, you may spend them at any time.
- Remember that you **can not** put points into your Generic Affinity. 
- These affinities will grant bonuses to magical or elemental skills, typically they effect play in 3 ways which can be found in [[2.1 Affinities#Affinity Bonus Tables]]. 
	- **Attack Roll Bonus:** Is added to your **spell attack rolls**, so the higher your affinity with that spell the more likely you are to hit. 
	- **Attack Damage Bonus:** Is sometimes added to the **damage** of specific spells and abilities. 
	- **Save DC Bonus:** Your spell save DC will be 10 + your Save DC Bonus, this means when you cast a spell or use a skill which requires someone else to roll against your save dc, they roll against this number. 
#### Step 5: Skill Proficiencies & Misc
The Skills section is grouped by their governing Ability Stat.

- **Calculate Skill Mods:** Write the corresponding Ability Modifier in the Mod column for each skill.

- **Mark Proficiency:** As you level up you will gain proficiency in certain skills. If you are proficient in a skill it becomes cheaper to level it up and it gains a bonus from your proficiency bonus. Your proficient bonus starts at +1 and goes up by +1 every 3 levels. You may also gain Expertise in a skill you are proficient in, that will make it even cheaper and grant advantage on rolls with that skill, but you will not have any expertise at level 1. At level one you must choose proficiency in: 
	- Two Skills (that are not saving throws)
	- One Saving Throw
	- One Weapon/Armor Type (see [[2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Tools]])
- Remember, you are always proficient with your fist, you may not add any bonuses (proficiency or stat) to any weapon attack with a weapon you are not proficient in. For more rulings on proficiency and expertise see [[1.2 Dice and Skills#Modifiers, Proficiency, and Expertise|The Proficiency Section]].

- **Add Bonuses:** You are given 5 Skill Points (Skp) which you may use freely on any skills you would like. **The cost to go up in a skill point raises as the skill raises (see chart below).** Please note you are not required to spend all of your points at this time, you may spend them at any time. If a bonus is raised directly from a skill or feature it **bypasses** the skill point cost chart. The same applies to increasing your stat points or gaining proficiency which will automatically increase the skill value ignoring the skill point costs, all future points spent will use the new value. You cannot ever spend Skill Points in your Saving Throws, only in specific skills. You must mark Proficiency, Add your Proficiency Bonus and add your Stat Mods **before** you spend your Skill Points. 

- **Languages:** At level 1 you know common and your racial language. If your intelligence is >14 you may learn an additional language for each 2 points in intelligence beyond 14 (+1 at 16, +2 at 18, +3 at 20). To see your racial language check your race in the race section. For more information see [[4.2 Languages]]. You may not start with more than 3 additional languages learned this way. 

- **Racial Abilities:** Most racial abilities are locked behind racial tiers, but not all. Earlier you used your race to determine things like your speed, affinities, and starting stats, now its time to look at the Racial Tiers. In each race section you will see a description of the race followed by a section labeled Racial Tiers. If you look at a race like the [[Eyum TTRPG/4.0 Races/Human and Subraces#Human|Human]] you will see that all abilities are locked behind racial tiers, if you want to use them you have to take levels in the Racial Path (we will come back to this later). Now look at the race [[Eyum TTRPG/4.0 Races/Human and Subraces#Giant|Giant]]. Below the Racial Tiers heading but above any of the actual Tiers is the note: You have disadvantage on any roll which uses Affinity. You have -5 to your Generic Affinity. These bonuses apply no matter what, weather you have racial tier levels or not. For the Giant this is a net negative, you just become really bad at using magic, but for some races like say the [[Eyum TTRPG/4.0 Races/Human and Subraces#Iceborn|Iceborn]] you get Gain +2 Ice/Cold Affinity and lesser weakness to Fire damage. That's extra affinity in a affinity which you normally can't even unlock this early. Always check what racial abilities you gain at level 1 and add those bonuses (or negatives) to your character sheet. 

- **Coinage/Inventory:** You will start with some money which you may keep or spend before the first session. You may buy any common item at the price listed in [[Eyum TTRPG/2.0 Reference Tables/2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Tools|2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Tools]] as long as you can afford it. Remember to check [[Eyum TTRPG/2.0 Reference Tables/2.6 Materials|2.6 Materials]] for any material modifier you may want to apply to your items (and a couple other items you may want to buy). To find how much money you start with you must do the following. You may **either** start with 7 Gold **or** start with 1d12 Gold, you may not change your mind after making the roll. If your GM permits it you may start with more or less money depending on your Background. You also automatically start with the following items:
	- 10 Poor Rations
	- 50 ft of Rope
	- Bedroll
	- Blanket
	- Waterskin
	- Flint and Steel
- Typically you do not have to worry about inventory, you are always assumed to be able to carry everything which you have. You do not need to worry about filling your waterskin (unless the GM decides you do), but you must sleep each night or gain exhaustion and eat at least 2 rations a day.
- Remember to buy a weapon, and probably armor as well, make sure you wright down your new **AC** after buying and equipping your armor. 
###### Skill Point Cost Chart

| Range | Normal | Proficient | Expert |
| ----- | ------ | ---------- | ------ |
| 1-3   | 1      | 1          | 1      |
| 4-6   | 3      | 1          | 1      |
| 7-9   | 6      | 2          | 1      |
| 10-12 | 9      | 4          | 2      |
| 13-15 | 12     | 6          | 3      |
| 16-18 | 15     | 8          | 4      |
| 19-20 | 18     | 10         | 5      |
| 21+   | 50     | 25         | 10     |

> All values within a range cost the listed amount per point. For example, increasing a skill to 7, 8, or 9 costs 6 points for each individual point gained. Skill Points are applied after all other bonuses. Stat Modifiers are calculated first, which means higher base stats increase the cost of improving related skills. Other sources of skill increases (such as proficiency or stat changes) raise the skill value normally and do not increase in cost. The value of each Skill Point depends on when it is allocated. 

> **Example**
>
> You want to get good at Athletics. Your Strength is 16 (+3), so your Athletics stat modifier starts at +3. That's your base you don't pay points for that.
>
> Now you want to add points on top. You have 5 Skill Points at level 1. Lets say you put all of them into athletics. You currently don't have proficiency with athletics yet so raising from 3 to 4 is going to cost 3 whole points. Now to go from 4 to 5 it would cost another 3 but you don't have that much. 
> 
> Now lets imagine you do have proficiency. Now raising from 3 to 4 would only cost 1 point, From 4 to 5 another 1, then to 6 for another 1 point. You've now made it higher than before and spent only 3 points. To raise to 7 you'll have to spend 2 points instead and that's all the points you have. 
> 
> Now lets imagine you have expertise. it will only cost 1 point for everything up to 10 so you can spend all 5 points to go to 8 no problem. 
> 
> Now lets imagine you gained +5 to your strength score over the course of some levels. Now you are at 21 (+5) strength. This puts your athletics modifier to a 10 so now even with expertise it will cost 2 to go higher. Raising your stat score will raise the points regardless of cost.
#### Step 6: Health, Vitality, and Mana
At level 1 you will have to roll for your health vitality and mana. These will be the dice you use each level. When you gain a Dice in any level you simply roll it and add it to your total.
- **Vitality Dice:** 1d8 (+ Con Mod)
- **Health Dice:** 1d6
- **Mana Dice:** 1d6 (+ Wis Mod)

Once you roll the dice you may add relevant modifiers to your vitality and mana stats. By default you would add your Constitution Modifier to your Vitality roll and your Wisdom Modifier to your Mana roll. Your health gets nothing added to it. 
#### Step 7: Level 1
Up to this point, you have only assigned your base stats (Technically you were level 0). Now you formally become Level 1.

At Level 1, you gain the standard “per-level” bonuses (See [[3.3 Leveling Up]]):

- +2 Skill Points
- +1 Vitality Dice
- +1 Health Dice
- +1 Mana Dice

These are in addition to the values already assigned earlier (including your initial 5 Skill Points and starting dice). This is because your character is treated as having progressed from Level 0 → Level 1.
##### Skill Tree (Level 1)
You also gain 1 Skill Tree Level at Level 1. For more information on skill tree levels see [[3.3 Leveling Up]]

Choose one of the following paths:

- **Physical Path:** +2 Base Melee or Ranged Damage and +1 Base Melee or Ranged Accuracy
- **Magical Path:** You learn a spell, learn the Magic Blast spell, and gain +1 to your generic affinity
- **Utility/Professional Path:** +2 Skill Points
- **Racial Path:** Unlock Tier 1 Racial Ability
- **Pact Path:** Gain access to Tier 1 Pacts

Now you are done building your character and ready to start!
