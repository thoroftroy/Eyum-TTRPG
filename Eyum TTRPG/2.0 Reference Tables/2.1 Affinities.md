> **Unlocking complex affinities**
>
> You want to cast Lightning spells. Lightning requires 10 Fire + 10 Air. That's 20 total affinity points in the base elements before you unlock it. Once unlocked, you can put Affinity Points directly into Lightning.
>
> **Here's a real path a Pyromancer might take:**
> - Start with 5 Fire, 5 Air (from racial bonuses and Affp at level 1).
> - Over the next few levels, put Affp into Fire and Air until both hit 10. That's 20 points total invested.
> - Now Lightning unlocks. You can put future Affp into Lightning instead of Fire/Air if you want.
>
> **What about advanced affinities like Obsidian?** That requires 10 points in Water + Air + Earth + 20 points in Necrotic (40 total). That's a major investment, you're probably level 15+ before you unlock it. And you (usually) can't shortcut it (some specific abilities and feats may directly raise affinities without first reaching the prerequisite affinity thresholds). You have to build up the tree from the roots.
> 
> When a feat or racial ability or so on grants an affinity it does not unlock that affinity in the same sense as the lightning from before. If you are able to raise it enough like that then you can use spells and features with that affinity but you cannot raise it with Affinity Points (Affp) until the prerequisites are met regardless of how high it is.

> **Example: a monster's fire breath:**
> 
> Say you're running a monster that has 15 Fire Affinity and its stat block lists an ability like "DC 10 + Save DC Bonus." The first thing you do is flip to the Save DC table and find where 15 lands, that's in the 14 to 19 range, which gives a **+3 bonus** from the Standard column. So the final DC for that fire breath is 10 plus 3, which is **DC 13**. That's it. 
>
> **Example: a player casting a fire spell:**
> 
> Now let's say you're a player with 25 Fire Affinity and 5 Generic Affinity, and you want to figure out your Spell Save DC and your Spell Attack Modifier for your fire spells. You'll need to look up four different numbers, two for Generic and two for Standard, because the formulas pull from both columns.
>
> Start with your **Spell Save DC.** The formula is 10 + your Generic Save DC Bonus + your Standard Save DC Bonus. Look up your 5 Generic Affinity on the Save DC table's Generic column, that falls in the 4 to 6 range, so your Generic Save DC Bonus is **+2**. Now look up your 25 Fire Affinity on the Save DC table's Standard column, that falls in the 20 to 27 range, so your Standard Save DC Bonus is **+4**. Add them together with the base of 10: 10 + 2 + 4 = **16**. That's the number enemies need to roll on their save to resist your fire spells.
>
> Next is your **Spell Attack Modifier.** The formula here is your Generic Attack Roll Bonus + your Standard Attack Roll Bonus + your Base Magic Accuracy. Flip over to the Attack Roll table. Your 5 Generic Affinity lands in the 3 to 5 range on the Generic column, giving you **+2**. Your 25 Fire Affinity lands in the 25 to 31 range on the Standard column, giving you **+6**. So before even adding your Base Magic Accuracy, you're sitting at +2 plus +6 which is **+8** to hit with fire spells. If your Base Magic Accuracy is, say, +3 from your skills, your total spell attack modifier would be +11 which is then added to 1d20 roll when making an attack and compared to the targets AC.
>
>Now lets say your fire spell said something like roll 1d10 + your **Attack Damage Bonus** for Fire. You have 25 Fire Affinity which lands in the 24 to 39 range which grants **+6** damage so 1d10 + 6 damage.
