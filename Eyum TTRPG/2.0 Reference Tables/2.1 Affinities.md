Some affinities cannot be increased until reaching high enough affinity with other affinities. Most affinities have a weakness, if a creature is of a type it is usually resistant to its affinity and weak to whatever that affinity is weak to (typically). For more detailed on Affinity see [[1.3 Player Stats]] in the section titled Affinity.

> **Unlocking complex affinities**
>
> You want to cast Lightning spells. Lightning requires 10 Fire + 10 Air. That's 20 total affinity points in the base elements before you unlock it. Once unlocked, you can put Affinity Points directly into Lightning.
>
> **Here's a real path a Pyromancer might take:**
> - Start with 5 Fire, 5 Air (from racial bonuses and Affp at level 1).
> - Over the next few levels, put Affp into Fire and Air until both hit 10. That's 20 points total invested.
> - Now Lightning unlocks. You can put future Affp into Lightning instead of Fire/Air if you want.
>
> **What about advanced affinities like Obsidian?** That requires 10 points in Water + Air + Earth + 20 points in Necrotic (40 total). That's a major investment, you're probably level 15+ before you unlock it. And you (usually) can't shortcut it (some specific abilities and feats may directly raise affinities without first reaching the prerequisite affinity thresholds). You have to build up the tree from the roots.
> 
> When a feat or racial ability or so on grants an affinity it does not unlock that affinity in the same sense as the lightning from before. If you are able to raise it enough like that then you can use spells and features with that affinity but you cannot raise it with Affinity Points (Affp) until the prerequisites are met regardless of how high it is.
