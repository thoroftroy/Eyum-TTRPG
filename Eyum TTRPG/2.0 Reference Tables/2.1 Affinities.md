> **Unlocking complex affinities**
>
> You want to cast Lightning spells. Lightning requires 10 Fire + 10 Air. That's 20 total affinity points in the base elements before you unlock it. Once unlocked, you can put Affinity Points directly into Lightning.
>
> **Here's a real path a Pyromancer might take:**
> - Start with 5 Fire, 5 Air (from racial bonuses and Affp at level 1).
> - Over the next few levels, put Affp into Fire and Air until both hit 10. That's 20 points total invested.
> - Now Lightning unlocks. You can put future Affp into Lightning instead of Fire/Air if you want.
>
> **What about advanced affinities like Obsidian?** That requires 10 points in Water + Air + Earth + 20 points in Necrotic (40 total). That's a major investment, you're probably level 15+ before you unlock it. And you (usually) can't shortcut it (some specific abilities and feats may directly raise affinities without first reaching the prerequisite affinity thresholds). You have to build up the tree from the roots.
> 
> When a feat or racial ability or so on grants an affinity it does not unlock that affinity in the same sense as the lightning from before. If you are able to raise it enough like that then you can use spells and features with that affinity but you cannot raise it with Affinity Points (Affp) until the prerequisites are met regardless of how high it is. 
## Affinity Bonus Tables
Affinity points have diminishing returns, the following tables will tell your your bonuses in each circumstance based on your affinity value.
- When anything lists a DC formula that includes "[[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]" (e.g., "DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]"), look up the creature or character's affinity on the Save DC table, add the listed bonus to the base DC, and that is the final DC.
- When anything lists a Damage Formula which includes + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]], look up the creature or character's affinity on the [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] Table, add the listed bonus to your attack roll, and that is your damage. 
- When making any spell attack you would use your [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Roll Bonus|Attack Roll Bonus]] from the table similarly. 
##### Information For Nerds
All six columns (three bonus types × Standard/Generic) use the same unified formula: **`min(b) = A × b² + B × b + C`** where `b` is the bonus number and `min(b)` is the lowest affinity that grants that bonus. This produces diminishing returns because each additional +1 costs more affinity than the last. After the table ends, the post-table flat rate is `+1 per D` points, where D = `2A × b + B` evaluated at the last listed bonus (the derivative of the formula at that point). `A` controls falloff (larger A = bonuses get expensive faster), `B` controls the initial slope (how fast the first few bonuses come), `C` is the base offset (minimum affinity for bonus 1 ≈ A + B + C). The Generic column uses the same A, B, C structure with smaller values (same shape, lower thresholds). Negative bonuses are handset based mostly on vibes. 

**Parameter values per table:**

|-------|-----------|---|---|---|-----------------|
| Attack Roll | Standard | 0.55 | 2.0 | 1.0 | +1 per 20 |
| Attack Roll | Generic | 0.25 | 1.0 | 0.5 | +1 per 10 |
| Attack Damage | Standard | 0.35 | 1.5 | 1.0 | +1 per 10 |
| Attack Damage | Generic | 0.17 | 0.7 | 0.5 | +1 per 5 |

> **Example: a monster's fire breath:**
> 
> Say you're running a monster that has 15 Fire Affinity and its stat block lists an ability like "DC 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]." The first thing you do is flip to the Save DC table and find where 15 lands: that's in the 11 to 15 range on the Standard column, which gives a **+3 bonus**. So the final DC for that fire breath is 10 plus 3, which is **DC 13**.
>
> **Example: a player casting a fire spell:**
> 
> Now let's say you're a player with 25 Fire Affinity and 5 Generic Affinity, and you want to figure out your Spell Save DC and your Spell Attack Modifier for your fire spells. You'll need to look up four different numbers, two for Generic and two for Standard, because the formulas pull from both columns.
>
> Start with your **Spell Save DC.** The formula is 10 + your Generic [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]] + your Standard [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]]. Look up your 5 Generic Affinity on the Save DC table's Generic column: that falls in the 4 to 4 range, giving a Generic [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]] of **+3**. Now look up your 25 Fire Affinity on the Save DC table's Standard column: that falls in the 22 to 28 range, giving a Standard [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]] of **+5**. Add them together with the base of 10: 10 + 3 + 5 = **DC 18**.
>
> Next is your **Spell Attack Modifier.** The formula here is your Generic [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Roll Bonus|Attack Roll Bonus]] + your Standard [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Roll Bonus|Attack Roll Bonus]] + your Base Magic Accuracy. Flip over to the Attack Roll table. Your 5 Generic Affinity lands in the 9 to 12 range on the Generic column, giving you a Generic [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Roll Bonus|Attack Roll Bonus]] of **+5**. Your 25 Fire Affinity lands in the 19 to 25 range on the Standard column, giving you a Standard [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Roll Bonus|Attack Roll Bonus]] of **+5**. So before adding your Base Magic Accuracy, you're at +5 plus +5 which is **+10** to hit. If your Base Magic Accuracy is +3, your total spell attack modifier would be +13 added to the d20 roll.
>
> Now let's say your fire spell says roll 1d10 + your [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] for Fire. You have 25 Fire Affinity which lands in the 24 to 29 range on the Attack Damage Standard column, granting a **+7** damage bonus. So 1d10 + 7 damage.
##### Python Code for Nerds

```python
# Eyum TTRPG Affinity Table Generator
# Formula: min_affinity(bonus) = round(A * bonus^2 + B * bonus + C)
# All six table columns use this same formula with different A, B, C parameters.
#
# A = falloff rate (larger A = bonuses get expensive faster, stronger diminishing returns)
# B = initial slope (how fast the first few bonuses arrive)
# C = base offset (minimum affinity to get bonus +1)
#
# The post-table flat rate is: +1 per D points
# where D = round(2*A*max_bonus + B / 5) * 5  (derivative at last bonus, rounded to 5)
#
# To customize: change the params dict below. Larger A = steeper falloff.
# Smaller A/B/C in Generic columns = bonuses come at lower affinity (easier to get).

params = {
    # (A, B, C, max_bonus, post_step_rounding)
    "Attack Roll Standard":   (0.55, 2.0, 1.0, 15, 5),
    "Attack Roll Generic":    (0.25, 1.0, 0.5, 15, 5),
    "Attack Damage Standard": (0.35, 1.5, 1.0, 15, 5),
    "Attack Damage Generic":  (0.17, 0.7, 0.5, 15, 5),
    "Save DC Standard":       (0.45, 1.8, 1.0, 15, 5),
    "Save DC Generic":        (0.20, 0.9, 0.5, 15, 5),
}

def generate_table(A, B, C, max_b):
    """Generate upper bounds and ranges for bonus 0 to max_b."""
    upper = []
    for b in range(0, max_b + 1):
        if b == 0:
            u = round(C)  # bonus 0 upper bound
        else:
            u = round(A * b * b + B * b + C)
        upper.append(u)
    # Ensure strictly increasing
    for i in range(1, len(upper)):
        if upper[i] <= upper[i-1]:
            upper[i] = upper[i-1] + 1
    # Post-table step from derivative at max bonus
    deriv = 2 * A * max_b + B
    step = max(5, round(deriv / 5) * 5)
    return upper, step

if __name__ == "__main__":
    for name, (A, B, C, max_b, _) in params.items():
        upper, step = generate_table(A, B, C, max_b)
        print(f"\n### {name}")
        print(f"min(b) = {A}b² + {B}b + {C}  |  Post: +1/{step} beyond {upper[-1]}")
        for b in range(0, max_b + 1):
            lo = 1 if b == 0 else upper[b-1] + 1
            hi = upper[b]
            print(f"  +{b:2d}: {lo:4d}-{hi:<4d}")
```
