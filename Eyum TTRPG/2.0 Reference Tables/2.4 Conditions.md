Conditions are status effects that change what a creature can do or impose some effect on the affected person for the duration. 
#### Stacking and contradictions
- If a creature has multiple status effects at once, resolve each independently.
- If two effects contradict one another (e.g., [[2.4 Conditions#Magical-Conditions|Hasted]] and [[2.4 Conditions#Magical-Conditions|Slowed]]), they cancel each other.
- You may have multiple levels or stacks of a condition if the condition specifies that it is possible. 
	- A condition that cannot be stacked will simply gain more time of it being active if reapplied
	- A condition that can be stacked will increase how long it is active as well as other potential effects
	- If a condition is stacked from a separate source with a separate DC or end condition the higher DC or more penalized end condition takes priority and effects the rest of the condition. For example, if you have 3 stacks of regular Bleeding but then a spell is cast on you to give you bleeding with a DC of 15 for 4 turns you will now have 7 stacks of bleeding and the higher DC applies to all of it. 

The DC to remove a condition may be different if specified by the source of the condition, such as a powerful spell but the specified DC is the default when none is provided. If a condition specifies a time (Lasts 3 turns) then it can still be removed with remove / end unless otherwise stated in the skill. 

If a damage type causes a condition which causes a the same damage type it will not cause the condition again. Conditions cannot activate/be caused by themselves.
