Alchemy is the processing of materials into workable Alchemic Mass and the recombining of those masses to produce effects. This page defines all material properties and the rules for changing materials between forms (Solid, Liquid, Powder, Gel).
## Material Properties
Every material usable in alchemy has the following properties:

| Property        | Meaning                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Affinity        | The elemental type carried by the material. Each material has exactly one.                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Natural Form    | Solid, Liquid, Gas, Powder, or Gel. The form the material is in when harvested or at room temperature.                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Hardness        | How hard the material is. To grind something, the grinding surface must have a higher Hardness.                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Moisture        | 0-100%. The percentage of the material that is liquid (usually water). 0% = bone dry. To be ground into powder the material must have 0-5% moisture. To be a gel the material must have 10-80% moisture.                                                                                             |
| Boiling Point   | Temperature (F) at which the material melts (if solid), boils (if liquid), or evaporates (if gel). "Burns" = organic, burns instead of melting. "Sublimes" = goes directly from solid to gas.                                                                                                        |
| Solubility      | 1-30. How resistant the material is to being dissolved. Higher = harder to dissolve.                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Solvent Potency | How effective this material is at dissolving other materials when in liquid or gel form. A material dissolves into a solvent when the solvent's Solvent Potency >= (the material's Solubility + 3). The material which is being dissolved into (the one doing the dissolving) must be liquid or gel. |
| Base Potency    | Starting alchemical potency. Higher quality = higher value.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Organic         | Yes/No. Determines interaction rules when combining with non-organic materials.                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |

## Processing: Changing Forms
- **Drying**: Materials with moisture above 0-5% must be dried before grinding. Spread thinly and apply heat (at least 200 F): 1 hour per 5% moisture per ounce. When gels are heated this way they lose all moister and become a powder without needing to be ground. This process may reduce a liquid to a gel see Thickening for more. 
- **Thickening:** Add a thickening agent to a liquid to turn it into a gel or boil off its liquid until it becomes more solid.
	- Some materials when dried will become a gel when they reach specific moisture thresholds. 
- **Grinding:** A solid is ground against a surface with higher Hardness. 
	- The surface can be anything: a stone slab, a harder piece of metal, a file, another mineral. 
	- If the surface's Hardness is not higher than the material's, grinding is impossible. 
	- In order to be ground the material must be at 0-5% moisture. 
	- It takes 10 minutes to grind one ounce of material. This time is reduced by 30 seconds per hardness point above the other material. 
- **Melting:** Heat the solid to its Boiling Point. If the material's Boiling Point says "Burns," it cannot be melted (it ignites instead). If it says "Sublimes," it turns directly to gas at that temperature instead of becoming a usable liquid. Any heat source capable of reaching the temperature works: fire magic, a forge, a campfire for low-temp materials like lead or fat.
	- It takes 10 minutes per ounce once temperature is reached, if temperature lowers below melting point then the time is extended by 10 min per 6 seconds the material is too cool at a maximum time of 10 min per ounce of material. 
- **Dissolving:** A solid or powder is placed into contact with a liquid or gel. If the liquid/gel's Solvent Potency >= (the solid/powder's Solubility + 3), the material dissolves. If the Potency is below the threshold, dissolution never occurs regardless of time.
	- It takes 6 seconds to dissolve 1 ounce of material per 3 full points the solvent's Potency. 
## Combining Materials
- **Adding Ingredients:** Ingredients are combined one at a time in a specific order. "A + B" means A is placed in the mixing vessel first, then B is added to it. "B + A" is a different combination that may produce a different result.
- **Recipe Tables:** Each valid combination of A.M. types produces exactly one outcome listed in the Recipe Tables. Any combination not listed produces the appropriate type of A.M. (Junk).
- **Form Interaction:** The physical forms being combined affect the difficulty and sometimes the result. Powders mixed dry are inert until triggered. Liquids react on contact. Gels must be kneaded together and react as they mix.
	-  **Powder + Powder**: Inert until triggered with liquid, heat, or mana. Can be stored indefinitely. Easiest to work with.
	- **Powder + Liquid**: Reaction begins on contact. The powder dissolves or suspends into the liquid.
	- **Powder + Gel**: Powder is kneaded into the gel. Reaction begins as the powder distributes.
	- **Liquid + Liquid**: Fastest reaction. Immediate on contact.
	- **Liquid + Gel**: Liquid stirred into gel. Gel absorbs the liquid and reaction begins as the liquid distributes. 
	- **Gel + Gel**: Slowest to combine. Must be thoroughly kneaded together for any reaction.
- **Triggers:** Dry powder combinations require one of the following to begin reacting:
	- Add any liquid, counts as an ingredient. 
	- Channel mana into the mixture. Spend mana equal to the total Potency of all ingredients x 2.
- **Organic Interaction Rule:** When organic and non-organic A.M. types are combined, they do not fully integrate unless they are Purified Organic. For all other Organic Material the result's Potency is reduced by 2.
- **The Combination Check:** After all ingredients are combined in order and triggers/mana/heat are applied, make the combination check.
	- **Modifier:** Your check modifier is the sum of contributions from each unique affinity present in the recipe. For each unique affinity:
		- Take your affinity modifier for that type
		- Divide it by the total number of unique affinities in the recipe
		- If that affinity appears more than once in the recipe, add (0.5 x number of times it appears)
		- Round up
		- Add the results for all affinities together
		- If you have fewer than 3 affinity in the specified affinity you treat it as having -5 in that affinity for this equation. 
		- **Example**: A recipe with 2 Fire, 1 Earth, and 3 Lightning ingredients has 3 unique affinities. Your Fire modifier is divided by 3, plus (0.5 x 2 = 1.0 bonus). Your Earth modifier is divided by 3, no bonus (appears once). Your Lightning modifier is divided by 3, plus (0.5 x 3 = 1.5 bonus). Round each up, then add all three for a total of 4 + 3 + 4 = 11.
	- **DC**: The DC is calculated as 10 + the Potency of each ingredient. 
		- **Success**: The product is created as described, the potency of the product is equal to all average potency of the ingredients (add all ingredients then divide by the number of ingredients).
			- **Natural 20**: The product's Potency is increased by +2.
		- **Failure** by 1-5: All ingredients are consumed. The mixture becomes the appropriate type of Junk (Organic if all ingredients were organic, Metal if metals were used, Crystal if crystals, Mixed if organic + non-organic).
			- **Failure by 6+**: As above, but the Junk is Contaminated and cannot be purified
			- **Natural 1**: If any ingredient has Potency 4+, the mixture explodes. Deals 1d6 damage of the dominant affinity per total Potency to all creatures within 5 ft. If no ingredient has Potency 4+, materials simply become Contaminated Junk.

> The form of the result is dependent on the recipe and will be listed on the table.
## Distilling
Any completed alchemical product can be distilled to increase its effectiveness. Distilling works by boiling the product and capturing only the most concentrated vapors, discarding the inert mass.

- **Process**: Heat the product in any enclosed vessel that can capture and re-condense vapors (an Alembic, a pot with a tight lid and a tube, etc.). Bring to a low boil and maintain for 1 hour.
- **Result**: The product's Potency doubles. The product's weight and volume are reduced to 1/4 of their previous amount.
- **Repeatable**: Distillation can be repeated without limit. Each round takes 1 hour, doubles Potency, reduces weight/volume to 1/4.
- **Minimum volume**: Once the product drops below 1/8 oz, it is too small to handle and must be dissolved into a solvent (add 1 oz of any clean liquid) to remain usable. The solvent dilutes the product but does not reduce Potency (the active essence is still there, just spread across more liquid).
- **Check**: No check is required for basic distillation. Anyone with the equipment and time can distill a product.
- **Natural 20 on original combination check**: A product created with a Natural 20 starts at Potency +2, which is then doubled by subsequent distillations. A Potency 3 product that was created with a Natural 20 becomes Potency 5, then 10, then 20, etc.
