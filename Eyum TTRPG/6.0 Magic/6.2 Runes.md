All runes can be purchased as single use items from stores, runes are drawn on Runic Material, explained lower down. They can then be used for enchanting and spells. Runes cannot be activated without a level in Runemaster path. 
# Runic Inscription (Enchanting)
Runes are ancient symbols etched into items to tap into the fundamental forces of the world.
- Xeran Runes: Used for Permanent Enchantments. Once applied, the item is permanently changed.
- Vindroth Runes: Used for complex Spellcasting. Will be explained lower down.
### Runic Logic & Constraints
- A single Xeran rune can be etched onto an item with no penalty. However, if you wish to place two or more effect runes on a single object, they must be bound within a Runic Circle.
- Enchanted items cannot be modified. You must decide the full runic array before the first inscription.
- A single runic circle can support a maximum of 10 Effect Runes.
- The Carbon Ratio: In any circle, the volatile energies must be balanced by Carbon Runes. The formula is:
	- *Number of Carbon Runes = Number of Effect Runes - 1*

## The Inscription Ritual
Inscribing a runic array is a delicate process of channeling mana through physical matter. To successfully enchant an item, a crafter must perform a sequence of Inscription Checks, one for every rune in the array (including Carbon runes).

1. The Inscription Check
	- For each rune, roll a d20 and add your Relevant Affinity Modifier.
	- The Base DC is 12 and it goes up depending on the DC Mod (see above table).
		- **Example**: Inscribing a Fire Rune has a total DC of 16.
	- When you make the check for the rune you must spend its mana cost. The cost is calculated by the cost of the rune and the size of the circle (the Mana Surcharge).
		- **Example**: The mana cost for a Fire rune in a 3 rune circle would be 4 (from the rune itself) + 20 (for the mana surcharge). This mana will be spent for EACH rune in the circle after a successful check is made. You do not need to spend any mana on the carbon runes. 

2. The Ritual Sequence
	- If you are creating a Runic Circle, you must roll for every rune in the circle, pick the first rune to inscribe (it doesn't matter which one) and then go around the circle at your leisure. Mana is spent as you go. You must outline the entire circle and put a blank space for each rune before you begin the inscription. 
	- If you pass the roll, the rune is successfully etched. Move to the next rune in the array. There is no time limit in between runic checks, you may wait as long as you need before attempting to inscribe the next rune. Until all runes in the circle are inscribed the runes will do nothing. 
	- **Critical Success (Natural 20**): The rune is etched perfectly; you gain Advantage on the next roll in the sequence, **no mana is spent on this rune**.

3. Failed Inscriptions & Unstable Mana
	- If a single roll in the sequence fails the ritual fails and the item becomes saturated with unstable mana.
	- The item cannot be enchanted further, and the current runes provide no bonuses and are destroyed.
	- To clear the Saturated state you must spend some mana to fix the item, the mana cost will change based on how much mana you spent so far using the below formula. Once fixed, the item is restored to its original, non-magical state and can be enchanted again from scratch, ALL runes are destroyed in this process.
		- **Desaturation formula:** (Mana Spent / 3) x (Number of Runes Inscribed + 5)
		- **Example:** Say you successfully inscribed 2 of 3 fire runes in a runic circle that was meant to be 3 fire runes. 
			- The cost of one Fire rune inscription would be 4 (from the rune itself) + 20 (for the mana surcharge) so that's 24 mana per rune meaning we spent 48 mana. 
			- Divide the mana we spent by 3 (always round down if applicable), 48 / 3 = 16. 
			- Now multiply the mana cost by the number of runes inscribed plus 5 (2 runes + 5 = 7). This would be 16 x 7 = 112.
			- So in order to desaturate the item/inscription you would need to spend 112 mana. 
		- **Note:** Carbon runes have no mana cost so they are not included in this, if you failed and have only done checks on Carbon runes and nothing else it will cost nothing to desaturate it. 

> **The ritual step by step, with a full walkthrough**
>
> Let's say you want to inscribe a 3-Fire-rune circle on a steel sword. You have +5 Fire Affinity and 80 mana to work with.
>
> **Step 1: Outline the circle:** You draw a circle big enough for 5 runes (3 Fire + 2 Carbon). Before you start inscribing, the runes do nothing.
>
> **Step 2: First rune:** You pick a spot and attempt the first Fire rune. The DC is 12 + 4 (DC Mod) = 16. You roll a 13 + 5 = 18. Success! You spend 2 (rune) + 20 (surcharge) = 22 mana. One down.
>
> **Step 3: Second rune:** Same DC. You roll a 9 + 5 = 14. Failure. The entire circle is ruined. You've spent 22 mana so far.
>
> **Step 4: Desaturation:** To clear the sword, you need to pay (22 / 3) × (1 + 5) = 7 × 6 = 42 mana. The sword is now clean but you're down 64 total mana and have nothing to show for it.
>
> **The smart approach:** If you had inscribed both Carbon runes first (free, no risk of losing mana since they cost 0), then attempted the three Fire runes, you'd have a buffer. And if you roll a natural 20 on any rune, that rune costs 0 mana and you get Advantage on the next roll.
>
> **Pro tip:** Materials with high Enchantability (like Gold, +12) turn DC 16 checks into DC 4 checks. A +5 Fire mage with a gold sword literally cannot fail a Fire rune inscription (except by rolling a natural 1 which always fails). Always factor your material's enchantability into your plans, but remember that if the material isn't listed in [[2.6 Materials]] it has no enchantability modifier. 
## Making New Elements
When you combine runes of different elements, you can create **new damage types** and their associated effects. Use the **[[2.1 Affinities]]** list to see which elements combine into which results. Rune combinations are influenced by the user’s intent, meaning intermediate pairings don’t have to occur if they aren’t the intended outcome. For example, Obsidian is made from Ice/Cold + Blight. Since there are no direct Ice/Cold or Blight runes, you instead build them from their components: (Water + Air) to represent Ice/Cold, and (Necrotic + Earth) to represent Blight, so the full set is Water + Air + Necrotic + Earth. In this case, Air + Necrotic does not automatically form Toxin/Poison, because the declared intent is to form Ice/Cold + Blight, which then combines into Obsidian. These combined elements can be used the same ways as normal elemental runes.

Greater Elemental runes and regular elemental runes will not combine together.
# Vindroth Runes
Vindroth runes are written as sentence structures (often called _paragraphs_ or spells), allowing rune magic to function like a programmed trigger system, when a condition is met, an effect occurs. Vindroth runes are not arranged in circles. Instead, they are inscribed as ordered “words” that form a readable sequence (ex: If … Than … Else …).
### Inscription Rules
- Each Vindroth rune occupies 1 square inch of space.
- All runes in a paragraph must be inscribed into one continuous piece of material.
- If the material has a complete break that separates words, the paragraph loses the separated words. This may weaken, change, or fully disable the effect.
### Vindroth Structure Limits
A Vindroth paragraph is limited by your Level.
- You may include 1 “If … Than” block per your Level/2.
- Every 2 Levels also increases how many logic-connectors you may include (Else / And / Or / Not).
### Using Xeran as Effect Payloads
Vindroth can call Xeran runes as effect payloads (usually to define damage type / element / output).
Example structure:
- If object (human) entered size (5) shape (sphere) Than Power (Xeran Fire)
To increase output, you may repeat effect runes:
- … Than Power (Xeran Fire) And Power (Xeran Fire)
With each And Power adding another damage as well as consuming 1 Structure Limit

# Making Your Own Runes
In order to make your own runes you must first be at least a level 5 Runemaster. See [[3.3 Leveling Up]] for more.

Rather than buying runes from a store you can make them yourself. The result is a single-use Xeran or Vindroth rune identical to a store-bought one. It can be sold, traded, or used in the Inscription Ritual.
### Materials
You need a Blank Runic Medium and Affinity Ink. See [[2.6 Materials]] for pricing or make them yourself using the rules below.
##### Making Blank Runic Medium
1. Take any material with an Enchantability of 5 or greater (see [[2.6 Materials]]). You need about 2 oz.
2. Flatten the material so it has at least 1 square inch of space to draw on, the flattening process will depend on the material you chose but they are typically metal so they can usually be forged.
3. Imbue it with 25 mana and make a DC 12 Arcana check. If you succeed it is now a Blank Runic Medium. On failure the material is destroyed.
##### Making Affinity Ink
1. Take 1 oz of crushed obsidian, voidsand, or void slime mass.
2. Imbue it with 25 mana and make a DC 12 Arcana check. If you succeed it is now Affinity Ink. On failure the material is destroyed.
### Manufacturing The Rune
Once you have a Blank Runic Medium and Affinity Ink, follow these steps:

1. **Draw the Rune.** Use your Affinity Ink to draw the rune onto the Blank Runic Medium. It does not matter what you draw with (a feather, a stick, your finger, etc). To draw the rune you must know what it looks like. You know what it looks like if you have another copy of it you can see or if you have successfully inscribed or made it 10 or more times. Make a Sleight of Hand check (DC 15). If you have another rune next to you to copy and have inscribed or made it 10+ times you have advantage on this roll.

2. **Activate the Rune.** Spend the rune's Mana Cost (see Xeran Rune Effects Table; for Vindroth runes the cost is 10) and make an Arcana check (DC = 12 + the rune's DC Mod; for Vindroth runes the DC mod is 5). You may add your Runic DC Check bonus (from Rune Master Level 3 / 3.5) to this roll.

**Success:** You create a fully functional rune.
**Failure:** The materials and mana are consumed and destroyed.
