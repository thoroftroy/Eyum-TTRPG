Spells are learned as base forms, with more powerful versions accessed through upcasting. When a spell is learned, only its base form is known. As the caster advances, they may expend additional mana to cast that same spell at higher tiers, transforming it into more powerful versions with expanded effects. These higher forms are not separate spells and do not need to be learned individually.

Upcasting increases a spell’s power based on its design. This may include increases to damage, range, area, duration, number of targets, or additional effects such as conditions or environmental impact. Each spell defines how it scales, and not all spells scale in the same way. Most spells use a multiplicative mana increase (commonly x2 per tier), though more powerful jumps may require higher scaling such as x3 or greater (and some are lesser). The final listed form of a spell is not a limit. Once a caster reaches that form, the spell may continue to be upcast further according to its scaling rules, allowing for extreme late-game power at significant mana cost.

Spells are governed by Affinity and Mana. The Affinity tied to the spell determines its effectiveness, while Mana determines how often and how strongly it can be cast. More specialized or difficult-to-obtain affinities produce significantly stronger spells, but require greater investment to access and scale.

Some spells require Concentration, remaining active only while the caster maintains focus. Casting another concentration spell ends the previous one immediately. If the caster takes damage to Vitality or Health, they must succeed a concentration check or lose the spell. When a spell applies a condition without specifying its duration, it uses the standard condition system, but the removal DC is replaced with the spell’s save DC. Each turn while concentrating on a spell you must spend 1/5 (rounded down) the mana cost of the spell, if you are unable to spend the mana at the start of your turn the spell ends. 

#### Quick Reference
- **Spell Attack Modifier** = [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Roll Bonus|Attack Roll Bonus]] (Generic) + [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Roll Bonus|Attack Roll Bonus]] (Relevant) + Base Magic Accuracy
- **Spell Attack Damage** = Specified Damage + Base Magic Damage
	- **Spell Attack Damage Bonus:** When a spell references your [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]], look up the relevant affinity on the [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] table.
- **Spell Save DC** = 10 + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]] (Generic) + [[2.1 Affinities#Save DC Bonus|Save DC Bonus]] (Relevant)
	- **Condition Saves:** Conditions imposed from spells use your Spell Save DC instead of their written DC in [[2.4 Conditions]] unless otherwise specified. 
- **Concentration Check** = 1d20 vs DC 12 on taking damage
	- **REMEMBER:** Each turn while concentrating on a spell you must spend 1/5 (rounded down) the mana cost of the spell, if you are unable to spend the mana at the start of your turn the spell ends.
- **Upcasting** = Defined per spell, typically x2 mana per tier


You can find the different types of spells in the following pages:

- [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]]

Elemental spells are spells which have levels. Each one has a base form which you can learn and upcasting them turns them into a more powerful and advanced version of the spell, after the final upcast they can be upcast infinitely like normal spells. When you learn an Elemental spell you only need to learn its base form and you automatically know all of the upcasts, you just can't use them until you meet their prerequisite. 

- [[6.1.2 Unique, Racial, and Healing Spells]]

Unique spells are spells which don't have a leveled path, they simply exist as they are. 
They can be used by anyone who unlocks their prerequisite and learns that spell. Racial spells are spells which are specific to races, they cannot be unlocked or used by any creature who is not of that race. 
All healing spells (in the Healing Spells section, there are technically other spells which heal) are known at all times by any creature with any levels in The Healer. Other prerequisites must still be met. These spells cannot be learned without having at least 1 level in The Healer. Casting a healing spell on an Undead creature will instead deal damage equal to the healing amount. If you use Hallowed instead of Generic affinity the damage is tripled. When a healing spell uses your Generic [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] or Hallowed [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]], look up the relevant affinity on the [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus|Attack Damage Bonus]] table in [[2.1 Affinities#Attack Damage Bonus]].
