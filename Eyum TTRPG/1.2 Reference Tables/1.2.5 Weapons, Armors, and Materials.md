## Weapons
If you are proficient with a weapon, you may add your Strength or Dexterity modifier (depending on the attack) to your attack roll. If you are not proficient, you do not add this modifier and simply roll the d20.

When an attack hits, you add your Strength or Dexterity modifier to the damage roll as normal.

You are always considered proficient with unarmed strikes (fists) and any natural or racial attacks you possess (such as claws, bites, or similar features).

If you have Expertise with a weapon or attack type, you gain Advantage on attack rolls made with it.

## Armors
Armor proficiency is divided into three types: **Light**, **Medium**, and **Heavy**.

You gain proficiency with armor by type. Unless otherwise specified, you must gain these in order: **Light → Medium → Heavy**. You cannot gain Medium proficiency without Light, and you cannot gain Heavy proficiency without both Light and Medium.

If you are proficient with an armor, you ignore all effects listed in its Penalty category. If you are not proficient, all listed penalties apply as written.

You may gain Expertise with any armor type you are already proficient in. If you have Expertise with an armor, increase its AC bonus based on its type:

- **Light Armor:** +1 AC
- **Medium Armor:** +2 AC
- **Heavy Armor:** +4 AC

These bonuses are applied in addition to the armor’s listed AC value.
