You fall prone if falling more than 10 ft.                                                                                                                                                                                                          | [[1.6 Other Rules]]                                      |

#### Character, Skills, and Progression

| Term | Definition | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Strength** | Core stat governing raw physical power. Modifier used for melee attacks, Athletics, and Strength saving throws. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Dexterity** | Core stat governing agility and precision. Modifier used for ranged attacks, finesse weapons, initiative, AC bonus, and Dexterity saving throws. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Constitution** | Core stat governing toughness and endurance. Modifier used for Vitality rolls, concentration checks, and Constitution saving throws. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Intelligence** | Core stat governing knowledge and reasoning. Modifier used for Spot, Nature, Religion, Medicine, and Intelligence saving throws. Determines bonus languages. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Wisdom** | Core stat governing perception and intuition. Modifier used for Mana rolls, Search, Arcana, History, and Wisdom saving throws. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Charisma** | Core stat governing social presence. Modifier used for Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion, and Charisma saving throws. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Stat Point (Stp)** | Points used to increase your six core stats. You start with 24 at level 1. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Stat Point Cost Progression** | Stats cost 1 point each from 0 to 20, 2 points each from 21 to 30, 3 points each from 31 to 40, and so on. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Skill Point (Skp)** | Points used to increase skill modifiers. You start with 5 at level 1. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |

| **Skills** | Trained capabilities tied to an attribute. Athletics (Str), Acrobatics (Dex), Sleight of Hand (Dex), Stealth (Dex), Spot (Int), Search (Wis), Arcana (Wis), History (Wis), Nature (Int), Religion (Int), Medicine (Int), Situational Insight (Wis), Social Insight (Cha), Deception (Cha), Intimidation (Cha), Performance (Cha), Persuasion (Cha), Barter (Cha). | [[1.2 Dice and Skills]] |
| **Saving Throw** | A raw stat roll (no skill proficiency) to resist effects. Six types matching the six core stats. | [[1.2 Dice and Skills]] |
| **Proficiency** | Training in a skill, weapon type, or armor type. Adds your Proficiency Bonus to relevant checks. Without proficiency you cannot add your stat modifier to weapon attacks or gain full armor benefits. | [[1.2 Dice and Skills]], [[2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Tools]] |
| **Proficiency Bonus** | A scaling bonus increasing every 3 levels from +1 to +3. Added to skills and gear you are proficient in. | [[1.2 Dice and Skills]] |
| **Expertise** | True mastery in a skill, weapon, or armor. For skills grants Advantage on checks. For specific weapons grants Advantage on attack rolls. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]], [[2.5 Weapons, Armors, and Tools]] |
| **Background** | Your character's life before the campaign. Grants advantage on tasks your character would logically excel at due to past experience. Options include Acolyte, Criminal, Soldier, Scholar, Artisan, Noble, Hermit, Urchin, Sailor, and Laborer. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]], [[3.2 Character Information]] |
| **Base Accuracy** | A class based accuracy bonus for melee, ranged, or magic attacks. | [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |
| **Base Damage** | A class based damage bonus divided into Melee, Ranged, and Magic categories. Increases primarily through class levels. | [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |
| **Feat** | A special ability gained when leveling up every 3 levels. Types include Passive (always active), Conditional (active under specific conditions), and Active (requires Ap, BAp, or Rp to use). | [[3.4 Feats]] |
| **Title** | A mark of reputation earned through significant achievements. Grants bonuses determined by the GM. Can be Self Assigned or God Granted. Multiple titles can be held simultaneously. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]], [[3.2 Character Information]] |
| **Inspiration** | A reward the GM grants for exceptional roleplay or creativity. Can be spent for advantage on any d20 roll. | [[1.7 Actually Playing The Game]] |
| **Alignment** | A label derived from your Karma score. Good (250 to 500), Neutral (249 to 249), Evil (500 to 250). | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Karma** | A numerical measure of moral standing ranging from 500 to 500. Determines your Alignment. Changed by morally significant actions. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]], [[3.2 Character Information]] |
| **Going Past Limits** | Rules for converting excess gains. Extra spell learning becomes +1 Affinity Point. Duplicate proficiency becomes 3 Skill Points. Duplicate expertise becomes 5 Skill Points. | [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |

Progression within an Archetype is linear and you can take Sub Levels as optional branches. | [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |
| **Skill Tree Level** | A point earned every level (and 1 at level 1) used to unlock or progress in a Path or Archetype. | [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |
| **Archetype** | A specific specialization within a skill tree Path (e.g., The Marksman within Physical Path). | [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |

Racial languages include Bugish, Infernal, Elvish, Fishish, Harpy, Ophedian, Therian, Tullish, and Dead Speak. See [[4.2 Languages]] for full details. | [[4.2 Languages]], [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |

| **Condition** | Temporary states applied to creatures across four categories: Environmental (e.g., On Fire, Soaked, Prone), Physical (e.g., Bleeding, Poisoned, Paralyzed), Mental (e.g., Charmed, Frightened, Demoralized), and Magical (e.g., Cursed, Hasted, Blessed). | **Overkill** | A scaling penalty that increases each time you succeed on an Injury Save. Makes subsequent injury saves harder. Resets when you fail an injury save. | [[2.3 Injuries]] |

#### Magic and Spells

| Term | Definition | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Generic Affinity** | Your baseline magical talent applying to all spells equally. Cannot be increased with Affinity Points. Gained from class features, race bonuses, and feats. | [[2.1 Affinities]] |
| **Specific Affinity** | Attunement to a particular element, force, or domain. Increased with Affinity Points. Higher affinity means stronger damage, higher save DCs, and access to more advanced spells. | [[2.1 Affinities]] |
| **Affinity Prerequisites** | Most affinities require minimum total points in other affinities before they can be unlocked. Fire, Earth, Water, and Air are the four basic elements accessible to all. Radiant and Necrotic each require 3 points in any of the four elements. Psychic requires 3 Radiant plus 3 Necrotic. Advanced affinities like Lightning (10 Fire plus 10 Air) require much higher investment. See [[2.1 Affinities]] for the full table. | [[2.1 Affinities]] |
| **Affinity Point (Affp)** | Points used to increase specific affinities. You start with 5 at level 1. Cannot be used on Generic Affinity. | [[3.1 Building Your Character]] |
| **Area of Effect (AoE)** | Spells or effects that hit an area rather than a single target. Half of Area of Effect damage bypasses Vitality, hitting Health directly. | [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]], [[1.2 Dice and Skills]] |
| **Mana Blast** | A basic spell attack granted by the Magical Path. Fires a bolt of raw magical energy using Generic Affinity for the attack roll. | [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]], [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |
| **Spell Attack Modifier** | Bonus added to the d20 for spell attacks. Formula: Attack Roll Bonus (Generic) plus Attack Roll Bonus (Relevant) plus Base Magic Accuracy. Both bonuses are looked up from the tables in [[2.1 Affinities#Affinity Bonus Tables]]. | [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]], [[2.1 Affinities]] |
| **Spell Attack Damage** | Total damage from a spell. Formula: Specified Damage plus Base Magic Damage. | [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]] |
| **Leveled Spells** | Spells organized in progressive tiers with increasing mana cost, damage, range, and prerequisites. Each tier upgrades the previous form. A spell learned at its base tier can be upcast at higher tiers without needing to learn each form separately. | [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]] |
| **Unique Spells** | A category of spells with standalone effects that do not follow the standard level progression. Each is learned individually. | [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]] |
| **Healing Spells** | A special school of spells known by any creature with levels in The Healer. Heals allies but deals damage equal to the healing amount to undead. Using Hallowed instead of Generic affinity triples the damage against undead. | [[6.1.1 Elemental Spells]], [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |
| **Rune** | Ancient symbols etched into items to tap into fundamental forces. Two types: Xeran for permanent enchantments and Vindroth for programmable spells with conditional logic. | [[6.2 Runes]] |
| **Xeran Rune** | A rune used for permanent enchantments on items. Once applied the item is permanently changed. | [[6.2 Runes]] |
| **Vindroth Rune** | A rune used for complex programmable spellcasting using conditional logic such as If/Then/Else. | [[6.2 Runes]] |
| **Runic Circle** | A binding structure for multiple effect runes on one item. Must include Carbon Runes as stabilizers. Maximum of 10 effect runes. | [[6.2 Runes]] |
| **Effect Rune** | A rune that provides a magical effect, as opposed to a Carbon Rune which only stabilizes. | [[6.2 Runes]] |
| **Carbon Rune** | A stabilizer rune with no magical effect, required to balance volatile energies in a Runic Circle. Carbon Runes must equal Effect Runes minus 1 in any circle (the Carbon Ratio). | [[6.2 Runes]] |
| **Inscription Check** | A d20 roll plus Relevant Affinity Modifier against a DC of 12 plus the rune's DC Mod. Required for each rune in a circle. Failure ruins the entire circle and leaves the item Saturated with unstable mana until Desaturated. | [[6.2 Runes]] |
| **Mana Surcharge** | Additional mana cost added to each rune in a Runic Circle during inscription based on the total circle size. | [[6.2 Runes]] |

#### Deities and Pacts

| Term | Definition | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Deity** | Vast celestial beings that appear as planets, stars, or moons. Three types: Gods (generally good), Fiends (generally evil), and Ancients (neutral eldest beings). Each has a Celestial Body as its physical form. | [[5.1 Deities and Pacts]] |
| **Celestial Body** | The planetary or stellar form of a deity. The deity is the celestial body. They cannot be destroyed by any known means. | [[5.1 Deities and Pacts]] |
| **Deity Ranks** | The hierarchy of deity power. The King (Darwin), Great Sovereigns (Ignivar, Aegirion, Da Vinci, Eldathis, Black Mercy, Iron Pit), Cosmic Law (Newton, Indravajra, and others), Major Forces of Nature, and Lesser Deities. Higher ranks grant greater power and access to higher pact tiers. | [[5.1 Deities and Pacts]] |
| **Pact** | A binding agreement between a character and a deity, fiend, or ancient. Each pact has four parts: The Gain (benefit), The Sacrifice (cost to form), The Time Frame (duration), and The Punishment (consequence for failure). | [[5.1 Deities and Pacts]], [[3.3 Leveling Up]] |
| **Pact Stone** | A physical object used to form a pact with a deity. Types include Godly Pact Stone (found in temples), Fiendish Pact Stone (acquired through cults or black markets), and Ancient Pact Stone (recovered from ruins). | [[5.1 Deities and Pacts]] |
| **Alignment Requirement** | Some deities require a specific Karma based alignment to form pacts. Good requires 250 to 500 Karma. Evil requires 500 to 250. | [[5.1 Deities and Pacts]] |

#### Monsters

| Term | Definition | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Monster Affinity** | A monster's primary damage type applied to all its attacks. Also grants Resistance to that damage type. | [[7.1 Monsters]] |
| **Monster Level** | Monsters can have levels that increase their power. Each level provides +1 Hit Die, +1 Generic Damage Mod, +1 AC, and +1 to all stats. | [[7.1 Monsters]] |
| **Monster Modifiers** | Templates applied to any base monster to alter its stats and abilities. Types include Strong, Fast, Tough, Smart, Savage, Large, Small, Eldritch, Veteran, and the undead modifiers Zombie and Ghost. Cannot stack with other modifiers. | [[7.3.5 Monster Modifiers]] |
| **Monster Drops** | Harvestable materials from slain monsters. See [[2.6 Materials]] for drop tables. | [[2.6 Materials]] |
| **Generic Damage Mod (Monster)** | Bonus damage added to all damage a monster deals. Typically the creature's Affinity damage type. Scales with monster level. | [[7.1 Monsters]] |
| **Difficulty Rating (DR)** | An estimate of how many level 5 players are required to defeat a single monster instance. DR 1 equals one level 5 player. DR 1/5 is closer to a level 1 player. | [[7.1 Monsters]] |
| **Universal Actives (Monster)** | Active abilities that can appear on any monster statblock. See [[7.2 Passives and Actives List]] for the full list. | [[7.2 Passives and Actives List]] |
| **Universal Passives (Monster)** | Passive abilities that can appear on any monster statblock. See [[7.2 Passives and Actives List]] for the full list. | [[7.2 Passives and Actives List]] |
| **Combat Roles** | A monster's tactical function in an encounter. Berserker frenzies attacks, Tanker absorbs hits, Guerrilla strikes and hides, Ranger attacks from range, Stealth ambushes, Mixed uses melee and range, Support buffs allies, Swarmer overwhelms with numbers. | [[7.1 Monsters]] |
| **Slime** | An elemental organism of semi liquid mass bound around a central core. Has no fixed anatomy. Drops collectible mass in jars. Universal passives include Slime Regeneration (core survives separately from body), Affinity Body (heals from its own element), and Scratch Resistance. | [[7.3.1.1 Slimes]] |
| **Golem** | An elemental construct of dense, solidified material bound around a single animating core. Rigid, durable, humanoid form. Role is typically Tanker. Universal passives include Golem Body (Vitality and Health split between body and core), Material Regeneration, and Golem Overcharge. | [[7.3.1.2 Golems]] |
| **Dragon** | A large winged reptilian creature made of biological tissue suffused with elemental energy. Fully organic with elemental infusion. Universal passives include Elemental Aura (damage per turn in radius), Flight, Elemental Form (immunity to own element), and Dragon Vitality (cannot take Health damage until Vitality is exhausted). | [[7.3.1.3 Dragons]] |
| **Chimera** | A hybrid animal hatched from an egg, its body formed from two or more different creatures. Includes griffins, hydras, and sphinxes. Levels determined by age rather than traditional monster leveling. | [[7.3.2.1 Chimera]] |
