The weapon balance analyzer should become the primary balancing tool for the entire equipment system. It should generate a complete data file containing every valid combination of weapon type, material, weapon modification, armor material, shield material, and other equipment variations defined in the handbook. These entries should remain grouped by the handbook's existing categories (Light Weapons, Finesse Weapons, Heavy Weapons, Two-Handed Weapons, Reach Weapons, Thrown Weapons, etc.) and include all calculated statistics such as damage, damage type, accuracy modifiers, AP costs, range, traits, material effects, special effects, average damage, expected damage per round, and any other derived combat values. This data should serve as the source for all graphs and comparisons so every possible equipment combination can be analyzed and compared directly.

The analyzer's interface should provide extensive customization. The left sidebar should contain controls for character attributes, level, attack bonuses, proficiency and expertise, AP availability, critical hit modifiers, extra attacks, and every skill tree node, feat, ability, or passive effect that can influence weapon performance, damage, accuracy, AP efficiency, critical hits, weapon traits, or material interactions. Every modifier should be individually toggleable so builds can be tested at any point in progression. The same sidebar should also allow complete customization of the target being attacked, including AC, armor type, armor material, shield type, shield material, health, vitality, resistances, conditions, cover, distance, and any other handbook-defined factors that affect weapon effectiveness.

The bottom toolbar should function as a filtering system, allowing individual weapon categories, materials, damage types, and equipment groups to be shown or hidden. Users should be able to display only specific categories such as Light Weapons, Heavy Weapons, Two-Handed Weapons, or Shields, and similarly filter by damage type (Piercing, Slashing, Bludgeoning), material, or any other relevant classification. Quick filters should also exist to show only specific damage types or categories while hiding everything else, making it easy to compare subsets of the equipment system without visual clutter.

Rather than graphing only raw damage, the analyzer should focus on meaningful combat metrics. Expected Damage Per Round (DPR) should show how damage output changes against different target defenses. Damage Over Time should show cumulative damage across multiple rounds, helping reveal whether high-accuracy weapons outperform high-damage weapons over realistic combat durations. Hit Chance graphs should show reliability against varying AC values. Damage Per AP should measure combat efficiency within the action economy. Most importantly, Time To Kill (TTK) should serve as the primary balancing metric because it naturally incorporates accuracy, damage, critical hits, AP costs, armor, shields, materials, skill tree bonuses, and other combat modifiers into a single value representing practical effectiveness. TTK answers the most important balancing question: how quickly a weapon defeats actual enemies under specific conditions.

The character build generator should be connected directly to the analyzer rather than relying on manually assigned equipment tiers. Instead of fixed categories such as "Dragon Gear," the analyzer should evaluate all legal equipment combinations and rank them by actual performance. The generator should then automatically classify equipment into No Gear, Worst Gear, Average Gear, and Best Gear tiers based on measured results. Worst Gear should use equipment from the lowest-performing group, Average Gear should use equipment near the statistical middle of all combinations, and Best Gear should use the highest-performing combinations discovered by the analyzer. This allows equipment rankings to update automatically whenever weapons, materials, armor, shields, or balance values change, ensuring that generated character builds always use performance-based gear selections rather than hardcoded assumptions.
