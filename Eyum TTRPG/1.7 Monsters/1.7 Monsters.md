Monsters are just creatures which the player will interact with in the World of Eyum. Most are aggressive but not all. Monsters can take all actions that players can take, however they are not as intelligent as a player so they shouldn't take super intelligent actions. 

Each monster fulfills a specific combat role, which reflects how it fights and what function it serves in an encounter. These roles include:
- Berserker - Constant frenzy of attacks
- Tanker - Tries to take hits to allow other monsters to deal damage
- Guerrilla - Attacks hard then disengages and runs away and hides
- Ranger - Attack from a distance
- Stealth - Sneak up on the player and deal lots of damage then sneak away
- Mixed - Is capable of attacking from melee and ranged
- Support - Makes other monsters stronger or summons other monsters
- Swarmer - Attack in large groups and simply overwhelm players with numbers

A monster’s role influences how it positions itself, what targets it prioritizes, and how it applies pressure during combat.

Monsters are also defined by an affinity, which represents the primary type of energy or damage they embody. Lower-difficulty monsters tend to use more common and easily understood affinities such as Fire, Earth, Water, Air, Radiant, and Necrotic, while stronger monsters may exhibit rarer or more complex affinities. Psychic-affinity creatures are uncommon and appear less frequently.

A monster’s main affinity is applied to its attacks as normal and typically grants it Resistance to that same damage type. Weaknesses usually align naturally with the chosen affinity.
### Difficulty Rating (DR)
A monster’s **Difficulty Rating (DR)** is an estimate of how many level 5 players are required to defeat a single instance of that monster.
- **DR 1** indicates a monster that a single level 5 player can reasonably defeat alone.
- **DR 2** indicates a monster that would require approximately two level 5 players.
- **DR 1/5** represents a much weaker creature, closer in strength to what a level 1 player could handle.
- Creatures weaker than this can be treated as **DR 0**.

DR is not exact. It is intended as a quick reference for encounter balancing rather than a strict calculation.
### Monster Levels
Monsters can have levels, which increase their overall power.

Each level provides the following:
- +1 Hit Die to Vitality
- +1 Hit Die to Health
- +1 Hit Die to Mana
- +1 Generic Damage Mod
- +1 to the monster’s main Affinity
- +1 AC
- +1 to all Stats (Str, Con, Dex (every 3 levels (3,6,9,etc) gain +1 to Wis, Int, and Char as well))

> Each time you get a Hit Die you can roll for it or just add the average number to the total. In the handbook the averages are always added together and shown as (#) next to the die. 
> The generic damage mod is applied to all damage the creature deals. Typically the Affinity of the creature is also added to the attacks so each level up adds around 2 damage to each attack. Note that the generic damage added is always the damage type of the affinity of the creature. 
## Example Monster
**Generic Fire Monster**
- Level 1
> The Level of the monster is 1, so this is its base form, in the next example we will look at the same monster at a higher level. In the handbook monsters are all written as level 1 and you must scale them up manually to your desired level. 
- DR: 1
- AC: 13
> One level five player should be enough to beat this, or hopefully five level one players. Obviously there are so many variables in a fight that this number becomes almost useless but it may give a rough idea of the strength of an enemy.
- Speed: 30
- Type: Berserker
> This is typically used as a guide on how to play the monster. So in combat you would have this monster constantly attacking with no regard to its own health, it would never retreat. 
- Affinity: Fire (+2)
> This functions similarly to player affinity with one exception, its added to all attacks not just magical ones. It's added to saving throw DC's as well. 
- Generic Damage Mod: +0
> Added to all damage dealt by this monster no matter what
- Resistance: Fire
> Always the same as the main Affinity
- Weakness: Water, Ice/Cold
> For weakness, use the opposing affinity. For composite affinities, determine the opposite of each component affinity, then combine those opposites into a resulting affinity; this combined affinity is the weakness. If multiple valid combinations exist, all resulting affinities are considered weaknesses. If no valid combination can be formed, each individual base opposite is treated as a weakness.
- Vitality: 2d10 (10)
> For every point in Con above 10 add +1 Vitality. Do not subtract any health below 10. 
- Health: 1d8 (4)
- Mana: 2d12 (12)
- Stats: STR: 10 | CON: 10 | DEX: 10 | WIS: 4 | INT: 4 | CHAR: 4 
> Unlike a player the Stats don't have much influence other than Con. 

**Passives**
- **Hot to the Touch (Thorns):** When hit by a melee or touch-range attack, the attacker takes 1d4 Affinity damage.
- **Flame Body (Affinity Body):** If this creature takes damage matching its Affinity during the round, it heals 1d4 + Affinity Vitality at the start of its next turn.
- **Creature of Mana:** If Mana falls below 1, the creature takes 1d4 true damage at the start of its turn.
- **Fast Action:** At levels 5 and above gain +1 AP.
> Being able to heal makes this creature really strong, it almost ensures its Kamikaze attacks will be able to happen until it runs out of mana. 

**Actives**
- **Claw:** Make a melee attack against a creature in rage, the target takes 1d6 piercing damage.
> Remember that generic damage mod is added to all damage. 
- **Kamikaze:** Creature explodes dealing 1d8+Affinity damage to all creatures within range, DC 10+Affinity Con save to half damage. The creature takes 2 Affinity damage.
> Kamikaze deals a damage to the creature ensuring they heal some and constantly explode until they run out of mana.

This creature is very powerful but all you have to do to win is simply outlast it. 
## Level 5 Example
**Generic Fire Monster**
- Level 5
- DR: 1
- AC: 18
> Note that the DR did not raise, this is because the DR is based on the level 1 monster. 
- Speed: 30
- Type: Berserker
- Affinity: Fire (+7)
- Generic Damage Mod: +7
> So for most attacks you would add +14 damage
- Resistance: Fire
- Weakness: Water, Ice/Cold
- Vitality: 10d10 + 5 (55)
- Health: 5d8 (20)
- Mana: 10d12 (60)
- Stats: STR: 15 | CON: 15 | DEX: 15 | WIS: 5 | INT: 5 | CHAR: 5 

**Passives**
- **Hot to the Touch (Thorns):** When hit by a melee or touch-range attack, the attacker takes 1d4 Affinity damage.
- **Flame Body (Affinity Body):** If this creature takes damage matching its Affinity during the round, it heals 1d4 + Affinity Vitality at the start of its next turn.
- **Creature of Mana:** If Mana falls below 1, the creature takes 1d4 true damage at the start of its turn.
- **Fast Action:** At levels 5 and above gain +1 AP.

**Actives**
- **Claw:** Make a melee attack against a creature in rage, the target takes 1d6 piercing damage.
- **Kamikaze:** Creature explodes dealing 1d8+Affinity damage to all creatures within range, DC 10+Affinity Con save to half damage. The creature takes 2 Affinity damage.

At higher levels, the same monster becomes significantly more dangerous due to increases in Generic Damage Mod and Affinity, both of which apply across all of its damage sources. This allows it to deal meaningful damage through both direct attacks and passive effects, while maintaining its role as an aggressive, high-pressure threat.
