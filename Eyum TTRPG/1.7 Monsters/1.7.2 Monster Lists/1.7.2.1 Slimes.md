Slimes are elemental organisms composed of semi-liquid matter bound around a central core. Their bodies lack fixed anatomy, instead forming unstable, shifting masses that flow, stretch, and reform continuously. The outer body, referred to as the _mass_, varies in viscosity depending on the slime's elemental affinity. Some are thick and sluggish, others loose and fluid, but all maintain cohesion through an internal anchor.

At the center of most slimes is a _core_, a dense concentration of elemental matter that stabilizes the creature. This core is the only truly vital component of a slime. The surrounding mass acts as both protection and function, allowing movement, interaction, and defense, but it is not required for survival. A slime reduced to only its core remains alive and will gradually regenerate its lost mass over time.

Slimes do not possess traditional organs, bones, or nervous systems. Instead, their awareness and control originate from the core, which directs the surrounding material as if it were an extension of itself. Their movement is typically achieved through slow shifting, rolling, or pulling their mass forward, though less viscous variants may flow rapidly or spread across surfaces. They are able to sense all creatures and objects around them at a range of 120 ft but do not possess any way to hear, smell, or actually see them.

Slimes reproduce through merging. Under normal conditions, slimes will only successfully combine with others of the same elemental type, splitting afterward into new individuals (two slimes merge into four all at one quarter the size of the original). In rare cases, incompatible slimes can be forcibly combined. When this occurs, their cores destabilize and reform into a new configuration, producing a higher-order slime aligned with a more complex affinity. These higher slimes follow the same structural rules as their base forms but exhibit stronger properties and more volatile behavior. When a slime reproduces the four remaining slimes levels will be one quarter of the level of the total levels of the original slimes. Slimes cannot reproduce unless their total levels are at least four.

A slimes size will be determined by its Level. Unless otherwise stated all slimes are approximately 3 ft diameter spheres which squish into a half sphere on the ground. Each level will increase their diameter by 1 ft. A slime's affinity determines all other aspects of its composition. The texture, color, density, and behavior of its mass are direct expressions of its element. All slimes are immune to their own affinity and exhibit weakness to the natural opposing force. While most slimes conform to the core-and-mass structure, exceptions exist. Some variants deviate from this pattern, sacrificing stability for adaptability or regenerative capability.

Slime (mass) which is removed from the core is no longer controllable by the core. It can be used as a material and has the same properties of its affinity. All slimes when killed will drop some amount of their Mass which can be collected and sold and used in potions and such, the mass must be collected in a bottle of some kind. The price of each slimes mass varies based on type. Slime is collected in 32 oz jars and measured accordingly. You can collect up to 1d4 jars of slime per slime killed. The remaining mass will quickly dissipate after one hour.

## Base Slime Descriptions

**Fire Slime**  
A dense, oil-like red mass constantly wreathed in flame. Its surface bubbles and ignites continuously, shedding sparks and heat. At its center floats a rough, charcoal-like core, glowing faintly beneath layers of burning liquid.

**Earth Slime**  
A heavy, compact body composed primarily of packed soil, grit, and loose stone. Its movement is slow and deliberate, often crumbling and reforming as it shifts. The core is a solid chunk of smooth stone embedded deep within its mass.

**Water Slime**  
A loose, gelatinous blue form with no defined core. Its body is entirely fluid, sloshing and reshaping freely. Without a central anchor, it is unstable but highly regenerative, rapidly reforming when in contact with water.

**Air Slime**  
A pale, semi-transparent mass that moves with unusual lightness. Its body flows thinly, almost like mist contained within a membrane. At its center rests a soft, cloud-like core, constantly shifting and dispersing before pulling back together.

**Radiant Slime**  
A luminous golden mass that emits constant light. Its surface is smooth and fluid, glowing from within. The core is a flawless golden sphere suspended at its center, radiating steady brilliance.

**Necrotic Slime**  
A thick, tar-like black substance that moves sluggishly and clings to surfaces. Its body appears oily and uneven, with strands stretching and snapping as it moves. It emits a persistent scent of decay.

**Psychic Slime**  
A smooth, violet mass that gives off a faint internal glow. Its surface is unnaturally still, with subtle ripples that do not match its movement. At its center floats an elongated, oval-shaped, light purple core, pulsing faintly.
## Higher Slime Descriptions

**Lightning Slime**  
A bright yellow-white mass of semi-liquid energy, constantly snapping and twitching in jagged, angular movements. Thin tendrils of electricity arc across its surface, briefly outlining sharp edges before collapsing back into a rounded form. Its body is not smooth, instead it appears fractured by glowing lines that flicker and shift. At its center floats a spiked, branching core resembling a cluster of glass spikes, flashing violently with every movement.

**Steam Slime**  
A pale gray-blue mass with a glossy, wet surface constantly venting thick white vapor. The body appears soft and swollen, expanding and shrinking in slow pulses as steam escapes in bursts. Its edges blur and reform where vapor condenses back into liquid. The core is a smooth, rounded shape, barely visible through layers of rolling fog, glowing faintly with a dull heat.

**Magma Slime**  
A heavy, slow-moving mass of blackened rock split by glowing orange cracks. Thick molten liquid seeps through its surface, dripping and reabsorbing as it moves. The body constantly hardens and breaks apart in chunks, only to melt and reform. At its center is a dense and smooth bright black rock core with orange cracks visible.

**Ice/Cold Slime**  
A translucent pale-blue body made of layered, frozen gel. Its surface is smooth but segmented with sharp crystalline edges that form and dissolve as it moves. Frost gathers along its outer edges, and thin shards occasionally break off and regrow. The core is a clear, faceted crystal suspended in the center, refracting light into faint blue hues.

**Dust Slime**  
A light brown-gray mass made of fine, dry particles barely holding together. Its body constantly sheds and reabsorbs grains, giving it a rough, uneven texture. Parts of it collapse into loose piles before pulling back into shape. The core is a compact, darker sphere that only becomes visible when the surrounding dust compresses inward.

**Mud Slime**  
A thick, dark brown mass with a wet, heavy consistency. It slumps and drags itself forward, leaving trails of sludge behind. Its surface bubbles slowly, with chunks of denser material rising and sinking. The core is buried deep within, a dense, compact sphere that slightly bulges the surface above it.

**Nova Slime**  
A bright white and gold mass that constantly pulses outward in expanding waves of light. Its body appears smooth at rest but rapidly swells and contracts in bursts, like a contained explosion. The outer edges glow intensely, fading slightly between pulses. The core is a brilliant sphere that flashes violently, barely contained within the surrounding mass.

**Solar Slime**  
A dense, glowing orange-gold mass that radiates steady heat. Its surface is smooth but constantly shimmering, as if the air around it is distorting. Unlike flame, its glow is uniform and oppressive. The core is a compact, intensely bright orb that burns steadily without flickering.

**Holy Water Slime**  
A clear, flowing mass of pure liquid with faint golden light drifting through it in slow currents. Its body is smooth and calm, moving with precise, controlled motion. The surface reflects light cleanly, almost mirror-like. At its center is a small, radiant core that glows softly, perfectly spherical and unmoving.

**Starlight Slime**  
A deep indigo mass filled with countless tiny white points of light. Its body appears like a night sky suspended in liquid form, with slow internal drifting motion. The surface is smooth and glassy. The core is nearly indistinguishable, appearing only as a slightly brighter cluster of stars at the center.

**Cursed Slime**  
A dark purple-black mass that constantly twists into unnatural shapes. Its surface is covered in a faint green glow. The texture appears strange, like many jagged pieces moving around on a smooth surface. The core is a small dark purple charcoal like ball wreathed in green flame.

**Ash Slime**  
A soft gray mass composed of fine ash held loosely together. Its body collapses easily into powder before reforming into shape. Thin clouds of ash constantly fall away and drift off before being reabsorbed. The core is a dim, smoldering ember ball, faintly glowing beneath layers of loose material.

**Blight Slime**  
A sickly green-black mass that appears partially decayed. Its surface bubbles with rot, forming and collapsing unevenly. Sections of it appear to dissolve before regrowing moments later. The core is discolored and uneven, leaking faint dark tendrils into the surrounding mass, it looks like a large black ball.

**Poison Slime**  
A thick, bright green liquid with a glossy, almost glassy surface. Its body stretches and clings to anything it touches, leaving behind viscous trails. Small bubbles rise and pop constantly across its surface. The core floats clearly within, a darker green sphere suspended in the liquid.

**Toxin Slime**  
A thin, semi-transparent green-yellow mass that constantly emits faint vapor. Its body is less stable, spreading and retracting rapidly. The edges blur into a haze before snapping back into form. The core is yellowish green and spherical but unstable, it pulses sharply, visible as a bright, unstable shape within the shifting liquid.

**Bloodfire Slime**  
A deep crimson mass that flows like thick blood while emitting faint flickers of dark flame. Its surface ripples constantly, as if under pressure. The color shifts between red and black in waves. The core resembles a beating heart, pulsing visibly and radiating heat with each contraction.

**Tremor Slime**  
A dense, dark brown mass that constantly vibrates. Its surface ripples in tight waves, as if something inside is pushing outward. The body appears compact and heavy, barely deforming despite movement. The core is a solid, stone-like sphere that trembles in place.

**Deluge Slime**  
A deep blue mass that moves in powerful, crashing motions. Its body surges outward in waves before collapsing inward again. The surface appears smooth but is constantly in motion. The core is engulfed in swirling currents, only briefly visible between surges. It looks like a small dark green lumpy rock.

**Shatter Slime**  
A pale, glass-like mass that forms sharp edges and cracks across its surface. Pieces break away into shards before dissolving back into liquid form. The texture constantly shifts between solid and fluid. The core is a small perfect glass sphere.

**Sorrow Slime**  
A dull gray-blue mass that moves slowly and unevenly. Its body seems heavy, sagging downward as it shifts. The surface is smooth but lacks energy, barely reacting to movement. The core is a small gray sphere which emits a faint, dim glow.

**Chaos Slime**  
A constantly rapidly shifting mass of all colors and textures. Sections of it change from liquid to solid to vapor without pattern. Its shape is never consistent, expanding and collapsing unpredictably. The core is a small thin rainbow tube which the mass liquid moves through in a constant loop beneath the surface.

**Infernal Slime**  
A deep red-black mass with contained internal fire. Unlike a fire slime, the flames are compressed inward, visible only as glowing veins beneath the surface. The body is dense and tight, barely expanding. The core is a compact, intensely glowing sphere.

**Metal Slime**  
A smooth, reflective silver mass that flows like liquid metal. Its surface mirrors its surroundings in distorted reflections. Sections harden briefly before melting back into fluid. The core is a polished metallic sphere, perfectly smooth and unmoving.

**Torrent Slime**  
A clear blue mass moving with constant directional force. Its body stretches forward in streams before snapping back into shape. The surface appears smooth but under tension. The core is a large blue-white cube which pulls the entire body forward, creating continuous flow towards it. It's mass lags behind the core.

**Thunder Slime**  
A dense, dark gray mass with faint blue flashes beneath its surface. Its body pulses outward in short bursts, like contained impacts. The surface bulges briefly with each internal surge. The core is a solid gray orb which occasionally flickers with bright white light.

**Mirage Slime**  
A pale, shifting mass of color which slowly transitions through a rainbow. Its surface appears hard and solid. Mirage slimes look very similar to a chaos slime just much more stable and slow. The core is a small rainbow orb.

**Vacuum Slime**  
A thin, nearly invisible mass that appears darker than its surroundings. Its edges stretch inward toward the center constantly. The body seems to collapse into itself. The core appears as a small, perfectly black point in the center.

**Warp Slime**  
A mass of deep purples and cyan blue swirling around a central core, never stopping. The mass always moves clockwise around the core. The core is a large mostly black shape that is so perfectly black it appears to just be a hole in space rather than a sphere.

**Storm Slime**  
A turbulent mass of dark gray liquid filled with flashes of blue-white energy. Its body churns constantly, forming spirals and collapsing currents. The surface appears unstable and violent. The core is surrounded by constant internal motion.

**Frostfire Slime**  
A split mass of pale blue and deep orange material constantly pushing against each other. Sections freeze solid before melting and igniting again. The surface cracks and reforms continuously. The core is a sphere which is about half orange and half blue, the colors constantly swirl around each other and are never quite even.

**Glacial Slime**  
A massive, dense body of pale blue-white material that moves slowly but with force. Its surface is thick and layered, with deep internal fractures. The core is buried deep within, barely visible through dense layers, it is a small blue orb with the texture of snow.

**Void Slime**  
The Void Slime is so perfectly black it looks like a hole in space, it isn't reflect any light at all. The core is a perfect black orb at its center but it cannot really be seen.

**Obsidian Slime**  
A dark, glossy mass with sharp, glass-like surfaces forming across it. Sections harden into rigid planes before shattering and melting again. The core is a solid dark purple black orb.

**Quake Slime**  
A heavy, compact body composed primarily of packed sandstone. It's surface cracks and leaks sand often as it moves. The core is a solid chunk of smooth brown stone embedded deep within its mass.

**Corruption Slime**  
A swollen, uneven mass of dark green and black material that constantly grows and retracts. Its surface mutates in irregular patches, forming and dissolving unpredictably. The core appears as a mushy dark green blob.

**Miasma Slime**  
A pale green-gray mass that partially disperses into gas. Its edges fade into thick, drifting fog before condensing again. The body is loose and constantly changing density. The core is a dark green blob.

**Gel Slime (Gelatinous Cube)**  
A perfectly cubic, transparent mass with sharp edges and flat surfaces. Its body is uniform and rigid compared to other slimes, holding its shape at all times. Objects inside remain suspended and clearly visible. The core is centered precisely, a solid cube within the larger form. A Gel Slime can be any color and slowly dissolves anything inside of it. They are about 2.5x the size of a regular slime and gain 2 ft instead of 1 for each level. The size is based on the length of any size instead of the diameter.

**Atomic Slime**  
A bright, unstable mass of constantly shifting color, reds, blues, greens, and white flashing rapidly across its surface. The body vibrates continuously, with fine distortions rippling across it. The core is an extremely small yellow sphere and is intensely bright and glowing.

**Eldritch Slime**  
A mass of dark red liquid with many black chunks floating around. The slime is lit with a dark purple flame which acts as a black light. Its movement is solid and quick, it's far heavier than the average slime. The core is a swirling black orb wreathed in purple flame.
