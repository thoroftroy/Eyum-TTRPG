Dragons are large, winged reptilian creatures composed of dense biological tissue suffused with elemental energy throughout every cell. Unlike slimes or golems, dragons are fully organic organisms with complete skeletal, muscular, circulatory, and nervous systems, all of which are infused with their elemental affinity. Their flesh, blood, and scales all carry traces of their element, but the core difference between a dragon and a mundane creature is the concentration of this energy. A dragon does not channel its element so much as it is physically made of it: fire dragons have blood that burns, ice dragons have lungs that freeze the air inside them, and lightning dragons have nervous systems that fire in constant, visible arcs.

A dragon's body follows a standard tetrapod layout with two forelimbs, two hind limbs, and two wings. The wings are supported by a modified skeletal structure where elongated finger bones stretch a membrane of thick, element-infused hide. The forelimbs and hind limbs are heavily muscled and end in claws that are dense, pointed growths of hardened keratin and crystallized elemental residue. The tail is long and heavy, used for balance during flight and as a blunt weapon on the ground. The head is large relative to the body, housing a developed brain, sensory organs, and the specialized organ clusters required for elemental breath attacks. The jaw structure allows for a wide gape and powerful bite force, with teeth that are broad, conical, and reinforced with the same crystalline deposits found in the claws.

At the center of a dragon's circulatory system sits the *dragon heart*, a large, multi-chambered organ that serves as both a biological pump and an elemental condenser. The heart draws ambient elemental energy from the dragon's surroundings and from its own metabolic processes, concentrating it into the bloodstream. This element-saturated blood circulates through the body, feeding each tissue with the energy needed to maintain the dragon's passive elemental effects and active abilities. The heart is the only truly vital organ—damage to the brain, lungs, or spine will impair the dragon but not kill it. Only the complete destruction of the heart results in death, and dragons have evolved dense layers of muscle, bone, and scale to protect this organ specifically.

Dragons grow throughout their lives, adding mass and elemental capacity as they age. Growth is driven by the heart's ability to condense elemental energy into new biological tissue. A dragon that feeds regularly and fights often will grow faster than one that does not, but even a sedentary dragon will continue accumulating elemental energy over time, slowly increasing in size and power. Each level represents a measurable increase in the dragon's physical dimensions and elemental output.

- A Level 1 dragon is approximately:
    - **Length:** 20 ft
    - **Wingspan:** 30 ft
    - **Weight:** 2,000 lbs
- Each level:
    - +5 ft length
    - +5 ft wingspan
    - +400 lbs weight

Dragons reproduce through sexual reproduction with distinct male and female individuals. Mating requires specific environmental conditions tied to the element of the dragons involved, fire dragons mate near active volcanoes, ice dragons meet in high-altitude frozen regions, storm dragons mate during severe thunderstorms. The specific trigger conditions are not fully understood, but it is known that dragons will only attempt reproduction when their territory is secure, their elemental energy reserves are full, and the environmental conditions are matched to their element. After mating, the female carries a clutch of fertilized eggs internally for several months. The number of eggs varies by type, typically ranging from 3 to 12. The eggs are laid in a carefully constructed nest infused with the parent's elemental energy, nests are built in locations that naturally provide the required elemental conditions, such as geothermal vents, deep earth fissures, or high-altitude cloud formations. The eggs are leathery and semi-translucent, with the developing embryo visible as a dark shape within a glowing elemental fluid. Incubation takes 8 to 14 months depending on the type. Hatchlings emerge fully formed and immediately capable of elemental breath attacks, though at a fraction of the power of an adult.

When a dragon dies, its elemental saturation causes a rapid release of stored energy. The body releases this energy in a visible discharge that lasts roughly an hour, during which the softer tissues dissolve into elemental vapor. The denser structures, scales, bones, and concentrated muscle tissue, remain intact and can be harvested. Approximately 80% of the dragon's pre-death weight is recoverable as solid material.

The harvested materials break down into three categories:
- **Dragon Scales:** Each dragon type has unique scales with distinct properties. Scales make up approximately 10-20% of a dragon's body weight. These scales retain the dragon's elemental affinity and are used to craft powerful equipment.
- **Dragon Meat:** The muscle tissue of dragons is infused with elemental energy. Dragon meat is a universal material that can be used in various ways. It makes up approximately 70-90% of a dragon's body weight.
- **Dragon Bones:** The skeletal structure of dragons is incredibly dense and retains elemental properties. Dragon bones are a universal material used for crafting and alchemy. Bones make up a portion of the total meat and bone weight.

Dragons can serve any role depending on their element and build.

**All dragons possess the following passive abilities:**
- **Elemental Aura (30 ft)**: Dragons emit an aura of their element in a 30 ft radius. Creatures within this aura at the start of their turn take damage equal to the dragon's Affinity + half their level. This damage does not trigger resistances or weaknesses. The dragon can choose to suppress this aura at any time.
- **Flight**: Dragons have a flying speed equal to their listed speed. They can hover.
- **Elemental Form**: Dragons are immune to damage from their own element. Additionally, Dragons can move through their own element without taking damage or being hindered.

**Unique Skills for Specific Dragons:**
- **Elemental Breath (Active)**: (4 Mana) 30 ft cone. Targets take 3d8 + Affinity damage of the dragon's element type. Targets who fail a DEX save (DC 15 + Affinity) take half damage.
- **Elemental Wing Buffet (Active)**: (3 Mana) All creatures in a 15 ft radius must make a DEX save (DC 15 + Affinity) or take 2d8 + Affinity damage of the dragon's element type and be pushed 10 ft away. Flying creatures have disadvantage on this save.
- **Solar Annihilation (Active)**: (6 Mana) 100 ft line. Targets take 10d10 + Affinity radiant damage. Undead and creatures vulnerable to radiant take an additional 5d10 damage.
- **Supernova (Active)**: (6 Mana) 100 ft radius. Targets must make a CON save (DC 20 + Affinity) or take 15d10 + Affinity radiant damage and be blinded for 3 turns. Those who succeed take half damage and aren't blinded. The dragon takes 10d10 damage from the exertion.
- **Molten Core (Active)**: (6 Mana) The dragon explodes in a 30 ft radius, dealing 8d10 + Affinity fire damage to all creatures. The dragon then reforms at full health after 3 rounds but is stunned during this time.
- **Absolute Zero (Active)**: (6 Mana) 60 ft radius. All creatures in range must make a CON save (DC 20 + Affinity) or take 6d10 + Affinity cold damage and gain 10 stacks of the Frozen condition. Those who succeed take half damage and 3 stacks.
- **Desert Domain (Active)**: (4 Mana) Transform the battlefield into a desert for 1 hour. While active, the dragon is hidden within the sand and can take the Hide action as a bonus action. Additionally, any creature that takes damage from the dragon loses 50% of its movement speed for 1 turn.
- **Swallowed by the Earth (Active)**: (6 Mana) 50 ft radius. The ground beneath all creatures becomes quicksand. Creatures must make a DEX save (DC 15 + Affinity) or be restrained and take 3d6 + Affinity bludgeoning damage at the start of each turn.
- **Solar Fusion (Active)**: (6 Mana) The dragon merges with the sun for 1 round. During this time, it is invulnerable and can take no actions, but all creatures in a 100 ft radius take 10d10 + Affinity radiant damage at the start of each of the dragon's turns. After 1 round, the dragon reappears.
- **Cosmic Form (Active)**: (4 Mana) The dragon transforms into pure starlight for 1 round. While in this form, it can move through any obstacle and cannot be targeted by attacks. At the end of its turn, it reforms and all creatures within 20 ft take 6d10 + Affinity radiant damage.
- **Curse of Ruin (Active)**: (6 Mana) 60 ft radius. All creatures must make a WIS save (DC 18 + Affinity) or have disadvantage on all rolls and all attacks against the dragon have advantage for 1 hour. Additionally, whenever an affected creature rolls a 1, they take 5d10 + Affinity curse damage.
- **Ash Zombie (Active)**: (3 Mana) When the dragon reduces a creature to 0 HP, that creature rises as an ash zombie under the dragon's control at the start of the dragon's next turn. Ash zombies have HP equal to the creature's Level x 5 and deal 1d6 + Level fire damage on each hit.
- **Plague of Wither (Active)**: (6 Mana) 60 ft radius. All creatures must make a CON save (DC 18 + Affinity) or gain 20 stacks of the Withered condition. Those who succeed gain 10 stacks.
- **Venom Empire (Active)**: (3 Mana) Spawn 1d4 giant poisonous snakes from its body each round as a bonus action. These snakes have HP equal to the dragon's Level x 5 and can make a bite attack dealing 2d8 + Level poison damage plus the poisoned condition.
- **Biochemical Warfare (Active)**: (6 Mana) 80 ft radius cloud of toxic gas. Creatures must make a CON save (DC 18 + Affinity) or take 8d10 + Affinity toxin damage and gain the Suffocating condition for 3 turns. Those who succeed take half damage.
- **Blood Ritual (Active)**: (4 Mana) Sacrifice 50% of current health to deal that same amount as damage to all creatures in a 40 ft radius. Creatures can make a DEX save for half damage. The dragon then regains health equal to the damage dealt.
- **Seismic Smash (Active)**: (6 Mana) 100 ft radius. All creatures must make a DEX save (DC 18 + Affinity) or take 10d10 + Affinity bludgeoning damage and be stunned for 2 turns. Those who succeed take half damage and aren't stunned.
- **Tsunami (Active)**: (6 Mana) 100 ft line, 40 ft wide. Creatures in the path must make a STR save (DC 18 + Affinity) or take 8d10 + Affinity bludgeoning damage and be carried 60 ft away. The wave also deals 4d10 + Affinity water damage on the following turn.
- **Crystal Shards (Active)**: (4 Mana) 60 ft radius. Each creature takes 4d10 + Affinity slashing damage and must make a DEX save (DC 16 + Affinity) or take an additional 2d10 + Affinity damage from embedded shards at the start of their next turn.
- **Despair Incarnate (Active)**: (6 Mana) 60 ft radius. All creatures must make a WIS save (DC 18 + Affinity) or gain the Despair condition and take 6d10 + Affinity psychic damage. Creatures that succeed are immune to this effect for 24 hours.
- **Reality Fracture (Active)**: (6 Mana) 50 ft radius. Each creature in the area must roll on the Wild Magic table. The dragon is immune to all effects and can choose the result of its own roll.
- **Hellfire Storm (Active)**: (6 Mana) 60 ft radius storm of infernal fire that lasts for 3 turns. Creatures that start their turn in the storm take 5d10 + Affinity infernal damage. The dragon can move this storm up to 30 ft each turn as a bonus action.
- **Iron Dominion (Active)**: (4 Mana) Summon 1d4 iron constructs that are under its control. These constructs have HP equal to the dragon's Level x 10 and AC 20. They act on the dragon's initiative and can make slam attacks dealing 3d8 + Level damage.
- **Flood (Active)**: (4 Mana) Transform into a massive wave for up to 3 turns. While in wave form, it has no attacks but can move through creatures and objects. Any creature the wave moves through takes 6d10 + Affinity water damage and must make a STR save (DC 16 + Affinity) or be carried 30 ft.
- **Sonic Devastation (Active)**: (6 Mana) 80 ft radius. All creatures must make a CON save (DC 18 + Affinity) or take 10d10 + Affinity thunder damage and be deafened for 1 minute. Those who succeed take half damage.
- **Illusory Domination (Active)**: (4 Mana) Create 3 perfect illusory copies of itself. These copies have HP equal to the dragon's Level x 5 and can make attacks that deal half the dragon's normal damage. The dragon can see through any of its copies' senses.
- **Event Horizon (Active)**: (6 Mana) 40 ft radius region of extreme gravity. Creatures in the area must make a STR save (DC 18 + Affinity) or be pulled to the center and restrained. Any creature that starts its turn in the area takes 4d10 + Affinity void damage.
- **Spatial Tear (Active)**: (6 Mana) 50 ft radius. All creatures in the area are pulled toward the center and take 8d10 + Affinity force damage. Additionally, the dragon can teleport to any location within 100 ft as a bonus action for 3 turns.
- **Tempest King (Active)**: (6 Mana) Control all weather within 1 mile. Can cause hurricanes, tornadoes, or thunderstorms at will. Additionally, summon 1d6 lightning elementals that obey its commands.
- **Thermal Cycle (Active)**: (4 Mana) Rapidly alternate between fire and ice attacks. Each attack that deals fire damage also deals 1d6 cold damage, and vice versa. Additionally, unleash a blast dealing 10d10 + Affinity fire and 10d10 + Affinity cold damage to all creatures in a 40 ft radius.
- **Ice Age (Active)**: (6 Mana) Plunge the entire battlefield into a glacial age for 10 turns. All water on the battlefield freezes, and all creatures take 2d10 + Affinity cold damage at the start of each turn. The dragon can move through ice at full speed.
- **Abyssal Hunger (Active)**: (6 Mana) 60 ft radius. All light is consumed. Creatures must make a CON save (DC 18 + Affinity) or take 6d10 + Affinity void damage and gain the Soul Shattered condition. Additionally, consume one corpse per turn to heal 3d10 + Affinity HP.
- **Volcanic Eruption (Active)**: (6 Mana) 100 ft radius. All creatures must make a DEX save (DC 20 + Affinity) or take 12d10 + Affinity fire damage and be buried under rubble (restrained). Those who succeed take half damage.
- **Earthbreaker (Active)**: (6 Mana) A fissure 10 ft wide and 100 ft long opens, dealing 10d10 + Affinity bludgeoning damage to all creatures in the area. Creatures that fall in take an additional 10d10 + Affinity damage and are trapped at the bottom.
- **Corruption Incarnate (Active)**: (4 Mana) Transform into a being of pure corruption for 3 turns. While transformed, all attacks deal an additional 3d10 + Affinity corruption damage, and any creature that hits the dragon with a melee attack gains the Corrupt condition.
- **Pandemic (Active)**: (6 Mana) 100 ft radius. All creatures must make a CON save (DC 20 + Affinity) or gain 15 stacks of the Plagued condition and 15 stacks of the Poisoned condition. Those who succeed gain 5 stacks of each.
- **Dissolution (Active)**: (6 Mana) Dissolve into a pool of gel and reform elsewhere. While dissolved, can move through any opening and cannot be targeted. At the end of the turn, reform and all creatures within 15 ft take 6d10 + Affinity acid damage.
- **Nuclear Fusion (Active)**: (6 Mana) 100 ft radius. All creatures must make a CON save (DC 22 + Affinity) or take 20d10 + Affinity radiant damage and gain the Radiation condition with 20 stacks. The dragon takes 10d10 damage from the exertion.
- **Reality Collapse (Active)**: (8 Mana) 200 ft radius. All creatures must make an INT save (DC 30 + Affinity) or take 30d10 + Affinity eldritch damage and be transported to a random plane. Those who succeed take 15d10 + Affinity damage. The dragon takes 20d10 damage from the exertion.

## Dragon Descriptions

**Fire Dragon**
Scales of overlapping orange and dark red plates, each one thick as a castle door and hot enough to blister bare skin at a distance. Smoke curls from the gaps between scales constantly. The head is broad and blocky, with nostrils that glow orange from within and release thin jets of flame with each exhale. The eyes are yellow-white coals with no visible pupil. When the dragon opens its mouth, the inside of its throat glows like a forge. The air within thirty feet shimmers with heat distortion, and any exposed paper or dry grass will ignite after prolonged proximity.

**Earth Dragon**
A squat, heavy-bodied dragon with scales that look like overlapping slabs of granite and sandstone. The scales are thick, rough-textured, and often covered in moss or small lichen growths. The head is wide and blunt, with no distinct neck—the skull sits directly on the shoulders. The eyes are small and dark, set deep in bony ridges. The claws are short but incredibly thick, designed for digging and anchoring rather than slashing. When it moves, the ground vibrates. Dust and small pebbles shake loose from the ceiling of any cave it passes through. Its tail is a solid mass of bone and scale that drags behind it, carving a shallow trench.

**Water Dragon**
A long, serpentine body covered in smooth, overlapping scales in shades of deep blue and teal. The scales are flat and polished, giving the dragon a sleek, almost eel-like appearance. The head is narrow and elongated, with a mouth that extends back past the eyes. Gills sit behind the jawline, fluttering open and closed, and the dragon can breathe equally well in air and water. The eyes are large and dark, with a transparent inner eyelid. Its movements are continuous and flowing, even on land, where it shifts its weight side to side in a rolling motion. The tail is broad and finned, used for propulsion. When submerged, the dragon becomes nearly invisible against deep water.

**Air Dragon**
A lean, lightweight body covered in pale gray-blue scales so thin they are translucent at the edges. The scales are small and overlap loosely, allowing maximum flexibility. The wings are unusually large relative to the body, with a broad surface area and long primary feathers made of the same scale material. The head is small and aerodynamic, with a pointed snout and large eyes positioned forward. The bones are hollow, reinforced with internal struts. The dragon weighs far less than its size suggests and can catch thermals to stay aloft for days. On the ground it is awkward, but in the air it moves with instant responsiveness, able to change direction faster than any other dragon.

**Radiant Dragon**
A dense, heavy-bodied dragon covered in overlapping metallic gold scales polished to a mirror finish. The scales are smooth and flat, reflecting the surroundings with perfect clarity. Light radiates from between the scales constantly, creating a soft halo around the dragon's entire body. The head is proportionally large, with a blunt snout and eyes that glow with steady golden light. The inside of the mouth glows bright enough to cast shadows. Wherever the dragon walks, plants in the vicinity lean toward it. The metal of the scales is dense—a single scale weighs as much as a steel shield. When the dragon moves, the scales produce a faint ringing sound like distant bells.

**Necrotic Dragon**
A lean, angular body covered in thin black scales with a matte, non-reflective surface. Fine green veins run through the scales, pulsing with a sickly light. The head is narrow and skeletal-looking, with flesh pulled tight over the skull and hollow eye sockets that glow with a dim green point deep inside. The teeth are discolored and uneven. The dragon's breath is visible as a faint green fog even when it is not attacking. Vegetation within fifty feet of its resting place turns brown and dies over the course of a few days. The air around it carries the smell of rot and damp earth. Its claws are dark and glossy.

**Psychic Dragon**
A long-bodied dragon with smooth, semi-translucent scales that shift slowly through shades of violet, indigo, and deep purple. The scales are thinner than most other dragons, giving the body a soft, almost fleshy appearance. The head is elongated and featureless except for three large eyes arranged in a triangle on the front of the skull. These eyes are solid purple with no visible pupil or iris. The dragon's mouth is small and rarely opens. The body pulses with faint internal light in rhythm with its heartbeat. Creatures near it experience a low-pressure sensation behind the eyes and a persistent feeling of being watched from all directions at once. The dragon does not make sound when it moves.

**Lightning Dragon**
A sharp-angled body covered in scales of oxidized copper and blue-white crystal formations. The scales are not smooth—many project outward as jagged points and ridges, creating a silhouette full of spikes and edges. Thin arcs of electricity jump constantly between these projections, creating a crackling sound that surrounds the dragon at all times. The head is angular, with crystal growths around the jaw and brow that glow brighter when the dragon is agitated. The eyes are white-blue and emit visible light. Ozone accumulates in the air around it. Hair stands on end and metal objects vibrate within thirty feet. The dragon moves in bursts—still for a moment, then suddenly in a different position with a thunder crack.

**Steam Dragon**
A bulky, round-bodied dragon barely visible through the constant thick cloud of white-gray steam that surrounds it. The scales beneath the steam are pale gray and wet-looking, slick with condensed moisture. The head is broad with small eyes and wide nostrils that vent steam in steady pulses. The mouth opens wide to release the pressurized breath attack. Scale surfaces near the mouth and throat are stained with mineral deposits from years of condensation. The dragon moves slowly and heavily, leaving damp footprints that linger for hours. The fog it generates extends roughly forty feet in all directions, obscuring vision and muffling sound.

**Magma Dragon**
An immensely heavy dragon built like a walking mountain of black obsidian and dark stone. The scales are thick, irregular plates of volcanic glass, split by glowing orange cracks where molten rock flows just beneath the surface. These cracks form a branching network across the entire body, brightest at the chest and throat. The head is massive and blocky, with a lower jaw that hangs slightly open to vent heat. The eyes are liquid orange, bright enough to leave afterimages. Each step melts the ground beneath its feet, leaving glowing footprints that cool to black glass over several minutes. Smoke rises from every joint. The air is thick with the smell of sulfur.

**Ice/Cold Dragon**
A crystalline body built from layers of clear and milky-white ice that form overlapping scale-like plates. The ice is dense and hard, and the scales are segmented by deep cracks that never quite reach the underlying flesh. The head is angular and sharp-featured, with a mouth that releases a constant stream of frozen mist. The eyes are pale blue and glow faintly. Frost crystallizes on everything within fifty feet of the dragon—leaves become brittle, puddles freeze solid, and breath condenses instantly into ice particles. Icicles hang from the jaw, wings, and tail at all times, constantly breaking off and regrowing. The dragon's body creaks and pops as it moves, like a frozen lake shifting.

**Dust Dragon**
A thin, insubstantial-looking body made of tightly compressed sand and fine grit held in a scale-like pattern that is constantly eroding and reforming. The scales are not solid—they are layers of compacted dust that slough off and reaccumulate as the dragon moves. The head is vaguely defined, with darker hollows where the eyes should be and a mouth that is just a constantly shifting crack in the dust. The body is lighter than it appears; the dragon can stand on surfaces that would not support other dragons. A trailing cloud of fine particles follows it everywhere. The air is thick and gritty within a wide radius. The dragon's voice, when it speaks, sounds like wind through a desert canyon.

**Mud Dragon**
A thick-bodied dragon whose entire surface is coated in a slow-moving layer of dark brown sludge several inches deep. The underlying scales are never visible—everything is covered in dripping, semi-liquid mud that constantly sloughs off and is replaced. The head is barely distinguishable from the body, just a rounded mass with two dim points where the eyes are and a sluggish opening for a mouth. Mud pools beneath it wherever it stands. The dragon moves slowly but continuously, leaving a wide trail of sludge behind. The smell is wet earth and decay.

**Nova Dragon**
A broad-chested dragon covered in white and gold scales that pulse with expanding rings of light. The pattern is not random—each pulse starts at the center of the chest and radiates outward across the body in waves. Between pulses, the scales cool to a dull white before flaring again. The head is large and boxy, with a mouth that glows from within even when closed. The eyes are white orbs that leave bright trails when they move. Looking directly at the dragon causes spots in your vision. The heat it radiates is dry and intense, like standing near a furnace door.

**Solar Dragon**
A heavily built dragon with scales of polished brass and bright gold arranged in tight, overlapping rows. The scales radiate steady, even heat and glow with a constant orange-gold light, brighter at the spine and dimmer toward the belly. The head is broad with a blunt snout and a lower jaw that hangs slightly open to release waves of hot air. The eyes are bright orange points. The dragon moves deliberately, with no wasted motion. Shadows cast by its body are unusually sharp and dark due to the intensity of its glow. The air around it shimmers constantly.

**Starlight Dragon**
A lean dragon covered in deep blue-black scales dotted with thousands of tiny bright points that resemble a clear night sky. The points shift slowly, mirroring the actual positions of stars overhead. Between the star-points, the scales are so dark they appear to absorb light. The head is narrow, with eyes that are small points of white light. The wings are broad and edged with a faint blue-white glow. When the dragon flies at night, it is nearly indistinguishable from the sky above. Its movements are silent and deliberate.

**Cursed Dragon**
A dragon with scales of dark purple-black that seem to absorb light rather than reflect it. Dark patterns in the shape of spirals and branches spread across the body, and these patterns shift slowly, as if something is moving just beneath the surface. The head is angular with a pronounced brow ridge that casts the eyes in permanent shadow. What is visible of the eyes is a dim green point. The scales near the mouth and claws are stained a darker, almost brown-black. The air around it feels heavy and still. Creatures near it report a persistent feeling of unease.

**Ash Dragon**
A thin, almost emaciated-looking body covered in loose gray ash that flakes off constantly. The underlying form is visible only when the ash shifts—a body of dark gray, compacted cinder material. The head is skull-like, with ash collecting in the hollows of the cheeks and eye sockets. A dim orange glow pulses in the chest cavity, visible through gaps in the ash layer. Embers fall from the mouth when it opens. The dragon leaves a trail of fine gray powder everywhere it goes. Its voice is dry and crackling, like embers settling.

**Blight Dragon**
A swollen, uneven body covered in lumpy, discolored scales that are patchy with rot. Sections of the hide are green-black and weeping, other sections are dry and cracked. Fleshy growths and fungal patches protrude between scales. The head appears partially decayed, with sections of the snout missing or eroded, revealing discolored bone beneath. The eyes are milky and unfocused. The dragon's breath smells like a compost heap turned in summer. Flies and other insects cluster around it constantly.

**Poison Dragon**
A sleek-bodied dragon with scales of bright, uneven green that transition to yellow at the edges. The scales are wet-looking, coated in a thin, clear film of venom that drips constantly. The head is narrow with a long snout, and venom pools in the corners of the mouth. The dragon's claws are discolored a darker green at the tips. Puddles of clear liquid collect beneath it wherever it rests. The smell is sharp and chemical, like bleach left in the sun. Small animals that come too close are found dead without visible wounds.

**Toxin Dragon**
A thin-bodied dragon whose form is difficult to see clearly because of the constant faint yellow-green haze that surrounds it. The scales underneath are pale green, but they are partially obscured at all times by the cloud of fine toxic particles that drifts from the body. The head is small and reclusive, often tucked down against the body. The eyes are barely visible slits. The dragon's skin is slightly tacky to the touch. The haze extends roughly fifteen feet in all directions, thick enough to cause coughing and watering eyes.

**Bloodfire Dragon**
A muscular dragon with deep crimson scales that look wet and uneven, like dried blood that has been layered repeatedly. Faint red-orange light flickers beneath the scales, visible at the edges and in the gaps between plates. The head is broad with a wide jaw and teeth that are stained red-brown. The eyes are dark red points with no visible white. The dragon's body temperature is noticeably higher than its surroundings—the air around it has a metallic, hot-copper smell. Veins visible on the neck and chest glow faintly through the skin between scales.

**Tremor Dragon**
An extremely heavy, low-slung dragon built close to the ground. The scales are thick plates of compressed earth-tone stone—browns, grays, and dull oranges—fitted tightly together with almost no gaps. The head is wide and flat, resting directly on broad shoulders. The eyes are small and dark, nearly hidden under a heavy brow ridge of solid rock. The tail is short and thick, used as a counterbalance. Every footstep produces a detectable vibration in the ground. Small objects on nearby tables will shift and fall. The dragon's voice is a low rumble felt through the feet as much as heard through the ears.

**Deluge Dragon**
A long, flowing body that never holds a fixed shape. The form is composed entirely of pressurized water held in a dragon-like silhouette by sheer force of will and elemental concentration. The outline shifts constantly—edges blur, the tail stretches and shortens, the wings ripple. The water is deep blue and semi-transparent, with debris and aquatic plants sometimes visible suspended inside. The head reforms continuously, with the "eyes" being momentary swirls in the currents. The sound of the dragon moving is the sound of a river in flood. Wherever it passes, everything is soaked.

**Shatter Dragon**
An angular dragon whose body is composed of sharp, faceted crystal plates in pale gray and white with faint blue internal reflections. Every edge is sharp. The scales do not overlap smoothly—they meet at hard angles, creating a surface of interlocking shards. When the dragon moves, the plates grind against each other with a sound like broken glass shifting. The head is all sharp planes, with crystal shards projecting from the jaw and brow. The eyes are clear crystal orbs with a blue point at the center. Small fragments constantly break off and regrow, leaving a trail of crystalline dust.

**Sorrow Dragon**
A heavy-bodied dragon with scales of flat, dull gray that reflect almost no light. The scales droop slightly at the edges, giving the dragon a sagging appearance. The head hangs low, with the neck curved down so the snout is near the ground. The eyes are half-lidded and a pale, washed-out blue. The dragon moves slowly, with a dragging quality to its step. Its wings are kept folded tight against the body. The air around it feels cold and still. Other animals avoid the area. The dragon seldom vocalizes, but when it does the sound is a low, drawn-out moan.

**Chaos Dragon**
A body that is never the same shape or color for more than a few seconds. The scales cycle through the full spectrum—red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple—without pause, and the texture of the body shifts between smooth, rough, wet, and dry unpredictably. The head reforms continuously; the number and position of eyes changes, the jaw elongates and shortens. Sometimes the dragon appears to have two heads, then one, then something else entirely. The wings are sometimes there, sometimes not. Looking at it for too long causes disorientation.

**Infernal Dragon**
A stocky, powerfully built dragon covered in scales of deep black and dark red edged with a faint orange glow. The scales do not reflect light—they seem to generate their own faint internal illumination, a deep red that is visible in the gaps between plates. The head is blocky, with horns that curve forward and slightly down. The eyes are red points. The mouth is always slightly open, revealing a throat lit by shifting orange flame. The heat it radiates is different from a fire dragon's—it feels pressing and invasive rather than simply hot. The smell is sulfur and burned metal. Its footsteps leave scorch marks.

**Metal Dragon**
A massive, blocky dragon whose entire body is composed of solid metal—primarily dark iron with streaks of nickel and chromium. The scales are thick plates that slide against each other with audible grinding sounds. The head is a solid metal block with no distinct features other than glowing orange slits for eyes and a mouth that opens along a visible seam.
